A Sense of US:
Group Quarters vs Households


In the 2010 Decennial Report, the US census reported an increase in the household-population from 2000 to 2010. This website aims to provide more information regarding the distribution of household-population growth and decline across the US.

The change that is being measured is the percent change in household-population per county. This is defined as the change in household-population percentile from 2000 to 2010. These household-population percentiles are in turn calculated by dividing total population by household-population.

The Census reported that population had grown from 281421906 in 2000 to 308729797 in 2010. This growth came with equally impressive growth in household-population, from 273643273 to 300742927. This suggests that the increase in percentage household-population may be the result of greater financial stability in the country. Census reports are used as the source for all the information included in this report.
