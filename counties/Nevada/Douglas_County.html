Douglas County, Nevada

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -4.8490763%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 5918 people in Douglas County, Nevada lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 35105 people in Douglas County, Nevada lived in family households.

In 2000, 236 people in Douglas County, Nevada lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 41259 people in Douglas County, Nevada lived in the county.

In 2010, 2172 people in York County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 10756 people in York County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2010, 737 people in York County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 13665 people in York County, Nebraska lived in the county.

Douglas County holds the first permanent settlement in Nevada. The town of Genoa was originally settled in 1851 by Mormon traders selling goods to settlers on their way to California. Named for Stephen A. Douglas, famous for his 1860 Presidential campaign and debates with Abraham Lincoln, Douglas County was one of the first nine counties formed in 1861 by the Nevada territorial legislature.
