Eureka County, Nevada

In 2000, 268 people in Eureka County, Nevada lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 1374 people in Eureka County, Nevada lived in family households.

In 2010, 7815 people in Douglas County, Nevada lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 38952 people in Douglas County, Nevada lived in family households.

Eureka County was established in 1873 and formed from Lander County after silver was discovered more than 100 mi east of Austin. The residents of the new mining camp complained that Austin was too far to go for county business and a new county was created. It was named for the ancient Greek term, ''Eureka'', meaning, "I have found it." This term was used earlier in California and other locations. Eureka has always been the county seat.
