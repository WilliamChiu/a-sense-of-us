White Pine County, Nevada

In 2000, 1287 people in White Pine County, Nevada lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6653 people in White Pine County, Nevada lived in family households.

In 2010, 768 people in Pershing County, Nevada lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 4304 people in Pershing County, Nevada lived in family households.

White Pine County was established in 1869 from Lander County and named after the heavy growth of Limber Pine trees in the area which were at the time called white pine. Hamilton was the first county seat from 1869 to 1887 when it was replaced after a fire by Ely.
