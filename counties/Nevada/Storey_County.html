Storey County, Nevada

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.9731531%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 639 people in Storey County, Nevada lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 2756 people in Storey County, Nevada lived in family households.

In 2000, 4 people in Storey County, Nevada lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 3399 people in Storey County, Nevada lived in the county.

In 2010, 1160 people in Mineral County, Nevada lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 3560 people in Mineral County, Nevada lived in family households.

In 2010, 52 people in Mineral County, Nevada lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 4772 people in Mineral County, Nevada lived in the county.

Storey County was created in 1861 and named for Captain Edward Farris Storey, who was killed in 1860 in the Pyramid Lake War. It was the most populous county in Nevada when organized in 1861. Virginia City is the county seat. It was originally to be named McClellan County after General George B. McClellan, who later ran unsuccessfully against Lincoln for President in the 1864 election. The County greatly benefited from the discovery of Comstock Lode silver. The population collapsed after the Lode gave out, and hit a minimum of 568 in the 1960 Census. Since then, its population has partially recovered due to its relative proximity to Reno.
