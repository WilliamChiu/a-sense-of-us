Nye County, Nevada

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -3.787303%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 5223 people in Nye County, Nevada lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 27031 people in Nye County, Nevada lived in family households.

In 2000, 231 people in Nye County, Nevada lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 32485 people in Nye County, Nevada lived in the county.

In 2010, 975 people in Lincoln County, Nevada lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 4131 people in Lincoln County, Nevada lived in family households.

In 2010, 239 people in Lincoln County, Nevada lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 5345 people in Lincoln County, Nevada lived in the county.

Nye County was established in 1864 and named after James W. Nye, who served as the first governor of the Nevada Territory and later as a U.S. Senator from the state. The first county seat was Ione in 1864, followed by Belmont in 1867, and finally by Tonopah in 1905.
