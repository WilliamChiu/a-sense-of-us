Saline County, Missouri

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 5.847442%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 3626 people in Saline County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 18475 people in Saline County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2000, 1655 people in Saline County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 23756 people in Saline County, Missouri lived in the county.

In 2010, 2416 people in Ste. Genevieve County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 15452 people in Ste. Genevieve County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2010, 277 people in Ste. Genevieve County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 18145 people in Ste. Genevieve County, Missouri lived in the county.

Historically Saline County was occupied for thousands of years by succeeding cultures of Missouri Native Americans. Saline County was organized by European-American settlers on November 25, 1820, and was named from the salinity of the springs found in the region. After periods of conflict as settlers competed for resources and encroached on their territory, the local Native Americans, including the Osage nation, were forced by the US to relocate to reservations in Indian Territory, first in Kansas and then in Oklahoma.
