Morgan County, Missouri

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -7.050556%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2702 people in Morgan County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 16320 people in Morgan County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2000, 287 people in Morgan County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 19309 people in Morgan County, Missouri lived in the county.

In 2010, 1897 people in Moniteau County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 12394 people in Moniteau County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2010, 1316 people in Moniteau County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 15607 people in Moniteau County, Missouri lived in the county.

Morgan County was organized in 1833 upon separation from Cooper County. It is named in honor of Revolutionary War General Daniel Morgan. Some parts of Morgan County touch the Lake of the Ozarks.
