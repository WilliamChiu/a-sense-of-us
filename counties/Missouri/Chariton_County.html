Chariton County, Missouri

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.72441%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 8438 people in Chariton County, Missouri lived in the county.

In 2010, 976 people in Carter County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5249 people in Carter County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2010, 40 people in Carter County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 6265 people in Carter County, Missouri lived in the county.

Chariton County was settled primarily from the states of the Upper South, especially Kentucky and Tennessee. They brought slaves and slaveholding traditions with them, and they quickly started cultivating crops similar to those in Middle Tennessee and Kentucky: hemp and tobacco. Chariton was one of several counties settled mostly by southerners to the north and south of the Missouri River. Given their culture and traditions, this area became known as Little Dixie and Chariton County was at its heart. It was heavily pro-Confederate during the American Civil War.
