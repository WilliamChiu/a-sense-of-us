Lafayette County, Missouri

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -4.2579594%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 4056 people in Lafayette County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 27967 people in Lafayette County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2000, 937 people in Lafayette County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 32960 people in Lafayette County, Missouri lived in the county.

In 2010, 9453 people in Johnson County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 39471 people in Johnson County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2010, 3671 people in Johnson County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 52595 people in Johnson County, Missouri lived in the county.

Lafayette County was settled primarily from migrants from the Upper Southern states of Kentucky, Tennessee and Virginia. They brought slaves and slaveholding traditions and started cultivating crops similar to those in Middle Tennessee and Kentucky: hemp and tobacco. As a result, this part of Missouri, and neighboring counties, became known as Little Dixie. In 1860 slaves made up 25 percent or more of the county's population, and the county was strongly pro-Confederate during the American Civil War.
