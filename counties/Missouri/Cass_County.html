Cass County, Missouri

In 2000, 8681 people in Cass County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 72445 people in Cass County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2010, 13635 people in Cape Girardeau County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 58216 people in Cape Girardeau County, Missouri lived in family households.

The Harrisonville area was originally inhabited by the "Dhegilha" Native American subgroup. The Osage, Quapaw, Omaha, Ponca and Kansa tribes make up this Siouan linguistic group. The Kansa tribal range extended southward from the Kansas-Missouri River junction as far as the northern edge of Bates County, Missouri, taking in the sites of modern Pleasant Hill, Garden City, Archie and Drexel. On their southeastern border they were neighbors of the Osage, although there is no evidence that either of these tribes ever had a truly permanent settlement in the territory of Cass County.
