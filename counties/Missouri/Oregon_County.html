Oregon County, Missouri

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.69540143%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1401 people in Oregon County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 8833 people in Oregon County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2000, 110 people in Oregon County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 10344 people in Oregon County, Missouri lived in the county.

In 2010, 2964 people in New Madrid County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 15660 people in New Madrid County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2010, 332 people in New Madrid County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 18956 people in New Madrid County, Missouri lived in the county.

Oregon County was created in 1845, at a time when the Oregon boundary dispute was a major issue.
