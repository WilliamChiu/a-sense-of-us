Scott County, Missouri

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -5.010557%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 5054 people in Scott County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 34798 people in Scott County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2000, 570 people in Scott County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 40422 people in Scott County, Missouri lived in the county.

In 2010, 3906 people in Saline County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 17980 people in Saline County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2010, 1484 people in Saline County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 23370 people in Saline County, Missouri lived in the county.

This area along the Mississippi River was long occupied by indigenous peoples. In historic times, the large and powerful Osage people dominated a large territory including this area and up and west from the later St. Louis, Missouri. Cape St. Croix, a rock island in the Mississippi River, has a cross erected by Jesuit missionary Father De Montigny, who traveled with early French explorers in 1599. Some French colonists established trading relationships with the Osage; the traders were based in St. Louis, founded in the late 18th century.
