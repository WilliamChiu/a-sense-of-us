Ripley County, Missouri

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -8.58407%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1778 people in Ripley County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 11572 people in Ripley County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2000, 159 people in Ripley County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 13509 people in Ripley County, Missouri lived in the county.

In 2010, 3691 people in Randolph County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 19268 people in Randolph County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2010, 2455 people in Randolph County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 25414 people in Randolph County, Missouri lived in the county.

Located deep in the rugged, wooded hills of Southern Missouri, the area remained sparsely settled throughout the 19th century. Although organized in 1833, the county did not assume its present size until 1872.
