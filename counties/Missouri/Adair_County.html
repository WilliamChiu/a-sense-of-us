Adair County, Missouri

In 2000, 6431 people in Adair County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 15756 people in Adair County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2010, 2514 people in Winston County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 16276 people in Winston County, Mississippi lived in family households.

The first permanent settlement in Adair County began in 1828. Many of the first settlers were from Kentucky, and Adair County was named for John Adair, a respected Governor of Kentucky. This was 25 years after the Louisiana Purchase, seven years after Missouri was granted statehood, and four years after the Sac and Fox Native American tribes surrendered their claims to the land. The original settlement was called "Cabins of White Folks," or simply, "The Cabins," and was located six miles (10 km) west of present-day Kirksville along the Chariton River. The area was named after John Adair who was Governor of Kentucky at the time.
