Wayne County, Missouri

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -3.0545592%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1970 people in Wayne County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 11147 people in Wayne County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2000, 142 people in Wayne County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 13259 people in Wayne County, Missouri lived in the county.

In 2010, 3348 people in Vernon County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 16945 people in Vernon County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2010, 866 people in Vernon County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 21159 people in Vernon County, Missouri lived in the county.

Wayne County was created in December 1818 by the last Missouri Territorial Legislature out of parts of Cape Girardeau and Lawrence counties and thus predates the state of Missouri being admitted to the Union. In March 1819, Congress established the Territory of Arkansas and most of Lawrence County, Missouri Territory became Lawrence County, Arkansas Territory. The small strip of “Old Lawrence County” that had been orphaned in Missouri was added to Wayne County by the Missouri State Constitution of 1820. The Osage Strip along the Kansas border was added in 1825. From 1825-1831, Wayne County was an enormous area larger than Massachusetts, Connecticut, Rhode Island, and Delaware combined. All or part of 32 counties in Missouri were at one point part of Wayne County.
