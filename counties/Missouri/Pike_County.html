Pike County, Missouri

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 12.276972%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2297 people in Pike County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 13801 people in Pike County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2000, 2253 people in Pike County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 18351 people in Pike County, Missouri lived in the county.

In 2010, 2636 people in Perry County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 16049 people in Perry County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2010, 286 people in Perry County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 18971 people in Perry County, Missouri lived in the county.

Pike County is a county located in the eastern portion of the U.S. state of Missouri. As of the 2010 census, the population was 18,516. Its county seat is Bowling Green. The county was organized December 4, 1818, and named for explorer Zebulon M. Pike. The folksong Sweet Betsy from Pike is generally thought to refer to Pike County, Missouri.
