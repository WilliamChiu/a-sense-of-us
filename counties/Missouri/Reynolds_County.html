Reynolds County, Missouri

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.8440604%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 934 people in Reynolds County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 5599 people in Reynolds County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2000, 156 people in Reynolds County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 6689 people in Reynolds County, Missouri lived in the county.

In 2010, 1337 people in Ralls County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 8776 people in Ralls County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2010, 54 people in Ralls County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 10167 people in Ralls County, Missouri lived in the county.

Reynolds County was officially organized on February 25, 1845. It is still an area of rugged beauty near the geologic center of the Ozark Highland. Reynolds County was formerly part of Ripley County which was formed in 1831 and part of Wayne County which was formed in 1818. It was also previously part of Washington County and part of Ste. Genevieve County.
