From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.23332834%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2308 people in Wright County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 15429 people in Wright County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2000, 218 people in Wright County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 17955 people in Wright County, Missouri lived in the county.

In 2010, 2201 people in Wayne County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 11187 people in Wayne County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2010, 133 people in Wayne County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 13521 people in Wayne County, Missouri lived in the county.

Wright County is bordered by Laclede County on the north, Texas County on the east, Douglas County on the south, and Webster County on the west. It is in the part of the state considered Southwest Missouri. Formed from part of Pulaski County on January 29, 1841, Wright County was named in honor of Silas Wright, a prominent New York Democrat. The county seat of Hartville was probably named after Hartsville, Tennessee, from where many early settlers originally came. Wright County lost part of its land in 1845 to Texas County, in 1849 to Laclede, and in 1855 a big chunk to Webster.
