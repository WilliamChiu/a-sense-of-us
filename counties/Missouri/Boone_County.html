Boone County, Missouri

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 5.109203%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 31042 people in Boone County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 95477 people in Boone County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2000, 8935 people in Boone County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 135454 people in Boone County, Missouri lived in the county.

In 2010, 2541 people in Bates County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 14197 people in Bates County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2010, 311 people in Bates County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 17049 people in Bates County, Missouri lived in the county.

Boone County was organized November 16, 1820, from a portion of the territorial Howard County. The area was then known as Boone's Lick Country, because of a salt lick which Daniel Boone's sons used for their stock.
