Clark County, Missouri

In 2000, 1018 people in Clark County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6276 people in Clark County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2010, 2273 people in Cedar County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 11553 people in Cedar County, Missouri lived in family households.

Missouri folklorist Margot Ford McMillen wrote that early settlers were attracted by Clark County's good and inexpensive agricultural land. One section was called "Bit Nation" because land was sold there for just twelve and one-half cents ("one bit" of a Spanish dollar) an acre.
