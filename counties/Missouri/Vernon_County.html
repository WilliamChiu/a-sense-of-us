Vernon County, Missouri

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 3.7370086%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2920 people in Vernon County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 16480 people in Vernon County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2000, 1054 people in Vernon County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 20454 people in Vernon County, Missouri lived in the county.

In 2010, 1149 people in Sullivan County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5457 people in Sullivan County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2010, 108 people in Sullivan County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 6714 people in Sullivan County, Missouri lived in the county.

Vernon County suffered considerable damage during the American Civil War. The courthouse was burned by Union Army soldiers on May 23, 1863, along with the entire town of Nevada. The present courthouse dates to 1907. Vernon County was one of four Missouri counties that were completely depopulated by Union General Thomas Ewing's notorious General Order No. 11 (1863). Most of the inhabitants would not be allowed to return to their homes until after the Civil War ended in May 1865.
