Sullivan County, Missouri

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.0368466%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1124 people in Sullivan County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 5944 people in Sullivan County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2000, 151 people in Sullivan County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 7219 people in Sullivan County, Missouri lived in the county.

In 2010, 963 people in Shelby County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5212 people in Shelby County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2010, 198 people in Shelby County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 6373 people in Shelby County, Missouri lived in the county.

In 1682, René-Robert Cavelier, Sieur de la Salle claimed the territory west of the Mississippi River for France, which included present-day Sullivan County. The United States acquired this region under terms of the Louisiana Purchase on July 4, 1803. Twenty-one years later, the Sac (Sauk), Meskwaki (Fox), and Iowa Native American nations ceded their tribal land to the U.S. government under two treaties in August 1824.
