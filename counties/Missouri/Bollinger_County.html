Bollinger County, Missouri

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.6464958%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1287 people in Bollinger County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10578 people in Bollinger County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2000, 164 people in Bollinger County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 12029 people in Bollinger County, Missouri lived in the county.

In 2010, 1760 people in Barton County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 10552 people in Barton County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2010, 90 people in Barton County, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 12402 people in Barton County, Missouri lived in the county.

Sixty million years ago, dinosaurs roamed the area that would eventually become known as Bollinger County. The "Missouri Dinosaur," a hadrosaur (duck-billed), was discovered at a dig near Glen Allen. It has produced bones from different dinosaurs and aquatic species.
