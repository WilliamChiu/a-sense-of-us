Polk County, Missouri

In 2000, 3453 people in Polk County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 21939 people in Polk County, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2010, 9032 people in Phelps County, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 33123 people in Phelps County, Missouri lived in family households.

Polk County was organized and separated from Greene County on January 5, 1835. Its original boundaries were later reduced to set up Dade, Dallas, and Hickory counties. The name was suggested by a local pioneer, Ezekiel Madison Campbell, to honor his grandfather Ezekiel Polk who was a colonel in the Revolutionary War and an early settler in western Tennessee.
