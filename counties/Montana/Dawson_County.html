Dawson County, Montana

In 2000, 1291 people in Dawson County, Montana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 7286 people in Dawson County, Montana lived in family households.

In 2010, 809 people in Chouteau County, Montana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 4869 people in Chouteau County, Montana lived in family households.

Dawson County is one of the older counties of the state. It was created January 15, 1869 from the northern half of the original Big Horn County. The namesake of the county is Major Andrew Dawson, manager of the Fort Benton Trading Post for the American Fur Company from 1856 to 1864.
