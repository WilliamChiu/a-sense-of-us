Carter County, Montana

In 2000, 180 people in Carter County, Montana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 1163 people in Carter County, Montana lived in family households.

In 2010, 852 people in Blaine County, Montana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5419 people in Blaine County, Montana lived in family households.

Carter County was named for Thomas Henry Carter, the first congressman from Montana. Prior to settlement the land was occupied by the Sioux tribe.
