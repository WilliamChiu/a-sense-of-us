Ravalli County, Montana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.38693547%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 4897 people in Ravalli County, Montana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 30586 people in Ravalli County, Montana lived in family households.

In 2000, 587 people in Ravalli County, Montana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 36070 people in Ravalli County, Montana lived in the county.

In 2010, 296 people in Powder River County, Montana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1412 people in Powder River County, Montana lived in family households.

In 2010, 35 people in Powder River County, Montana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 1743 people in Powder River County, Montana lived in the county.

Ravalli County was once home to the Bitterroot Salish tribe. The tribe was first encountered in 1805 by the Lewis and Clark Expedition, which noted the friendly nature of the tribe. The Catholic Church took interest in creating a mission in the area, and in 1841, Stevensville was founded. In 1891, the Salish tribe was relocated to the current Flathead Reservation under the Treaty of Hellgate.
