Missoula County, Montana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.7233133%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 21667 people in Missoula County, Montana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 70516 people in Missoula County, Montana lived in family households.

In 2000, 3619 people in Missoula County, Montana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 95802 people in Missoula County, Montana lived in the county.

In 2010, 1605 people in Madison County, Montana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5923 people in Madison County, Montana lived in family households.

In 2010, 163 people in Madison County, Montana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 7691 people in Madison County, Montana lived in the county.

Missoula County, Washington Territory was incorporated in 1860. Missoula County encompassed present-day Missoula and Deer Lodge Counties, as well as a good portion of land north and south of present-day Missoula County. Hell Gate Town, the county seat, was located at the confluence of the Clark Fork and Bitterroot Rivers.
