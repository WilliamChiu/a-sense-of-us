Meagher County, Montana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.28802156%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 300 people in Meagher County, Montana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 1606 people in Meagher County, Montana lived in family households.

In 2000, 26 people in Meagher County, Montana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 1932 people in Meagher County, Montana lived in the county.

In 2010, 3844 people in Lincoln County, Montana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 19687 people in Lincoln County, Montana lived in the county.

The first county seat was Diamond City, the main city of the Confederate Gulch mining district. This entire area is no longer part of Meagher County, but today lies in neighboring Broadwater County.
