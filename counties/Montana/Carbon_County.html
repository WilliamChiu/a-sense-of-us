Carbon County, Montana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.0076174736%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1575 people in Carbon County, Montana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 7876 people in Carbon County, Montana lived in family households.

In 2000, 101 people in Carbon County, Montana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 9552 people in Carbon County, Montana lived in the county.

In 2010, 1175 people in Big Horn County, Montana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 11553 people in Big Horn County, Montana lived in family households.

In 2010, 137 people in Big Horn County, Montana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 12865 people in Big Horn County, Montana lived in the county.

Carbon County was named for the rich coal deposits found in the area. Land from Park and Yellowstone counties was used to form Carbon County on March 4, 1895. More than sixty federally designated historic sites are located in the county, including Petroglyph Canyon, one of the state's most important rock art sites.
