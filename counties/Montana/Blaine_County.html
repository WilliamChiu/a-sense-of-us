Blaine County, Montana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.8364658%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 776 people in Blaine County, Montana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6167 people in Blaine County, Montana lived in family households.

In 2000, 66 people in Blaine County, Montana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 7009 people in Blaine County, Montana lived in the county.

In 2010, 91680 people in St. Louis city, Missouri lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 215636 people in St. Louis city, Missouri lived in family households.

In 2010, 11978 people in St. Louis city, Missouri lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 319294 people in St. Louis city, Missouri lived in the county.

In 1912 Blaine, Phillips and Hill counties withdrew from Chouteau County to form their own counties. The original boundary of Blaine County included a portion of land in the west that is included in Phillips County.
