Daniels County, Montana

In 2000, 367 people in Daniels County, Montana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 1613 people in Daniels County, Montana lived in family households.

In 2010, 14934 people in Cascade County, Montana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 63831 people in Cascade County, Montana lived in family households.

Daniels County was created in 1920 from portions of Sheridan and Valley Counties. The name comes from Mansfield Daniels, a local rancher.
