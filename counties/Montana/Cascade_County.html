Cascade County, Montana

In 2000, 13214 people in Cascade County, Montana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 65202 people in Cascade County, Montana lived in family households.

In 2010, 871 people in Broadwater County, Montana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 4689 people in Broadwater County, Montana lived in family households.

At the time of the Lewis and Clark Expedition, Cascade County was the territory of the Blackfeet. The county was named for the falls on the Missouri River.
