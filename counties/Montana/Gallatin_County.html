Gallatin County, Montana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 3.5818458%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 15831 people in Gallatin County, Montana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 48884 people in Gallatin County, Montana lived in family households.

In 2000, 3116 people in Gallatin County, Montana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 67831 people in Gallatin County, Montana lived in the county.

In 2010, 481 people in Fallon County, Montana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 2375 people in Fallon County, Montana lived in family households.

In 2010, 34 people in Fallon County, Montana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 2890 people in Fallon County, Montana lived in the county.

Lost Dakota, is a portion of land that was left over after the division of the Dakota Territory into other states in the late 19th century, was made part of Gallatin County in 1873.
