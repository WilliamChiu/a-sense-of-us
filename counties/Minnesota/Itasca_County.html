Itasca County, Minnesota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.2675295%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 6407 people in Itasca County, Minnesota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 36875 people in Itasca County, Minnesota lived in family households.

In 2000, 710 people in Itasca County, Minnesota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 43992 people in Itasca County, Minnesota lived in the county.

In 2010, 2876 people in Houston County, Minnesota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 15894 people in Houston County, Minnesota lived in family households.

In 2010, 257 people in Houston County, Minnesota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 19027 people in Houston County, Minnesota lived in the county.

Itasca County was first formed in 1849, upon the creation of the Minnesota Territory. It was originally a much larger county, which covered many of today's northeastern Minnesota counties. The original Itasca County stretched over Cook, Lake, Saint Louis, Koochiching, eastern Lake of the Woods, eastern Beltrami, Itasca, northern Aitkin, and northern Carlton counties, today in Minnesota.
