Dodge County, Minnesota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.0159433%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1925 people in Dodge County, Minnesota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 15614 people in Dodge County, Minnesota lived in family households.

In 2000, 192 people in Dodge County, Minnesota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 17731 people in Dodge County, Minnesota lived in the county.

In 2010, 244 people in Cottonwood County, Minnesota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 11687 people in Cottonwood County, Minnesota lived in the county.

Dodge County was originally inhabited by American Indians, as many counties in Minnesota were. This area was a common hunting and battle ground for the Mdewakanton Sioux, often fighting the Sauk and Fox Indians who had wandered into their territory. The first white person to visit Dodge County, however, is not known. It is believed by some that a French fur trader from Canada was the first, setting foot on its soil in the spring of 1655. Guides deemed this area unsafe due to the Natives living there. But, it was not until over two hundred years later that the locality truly came to life.
