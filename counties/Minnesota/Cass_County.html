Cass County, Minnesota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.7395844%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 3681 people in Cass County, Minnesota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 23036 people in Cass County, Minnesota lived in family households.

In 2000, 433 people in Cass County, Minnesota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 27150 people in Cass County, Minnesota lived in the county.

In 2010, 4340 people in Brown County, Minnesota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 20442 people in Brown County, Minnesota lived in family households.

In 2010, 1111 people in Brown County, Minnesota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 25893 people in Brown County, Minnesota lived in the county.

Cass County was created on September 1, 1851 by the legislature of the Minnesota Territory. It was not formally organized until 1897. Cass County was formed from portions of Dakota, Mahkatah, Pembina and Wahnata Counties. The county was named in honor of Lewis Cass. In turn, before formal organization, land from Cass County was split off to form other of the surrounding counties.
