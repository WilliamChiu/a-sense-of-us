Winona County, Minnesota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 6.6191435%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 9947 people in Winona County, Minnesota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 36232 people in Winona County, Minnesota lived in family households.

In 2000, 3806 people in Winona County, Minnesota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 49985 people in Winona County, Minnesota lived in the county.

In 2010, 29149 people in Washington County, Minnesota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 205417 people in Washington County, Minnesota lived in family households.

In 2010, 3570 people in Washington County, Minnesota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 238136 people in Washington County, Minnesota lived in the county.

The name of the county is said to derive from a Dakota legend regarding a woman, referred to Winona, who was betrothed to marry a warrior whom she did not love. Rather than marry him, she is said to have leapt to her death from a rock now called "Maiden's Rock" on Lake Pepin. This legend is colloquially referred to as the Princess Winona legend.
