Goodhue County, Minnesota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.1887083%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 6116 people in Goodhue County, Minnesota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 36911 people in Goodhue County, Minnesota lived in family households.

In 2000, 1100 people in Goodhue County, Minnesota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 44127 people in Goodhue County, Minnesota lived in the county.

In 2010, 2451 people in Faribault County, Minnesota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 11766 people in Faribault County, Minnesota lived in family households.

In 2010, 336 people in Faribault County, Minnesota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 14553 people in Faribault County, Minnesota lived in the county.

The county was formed from territory within Wabasha County on March 5, 1853. County boundaries were defined in 1854 and further refined again in 1855.
