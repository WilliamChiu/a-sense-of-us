Lake of the Woods County, Minnesota

In 2000, 721 people in Lake of the Woods County, Minnesota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 3754 people in Lake of the Woods County, Minnesota lived in family households.

In 2010, 2600 people in Koochiching County, Minnesota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 10470 people in Koochiching County, Minnesota lived in family households.

Lake of the Woods County was named because the lake covers a large portion of the county. Jacques de Noyon, a Frenchman who came from Trois Rivières, (Quebec), explored this area in 1688 and became the first white man to view the lake. He named the lake ''Lac aux Îles'' which means "Lake of the Islands" in English.
