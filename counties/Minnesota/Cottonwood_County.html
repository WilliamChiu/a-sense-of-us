Cottonwood County, Minnesota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -3.1110942%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1763 people in Cottonwood County, Minnesota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 9998 people in Cottonwood County, Minnesota lived in family households.

In 2000, 406 people in Cottonwood County, Minnesota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 12167 people in Cottonwood County, Minnesota lived in the county.

In 2010, 11367 people in Clay County, Minnesota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 43889 people in Clay County, Minnesota lived in family households.

In 2010, 3743 people in Clay County, Minnesota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 58999 people in Clay County, Minnesota lived in the county.

Cottonwood County was established July 29, 1870. It was named after the Cottonwood River which runs through the northeast corner of Germantown Township. The river received its name from the abundance of cottonwood trees on its banks.
