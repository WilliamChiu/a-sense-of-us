Kandiyohi County, Minnesota

In 2000, 6137 people in Kandiyohi County, Minnesota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 34201 people in Kandiyohi County, Minnesota lived in family households.

In 2010, 7713 people in Itasca County, Minnesota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 36324 people in Itasca County, Minnesota lived in family households.

Kandiyohi County is named after a Dakota word meaning "where the buffalo fish come." Kandiyohi County was organized in 1858 with its county seat in Kandiyohi, which was then called Kandiyohi Station and was just a railroad stop. The original county occupied only the southern half of its current area.
