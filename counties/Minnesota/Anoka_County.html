Anoka County, Minnesota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.31405687%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 35330 people in Anoka County, Minnesota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 259776 people in Anoka County, Minnesota lived in family households.

In 2000, 2978 people in Anoka County, Minnesota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 298084 people in Anoka County, Minnesota lived in the county.

In 2010, 297946 people in Wayne County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1498789 people in Wayne County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2010, 23849 people in Wayne County, Michigan lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 1820584 people in Wayne County, Michigan lived in the county.

Anoka County was organized by an act of the territorial Legislature on May 23, 1857, the year prior to Minnesota's admission to the Union. It was formed from parts of Ramsey County and Benton County; the Rum River previously divided the line between the two counties. The boundaries were mainly the same as they are now, except for a small part of the southeastern tip along the Mississippi River and at the south. It was a small portion that connected to Ramsey and occupied one-third of the congressional township. It was then attached to Anoka County by constitutional amendment November 2, 1869. It later became known as Fridley in 1879.
