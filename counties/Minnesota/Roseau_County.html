Roseau County, Minnesota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.7095635%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2031 people in Roseau County, Minnesota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14066 people in Roseau County, Minnesota lived in family households.

In 2000, 241 people in Roseau County, Minnesota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 16338 people in Roseau County, Minnesota lived in the county.

In 2010, 2563 people in Renville County, Minnesota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 12825 people in Renville County, Minnesota lived in family households.

In 2010, 342 people in Renville County, Minnesota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 15730 people in Renville County, Minnesota lived in the county.

Roseau County was once the home of many Chippewa, Sioux, and Mandan tribes. Archeologists have found artifacts within the county belonging to these tribes that date back 7,200 years ago.
