Nobles County, Minnesota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.2583742%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2855 people in Nobles County, Minnesota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 17588 people in Nobles County, Minnesota lived in family households.

In 2000, 389 people in Nobles County, Minnesota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 20832 people in Nobles County, Minnesota lived in the county.

In 2010, 6781 people in Mower County, Minnesota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 31750 people in Mower County, Minnesota lived in family households.

In 2010, 632 people in Mower County, Minnesota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 39163 people in Mower County, Minnesota lived in the county.

Nobles County was first occupied by the Sisseton Sioux. The first white man to set foot on the land was Joseph Nicollet who came to map out the area in 1842. Nicollet named Lake Okabena (there were two Lake Okabenas at the time), Lake Ocheda, East and West Graham Lake and the Kanaranzi Creek.
