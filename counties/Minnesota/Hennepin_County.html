Hennepin County, Minnesota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.502491%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 245829 people in Hennepin County, Minnesota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 842155 people in Hennepin County, Minnesota lived in family households.

In 2000, 28216 people in Hennepin County, Minnesota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 1116200 people in Hennepin County, Minnesota lived in the county.

In 2010, 5456 people in Freeborn County, Minnesota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 25162 people in Freeborn County, Minnesota lived in family households.

In 2010, 637 people in Freeborn County, Minnesota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 31255 people in Freeborn County, Minnesota lived in the county.

Hennepin County was created in 1852 by the Minnesota Territorial Legislature. Father Louis Hennepin's name was chosen because he originally named St. Anthony Falls and recorded some of the earliest accounts of the area for the Western world. Hennepin County's early history is closely linked to the establishment of the cities of Minneapolis and St. Anthony.
