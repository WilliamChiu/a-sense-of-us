Becker County, Minnesota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.42856932%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4224 people in Becker County, Minnesota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 25293 people in Becker County, Minnesota lived in family households.

In 2000, 483 people in Becker County, Minnesota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 30000 people in Becker County, Minnesota lived in the county.

In 2010, 5013 people in Wexford County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 27333 people in Wexford County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2010, 389 people in Wexford County, Michigan lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 32735 people in Wexford County, Michigan lived in the county.

Becker County became a county on March 18, 1858. It was named after Brigadier General George Loomis Becker (1829-1904). Becker was one of three men elected to congress when Minnesota became a state, but since Minnesota could only send two, Becker elected to stay behind, and he was promised to have a county named after him.
