Camden County, North Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -3.631097%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 738 people in Camden County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6143 people in Camden County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 4 people in Camden County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 6885 people in Camden County, North Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 13384 people in Burke County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 74176 people in Burke County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 3352 people in Burke County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 90912 people in Burke County, North Carolina lived in the county.

The county was formed in 1777 from the northeastern part of Pasquotank County. It was named for Charles Pratt, 1st Lord Camden, who had opposed the Stamp Act. The county is the site of the southern terminus of the Dismal Swamp Canal. It was the site of the Battle of South Mills on April 19, 1862 during the American Civil War, which was a minor victory to the Confederacy.
