Moore County, North Carolina

In 2000, 10257 people in Moore County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 62850 people in Moore County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 176833 people in Mecklenburg County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 726780 people in Mecklenburg County, North Carolina lived in family households.

The county was formed in 1785 from Cumberland County. It was named for Alfred Moore, an officer in the American Revolutionary War and associate justice of the Supreme Court of the United States.
