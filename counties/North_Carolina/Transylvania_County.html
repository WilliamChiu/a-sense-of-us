Transylvania County, North Carolina

In 2000, 4186 people in Transylvania County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 24104 people in Transylvania County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 6768 people in Stokes County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 40177 people in Stokes County, North Carolina lived in family households.

Founded in 1861, the county's name is derived from the colonial Transylvania Company and has Latin origins: ''trans'' ("across") and ''silva'' ("woods").
