Granville County, North Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 10.952663%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 5431 people in Granville County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 37576 people in Granville County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 5491 people in Granville County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 48498 people in Granville County, North Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 29250 people in Gaston County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 173519 people in Gaston County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 3317 people in Gaston County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 206086 people in Gaston County, North Carolina lived in the county.

The county was formed in 1746 from Edgecombe County. It was named for John Carteret, 2nd Earl Granville, who as heir to one of the eight original Lords Proprietors of the Province of Carolina, claimed one eighth of the land granted in the charter of 1665. The claim was established as consisting of approximately the northern half of North Carolina and this territory came to be known as the Granville District, also known as Oxford.
