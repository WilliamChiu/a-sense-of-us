Montgomery County, North Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 3.5361886%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 3072 people in Montgomery County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 22669 people in Montgomery County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 1081 people in Montgomery County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 26822 people in Montgomery County, North Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 3859 people in Martin County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 20490 people in Martin County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 156 people in Martin County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 24505 people in Martin County, North Carolina lived in the county.

The county was formed in 1779 from Anson County. It was named in honor and respect of Richard Montgomery, an American Revolutionary War general killed in 1775 while attempting to capture Quebec City, Canada.
