Jones County, North Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.33986568%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1283 people in Jones County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 8979 people in Jones County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 119 people in Jones County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 10381 people in Jones County, North Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 21129 people in Iredell County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 137016 people in Iredell County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 1292 people in Iredell County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 159437 people in Iredell County, North Carolina lived in the county.

The county was formed in 1779 from the southwestern part of Craven County. It was named for Willie Jones, a Revolutionary leader and president of the North Carolina Council of Safety; he was later the state's chief opponent of ratification of the United States Constitution He was also a plantation slave owner.
