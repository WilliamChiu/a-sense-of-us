Richmond County, North Carolina

In 2000, 6111 people in Richmond County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 38751 people in Richmond County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 39016 people in Pitt County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 122606 people in Pitt County, North Carolina lived in family households.

The county was formed in 1779 from Anson County. It was named for Charles Lennox, 3rd Duke of Richmond and Lennox who was an Englishman and a member of the Parliament of the United Kingdom who sided with the colonists in America during the American Revolution.
