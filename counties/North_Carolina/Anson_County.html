Anson County, North Carolina

In 2000, 2811 people in Anson County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 21037 people in Anson County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 24858 people in Alamance County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 122044 people in Alamance County, North Carolina lived in family households.

The county was formed in 1750 from Bladen County. It was named for George Anson, Baron Anson, a British admiral, who circumnavigated the globe from 1740 to 1744, and later became First Lord of the Admiralty.
