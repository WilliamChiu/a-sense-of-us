Macon County, North Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.30509233%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 4462 people in Macon County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 24816 people in Macon County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 533 people in Macon County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 29811 people in Macon County, North Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 9739 people in Lenoir County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 48514 people in Lenoir County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 1242 people in Lenoir County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 59495 people in Lenoir County, North Carolina lived in the county.

The county was formed in 1828 from the western part of Haywood County. It was named for Nathaniel Macon, who represented North Carolina in the United States House of Representatives from 1791 to 1815 (serving as Speaker of the House from 1801 to 1807), and in the United States Senate from 1815 to 1828.
