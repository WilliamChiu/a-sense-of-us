Wilson County, North Carolina

In 2000, 10537 people in Wilson County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 61412 people in Wilson County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 16114 people in Watauga County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 29642 people in Watauga County, North Carolina lived in family households.

The county was formed in 1855 from parts of Edgecombe County, Johnston County, Nash County, and Wayne County. It was named for Louis Dicken Wilson, a state legislator from Edgecombe County and a colonel in the Mexican-American War, who died of fever at Veracruz in 1847.
