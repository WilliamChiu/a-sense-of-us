Household Populations

Hyde County, North Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 11.532927%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 849 people in Hyde County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 4311 people in Hyde County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 666 people in Hyde County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 5826 people in Hyde County, North Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 17927 people in Henderson County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 87514 people in Henderson County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 1299 people in Henderson County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 106740 people in Henderson County, North Carolina lived in the county.

The county was formed December 3, 1705, as '''Wickham Precinct''', one of three precincts within Bath County. The name "Wickham" was derived from the manor of "Temple Wycombe" in Buckinghamshire, United Kingdom, the family home of John Archdale, Governor of North and South Carolina from 1695 to 1696. In 1712 it was renamed '''Hyde Precinct''', for Edward Hyde, Governor of North Carolina from 1711 to 1712. In 1739 Bath County was abolished, and Hyde Precinct became '''Hyde County'''.
