Cherokee County, North Carolina

In 2000, 3351 people in Cherokee County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 20663 people in Cherokee County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 3315 people in Caswell County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 19036 people in Caswell County, North Carolina lived in family households.

The county was formed in 1839 from the western part of Macon County. It was named for the Cherokee Native Americans, some of whom still live in the area.
