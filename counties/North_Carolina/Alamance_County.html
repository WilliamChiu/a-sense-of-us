Alamance County, North Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.16118884%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 19765 people in Alamance County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 107284 people in Alamance County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 3751 people in Alamance County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 130800 people in Alamance County, North Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 132838 people in Westchester County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 787571 people in Westchester County, New York lived in family households.

In 2010, 28704 people in Westchester County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 949113 people in Westchester County, New York lived in the county.

Before being formed as a county, the region had at least one known small Southeastern tribe of Native American in the 18th century - the Sissipahaw who lived in the area bound by modern Saxapahaw, the area known as the Hawfields, and the Haw River. European settlers entered the region in the late 17th century chiefly following Native American trading paths, and set up their farms in what they called the "Haw Old Fields", fertile ground previously tilled by the Sissipahaw. The paths later became the basis of the railroad and interstate highway routes.
