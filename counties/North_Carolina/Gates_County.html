Gates County, North Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.3361812%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1130 people in Gates County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 9229 people in Gates County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 157 people in Gates County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 10516 people in Gates County, North Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 60874 people in Forsyth County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 279945 people in Forsyth County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 9851 people in Forsyth County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 350670 people in Forsyth County, North Carolina lived in the county.

As in other areas along the waterways, centuries of Native Americans created settlements, increasingly permanent, along the Chowan River. At the time of European contact, the Chowanoke occupied most of the territory along the river. After suffering dramatic population decreases likely due to European infectious diseases, to which they had no immunity, most were pushed out by encroaching Tuscarora.
