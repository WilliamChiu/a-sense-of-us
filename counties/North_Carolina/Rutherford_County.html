Rutherford County, North Carolina

In 2000, 8328 people in Rutherford County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 53039 people in Rutherford County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 17640 people in Robeson County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 112423 people in Robeson County, North Carolina lived in family households.

The county was formed in 1779 from the western part of the former Tryon County. It was named for Griffith Rutherford, leader of an expedition against the Cherokee in 1776 and a general in the American Revolutionary War.
