Guilford County, North Carolina

In 2000, 74361 people in Guilford County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 332710 people in Guilford County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 1294 people in Graham County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7474 people in Graham County, North Carolina lived in family households.

At the time of European encounter, the inhabitants of the area that became Guilford County were a Siouan-speaking people called the Saura. Beginning in the 1740s, settlers arrived in the region in search of fertile and affordable land. These first settlers included American Quakers from Pennsylvania, Maryland, and New England at what is now Greensboro, as well as German Reformed and Lutherans in the east, British Quakers in the south and west, and Scotch-Irish Presbyterians in the center of today's Guilford County.
