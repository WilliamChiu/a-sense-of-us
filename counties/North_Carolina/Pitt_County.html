Pitt County, North Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.7772665%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 27614 people in Pitt County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 99851 people in Pitt County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 6333 people in Pitt County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 133798 people in Pitt County, North Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 7129 people in Pender County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 43998 people in Pender County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 1090 people in Pender County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 52217 people in Pender County, North Carolina lived in the county.

The county was formed in 1760 from Beaufort County, though the legislative act that created it did not become effective until January 1, 1761. It was named for William Pitt the Elder, who was then Secretary of State for the Southern Department and Leader of the House of Commons. William Pitt was an English statesman and orator, born in London, England. He studied at Oxford University and in 1731 joined the army. Pitt led the young "Patriot" Whigs and in 1756 became secretary of state, where he was a pro-freedom speaker in British Colonial government.
