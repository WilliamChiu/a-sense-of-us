Watauga County, North Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 11.789107%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 10536 people in Watauga County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 26767 people in Watauga County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 5392 people in Watauga County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 42695 people in Watauga County, North Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 155112 people in Wake County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 724898 people in Wake County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 20983 people in Wake County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 900993 people in Wake County, North Carolina lived in the county.

The county was formed in 1849 from parts of Ashe, Caldwell, Wilkes, and Yancey counties. It was named for the Watauga River, whose name is said to be a Native American word, the meaning of which is in dispute among various histories with translations ranging from beautiful water, whispering waters, village of many springs, and river of islands, to name a few.
