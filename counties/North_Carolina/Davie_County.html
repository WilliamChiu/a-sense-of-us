Davie County, North Carolina

In 2000, 4030 people in Davie County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 30429 people in Davie County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 2859 people in Currituck County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 20553 people in Currituck County, North Carolina lived in family households.

The county was formed in 1836 from Rowan County. It was named for William R. Davie, Governor of North Carolina from 1798 to 1799. 1,147 soldiers from Davie County fought in the American Civil War for the Confederate States of America.
