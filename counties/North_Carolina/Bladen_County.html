Bladen County, North Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -11.6536255%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 4478 people in Bladen County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 27102 people in Bladen County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 698 people in Bladen County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 32278 people in Bladen County, North Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 2757 people in Avery County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 12626 people in Avery County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 2414 people in Avery County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 17797 people in Avery County, North Carolina lived in the county.

Bladen County was formed in 1734 as '''Bladen Precinct''' of Bath County, from New Hanover Precinct. It was named for Martin Bladen, a member of the Board of Trade. With the abolition of Bath County in 1739, all of its constituent precincts became counties.
