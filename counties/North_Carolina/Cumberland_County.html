Cumberland County, North Carolina

In 2000, 36978 people in Cumberland County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 247519 people in Cumberland County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 13638 people in Cleveland County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 82390 people in Cleveland County, North Carolina lived in family households.

The county was formed in 1754 from Bladen County. It was named for Prince William Augustus, Duke of Cumberland (1721-1765), captain-general of the British army and victorious commander at the Battle of Culloden.
