Buncombe County, North Carolina

In 2000, 36954 people in Buncombe County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 162611 people in Buncombe County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 3222 people in Bertie County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 16736 people in Bertie County, North Carolina lived in family households.

The county was formed in 1791 from parts of Burke County and Rutherford County. It was named for Edward Buncombe, a colonel in the American Revolutionary War, who was captured at the Battle of Germantown.
