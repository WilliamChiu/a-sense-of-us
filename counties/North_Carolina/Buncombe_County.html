Buncombe County, North Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -3.042239%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 36954 people in Buncombe County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 162611 people in Buncombe County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 6765 people in Buncombe County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 206330 people in Buncombe County, North Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 3222 people in Bertie County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 16736 people in Bertie County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 1324 people in Bertie County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 21282 people in Bertie County, North Carolina lived in the county.

The county was formed in 1791 from parts of Burke County and Rutherford County. It was named for Edward Buncombe, a colonel in the American Revolutionary War, who was captured at the Battle of Germantown.
