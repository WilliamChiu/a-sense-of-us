Jackson County, North Carolina

In 2000, 5905 people in Jackson County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 24464 people in Jackson County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 5228 people in Hoke County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 41054 people in Hoke County, North Carolina lived in family households.

This area was part of Cherokee Nation territory at the time of European encounter. The trust land known as Qualla Boundary extends into part of Jackson County and is the base for the Federally recognized tribe of the Eastern Band of Cherokee Indians. They contribute to the high proportion of Native Americans among the county population - more than 10%.
