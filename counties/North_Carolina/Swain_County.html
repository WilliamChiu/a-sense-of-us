Swain County, North Carolina

In 2000, 1727 people in Swain County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10826 people in Swain County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 8106 people in Stanly County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 50428 people in Stanly County, North Carolina lived in family households.

The county was formed in 1871 from parts of Jackson County and Macon County. It was named for David L. Swain, governor of North Carolina from 1832 to 1835, and president of the University of North Carolina from 1835 to 1868.
