Brunswick County, North Carolina

In 2000, 10225 people in Brunswick County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 62194 people in Brunswick County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 7490 people in Beaufort County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 39766 people in Beaufort County, North Carolina lived in family households.

The county was formed in 1764 from parts of Bladen County and New Hanover County. It was named for the colonial port of Brunswick Town (now in ruins) which was itself named for Duchy of Brunswick-Lüneburg; at the time held by the British kings of the House of Hanover.
