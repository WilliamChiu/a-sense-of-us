Surry County, North Carolina

In 2000, 9072 people in Surry County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 60877 people in Surry County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 4861 people in Scotland County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 29566 people in Scotland County, North Carolina lived in family households.

The county was formed in 1771 from Rowan County. It was named for the county of Surrey in England, birthplace of William Tryon, Governor of North Carolina from 1765 to 1771.
