New Hanover County, North Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.3055384%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 35623 people in New Hanover County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 120418 people in New Hanover County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 4266 people in New Hanover County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 160307 people in New Hanover County, North Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 3626 people in Montgomery County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 23079 people in Montgomery County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 1093 people in Montgomery County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 27798 people in Montgomery County, North Carolina lived in the county.

The county was formed in 1729 as '''New Hanover Precinct''' of Bath County, from Craven Precinct. It was named for the House of Hanover, which was then ruling Great Britain.
