Haywood County, North Carolina

In 2000, 8111 people in Haywood County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 44961 people in Haywood County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 92573 people in Guilford County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 380341 people in Guilford County, North Carolina lived in family households.

The county was formed by European Americans in 1808 from the western part of Buncombe County. It was named for John Haywood, who served as the North Carolina State Treasurer from 1787 to 1827.
