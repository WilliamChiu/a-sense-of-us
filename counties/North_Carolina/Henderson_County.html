Henderson County, North Carolina

In 2000, 12861 people in Henderson County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 74471 people in Henderson County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 8499 people in Halifax County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 44776 people in Halifax County, North Carolina lived in family households.

The county was formed in 1838 from the southern part of Buncombe County. It was named for Leonard Henderson, Chief Justice of the North Carolina Supreme Court from 1829 to 1833.
