Stokes County, North Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.43771863%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 5224 people in Stokes County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 38914 people in Stokes County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 573 people in Stokes County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 44711 people in Stokes County, North Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 8296 people in Sampson County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 54048 people in Sampson County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 1087 people in Sampson County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 63431 people in Sampson County, North Carolina lived in the county.

The county was formed in 1789 from Surry County. It was named for John Stokes, an American Revolutionary War captain severely wounded when British Colonel Banastre Tarleton's cavalry practically destroyed Col. Abraham Buford's Virginia regiment in the Waxhaws region in 1780. After the war, Captain Stokes was appointed a judge of the United States district court for North Carolina. In 1849 the southern half of Stokes County became Forsyth County.
