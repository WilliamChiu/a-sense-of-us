Scotland County, North Carolina

In 2000, 4299 people in Scotland County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 30722 people in Scotland County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 19442 people in Rowan County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 114700 people in Rowan County, North Carolina lived in family households.

The county was formed in 1899 from the southeastern part of Richmond County. It was named after Scotland, as many of its early European settlers were Scots-Irish and Scottish Highlanders.
