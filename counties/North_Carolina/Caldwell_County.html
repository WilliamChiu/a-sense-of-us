Caldwell County, North Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.7615795%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 10091 people in Caldwell County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 66175 people in Caldwell County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 1149 people in Caldwell County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 77415 people in Caldwell County, North Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 50621 people in Buncombe County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 180024 people in Buncombe County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 7673 people in Buncombe County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 238318 people in Buncombe County, North Carolina lived in the county.

The county was formed in 1841 from parts of Burke County and Wilkes County. It was named for Joseph Caldwell, presiding professor (1796–1797, 1799–1804) and the first president (1804–1812, 1816–1835) of the University of North Carolina.
