Tyrrell County, North Carolina

In 2000, 543 people in Tyrrell County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 3169 people in Tyrrell County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 10480 people in Surry County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 62323 people in Surry County, North Carolina lived in family households.

The county was formed in 1729 as Tyrrell Precinct of Albemarle County, from parts of Bertie Precinct, Chowan Precinct, Currituck Precinct, and Pasquotank Precinct. It was named for Sir John Tyrrell, one of the Lords Proprietors of Carolina.
