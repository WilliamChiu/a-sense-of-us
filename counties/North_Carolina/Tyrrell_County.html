Tyrrell County, North Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 10.452711%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 543 people in Tyrrell County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 3169 people in Tyrrell County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 4149 people in Tyrrell County, North Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 870 people in Surry County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 73673 people in Surry County, North Carolina lived in the county.

The county was formed in 1729 as Tyrrell Precinct of Albemarle County, from parts of Bertie Precinct, Chowan Precinct, Currituck Precinct, and Pasquotank Precinct. It was named for Sir John Tyrrell, one of the Lords Proprietors of Carolina.
