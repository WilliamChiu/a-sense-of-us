Wake County, North Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -11.639828%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 112128 people in Wake County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 496526 people in Wake County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 19192 people in Wake County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 627846 people in Wake County, North Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 605 people in Tyrrell County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 3170 people in Tyrrell County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 632 people in Tyrrell County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 4407 people in Tyrrell County, North Carolina lived in the county.

Present day Wake County was once part of the Tuscarora nation.
