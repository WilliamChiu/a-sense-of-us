Hoke County, North Carolina

In 2000, 3321 people in Hoke County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 29199 people in Hoke County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 9987 people in Haywood County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 48236 people in Haywood County, North Carolina lived in family households.

The county was formed in 1911 from parts of Cumberland County and Robeson County. It was named for Robert F. Hoke, a Confederate general in the American Civil War.
