Lenoir County, North Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.9756436%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 8905 people in Lenoir County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 48974 people in Lenoir County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 1769 people in Lenoir County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 59648 people in Lenoir County, North Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 20157 people in Johnston County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 146950 people in Johnston County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 1771 people in Johnston County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 168878 people in Johnston County, North Carolina lived in the county.

The county was formed by European Americans in 1791 from the southern part of Dobbs County. It was named for William Lenoir (1751-1839), an officer in the American Revolutionary War who took part in the Battle of Kings Mountain. He was a prominent political leader; when the county was established, he was serving as Speaker of the North Carolina Senate.
