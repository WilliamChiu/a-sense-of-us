Cleveland County, North Carolina

In 2000, 11711 people in Cleveland County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 82096 people in Cleveland County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 4178 people in Cherokee County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 22830 people in Cherokee County, North Carolina lived in family households.

The county was formed in 1841 from parts of Lincoln County and Rutherford County. It was named for Benjamin Cleveland, a colonel in the American Revolutionary War, who took part in the Battle of King's Mountain. From 1841 to 1887 "Cleaveland" was the spelling used; the present spelling was adopted in 1887.
