Martin County, North Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.5950253%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 3153 people in Martin County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 291 people in Martin County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 25593 people in Martin County, North Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 6354 people in McDowell County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 36976 people in McDowell County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 1666 people in McDowell County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 44996 people in McDowell County, North Carolina lived in the county.

The county was formed in 1774 from the southeastern part of Halifax County and the western part of Tyrrell County. It was named for Josiah Martin, the last royal governor of North Carolina (1771-1775). Whereas Dobbs County and Tryon County, named for Martin's predecessors Arthur Dobbs and William Tryon, were abolished after American independence, Martin County was neither abolished nor renamed, a fact which has been attributed to the popularity of Alexander Martin, twice governor of the state (1782-1784, 1789-1792).
