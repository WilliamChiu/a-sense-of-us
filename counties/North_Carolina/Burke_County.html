Burke County, North Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.706945%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 12164 people in Burke County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 73374 people in Burke County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 3610 people in Burke County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 89148 people in Burke County, North Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 5487 people in Bladen County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 29192 people in Bladen County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 511 people in Bladen County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 35190 people in Bladen County, North Carolina lived in the county.

Indigenous peoples inhabited the interior as well as the coastal areas for thousands of years. Native Americans of the Mississippian culture inhabited the county long before Europeans arrived in the New World. The largest Mound Builder settlement was at Joara, a 12 acre site and regional chiefdom near present-day Morganton. It was the center of the largest Native American settlement in North Carolina, dating from about 1000 AD and expanding into the next centuries.
