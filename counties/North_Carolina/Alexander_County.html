Alexander County, North Carolina

In 2000, 4041 people in Alexander County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 29327 people in Alexander County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 6153 people in Wyoming County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 32041 people in Wyoming County, New York lived in family households.

Alexander County was chartered by the North Carolina General Assembly in 1847. It was formed from portions of what was then Iredell, Caldwell, Wilkes counties. The county was named for William Julius Alexander who was a Speaker of the North Carolina House of Commons.
