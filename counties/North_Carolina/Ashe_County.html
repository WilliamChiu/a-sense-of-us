Ashe County, North Carolina

In 2000, 3396 people in Ashe County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 20687 people in Ashe County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 4833 people in Alexander County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 31204 people in Alexander County, North Carolina lived in family households.

Historical evidence shows that Ashe county was inhabited by Native Americans, which included the Cherokee, Creek, and Shawnee tribes. Pieces of broken pottery, arrowheads, and other Native American artifacts have been found, indicating their presence. Most of these artifacts have been found in the Old Fields area of Ashe County.
