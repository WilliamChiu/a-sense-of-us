Household Populations

Sampson County, North Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.1412859%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 7253 people in Sampson County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 51559 people in Sampson County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 1349 people in Sampson County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 60161 people in Sampson County, North Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 14636 people in Rockingham County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 77952 people in Rockingham County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 1055 people in Rockingham County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 93643 people in Rockingham County, North Carolina lived in the county.

Sampson County was formally and legally established in April of 1784 by the North Carolina General Assembly from an area taken from neighboring Duplin County. Land from neighboring Wayne County and New Hanover counties would be annexed later.
