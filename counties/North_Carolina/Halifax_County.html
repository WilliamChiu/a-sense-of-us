Halifax County, North Carolina

In 2000, 7675 people in Halifax County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 47935 people in Halifax County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 7106 people in Granville County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 45862 people in Granville County, North Carolina lived in family households.

The county was formed in 1758 from Edgecombe County. It was named for George Montague-Dunk, 2nd Earl of Halifax, President of the Board of Trade from 1748 to 1761.
