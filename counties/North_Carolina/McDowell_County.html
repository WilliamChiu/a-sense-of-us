Group Quarters vs Households

McDowell County, North Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.6348243%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 5421 people in McDowell County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 35336 people in McDowell County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 1394 people in McDowell County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 42151 people in McDowell County, North Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 7751 people in Lee County, North Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 49116 people in Lee County, North Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 999 people in Lee County, North Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 57866 people in Lee County, North Carolina lived in the county.

Archaeological excavations performed by Dr. David Moore, during the early 1980s, revealed the earliest inhabitants of McDowell County to be from the Mississippian and Woodland eras. Dr. Moore discovered evidence in an area close to the Catawba River in and around an unusual topographical site known as Round Hill. These early Native Americans lived in this section prior to Juan Pardo's exploration of the region.
