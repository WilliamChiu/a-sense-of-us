Pratt County, Kansas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -11.740505%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1466 people in Pratt County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 7856 people in Pratt County, Kansas lived in family households.

In 2000, 325 people in Pratt County, Kansas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 9647 people in Pratt County, Kansas lived in the county.

In 2010, 1165 people in Pawnee County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 4782 people in Pawnee County, Kansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 1026 people in Pawnee County, Kansas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 6973 people in Pawnee County, Kansas lived in the county.

In 1887, the Chicago, Kansas and Nebraska Railway extended its main line from Herington to Pratt. This main line connected Herington, Ramona, Tampa, Durham, Waldeck, Canton, Galva, McPherson, Groveland, Inman, Medora, Hutchinson, Whiteside, Partridge, Arlington, Langdon, Turon, Preston, Natrona, Pratt. In 1888, this main line was extended to Liberal. Later, this line was extended to Tucumcari, New Mexico and El Paso, Texas. This line is called the "Golden State Limited".
