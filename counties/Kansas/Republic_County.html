Republic County, Kansas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.5738249%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 942 people in Republic County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 4753 people in Republic County, Kansas lived in family households.

In 2000, 140 people in Republic County, Kansas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 5835 people in Republic County, Kansas lived in the county.

In 2010, 1650 people in Pratt County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7626 people in Pratt County, Kansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 380 people in Pratt County, Kansas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 9656 people in Pratt County, Kansas lived in the county.

Republic County was first settled around 1860 by Daniel and Conrad Myers. The territory was principally inhabited by Pawnees, Iowas, and Otoes who greeted the incoming settlers - mostly farmers - peaceably. By 1868, Republic County was holding elections.
