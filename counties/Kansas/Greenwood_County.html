Greenwood County, Kansas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.5179634%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1196 people in Greenwood County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6286 people in Greenwood County, Kansas lived in family households.

In 2000, 191 people in Greenwood County, Kansas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 7673 people in Greenwood County, Kansas lived in the county.

In 2010, 735 people in Grant County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7015 people in Grant County, Kansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 79 people in Grant County, Kansas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 7829 people in Grant County, Kansas lived in the county.

Greenwood County was named for Alfred B. Greenwood, a U.S. Congressman from Arkansas. The first railroad in Greenwood County was built through that territory in 1879.
