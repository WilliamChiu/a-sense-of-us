Douglas County, Kansas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 6.5529943%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 26940 people in Douglas County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 64308 people in Douglas County, Kansas lived in family households.

In 2000, 8714 people in Douglas County, Kansas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 99962 people in Douglas County, Kansas lived in the county.

In 2010, 587 people in Decatur County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 2293 people in Decatur County, Kansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 81 people in Decatur County, Kansas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 2961 people in Decatur County, Kansas lived in the county.

Douglas County was opened for settlement on May 15, 1854, and was named for Stephen A. Douglas, a senator from Illinois. The county was practically at the center of the Bleeding Kansas years as leaders in Lecompton, the territorial capital, wanted Kansas to be a slave state and leaders in Lawrence wanted Kansas to be a free state. Because of this, multiple events took place, including the drafting of the Lecompton Constitution admitting Kansas as a slave state, the sacking of Lawrence, and the Battle of Black Jack.
