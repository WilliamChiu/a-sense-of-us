Cloud County, Kansas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 4.970145%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1702 people in Cloud County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 7904 people in Cloud County, Kansas lived in family households.

In 2000, 662 people in Cloud County, Kansas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 10268 people in Cloud County, Kansas lived in the county.

In 2010, 518 people in Cheyenne County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 2159 people in Cheyenne County, Kansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 49 people in Cheyenne County, Kansas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 2726 people in Cheyenne County, Kansas lived in the county.

Cloud county was originally named "Shirley" county, but civic leaders feared that sounded too much like the name of a prostitute. It was then renamed "Cloud" county in honor of Colonel William F. Cloud.
