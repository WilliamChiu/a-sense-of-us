Elk County, Kansas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.8113663%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 518 people in Elk County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 2657 people in Elk County, Kansas lived in family households.

In 2000, 86 people in Elk County, Kansas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 3261 people in Elk County, Kansas lived in the county.

In 2010, 1245 people in Doniphan County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 6273 people in Doniphan County, Kansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 427 people in Doniphan County, Kansas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 7945 people in Doniphan County, Kansas lived in the county.

Elk County was founded in 1875. It was named from the Elk River. The first railroad in Elk County was built through that territory in 1879.
