Montgomery County, Kansas

In 2000, 5617 people in Montgomery County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 29735 people in Montgomery County, Kansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 527 people in Meade County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 3929 people in Meade County, Kansas lived in family households.

Montgomery County was established February 26, 1867. It was named in honor of Richard Montgomery, an American Revolutionary War general killed in 1775 while attempting to capture Quebec City, in Canada, after successfully capturing two forts and the city of Montreal.
