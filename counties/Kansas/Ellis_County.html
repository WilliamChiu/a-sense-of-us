Ellis County, Kansas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -3.517121%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 5889 people in Ellis County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 20359 people in Ellis County, Kansas lived in family households.

In 2000, 1259 people in Ellis County, Kansas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 27507 people in Ellis County, Kansas lived in the county.

In 2010, 31417 people in Douglas County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 110826 people in Douglas County, Kansas lived in the county.

Ellis County was founded on February 26, 1867. It is named for George Ellis, first lieutenant of the Twelfth Kansas Infantry.
