Gove County, Kansas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.37059784%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 399 people in Gove County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 2616 people in Gove County, Kansas lived in family households.

In 2000, 53 people in Gove County, Kansas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 3068 people in Gove County, Kansas lived in the county.

In 2010, 4114 people in Ford County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 29026 people in Ford County, Kansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 708 people in Ford County, Kansas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 33848 people in Ford County, Kansas lived in the county.

Gove County was founded in 1868. It is named for Grenville L. Gove, member of the 11th Regiment Kansas Volunteer Cavalry, and son of Moses Gove, a former mayor of Manhattan, Kansas.
