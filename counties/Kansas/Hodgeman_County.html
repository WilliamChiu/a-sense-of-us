Hodgeman County, Kansas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.46706796%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1816 people in Hodgeman County, Kansas lived in family households.

In 2000, 2085 people in Hodgeman County, Kansas lived in the county.

In 2010, 6034 people in Harper County, Kansas lived in the county.

Hodgeman County was founded in 1867. It was named for Amos Hodgman, member of the 7th Regiment Kansas Volunteer Cavalry. The letter E was later added to the namesake's name.
