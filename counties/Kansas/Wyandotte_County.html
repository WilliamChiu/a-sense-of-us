Wyandotte County, Kansas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.15707016%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 24972 people in Wyandotte County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 131318 people in Wyandotte County, Kansas lived in family households.

In 2000, 1592 people in Wyandotte County, Kansas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 157882 people in Wyandotte County, Kansas lived in the county.

In 2010, 281 people in Wichita County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1927 people in Wichita County, Kansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 26 people in Wichita County, Kansas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 2234 people in Wichita County, Kansas lived in the county.

The county is named after the Wyandot (also known as Wyandott or Wyandotte) Indians. They were called the Huron by the French in Canada, but they called themselves Wendat. They were distantly related to the Iroquois, with whom they sometimes fought. They had hoped to hold off movement by white Americans into their territory and had hoped to make the Ohio River the border between the United States and Canada.
