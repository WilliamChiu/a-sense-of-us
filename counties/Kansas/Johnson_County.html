Johnson County, Kansas

In 2000, 65666 people in Johnson County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 380442 people in Johnson County, Kansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 11549 people in Jackson County, Kansas lived in family households.

Johnson County is named for missionary Thomas Johnson, and was one of the first counties established in the Kansas Territory in 1855. The Oregon-California and Santa Fe Trails, which pass through nearby Independence, Missouri, also passed through the county. The renowned gunfighter Wild Bill Hickok settled for a time in the county, becoming constable of Monticello Township in 1858. Johnson County was the site of many battles between abolitionists and pro-slavery advocates during Bleeding Kansas. In 1862 Confederate Guerrillas from nearby Missouri led by William Quantrill raided the Johnson County communities of Olathe and Spring Hill, killing half a dozen men and destroying numerous homes and businesses.
