Riley County, Kansas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 11.705626%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 16114 people in Riley County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 37415 people in Riley County, Kansas lived in family households.

In 2000, 9314 people in Riley County, Kansas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 62843 people in Riley County, Kansas lived in the county.

In 2010, 10653 people in Reno County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 50729 people in Reno County, Kansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 3129 people in Reno County, Kansas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 64511 people in Reno County, Kansas lived in the county.

Riley County, named for Mexican-American War general Bennet Riley, was on the western edge of the 33 original counties established by the Kansas Territorial Legislature in August 1855. For organizational purposes, Riley County initially had attached to it Geary County and all land west of Riley County, across Kansas Territory into present-day Colorado.
