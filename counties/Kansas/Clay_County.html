Clay County, Kansas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.9530902%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1226 people in Clay County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 7431 people in Clay County, Kansas lived in family households.

In 2000, 165 people in Clay County, Kansas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 8822 people in Clay County, Kansas lived in the county.

In 2010, 3158 people in Cherokee County, Kansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 18243 people in Cherokee County, Kansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 202 people in Cherokee County, Kansas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 21603 people in Cherokee County, Kansas lived in the county.

When the first counties were created by the Kansas legislature in 1855, the territory within the present limits of the county was attached to Riley County for all revenue and judicial purposes. Subsequently Clay was attached to Geary County. In 1857, Clay was created and named in honor of the famous American statesman Henry Clay, a member of the United States Senate from Kentucky and United States Secretary of State in the 19th century.
