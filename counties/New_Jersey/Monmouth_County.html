Monmouth County, New Jersey

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.9607425%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 77404 people in Monmouth County, New Jersey lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 527861 people in Monmouth County, New Jersey lived in family households.

In 2000, 10036 people in Monmouth County, New Jersey lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 615301 people in Monmouth County, New Jersey lived in the county.

In 2010, 128349 people in Hunterdon County, New Jersey lived in the county.

An English navigator, Henry Hudson, and his crew aboard the Dutch vessel ''Half Moon'', in 1609, spotted land in what is now Monmouth County, most likely off Sandy Hook; however, some historical accounts credit this landing to present-day Keansburg.
