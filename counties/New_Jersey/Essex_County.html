Essex County, New Jersey

In 2000, 109784 people in Essex County, New Jersey lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 661060 people in Essex County, New Jersey lived in family households.

In 2010, 74509 people in Camden County, New Jersey lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 431734 people in Camden County, New Jersey lived in family households.

Essex was originally formed as one of four administrative districts within Province of East Jersey in 1675, together with Bergen, Middlesex and Monmouth districts. Essex County was formed within East Jersey on March 7, 1683. The county was named in honor the English county of Kingdom of Essex, one of the historical kingdoms of England. When the provinces of East Jersey and West Jersey were combined in 1702, the county boundaries were retained.
