Iron County, Utah

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.694572%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4602 people in Iron County, Utah lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 28484 people in Iron County, Utah lived in family households.

In 2000, 693 people in Iron County, Utah lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 33779 people in Iron County, Utah lived in the county.

In 2010, 896 people in Emery County, Utah lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 10037 people in Emery County, Utah lived in family households.

In 2010, 43 people in Emery County, Utah lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 10976 people in Emery County, Utah lived in the county.

Iron County is a county located in the U.S. state of Utah. As of the 2010 census, the population was 46,163. Its county seat is Parowan, and the largest city is Cedar City. It was originally named Little Salt Lake Valley, and renamed for the iron mines west of Cedar City.
