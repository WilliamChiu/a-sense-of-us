Garfield County, Utah

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.674068%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 438 people in Garfield County, Utah lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 4169 people in Garfield County, Utah lived in family households.

In 2000, 128 people in Garfield County, Utah lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 4735 people in Garfield County, Utah lived in the county.

In 2010, 21554 people in Davis County, Utah lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 281632 people in Davis County, Utah lived in family households.

In 2010, 3293 people in Davis County, Utah lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 306479 people in Davis County, Utah lived in the county.

Garfield County was founded in 1882. It was named for James A. Garfield, late President of the United States, who had been assassinated in 1881.
