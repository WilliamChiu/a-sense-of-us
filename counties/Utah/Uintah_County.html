Uintah County, Utah

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.46839118%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1970 people in Uintah County, Utah lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 23006 people in Uintah County, Utah lived in family households.

In 2000, 248 people in Uintah County, Utah lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 25224 people in Uintah County, Utah lived in the county.

In 2010, 1858 people in Sevier County, Utah lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 18643 people in Sevier County, Utah lived in family households.

In 2010, 301 people in Sevier County, Utah lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 20802 people in Sevier County, Utah lived in the county.

Archeologic evidence suggests that portions of the Uinta Basin have been inhabited by Archaic peoples and Fremont peoples. By the time of recorded history its inhabitants were the Ute people. The first known traverse by non-Indians was made by Fathers Dominguez and Escalante (1776), as they sought to establish a land route between California and Spanish America.
