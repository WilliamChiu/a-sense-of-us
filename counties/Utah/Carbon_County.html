Carbon County, Utah

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.323556%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2394 people in Carbon County, Utah lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 17492 people in Carbon County, Utah lived in family households.

In 2000, 536 people in Carbon County, Utah lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 20422 people in Carbon County, Utah lived in the county.

In 2010, 663 people in Beaver County, Utah lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5942 people in Beaver County, Utah lived in family households.

In 2010, 24 people in Beaver County, Utah lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 6629 people in Beaver County, Utah lived in the county.

Carbon County historically has been the base of Democratic Party support in strongly Republican Utah with its sizable blue-collar population. It voted for Bill Clinton in 1992 and 1996 by wide margins. In 1964 Lyndon Johnson carried 72.7% of votes in the county. At the state level it was no less Democratic; in the 1992 gubernatorial election it was one of two counties (the other being Summit County) that voted for Democratic candidate Stewart Hanson over Republican Michael Leavitt.
