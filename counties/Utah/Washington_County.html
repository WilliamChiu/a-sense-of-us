Household Populations

Washington County, Utah

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.9288788%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 8773 people in Washington County, Utah lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 80222 people in Washington County, Utah lived in family households.

In 2000, 1359 people in Washington County, Utah lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 90354 people in Washington County, Utah lived in the county.

In 2010, 3047 people in Uintah County, Utah lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 29349 people in Uintah County, Utah lived in family households.

In 2010, 192 people in Uintah County, Utah lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 32588 people in Uintah County, Utah lived in the county.

Established as colonies to grow cotton, before the beginning of the American Civil War, Utah's Dixie as the early settlements in Washington County were known, St. George, Grafton, Duncans Retreat, Adventure, and Northrop were nearly destroyed by the Great Flood of 1862 that followed forty-four days of rainfall in January and February 1862. Springdale and Rockville were founded in 1862 by settlers flooded out of Adventure, Northup and other places in the vicinity.
