Daggett County, Utah

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 8.511567%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 113 people in Daggett County, Utah lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 730 people in Daggett County, Utah lived in family households.

In 2000, 78 people in Daggett County, Utah lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 921 people in Daggett County, Utah lived in the county.

In 2010, 3721 people in Box Elder County, Utah lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 45915 people in Box Elder County, Utah lived in family households.

In 2010, 339 people in Box Elder County, Utah lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 49975 people in Box Elder County, Utah lived in the county.

Daggett County was formed in 1918. It received its name from Ellsworth Daggett, the first surveyor.
