Davis County, Utah

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.7593443%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 14969 people in Davis County, Utah lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 220544 people in Davis County, Utah lived in family households.

In 2000, 3481 people in Davis County, Utah lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 238994 people in Davis County, Utah lived in the county.

In 2010, 13548 people in Cache County, Utah lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 95514 people in Cache County, Utah lived in family households.

In 2010, 3594 people in Cache County, Utah lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 112656 people in Cache County, Utah lived in the county.

Davis County was created on October 5, 1850, with Farmington designated as its county seat, because of its geographic location midway between boundaries at the Weber River on the north and the Jordan River on the south. During its first 50 years, Davis County grew slowly. With the advent of the Utah Central Rail Road in 1870, a transition to mechanized agriculture and a surge of commerce, banking, improved roads, new water systems, and electrification of homes began. However, by 1940, the population was barely 16,000.
