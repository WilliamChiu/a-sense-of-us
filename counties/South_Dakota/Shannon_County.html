Shannon County, South Dakota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.22382736%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 511 people in Shannon County, South Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 11645 people in Shannon County, South Dakota lived in family households.

In 2000, 310 people in Shannon County, South Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 12466 people in Shannon County, South Dakota lived in the county.

In 2010, 455 people in Potter County, South Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1811 people in Potter County, South Dakota lived in family households.

In 2010, 63 people in Potter County, South Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 2329 people in Potter County, South Dakota lived in the county.

The county was originally named for Peter C. Shannon, Chief Justice of the Dakota Territory Supreme Court. Until 1982, Shannon County and Washabaugh County, South Dakota, were the last unorganized counties in the United States. Although it was organized and received a home rule charter that year, the county, as noted above, contracts with Fall River County for its Auditor, Treasurer, and Registrar of Deeds.
