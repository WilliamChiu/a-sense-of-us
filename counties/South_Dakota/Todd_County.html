Household Populations

Todd County, South Dakota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -4.974735%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 651 people in Todd County, South Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 8273 people in Todd County, South Dakota lived in family households.

In 2000, 126 people in Todd County, South Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 9050 people in Todd County, South Dakota lived in the county.

In 2010, 1050 people in Spink County, South Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 4961 people in Spink County, South Dakota lived in family households.

In 2010, 404 people in Spink County, South Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 6415 people in Spink County, South Dakota lived in the county.

Until 1981 Todd, Oglala Lakota (formerly Shannon) and Washabaugh County, South Dakota, were the last unorganized counties in the United States. Although then organized, Todd did not receive a home rule charter until 1983. As noted above, it contracts with Tripp County for its Auditor, Treasurer, and Registrar of Deeds.
