Campbell County, South Dakota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.4612556%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 227 people in Campbell County, South Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 1536 people in Campbell County, South Dakota lived in family households.

In 2000, 19 people in Campbell County, South Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 1782 people in Campbell County, South Dakota lived in the county.

In 2010, 896 people in Brule County, South Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 4227 people in Brule County, South Dakota lived in family households.

In 2010, 132 people in Brule County, South Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 5255 people in Brule County, South Dakota lived in the county.

Campbell County was formed in 1873 and organized in 1884. La Grace served as the first county seat; in 1888 the seat was transferred to Mound City.
