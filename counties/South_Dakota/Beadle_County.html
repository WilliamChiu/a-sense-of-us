Beadle County, South Dakota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.7305017%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 3066 people in Beadle County, South Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 13514 people in Beadle County, South Dakota lived in family households.

In 2000, 443 people in Beadle County, South Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 17023 people in Beadle County, South Dakota lived in the county.

In 2010, 4624 people in Williamsburg County, South Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1476 people in Williamsburg County, South Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 34423 people in Williamsburg County, South Carolina lived in the county.

Beadle County, named in honor of Brigadier General William Henry Harrison Beadle, was created by the Dakota Territory Legislature in 1879 and formally organized in 1880 with the appointment of three county commissioners by Governor Nehemiah G. Ordway. The first town within Beadle County was Cavour, but Huron was named the county seat when the county commissioners first met there in July 1880.
