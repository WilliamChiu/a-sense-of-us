Marshall County, South Dakota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.9786129%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 638 people in Marshall County, South Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 3849 people in Marshall County, South Dakota lived in family households.

In 2000, 89 people in Marshall County, South Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 4576 people in Marshall County, South Dakota lived in the county.

In 2010, 481 people in Lyman County, South Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 3237 people in Lyman County, South Dakota lived in family households.

In 2010, 37 people in Lyman County, South Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 3755 people in Lyman County, South Dakota lived in the county.

Marshall County is a county located in the U.S. state of South Dakota. As of the 2010 census, the population was 4,656. Its county seat is Britton. The county is named after Marshall Vincent, who homesteaded near Andover, South Dakota.
