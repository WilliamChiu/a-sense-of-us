Butte County, South Dakota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.329594%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1229 people in Butte County, South Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 7734 people in Butte County, South Dakota lived in family households.

In 2000, 131 people in Butte County, South Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 9094 people in Butte County, South Dakota lived in the county.

In 2010, 7398 people in Brown County, South Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 27768 people in Brown County, South Dakota lived in family households.

In 2010, 1365 people in Brown County, South Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 36531 people in Brown County, South Dakota lived in the county.

In 1975, Plesiosaurus fossils dating back to the Cretaceous Period were discovered in Butte County. These fossils were approximately 110 million years old. In the 1980s, a 90-million-year-old fossil of a clawless lobster was discovered. A tyrannosaurus rex skeleton was found in 1981.
