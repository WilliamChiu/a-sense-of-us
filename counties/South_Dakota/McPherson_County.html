McPherson County, South Dakota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.7490268%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 431 people in McPherson County, South Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 2405 people in McPherson County, South Dakota lived in family households.

In 2000, 68 people in McPherson County, South Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 2904 people in McPherson County, South Dakota lived in the county.

In 2010, 5610 people in Lincoln County, South Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 38934 people in Lincoln County, South Dakota lived in family households.

In 2010, 284 people in Lincoln County, South Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 44828 people in Lincoln County, South Dakota lived in the county.

The county was created in 1873 and organized in 1884. It is named after American Civil War General James B. McPherson.
