Household Populations

Jerauld County, South Dakota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -9.757673%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 375 people in Jerauld County, South Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 1880 people in Jerauld County, South Dakota lived in family households.

In 2000, 40 people in Jerauld County, South Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 2295 people in Jerauld County, South Dakota lived in the county.

In 2010, 1162 people in Hutchinson County, South Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5349 people in Hutchinson County, South Dakota lived in family households.

In 2010, 832 people in Hutchinson County, South Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 7343 people in Hutchinson County, South Dakota lived in the county.

In 1873, the area occupied by the present Jerauld county was organized into '''Wetmore County'''. In 1881, Wetmore and its neighbor county to the south, Cragin County, were combined to form Aurora County. In 1883, the area of the former Wetmore County was reincorporated as present-day Jerauld County.
