McCook County, South Dakota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.852864%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 709 people in McCook County, South Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 4969 people in McCook County, South Dakota lived in family households.

In 2000, 154 people in McCook County, South Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 5832 people in McCook County, South Dakota lived in the county.

In 2010, 5458 people in Lawrence County, South Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 17568 people in Lawrence County, South Dakota lived in family households.

In 2010, 1071 people in Lawrence County, South Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 24097 people in Lawrence County, South Dakota lived in the county.

McCook County is a county located in the U.S. state of South Dakota. At the 2010 census, the population was 5,618. Its county seat is Salem. The county was established in 1873 and organized in 1878. It was named for the former governor of the Dakota Territory and Civil War general Edwin Stanton McCook.
