Mora County, New Mexico

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.5635023%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 702 people in Mora County, New Mexico lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 4422 people in Mora County, New Mexico lived in family households.

In 2000, 56 people in Mora County, New Mexico lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 5180 people in Mora County, New Mexico lived in the county.

In 2010, 2795 people in Los Alamos County, New Mexico lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 15061 people in Los Alamos County, New Mexico lived in family households.

In 2010, 94 people in Los Alamos County, New Mexico lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 17950 people in Los Alamos County, New Mexico lived in the county.

Prior to Spanish conquest, the Mora area was Indian country. Although not an area of heavy Indian settlement by such tribes as the Pueblo Indians, the Mora valley was much used by nomadic tribes: the Utes, Navajos and Apache.
