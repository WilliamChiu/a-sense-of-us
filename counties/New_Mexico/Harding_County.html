Harding County is a county located in the U.S. state of New Mexico. As of the 2010 census, the population was 695, making it the least populous county in the state. Its county seat is the Village of Mosquero. The county is named for United States President Warren G. Harding, and was created (from parts of Union and Mora Counties) on the day of his inauguration as President on March 4, 1921.
