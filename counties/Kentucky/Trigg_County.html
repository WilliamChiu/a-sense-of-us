Trigg County, Kentucky

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.2207756%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1620 people in Trigg County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10866 people in Trigg County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2000, 111 people in Trigg County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 12597 people in Trigg County, Kentucky lived in the county.

In 2010, 1597 people in Spencer County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 15351 people in Spencer County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 113 people in Spencer County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 17061 people in Spencer County, Kentucky lived in the county.

Trigg County was named in honor of Lt. Col. Stephen Trigg, of Virginia. Lt. Col. Trigg settled near Harrodsburg, Kentucky and was killed on August 19, 1782, in the Battle of Blue Licks.
