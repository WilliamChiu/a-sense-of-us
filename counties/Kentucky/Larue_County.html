LaRue County is a county located in the U.S. state of Kentucky. As of the 2010 census, the population was 14,193. Its county seat is Hodgenville, a city better known for being the birthplace of United States President Abraham Lincoln. The county was formed on March 4, 1843 from portions of Hardin County and named after John LaRue, an early settler in this area.
