Nelson County, Kentucky

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.54416656%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4391 people in Nelson County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 32381 people in Nelson County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2000, 705 people in Nelson County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 37477 people in Nelson County, Kentucky lived in the county.

In 2010, 3870 people in Montgomery County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 22272 people in Montgomery County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 357 people in Montgomery County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 26499 people in Montgomery County, Kentucky lived in the county.

The fourth county created in what is now Kentucky, it was formed from Jefferson County, Virginia in 1784 shortly after the Revolutionary War. The county is named for Thomas Nelson, Jr., a Virginia Governor who signed the Declaration of Independence. In 1808, another new Virginia county was named for Governor Nelson. (see Nelson County, Virginia).
