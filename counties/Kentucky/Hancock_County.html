Hancock County, Kentucky

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.1434128%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 898 people in Hancock County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 7422 people in Hancock County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2000, 72 people in Hancock County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 8392 people in Hancock County, Kentucky lived in the county.

In 2010, 3495 people in Grayson County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 21483 people in Grayson County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 768 people in Grayson County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 25746 people in Grayson County, Kentucky lived in the county.

Hancock County was formed in 1829 from portions of Breckinridge, Daviess, and Ohio counties. The county is named for John Hancock, President of the Continental Congress and one of the signers of the Declaration of Independence.
