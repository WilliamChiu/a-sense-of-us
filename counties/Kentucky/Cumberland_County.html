Cumberland County, Kentucky

In 2000, 1036 people in Cumberland County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6003 people in Cumberland County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 2477 people in Clay County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 17013 people in Clay County, Kentucky lived in family households.

Cumberland County was created in 1798 from land given by Green County. In 1829, the first oil well in the United States was dug three miles north of Burkesville. However, it is usually not recognized as such because the drillers were looking for salt brine.
