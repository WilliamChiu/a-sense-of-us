Cumberland County, Kentucky

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -8.932185%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1036 people in Cumberland County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6003 people in Cumberland County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2000, 108 people in Cumberland County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 7147 people in Cumberland County, Kentucky lived in the county.

In 2010, 2477 people in Clay County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 2240 people in Clay County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 21730 people in Clay County, Kentucky lived in the county.

Cumberland County was created in 1798 from land given by Green County. In 1829, the first oil well in the United States was dug three miles north of Burkesville. However, it is usually not recognized as such because the drillers were looking for salt brine.
