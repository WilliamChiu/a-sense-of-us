Hopkins County, Kentucky

In 2000, 6142 people in Hopkins County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 39572 people in Hopkins County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 7315 people in Henderson County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 37770 people in Henderson County, Kentucky lived in family households.

The earliest inhabitants were prehistoric Native Americans who lived, hunted, and farmed in the region. One of their settlements was a rough stone structure on Fort Ridge, which has since been destroyed by strip mining. Some of the early settlers were veterans of the Revolutionary War who received land grants from Virginia in the area southwest of the Green River. Among these was Baron Von Steuben, a Prussian general who had instructed the George Washington's Continental Army at Valley Forge in the winter of 1776-77. According to tradition, Von Steuben was wounded during an Indian attack while on his first visit and quit-claimed his property. A salt spring on his grant of several thousand acres in the northwest part of the county came to be known as Steuben's Lick. By the 1880s, the community at the lick was called Manitou.
