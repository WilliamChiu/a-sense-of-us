Lee County, Kentucky

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 7.4750423%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 947 people in Lee County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6256 people in Lee County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2000, 713 people in Lee County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 7916 people in Lee County, Kentucky lived in the county.

In 2010, 1958 people in Larue County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 11922 people in Larue County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 313 people in Larue County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 14193 people in Larue County, Kentucky lived in the county.

Lee County is a county located in the U.S. state of Kentucky. As of the 2010 census, the population was 7,887. Its county seat is Beattyville. The county was formed in 1870 from parts of Breathitt, Estill, Owsley and Wolfe counties.
