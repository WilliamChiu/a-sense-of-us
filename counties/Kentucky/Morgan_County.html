Morgan County, Kentucky

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 13.844562%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1319 people in Morgan County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10786 people in Morgan County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2000, 1843 people in Morgan County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 13948 people in Morgan County, Kentucky lived in the county.

In 2010, 1452 people in Metcalfe County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 8526 people in Metcalfe County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 121 people in Metcalfe County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 10099 people in Metcalfe County, Kentucky lived in the county.

Morgan County was formed on December 7, 1822 from portions of Bath County and Floyd County. It was named for Daniel Morgan, a general in the American Revolutionary War.
