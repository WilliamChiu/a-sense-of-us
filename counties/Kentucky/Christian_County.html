Christian County, Kentucky

In 2000, 7712 people in Christian County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 58470 people in Christian County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 1591 people in Carroll County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 8895 people in Carroll County, Kentucky lived in family households.

The county is named for Colonel William Christian, a native of Augusta County, Virginia, and a veteran of the Revolutionary War. He settled near Louisville, Kentucky in 1785, and was killed by Native Americans in southern Indiana in 1786.
