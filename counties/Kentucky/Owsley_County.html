Owsley County, Kentucky

In 2000, 552 people in Owsley County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 4210 people in Owsley County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 3088 people in Ohio County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 20447 people in Ohio County, Kentucky lived in family households.

Owsley County was formed in 1843 from portions of Clay, Breathitt, and Estill Counties and was named for Governor William Owsley. Owsley County was Kentucky's 96th county. Parts of Owsley County were used to form Jackson County in 1858 and Lee County in 1870.
