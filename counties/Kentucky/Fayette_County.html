Fayette County, Kentucky

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.3422003%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 60331 people in Fayette County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 187458 people in Fayette County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2000, 12723 people in Fayette County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 260512 people in Fayette County, Kentucky lived in the county.

In 2010, 1631 people in Edmonson County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 10207 people in Edmonson County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 323 people in Edmonson County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 12161 people in Edmonson County, Kentucky lived in the county.

Fayette County was one of the original three counties created from the former Kentucky County, Virginia, by the Virginia Act in 1780, along with Jefferson and Lincoln counties. Together they separated from Virginia in 1792 to become the Commonwealth of Kentucky.
