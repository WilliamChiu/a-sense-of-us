Robertson County, Kentucky

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.4420033%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 280 people in Robertson County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 1916 people in Robertson County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2000, 70 people in Robertson County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 2266 people in Robertson County, Kentucky lived in the county.

In 2010, 9288 people in Pike County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 54636 people in Pike County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 1100 people in Pike County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 65024 people in Pike County, Kentucky lived in the county.

Robertson County was formed on February 11, 1867 from portions of Bracken County, Harrison County, Mason County and Nicholas County. It was named after George Robertson, a judge and member of Congress.
