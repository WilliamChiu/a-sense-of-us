Franklin County, Kentucky

In 2000, 8273 people in Franklin County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 37589 people in Franklin County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 71812 people in Fayette County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 211187 people in Fayette County, Kentucky lived in family households.

The three original counties of Kentucky (Jefferson, Fayette and Lincoln counties) intersected in what is today Franklin County. Franklin County was established in 1795 from land given by Mercer, Shelby, and Woodford counties. Franklin was the 18th Kentucky county in order of formation.
