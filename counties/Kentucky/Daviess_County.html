Daviess County, Kentucky

In 2000, 13011 people in Daviess County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 75939 people in Daviess County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 1656 people in Clinton County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 8480 people in Clinton County, Kentucky lived in family households.

Daviess County was established in 1815. The county is named for Major Joseph Hamilton Daveiss (a recording error in the State Clerk's office accounts for the error in spelling, which was never corrected), the United States Attorney who unsuccessfully prosecuted Aaron Burr. The county's borders were altered in 1829 to form Hancock County, in 1830 to absorb a small area surrounding Whitesville, in 1854 to cede land to McLean County, and in 1860 to annex 44 sqmi from Henderson County.
