Johnson County, Kentucky

In 2000, 2509 people in Johnson County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 20420 people in Johnson County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 1888 people in Jackson County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 11496 people in Jackson County, Kentucky lived in family households.

Johnson County was formed in 1843 from land given by Floyd County. At that time, its county seat of Paintsville had already been a chartered city for nine years. Homes had been built in Paintsville as early as the 1810s.
