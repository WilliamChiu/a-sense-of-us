Breckinridge County, Kentucky

In 2000, 2282 people in Breckinridge County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 16093 people in Breckinridge County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 4186 people in Boyle County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 21945 people in Boyle County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In August 1779, Sinclair Hardin (first cousin of Captain William Hardin, the founder of Hardin's Fort), became the first white man in Breckinridge County who was killed by native Americans (the Shawnee). Sinclair Hardin was killed at Big Springs while taking a drink from its cool waters.
