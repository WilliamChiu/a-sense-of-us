Simpson County, Kentucky

In 2000, 2062 people in Simpson County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14107 people in Simpson County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 2773 people in Russell County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 14615 people in Russell County, Kentucky lived in family households.

Simpson County was established in 1819 from Allen, Logan, and Warren Counties. The county is named for Captain John Simpson, a Kentucky militia officer who fought in Battle of Fallen Timbers in the Northwest Indian War, and was killed during the War of 1812 in the Battle of River Raisin.
