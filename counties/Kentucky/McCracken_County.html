McCracken County, Kentucky

In 2000, 10515 people in McCracken County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 53665 people in McCracken County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 1432 people in Livingston County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 8018 people in Livingston County, Kentucky lived in family households.

McCracken County was founded in 1825, from Hickman County and was named for Captain Virgil McCracken of Woodford County, Kentucky, who was killed in the Battle of Frenchtown in southeastern Michigan during the War of 1812.
