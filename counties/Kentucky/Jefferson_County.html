Jefferson County, Kentucky

In 2000, 124462 people in Jefferson County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 556347 people in Jefferson County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 778 people in Hickman County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 3912 people in Hickman County, Kentucky lived in family households.

Jefferson County was organized in 1780 and one of the first three counties formed out of the original Kentucky County, which was still part of Virginia at the time (the other two being Fayette and Lincoln). The county is named for Thomas Jefferson, who was governor of Virginia at the time.
