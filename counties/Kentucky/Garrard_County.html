Garrard County, Kentucky

In 2000, 1638 people in Garrard County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 13056 people in Garrard County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 9321 people in Franklin County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 37903 people in Franklin County, Kentucky lived in family households.

Garrard County was formed in 1796 from parts of Lincoln County, Madison County and Mercer County. It was named for Col. James Garrard, second Governor of Kentucky and acting governor at the time of the county's establishment.
