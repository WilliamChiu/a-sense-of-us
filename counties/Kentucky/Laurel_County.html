Laurel County, Kentucky

In 2000, 5694 people in Laurel County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 46352 people in Laurel County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 2196 people in Knott County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 13473 people in Knott County, Kentucky lived in family households.

Laurel County, the 80th county to be organized in Kentucky, was established by an act of the general assembly, December 21, 1825, from parts of Rockcastle, Clay, Knox and Whitley Counties. Laurel County was named from the Laurel River, noted for dense laurel thickets along its banks.
