Kenton County, Kentucky

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.68621635%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 24266 people in Kenton County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 125332 people in Kenton County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2000, 1866 people in Kenton County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 151464 people in Kenton County, Kentucky lived in the county.

In 2010, 146508 people in Jefferson County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 580435 people in Jefferson County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 14153 people in Jefferson County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 741096 people in Jefferson County, Kentucky lived in the county.

Kenton County was established on January 29, 1840, from land given by Campbell County. It was named in honor of Simon Kenton, a pioneer of Kentucky.
