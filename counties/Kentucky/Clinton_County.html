Clinton County, Kentucky

In 2000, 1427 people in Clinton County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 8144 people in Clinton County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 9221 people in Christian County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 59345 people in Christian County, Kentucky lived in family households.

Clinton County was formed on February 20, 1835 from portions of Cumberland and Wayne counties. It was named for DeWitt Clinton, governor of New York and driving force behind the Erie Canal.
