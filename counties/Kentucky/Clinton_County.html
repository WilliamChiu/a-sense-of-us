Clinton County, Kentucky

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -6.6765904%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1427 people in Clinton County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 8144 people in Clinton County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2000, 63 people in Clinton County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 9634 people in Clinton County, Kentucky lived in the county.

In 2010, 9221 people in Christian County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 59345 people in Christian County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 5389 people in Christian County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 73955 people in Christian County, Kentucky lived in the county.

Clinton County was formed on February 20, 1835 from portions of Cumberland and Wayne counties. It was named for DeWitt Clinton, governor of New York and driving force behind the Erie Canal.
