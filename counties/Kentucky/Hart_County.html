Hart County, Kentucky

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.7102361%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2258 people in Hart County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14943 people in Hart County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2000, 244 people in Hart County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 17445 people in Hart County, Kentucky lived in the county.

In 2010, 13862 people in Hardin County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 88425 people in Hardin County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 3256 people in Hardin County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 105543 people in Hardin County, Kentucky lived in the county.

Hart County was formed in 1819 from portions of Hardin and Barren counties. The county is named for Captain Nathaniel G. S. Hart, a Kentucky militia officer in the War of 1812 who was wounded at the Battle of Frenchtown and died in the Massacre of the River Raisin.
