Rowan County, Kentucky

In 2000, 3475 people in Rowan County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 15463 people in Rowan County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 9492 people in Pulaski County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 52609 people in Pulaski County, Kentucky lived in family households.

It is believed that Rowan County was first explored in 1773 by a party of surveyors from Pennsylvania. The first settlement was established in Farmers, a town 10 miles west of Morehead. Its population rapidly increased due its fertile farming land and proximity to water sources. Additional settlers came to Rowan County from Virginia in the late 18th century after being awarded land grants at the end of the American Revolutionary War. Clearfield was the second settlement established in the county, being colonized by a Virginia aristocrat named Dixon Clack in the early 1800s. It was the site of the first and largest sawmill in the county.
