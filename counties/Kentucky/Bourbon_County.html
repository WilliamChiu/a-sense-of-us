Bourbon County, Kentucky

In 2000, 2674 people in Bourbon County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 16487 people in Bourbon County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 1605 people in Bath County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 9883 people in Bath County, Kentucky lived in family households.

Bourbon County was established in 1785 from land given by Fayette County, Virginia, and named after the French House of Bourbon, in gratitude for Louis XVI of France's assistance during the American Revolutionary War. Bourbon became part of the new state of Kentucky when it was created in 1792.
