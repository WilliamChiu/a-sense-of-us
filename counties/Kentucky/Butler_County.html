Butler County, Kentucky

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.5000055%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1556 people in Butler County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 11216 people in Butler County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2000, 238 people in Butler County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 13010 people in Butler County, Kentucky lived in the county.

In 2010, 1946 people in Breathitt County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 11610 people in Breathitt County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 322 people in Breathitt County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 13878 people in Breathitt County, Kentucky lived in the county.

Numerous archaeological sites are located along the Green River in Butler County. A 1932 survey found nine sites, many of which were a group of shell mounds, including the Carlston Annis and DeWeese Shell Mounds.
