Carter County, Kentucky

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.9783447%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2962 people in Carter County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 23302 people in Carter County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2000, 625 people in Carter County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 26889 people in Carter County, Kentucky lived in the county.

In 2010, 16588 people in Campbell County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 70785 people in Campbell County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 2963 people in Campbell County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 90336 people in Campbell County, Kentucky lived in the county.

Carter County was formed on February 9, 1838 from portions of Greenup County and Lawrence County. It was named after Colonel William Grayson Carter, a Kentucky state Senator.
