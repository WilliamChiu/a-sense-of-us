Knox County, Kentucky

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.23684502%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 3838 people in Knox County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 27312 people in Knox County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2000, 645 people in Knox County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 31795 people in Knox County, Kentucky lived in the county.

In 2010, 3161 people in Johnson County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 19667 people in Johnson County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 528 people in Johnson County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 23356 people in Johnson County, Kentucky lived in the county.

Knox County was formed on December 19, 1799, from portions of Lincoln County. It was named for Henry Knox, a general and the first United States Secretary of War.
