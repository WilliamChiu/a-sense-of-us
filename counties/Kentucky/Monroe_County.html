Monroe County, Kentucky

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.5615394%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1529 people in Monroe County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10096 people in Monroe County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2000, 131 people in Monroe County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 11756 people in Monroe County, Kentucky lived in the county.

In 2010, 828 people in Menifee County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5248 people in Menifee County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 230 people in Menifee County, Kentucky lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 6306 people in Menifee County, Kentucky lived in the county.

Monroe County is the only county of the 2,957 in the United States named for a President where the county seat is named for his Vice-President. The county was formed in 1820; and named for James Monroe the fifth President, author of the Monroe Doctrine. The county seat was named for Daniel Tompkins. They both served from 1817 to 1825.
