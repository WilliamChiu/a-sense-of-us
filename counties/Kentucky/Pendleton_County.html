Pendleton County, Kentucky

In 2000, 1432 people in Pendleton County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 12779 people in Pendleton County, Kentucky lived in family households.

In 2010, 4513 people in Oldham County, Kentucky lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 51235 people in Oldham County, Kentucky lived in family households.

Pendleton County was created from parts of Campbell and Bracken counties in 1798. The county was named after Edmund Pendleton, a longtime member of the Virginia House of Burgesses, the Continental Congress and chief justice of Virginia.
