Garfield County, Washington

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.8218338%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 367 people in Garfield County, Washington lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 1993 people in Garfield County, Washington lived in family households.

In 2000, 37 people in Garfield County, Washington lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 2397 people in Garfield County, Washington lived in the county.

In 2010, 1370 people in Ferry County, Washington lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5929 people in Ferry County, Washington lived in family households.

In 2010, 252 people in Ferry County, Washington lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 7551 people in Ferry County, Washington lived in the county.

Garfield County was created out of a large piece of Columbia County on November 29, 1881 Roughly half of the county was split off to form Asotin County in 1883. and was named for the late U.S. President James A. Garfield, who had been assassinated in September, 1881.
