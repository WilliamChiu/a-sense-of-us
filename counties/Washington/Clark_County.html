Clark County, Washington

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.40267706%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 47208 people in Clark County, Washington lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 294986 people in Clark County, Washington lived in family households.

In 2000, 3044 people in Clark County, Washington lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 345238 people in Clark County, Washington lived in the county.

In 2010, 11204 people in Chelan County, Washington lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 60321 people in Chelan County, Washington lived in family households.

In 2010, 928 people in Chelan County, Washington lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 72453 people in Chelan County, Washington lived in the county.

Clark County began as the ''District of Vancouver'' on July 27, 1844. It included all the land north of the Columbia River, west of the Rocky Mountains, and south of Alaska. In 1845 the provisional government changed its name to Vancouver County. At that time it stretched from the Columbia River to 54 degrees 40 minutes North Latitude in British Columbia. On June 15, 1846 the United States Senate approved the present boundary between the U.S. and Canada at the 49th Parallel.
