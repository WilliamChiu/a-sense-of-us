Sacramento County, California

In 2000, 201037 people in Sacramento County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 996967 people in Sacramento County, California lived in family households.

In 2010, 4068 people in Plumas County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 15662 people in Plumas County, California lived in family households.

The county was named after the Sacramento River, which forms its western border. The river was named by Spanish cavalry officer Gabriel Moraga for the ''Santisimo Sacramento'' (Most Holy Sacrament), referring to the Catholic Eucharist.
