Amador County, California

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 12.159038%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4466 people in Amador County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 26053 people in Amador County, California lived in family households.

In 2000, 4581 people in Amador County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 35100 people in Amador County, California lived in the county.

In 2010, 265032 people in Alameda County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1207797 people in Alameda County, California lived in family households.

In 2010, 37442 people in Alameda County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 1510271 people in Alameda County, California lived in the county.

Amador County was created by the California Legislature on May 11, 1854. The county later split into Amador, Calaveras and El Dorado Counties. It was organized on July 3, 1854. In 1864, part of the county's territory was given to Alpine County.
