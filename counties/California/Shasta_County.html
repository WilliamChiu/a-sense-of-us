Shasta County, California

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.3614545%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 24324 people in Shasta County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 135573 people in Shasta County, California lived in family households.

In 2000, 3359 people in Shasta County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 163256 people in Shasta County, California lived in the county.

In 2010, 244168 people in Santa Clara County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1507124 people in Santa Clara County, California lived in family households.

In 2010, 30350 people in Santa Clara County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 1781642 people in Santa Clara County, California lived in the county.

Shasta County was one of the original counties of California, created in 1850 at the time of statehood. Parts of the county's territory were given to Siskiyou County in 1852, and to Tehama County in 1856.
