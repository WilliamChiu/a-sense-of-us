Calaveras County, California

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -11.015153%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 5794 people in Calaveras County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 34335 people in Calaveras County, California lived in family households.

In 2000, 425 people in Calaveras County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 40554 people in Calaveras County, California lived in the county.

In 2010, 5783 people in Amador County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 27757 people in Amador County, California lived in family households.

In 2010, 4551 people in Amador County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 38091 people in Amador County, California lived in the county.

Calaveras County was one of the original counties of the state of California, created in 1850 at the time of admission to the Union. Parts of the county's territory were reassigned to Amador County in 1854 and to Alpine County in 1864.
