Household Populations

Modoc County, California

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.1260262%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1444 people in Modoc County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 7593 people in Modoc County, California lived in family households.

In 2000, 412 people in Modoc County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 9449 people in Modoc County, California lived in the county.

In 2010, 17195 people in Mendocino County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 68602 people in Mendocino County, California lived in family households.

In 2010, 2044 people in Mendocino County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 87841 people in Mendocino County, California lived in the county.

Prior to the arrival of Europeans in the region, varying cultures of Native Americans inhabited the county for thousands of years. At the time of European encounter, the Modoc people lived in what is now northern California, near Lost River and Tule Lake. The county was named after them. The Achumawi (or Pit River Indians, for which the Pit River is named), and the Paiute also lived in the area. To the north were the Klamath in present-day Oregon.
