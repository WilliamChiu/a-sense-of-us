Mono County, California

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.8962631%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2846 people in Mono County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 9649 people in Mono County, California lived in family households.

In 2000, 358 people in Mono County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 12853 people in Mono County, California lived in the county.

In 2010, 22487 people in Merced County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 228410 people in Merced County, California lived in family households.

In 2010, 4896 people in Merced County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 255793 people in Merced County, California lived in the county.

Mono County was formed in 1861 from parts of Calaveras, Fresno and Mariposa counties. Parts of the county's territory were given to Inyo County in 1866.
