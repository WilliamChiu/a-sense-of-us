Tehama County, California

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.5781889%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 7497 people in Tehama County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 47537 people in Tehama County, California lived in family households.

In 2000, 1005 people in Tehama County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 56039 people in Tehama County, California lived in the county.

In 2010, 53724 people in Stanislaus County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 454424 people in Stanislaus County, California lived in family households.

In 2010, 6305 people in Stanislaus County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 514453 people in Stanislaus County, California lived in the county.

The county is named for the City of Tehama. The origin of the name is not known. Suggested possible roots are the Arabic word تهامة ''tehama'' ("hot low-lands"), the Spanish word ''tejamanil'' (shingle), or "high water" in the dialect of local Native Americans.
