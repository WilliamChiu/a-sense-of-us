Tehama County, California

In 2000, 7497 people in Tehama County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 47537 people in Tehama County, California lived in family households.

In 2010, 53724 people in Stanislaus County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 454424 people in Stanislaus County, California lived in family households.

The county is named for the City of Tehama. The origin of the name is not known. Suggested possible roots are the Arabic word تهامة ''tehama'' ("hot low-lands"), the Spanish word ''tejamanil'' (shingle), or "high water" in the dialect of local Native Americans.
