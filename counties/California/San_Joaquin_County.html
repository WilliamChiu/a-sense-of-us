San Joaquin County, California

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.03761053%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 18771 people in San Joaquin County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 563598 people in San Joaquin County, California lived in the county.

In 2010, 513694 people in San Diego County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 2479653 people in San Diego County, California lived in family households.

In 2010, 101966 people in San Diego County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 3095313 people in San Diego County, California lived in the county.

The county takes its name from the San Joaquin River. In the early 19th century Lieutenant Gabriel Moraga, commanding an expedition in the lower great California Central Valley, gave the name of ''San Joaquin'' (meaning Joachim) to the San Joaquin River that springs from the southern Sierra Nevada. San Joaquin County is also home to the site of the San Joaquin Valley's first permanent residence.
