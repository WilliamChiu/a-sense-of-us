Colusa County, California

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.13397932%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1837 people in Colusa County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 16520 people in Colusa County, California lived in family households.

In 2000, 447 people in Colusa County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 18804 people in Colusa County, California lived in the county.

In 2010, 51453 people in Butte County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 163605 people in Butte County, California lived in family households.

In 2010, 4942 people in Butte County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 220000 people in Butte County, California lived in the county.

Colusa County is one of the original counties of California, created in 1850 at the time of statehood. Parts of the county's territory were given to Tehama County in 1856 and to Glenn County in 1891.
