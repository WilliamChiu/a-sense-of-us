Sierra County, California

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -3.2002926%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 646 people in Sierra County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 2873 people in Sierra County, California lived in family households.

In 2000, 36 people in Sierra County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 3555 people in Sierra County, California lived in the county.

In 2010, 55228 people in Santa Cruz County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 196185 people in Santa Cruz County, California lived in family households.

In 2010, 10969 people in Santa Cruz County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 262382 people in Santa Cruz County, California lived in the county.

Sierra County was formed from parts of Yuba County in 1852. The county derives its name from the Sierra Nevada.
