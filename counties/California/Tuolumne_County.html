Tuolumne County, California

In 2000, 8345 people in Tuolumne County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 41320 people in Tuolumne County, California lived in family households.

In 2010, 3083 people in Trinity County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 10318 people in Trinity County, California lived in family households.

Tuolumne County is one of the original counties of California, created in 1850 at the time of statehood. Prior to statehood, it had been referred to as Oro County. Parts of the county were given to Stanislaus County in 1854 and to Alpine County in 1864. The county, and Sonora, have achieved some notoriety as the actual setting of Dale Koby's cult/underground classic, ''Campus Sexpot''; Sonora was given the name Wattsville in that novel. The sequel, ''From Roundheel To Revolutionary'', also takes place primarily in "Wattsville". See the Wikipedia page, '''David Carkeet'''.
