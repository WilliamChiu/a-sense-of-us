Siskiyou County, California

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.060927868%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 7557 people in Siskiyou County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 36054 people in Siskiyou County, California lived in family households.

In 2000, 690 people in Siskiyou County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 44301 people in Siskiyou County, California lived in the county.

In 2010, 30550 people in Shasta County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 144019 people in Shasta County, California lived in family households.

In 2010, 2654 people in Shasta County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 177223 people in Shasta County, California lived in the county.

Siskiyou County was created on March 22, 1852, from parts of Shasta and Klamath Counties, and named after the Siskiyou mountain range. Parts of the county's territory were given to Modoc County in 1855.
