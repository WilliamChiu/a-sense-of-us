Humboldt County, California

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.3804674%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 28425 people in Humboldt County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 94020 people in Humboldt County, California lived in family households.

In 2000, 4073 people in Humboldt County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 126518 people in Humboldt County, California lived in the county.

In 2010, 100585 people in Fresno County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 812342 people in Fresno County, California lived in family households.

In 2010, 17523 people in Fresno County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 930450 people in Fresno County, California lived in the county.

The original inhabitants of the area now known as Humboldt County include the Wiyot, Yurok, Hupa, Karuk, Chilula, Whilkut, and the Eel River Athapaskan peoples, including the Wailaki, Mattole and Nongatl. Andrés de Urdaneta found the coast near Cape Mendocino then followed the coast south to Acapulco in 1565. Spanish traders made unintended visits to California with the Manila Galleons on their return trips from the Philippines beginning in 1565. Humboldt County was formed in 1853 from parts of Trinity County. The first recorded entry by people of European origin was a landing by the Spanish in 1775 in Trinidad.
