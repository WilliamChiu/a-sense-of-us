Mariposa County, California

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.845037%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2555 people in Mariposa County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 13149 people in Mariposa County, California lived in family households.

In 2000, 1426 people in Mariposa County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 17130 people in Mariposa County, California lived in the county.

In 2010, 12230 people in Madera County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 130011 people in Madera County, California lived in family households.

In 2010, 8624 people in Madera County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 150865 people in Madera County, California lived in the county.

Mariposa County was one of the original counties of California, created at the time of statehood in 1850. While it began as the state's largest county, territory that was once part of Mariposa was ceded over time to form twelve other counties: Fresno, Inyo, Kern, Kings, Los Angeles, Madera, Merced, Mono, San Benito, San Bernardino, San Luis Obispo, and Tulare. Thus, Mariposa County is known as the "Mother of Counties".
