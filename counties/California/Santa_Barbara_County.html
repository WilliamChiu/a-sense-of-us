Santa Barbara County, California

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.239132%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 70399 people in Santa Barbara County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 312331 people in Santa Barbara County, California lived in family households.

In 2000, 16617 people in Santa Barbara County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 399347 people in Santa Barbara County, California lived in the county.

In 2010, 56553 people in San Luis Obispo County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 196078 people in San Luis Obispo County, California lived in family households.

In 2010, 17006 people in San Luis Obispo County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 269637 people in San Luis Obispo County, California lived in the county.

The Santa Barbara County area, including the Northern Channel Islands, was first settled by Native Americans at least 13,000 years ago. Evidence for a Paleoindian presence has been found in the form of a fluted Clovis-like point found in the 1980s along the western Santa Barbara Coast, as well as the remains of Arlington Springs Man found on Santa Rosa Island in the 1960s. For thousands of years, the area was home to the Chumash tribe of Native Americans, complex hunter-gatherers who lived along the coast and in interior valleys leaving rock art in many locations including Painted Cave.
