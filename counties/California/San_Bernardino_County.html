San Bernardino County, California

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.9836674%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 161200 people in San Bernardino County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 1503202 people in San Bernardino County, California lived in family households.

In 2000, 45032 people in San Bernardino County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 1709434 people in San Bernardino County, California lived in the county.

In 2010, 229568 people in Sacramento County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1165433 people in Sacramento County, California lived in family households.

In 2010, 23787 people in Sacramento County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 1418788 people in Sacramento County, California lived in the county.

Spanish Missionaries from Mission San Gabriel Arcángel established a church at the village of Politania in 1810. Father Francisco Dumetz named the church San Bernardino on May 20, 1810, after the feast day of St. Bernardino of Siena. The Franciscans also gave the name San Bernardino to the snowcapped peak in Southern California, in honor of the saint and it is from him that the county derives its name. In 1819, they established the San Bernardino de Sena Estancia, a mission farm in what is now Redlands.
