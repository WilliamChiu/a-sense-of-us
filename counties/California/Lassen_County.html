Lassen County, California

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 16.607046%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 3477 people in Lassen County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 21441 people in Lassen County, California lived in family households.

In 2000, 8910 people in Lassen County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 33828 people in Lassen County, California lived in the county.

In 2010, 12292 people in Kings County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 119110 people in Kings County, California lived in family households.

In 2010, 21580 people in Kings County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 152982 people in Kings County, California lived in the county.

Lassen County was formed on April 1, 1864 from parts of Plumas and Shasta Counties following the two-day conflict known as the "Sagebrush War", also called the Roop County War, that started on February 15, 1863. Due to uncertainties over the California border, the area that is now Lassen County was part of the unofficial Nataqua Territory and Roop County, Nevada during the late 1850s and early 1860s.
