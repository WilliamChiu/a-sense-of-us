Group Quarters vs Households

Napa County, California

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.7639031%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 18767 people in Napa County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 100279 people in Napa County, California lived in family households.

In 2000, 5233 people in Napa County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 124279 people in Napa County, California lived in the county.

In 2010, 3446 people in Mono County, California lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 10534 people in Mono County, California lived in family households.

In 2010, 222 people in Mono County, California lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 14202 people in Mono County, California lived in the county.

In prehistoric times, the valley was inhabited by the Patwin Native Americans, with possible habitation by Wappo tribes in the northwestern foothills. Most villages are thought to have been constructed near the floodplains of watercourses that drain the valley. Their food consisted of wild roots, acorns, small animals, earthworms, grasshoppers, and bread made from crushed California buckeye kernels. In winter they would construct huts made of tree branches. In summer they camped near rivers and streams. In winter months, they were half clad in wild animal skins and at other times they wore no clothing. The maximum prehistoric population is thought not to have exceeded 5000 persons.
