Bristol County, Rhode Island

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 3.4876704%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 6817 people in Bristol County, Rhode Island lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 41057 people in Bristol County, Rhode Island lived in family households.

In 2000, 2774 people in Bristol County, Rhode Island lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 50648 people in Bristol County, Rhode Island lived in the county.

In 2010, 60301 people in Westmoreland County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 296906 people in Westmoreland County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2010, 7962 people in Westmoreland County, Pennsylvania lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 365169 people in Westmoreland County, Pennsylvania lived in the county.

The county was formed by the transfer of part of Bristol County, Massachusetts, to the state of Rhode Island, and was the subject of a long-running border dispute.
