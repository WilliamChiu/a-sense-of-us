Park County, Wyoming

In 2000, 3872 people in Park County, Wyoming lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 21047 people in Park County, Wyoming lived in family households.

In 2010, 13644 people in Natrona County, Wyoming lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 60161 people in Natrona County, Wyoming lived in family households.

Park County was created on February 15, 1909 with land detached from Big Horn County. The county was named for Yellowstone National Park which is mostly within the limits of Park County.
