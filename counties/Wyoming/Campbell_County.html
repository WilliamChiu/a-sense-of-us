Campbell County, Wyoming

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -5.29083%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 4162 people in Campbell County, Wyoming lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 29215 people in Campbell County, Wyoming lived in family households.

In 2000, 321 people in Campbell County, Wyoming lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 33698 people in Campbell County, Wyoming lived in the county.

In 2010, 12335 people in Albany County, Wyoming lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 21716 people in Albany County, Wyoming lived in family households.

In 2010, 2248 people in Albany County, Wyoming lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 36299 people in Albany County, Wyoming lived in the county.

Campbell County was created on February 21, 1911 with land detached from Crook County and Weston County.
