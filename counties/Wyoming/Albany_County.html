Albany County, Wyoming

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.8089166%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 9279 people in Albany County, Wyoming lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 20324 people in Albany County, Wyoming lived in family households.

In 2000, 2411 people in Albany County, Wyoming lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 32014 people in Albany County, Wyoming lived in the county.

In 2010, 34495 people in Winnebago County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 124260 people in Winnebago County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2010, 8239 people in Winnebago County, Wisconsin lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 166994 people in Winnebago County, Wisconsin lived in the county.

Albany County was organized December 16, 1868, from Laramie County in Dakota Territory, which at the time had jurisdiction over part of modern-day Wyoming. It became a county in Wyoming Territory when that territory's government was formally organized on May 19, 1869.
