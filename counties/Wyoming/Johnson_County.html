Johnson County, Wyoming

In 2000, 1084 people in Johnson County, Wyoming lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 5896 people in Johnson County, Wyoming lived in family households.

In 2010, 2156 people in Goshen County, Wyoming lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 10023 people in Goshen County, Wyoming lived in family households.

Johnson County was created on December 8, 1875 as Pease County from parts of Albany, Carbon, and Sweetwater Counties. The county was originally named for Dr. E. L. Pease of Uinta County. In 1879, the county was renamed Johnson, for E. P. Johnson, a Cheyenne attorney.
