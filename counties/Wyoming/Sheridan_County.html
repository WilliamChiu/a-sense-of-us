Sheridan County, Wyoming

In 2000, 4861 people in Sheridan County, Wyoming lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 20984 people in Sheridan County, Wyoming lived in family households.

In 2010, 4989 people in Park County, Wyoming lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 22274 people in Park County, Wyoming lived in family households.

Sheridan County was created by the legislature of the Wyoming Territory on March 9, 1888. The county was formed from land ceded from Johnson County. Sheridan County was named for Philip Sheridan, a general in the American Civil War and controversial Indian fighter.
