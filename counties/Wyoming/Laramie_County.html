Laramie County, Wyoming

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.3516655%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 12322 people in Laramie County, Wyoming lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 65985 people in Laramie County, Wyoming lived in family households.

In 2000, 3300 people in Laramie County, Wyoming lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 81607 people in Laramie County, Wyoming lived in the county.

In 2010, 952 people in Hot Springs County, Wyoming lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 3774 people in Hot Springs County, Wyoming lived in family households.

In 2010, 86 people in Hot Springs County, Wyoming lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 4812 people in Hot Springs County, Wyoming lived in the county.

Laramie County was originally created in 1867 as a county within the Dakota Territory. The county was named for Jacques La Ramee, a French-Canadian fur-trader. In late 1867, Laramie County lost territory when Sweetwater County was created.
