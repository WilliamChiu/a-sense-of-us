Aroostook County, Maine

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -31.767195%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 11774 people in Aroostook County, Maine lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 59852 people in Aroostook County, Maine lived in family households.

In 2000, 2312 people in Aroostook County, Maine lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 73938 people in Aroostook County, Maine lived in the county.

In 2010, 1277 people in West Feliciana Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5297 people in West Feliciana Parish, Louisiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 15625 people in West Feliciana Parish, Louisiana lived in the county.

Aroostook County was formed in 1839 from parts of Penobscot and Washington counties. In 1843, Aroostook gained land from Penobscot County; in 1844, Aroostook again gained land from Penobscot, plus it exchanged land with Piscataquis County. In 1889, Aroostook gained slightly from Penobscot, but gave back the land in 1903 when Aroostook County gained its final form.
