Hettinger County, North Dakota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -4.0074587%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 388 people in Hettinger County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 2262 people in Hettinger County, North Dakota lived in family households.

In 2000, 65 people in Hettinger County, North Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 2715 people in Hettinger County, North Dakota lived in the county.

In 2010, 17170 people in Grand Forks County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 45475 people in Grand Forks County, North Dakota lived in family households.

In 2010, 4216 people in Grand Forks County, North Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 66861 people in Grand Forks County, North Dakota lived in the county.

Hettinger County was named by Tom Hettinger, the great-grandson of Erastus A. Williams, who was Speaker of the Dakota Territory House of Representatives the year Hettinger was established, in honor of his father-in-law Mathias Hettinger. The county was founded by the Dakota Territory Legislature in 1883, and was formally organized with its own county government on April 17, 1907, by a proclamation signed by Governor John Burke.
