Eddy County, North Dakota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.9063046%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 445 people in Eddy County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 2234 people in Eddy County, North Dakota lived in family households.

In 2000, 78 people in Eddy County, North Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 2757 people in Eddy County, North Dakota lived in the county.

In 2010, 894 people in Dickey County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 4096 people in Dickey County, North Dakota lived in family households.

In 2010, 299 people in Dickey County, North Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 5289 people in Dickey County, North Dakota lived in the county.

Eddy County was formed in 1885 from a portion of Foster County. It was named after Ezra B. Eddy, one of the first bankers of the region in Fargo.
