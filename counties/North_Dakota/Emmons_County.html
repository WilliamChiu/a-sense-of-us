Emmons County, North Dakota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.2487769%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 592 people in Emmons County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 3654 people in Emmons County, North Dakota lived in family households.

In 2000, 85 people in Emmons County, North Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 4331 people in Emmons County, North Dakota lived in the county.

In 2010, 431 people in Divide County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1574 people in Divide County, North Dakota lived in family households.

In 2010, 66 people in Divide County, North Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 2071 people in Divide County, North Dakota lived in the county.

Emmons County was established before the state of North Dakota. The county was originated by an act of territorial legislation during February 1879. However, it was not officially organized until November 9, 1883. It was named for James A. Emmons, who was a steamboat navigator from Virginia and merchant in Bismarck.
