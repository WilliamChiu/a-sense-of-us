Traill County, North Dakota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 5.330515%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1277 people in Traill County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6771 people in Traill County, North Dakota lived in family households.

In 2000, 429 people in Traill County, North Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 8477 people in Traill County, North Dakota lived in the county.

In 2010, 313 people in Steele County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1662 people in Steele County, North Dakota lived in family households.

In 2010, 0 people in Steele County, North Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 1975 people in Steele County, North Dakota lived in the county.

Traill County was created in 1875 by the 1874-75 territorial legislature. It was named after Walter John Strickland Traill, an employee of the Hudson's Bay Company and son of Canadian pioneer Catharine Parr Traill.
