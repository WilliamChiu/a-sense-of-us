Traill County, North Dakota

In 2000, 1277 people in Traill County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6771 people in Traill County, North Dakota lived in family households.

In 2010, 313 people in Steele County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1662 people in Steele County, North Dakota lived in family households.

Traill County was created in 1875 by the 1874-75 territorial legislature. It was named after Walter John Strickland Traill, an employee of the Hudson's Bay Company and son of Canadian pioneer Catharine Parr Traill.
