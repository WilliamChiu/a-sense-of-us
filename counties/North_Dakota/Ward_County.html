Household Populations

Ward County, North Dakota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.8461466%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 9452 people in Ward County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 47172 people in Ward County, North Dakota lived in family households.

In 2000, 2171 people in Ward County, North Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 58795 people in Ward County, North Dakota lived in the county.

In 2010, 438 people in Towner County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1765 people in Towner County, North Dakota lived in family households.

In 2010, 43 people in Towner County, North Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 2246 people in Towner County, North Dakota lived in the county.

Ward County was created by the 1885 Dakota Territory legislature and named for Mark Ward, chairman of the House of Representatives Committee on Counties during the session. The county government was first organized on November 23, 1885 with Burlington as the county seat. This was changed to Minot in 1888.
