Golden Valley County, North Dakota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.6111841%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 271 people in Golden Valley County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 1538 people in Golden Valley County, North Dakota lived in family households.

In 2000, 115 people in Golden Valley County, North Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 1924 people in Golden Valley County, North Dakota lived in the county.

In 2010, 458 people in Eddy County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1843 people in Eddy County, North Dakota lived in family households.

In 2010, 84 people in Eddy County, North Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 2385 people in Eddy County, North Dakota lived in the county.

Golden Valley was originally part of Billings County. Voters elected to separate from Billings County in 1910, but litigation prevented formal organization of the county until 1912. The territory in the surrounding area was colloquially referred to as, Rattlesnake Flats, due to the large number of the snakes found in the area. The region was dubbed Golden Valley in 1902 after a group of land surveyors noticed that the sunlight gave the surrounding grasses a distinct golden color.
