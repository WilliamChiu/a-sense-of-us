Grant County, North Dakota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.3502955%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 409 people in Grant County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 2343 people in Grant County, North Dakota lived in family households.

In 2000, 89 people in Grant County, North Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 2841 people in Grant County, North Dakota lived in the county.

In 2010, 644 people in Foster County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 2638 people in Foster County, North Dakota lived in family households.

In 2010, 61 people in Foster County, North Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 3343 people in Foster County, North Dakota lived in the county.

Grant County is a county located in the U.S. state of North Dakota. As of the 2010 census, the population was 2,394. Its county seat is Carson. Until November 28, 1916, Grant County was a part of Morton County.
