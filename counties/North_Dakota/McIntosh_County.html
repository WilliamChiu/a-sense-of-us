McIntosh County, North Dakota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 3.755045%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 520 people in McIntosh County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 2696 people in McIntosh County, North Dakota lived in family households.

In 2000, 174 people in McIntosh County, North Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 3390 people in McIntosh County, North Dakota lived in the county.

In 2010, 699 people in LaMoure County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 3375 people in LaMoure County, North Dakota lived in family households.

In 2010, 65 people in LaMoure County, North Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 4139 people in LaMoure County, North Dakota lived in the county.

McIntosh County was created by the 1883 territorial legislature after the southern half of Logan County was divided. The name refers to Edward H. McIntosh, a member of the legislature at the time. The county seat was originally Hoskins, but changed in 1888 after everything in Hoskins but the school was moved three miles east to the new Soo Line Railroad townsite of Ashley.
