Household Populations

Cass County, North Dakota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.74129105%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 28544 people in Cass County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 90507 people in Cass County, North Dakota lived in family households.

In 2000, 4087 people in Cass County, North Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 123138 people in Cass County, North Dakota lived in the county.

In 2010, 570 people in Bowman County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 2499 people in Bowman County, North Dakota lived in family households.

In 2010, 82 people in Bowman County, North Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 3151 people in Bowman County, North Dakota lived in the county.

Cass County dates back to the days of the Dakota Territory. It was one of the original counties defined in 1872, and was organized on October 27, 1873, at a meeting in Fargo. It was named after railroad executive George Washington Cass (1810 - 1888).
