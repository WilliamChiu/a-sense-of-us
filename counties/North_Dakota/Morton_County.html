Morton County, North Dakota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.5024612%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 3466 people in Morton County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 21357 people in Morton County, North Dakota lived in family households.

In 2000, 480 people in Morton County, North Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 25303 people in Morton County, North Dakota lived in the county.

In 2010, 862 people in McKenzie County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5346 people in McKenzie County, North Dakota lived in family households.

In 2010, 152 people in McKenzie County, North Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 6360 people in McKenzie County, North Dakota lived in the county.

The county was created by the 1872-1873 territorial legislature and named for Oliver Hazard Perry Throck Morton (1823-1877), governor of Indiana during the American Civil War and later a United States Senator. It was organized on March 23, 1878, but the action of the 1878 legislature in attaching the eastern portion to Burleigh County effectively nullified this action. The county was reorganized on February 18, 1881, after the detached portion had been returned to Morton County by the 1881 legislature. Mandan has always been the county seat, though during a period from 1878 to 1879, Mandan, and thus also the county seat, was known as Lincoln.
