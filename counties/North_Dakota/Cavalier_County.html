Cavalier County, North Dakota

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.4212718%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 698 people in Cavalier County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 4014 people in Cavalier County, North Dakota lived in family households.

In 2000, 119 people in Cavalier County, North Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 4831 people in Cavalier County, North Dakota lived in the county.

In 2010, 396 people in Burke County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1570 people in Burke County, North Dakota lived in family households.

In 2010, 2 people in Burke County, North Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 1968 people in Burke County, North Dakota lived in the county.

Cavalier County was created from the western part of Pembina County, North Dakota in 1873 and named by the Territorial Legislature for Charles Turner Cavileer (1818–1902), a well known fur trader, customs agent and postmaster. There is no explanation for the difference in the spelling of "Cavalier," though one placename historian suspects it was an attempt to Gallicize, or make the name appear French.
