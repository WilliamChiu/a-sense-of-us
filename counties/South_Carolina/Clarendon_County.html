Clarendon County, South Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 3.5859466%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 3622 people in Clarendon County, South Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 27354 people in Clarendon County, South Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 1526 people in Clarendon County, South Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 32502 people in Clarendon County, South Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 7890 people in Cherokee County, South Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 46745 people in Cherokee County, South Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 707 people in Cherokee County, South Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 55342 people in Cherokee County, South Carolina lived in the county.

Clarendon County was officially established in 1785, shortly after the American Revolutionary War, when the legislature divided Camden District into seven counties. One was Clarendon County. It was named after Edward Hyde, who was a Lord Proprietor and Earl of Clarendon.
