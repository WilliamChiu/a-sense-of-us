Barnwell County, South Carolina

In 2000, 3011 people in Barnwell County, South Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 20190 people in Barnwell County, South Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 1532 people in Allendale County, South Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7545 people in Allendale County, South Carolina lived in family households.

The Barnwell District was created in 1798 (effective in 1800) from the southwestern portion of the Orangeburg District, along the Savannah River. It was named after John Barnwell, a local figure in the Revolutionary War.
