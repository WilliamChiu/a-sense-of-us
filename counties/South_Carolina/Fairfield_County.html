Fairfield County, South Carolina

In 2000, 2694 people in Fairfield County, South Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 20339 people in Fairfield County, South Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 4106 people in Dillon County, South Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 27505 people in Dillon County, South Carolina lived in family households.

It is alleged that the county name originated from a statement made by General Cornwallis when he declared "How Fair These Fields" during the British occupation of the area in 1780-81. The house Cornwallis stayed in during the occupation is still standing.
