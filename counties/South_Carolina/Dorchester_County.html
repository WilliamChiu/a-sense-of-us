Dorchester County, South Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.1949778%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 10190 people in Dorchester County, South Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 84134 people in Dorchester County, South Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 2089 people in Dorchester County, South Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 96413 people in Dorchester County, South Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 5452 people in Colleton County, South Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 33052 people in Colleton County, South Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 388 people in Colleton County, South Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 38892 people in Colleton County, South Carolina lived in the county.

Dorchester County is named for its first settlement area, which was established by Congregationalists in 1696. These settlers applied the name "Dorchester" after their last residence in Dorchester, Massachusetts.
