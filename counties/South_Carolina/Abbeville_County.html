Abbeville County, South Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.645565%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 3199 people in Abbeville County, South Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 22258 people in Abbeville County, South Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 710 people in Abbeville County, South Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 26167 people in Abbeville County, South Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 17158 people in Newport County, Rhode Island lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 62154 people in Newport County, Rhode Island lived in family households.

In 2010, 3576 people in Newport County, Rhode Island lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 82888 people in Newport County, Rhode Island lived in the county.

Both Abbeville County and the county seat, Abbeville, SC, get their name from the town of Abbeville, France. The county was originally part of Ninety-Six District, South Carolina, but was designated Abbeville County in 1785, with parts of the county later going to the creation of the counties of Greenwood and McCormick. Abbeville County was settled by mostly Scotch Irish and French-Huguenot farmers in the mid-18th century.
