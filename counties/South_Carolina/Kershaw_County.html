Kershaw County, South Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -6.334215%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 6154 people in Kershaw County, South Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 45973 people in Kershaw County, South Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 520 people in Kershaw County, South Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 52647 people in Kershaw County, South Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 2695 people in Hampton County, South Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 16864 people in Hampton County, South Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 1531 people in Hampton County, South Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 21090 people in Hampton County, South Carolina lived in the county.

Kershaw County was named for Joseph Kershaw (1727-1791), an early settler. Originally part of Camden District, Kershaw County was formed in 1791 from parts of Claremont, Lancaster, Fairfield, and Richland counties. The county seat is Camden, the oldest inland city in South Carolina. This site was settled around 1732 by English traders and farmers who moved inland from Charleston.
