Beaufort County, South Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 4.2182684%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 16205 people in Beaufort County, South Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 98123 people in Beaufort County, South Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 6609 people in Beaufort County, South Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 120937 people in Beaufort County, South Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 25817 people in Anderson County, South Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 158545 people in Anderson County, South Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 2764 people in Anderson County, South Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 187126 people in Anderson County, South Carolina lived in the county.

From the early days of plantations, African slaves outnumbered the European minority in the colony. The plantations on the Sea Islands had especially large concentrations of slaves, and frequently limited interaction with whites. The islands were sites of the development of the Gullah culture, which preserved elements from a variety of West African roots; the people also developed the Gullah language, a creole language. The county was majority black nearly to the mid-20th century.
