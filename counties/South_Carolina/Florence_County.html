Florence County, South Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.649189%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 15582 people in Florence County, South Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 106313 people in Florence County, South Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 3866 people in Florence County, South Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 125761 people in Florence County, South Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 16621 people in Dorchester County, South Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 117919 people in Dorchester County, South Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 2015 people in Dorchester County, South Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 136555 people in Dorchester County, South Carolina lived in the county.

Florence County was formed from a section of the old Marion District in 1888. Florence County was named for the daughter of General W. W. Hardlee.
