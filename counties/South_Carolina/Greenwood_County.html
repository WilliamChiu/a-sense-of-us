Greenwood County, South Carolina

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.1698008%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 9585 people in Greenwood County, South Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 54375 people in Greenwood County, South Carolina lived in family households.

In 2000, 2311 people in Greenwood County, South Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 66271 people in Greenwood County, South Carolina lived in the county.

In 2010, 19374 people in Florence County, South Carolina lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 114283 people in Florence County, South Carolina lived in family households.

In 2010, 3228 people in Florence County, South Carolina lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 136885 people in Florence County, South Carolina lived in the county.

Greenwood County was formed in 1897 from portions of Abbeville County and Edgefield County, which had originally been part of the old Ninety-Six District. It was named for its county seat, Greenwood, which had been named around 1824 after a plantation owned by John McGehee, an early resident.
