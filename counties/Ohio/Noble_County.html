Noble County, Ohio

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 17.210722%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1391 people in Noble County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10453 people in Noble County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2000, 2214 people in Noble County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 14058 people in Noble County, Ohio lived in the county.

In 2010, 2274 people in Morgan County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 12592 people in Morgan County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2010, 188 people in Morgan County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 15054 people in Morgan County, Ohio lived in the county.

Noble County was formed on March 11, 1851 from portions of Guernsey, Morgan, Monroe and Washington counties. It was the last county to be formed in Ohio and, therefore, represents the youngest county in the state. It was named for either James Noble or Warren P. Noble, both of whom were early settlers in this region.
