Ashland County, Ohio

In 2000, 6569 people in Ashland County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 43726 people in Ashland County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2010, 4355 people in Williams County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 17483 people in Williams County, North Dakota lived in family households.

Ashland County was formed on February 24, 1846 from portions of Huron, Lorain, Richland, and Wayne counties. Like the county seat, it was named after Ashland, the Lexington, Kentucky-area home of Henry Clay, a Kentucky senator.
