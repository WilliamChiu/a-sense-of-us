Household Populations

Hamilton County, Ohio

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.349771%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 160187 people in Hamilton County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 666142 people in Hamilton County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2000, 18974 people in Hamilton County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 845303 people in Hamilton County, Ohio lived in the county.

In 2010, 10225 people in Geauga County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 82300 people in Geauga County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2010, 864 people in Geauga County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 93389 people in Geauga County, Ohio lived in the county.

Most of Hamilton County was originally owned and surveyed by John Cleves Symmes, and the region was a part of the Symmes Purchase. The first European-American settlers rafted down the Ohio River in 1788 following the American Revolutionary War. They established the towns of Losantiville (later Cincinnati) and Cleves.
