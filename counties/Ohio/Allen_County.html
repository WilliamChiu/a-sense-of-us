Allen County, Ohio

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.9745102%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 14625 people in Allen County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 87735 people in Allen County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2000, 6113 people in Allen County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 108473 people in Allen County, Ohio lived in the county.

In 2010, 782 people in Wells County, North Dakota lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 3306 people in Wells County, North Dakota lived in family households.

In 2010, 119 people in Wells County, North Dakota lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 4207 people in Wells County, North Dakota lived in the county.

Under the terms of the Treaty of Greenville signed in 1795, northwestern Ohio was reserved for Native Americans. Thus the area now comprising Allen County was off-limits to European settlement until the Treaty of Maumee Rapids in 1817.
