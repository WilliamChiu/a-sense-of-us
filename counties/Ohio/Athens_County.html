Athens County, Ohio

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 8.827507%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 15748 people in Athens County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 8238 people in Athens County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 62223 people in Athens County, Ohio lived in the county.

In 2010, 16819 people in Allen County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 83578 people in Allen County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2010, 5934 people in Allen County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 106331 people in Allen County, Ohio lived in the county.

Athens County is a county located in the southeastern part of the U.S. state of Ohio. As of the 2010 census, the population was 64,757. Its county seat is Athens. The county was formed in 1805 from Washington County. Because the original state university (Ohio University) was founded there in 1804, the town and the county were named for the ancient center of learning, Athens, Greece.
