Ashtabula County, Ohio

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.54358244%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 13984 people in Ashtabula County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 86979 people in Ashtabula County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2000, 1765 people in Ashtabula County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 102728 people in Ashtabula County, Ohio lived in the county.

In 2010, 3934 people in Adams County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 24278 people in Adams County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2010, 338 people in Adams County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 28550 people in Adams County, Ohio lived in the county.

After the discovery of the New World, the land that became Ashtabula County was originally part of the French colony of Canada (New France), which was ceded in 1763 to Great Britain and renamed Province of Quebec. In the late 18th century the land became part of the Connecticut Western Reserve in the Northwest Territory, then was purchased by the Connecticut Land Company in 1795.
