Carroll County, Ohio

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -4.2304754%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 3492 people in Carroll County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 24978 people in Carroll County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2000, 366 people in Carroll County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 28836 people in Carroll County, Ohio lived in the county.

In 2010, 11515 people in Belmont County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 55051 people in Belmont County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2010, 3834 people in Belmont County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 70400 people in Belmont County, Ohio lived in the county.

Carroll County was formed on December 25, 1833 from portions of Columbiana, Harrison, Jefferson, Stark, and Tuscarawas counties.
