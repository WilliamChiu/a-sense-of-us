Huron County, Ohio

In 2000, 7280 people in Huron County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 51628 people in Huron County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2010, 5874 people in Highland County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 37231 people in Highland County, Ohio lived in family households.

The county was named in honor of the Huron Indians who once lived in the Great Lakes region. The word "Huron" may be French, although this origin is disputed. The county lies in the former Connecticut Western Reserve and more specifically, in a subregion called the Firelands.
