Pickaway County, Ohio

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 12.7074%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 5182 people in Pickaway County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 41048 people in Pickaway County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2000, 6497 people in Pickaway County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 52727 people in Pickaway County, Ohio lived in the county.

In 2010, 6637 people in Ottawa County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 34302 people in Ottawa County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2010, 489 people in Ottawa County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 41428 people in Ottawa County, Ohio lived in the county.

Pickaway County is a county located in the U.S. state of Ohio. As of the 2010 census, the population was 55,698. Its county seat is Circleville. Its name derives from the Pekowi band of Shawnee Indians, who inhabited the area. (See List of Ohio county name etymologies.)
