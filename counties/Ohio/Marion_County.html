Marion County, Ohio

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 4.957986%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 8822 people in Marion County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 52737 people in Marion County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2000, 4658 people in Marion County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 66217 people in Marion County, Ohio lived in the county.

In 2010, 85206 people in Lucas County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 345894 people in Lucas County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2010, 10715 people in Lucas County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 441815 people in Lucas County, Ohio lived in the county.

Marion County is a county located in the U.S. state of Ohio. As of the 2010 census, the population was 66,501. Its county seat is Marion. The county was created in 1820 and later organized in 1824. It is named for General Francis "The Swamp Fox" Marion, a South Carolinian officer in the Revolutionary War.
