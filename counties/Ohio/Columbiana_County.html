Columbiana County, Ohio

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.3822317%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 14350 people in Columbiana County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 93961 people in Columbiana County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2000, 3764 people in Columbiana County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 112075 people in Columbiana County, Ohio lived in the county.

In 2010, 22380 people in Clark County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 113155 people in Clark County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2010, 2798 people in Clark County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 138333 people in Clark County, Ohio lived in the county.

The principal historic Indian tribes in the area were the Wyandots, Mingoes, and Delawares. Throughout the second half of the 18th century white explorers, starting with Christopher Gist in 1750, came to the area. George Washington, while engaged in land examinations, camped in the area of present-day East Liverpool in 1774.
