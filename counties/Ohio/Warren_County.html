Warren County, Ohio

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.6415536%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 15258 people in Warren County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 136741 people in Warren County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2000, 6384 people in Warren County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 158383 people in Warren County, Ohio lived in the county.

In 2010, 5500 people in Union County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 43868 people in Union County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2010, 2932 people in Union County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 52300 people in Union County, Ohio lived in the county.

Warren County is a county located in Ohio. As of the 2010 census, the population was 212,693. Its county seat is Lebanon. The county was erected May 1, 1803, from Hamilton County, and named for Dr. Joseph Warren, a hero of the Revolution who sent Paul Revere on his ride and who died at the Battle of Bunker Hill.
