Madison County, Ohio

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 11.264968%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4427 people in Madison County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 31360 people in Madison County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2000, 4426 people in Madison County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 40213 people in Madison County, Ohio lived in the county.

In 2010, 6717 people in Logan County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 38691 people in Logan County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2010, 450 people in Logan County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 45858 people in Logan County, Ohio lived in the county.

In 1850, the county contained 24 churches and one newspaper office, had a total population of 10,015, and the county's public school system had 3838 pupils. With agriculture as the primary business, that same year the county produced 726,451 bushels of corn, 19,308 tons of hay, 120,696 pounds of wool, and 128,948 pounds of butter. In 1900, the county had a population of 20,590.
