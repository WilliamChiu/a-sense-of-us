Monroe County, Ohio

In 2000, 1806 people in Monroe County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 13230 people in Monroe County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2010, 3405 people in Meigs County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 20153 people in Meigs County, Ohio lived in family households.

Monroe County was formed on January 28, 1813 from portions of Belmont, Guernsey and Washington counties. It was named after James Monroe, the U.S. Secretary of State when the county was formed, and later fifth President of the United States.
