Portage County, Ohio

In 2000, 23062 people in Portage County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 121447 people in Portage County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2010, 4781 people in Perry County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 30970 people in Perry County, Ohio lived in family households.

The name "Portage" comes from an old Native American path called "Portage Path", which ran between the Cuyahoga and Tuscarawas rivers, where travelers portaged their canoes. The location of the trail today is within the boundaries of neighboring Summit County.
