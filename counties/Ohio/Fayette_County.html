Fayette County, Ohio

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.94851255%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 3869 people in Fayette County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 23914 people in Fayette County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2000, 650 people in Fayette County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 28433 people in Fayette County, Ohio lived in the county.

In 2010, 18273 people in Delaware County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 153573 people in Delaware County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2010, 2368 people in Delaware County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 174214 people in Delaware County, Ohio lived in the county.

Fayette County was formed on March 1, 1810 from portions of Highland County and Ross County. It was named after Marie-Joseph Motier, Marquis de La Fayette, a French general and politician who took the side of the Colonials during the American Revolutionary War and who played an important role in the French Revolution.
