Holmes County, Ohio

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.1396766%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2563 people in Holmes County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 35469 people in Holmes County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2000, 911 people in Holmes County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 38943 people in Holmes County, Ohio lived in the county.

In 2010, 3646 people in Henry County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 24223 people in Henry County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2010, 346 people in Henry County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 28215 people in Henry County, Ohio lived in the county.

Holmes County was formed on January 20, 1824 from portions of Coshocton, Tuscarawas and Wayne counties. It was named after Andrew Holmes, an officer in the War of 1812. During the Civil War, small-scale riots broke out in ethnic German areas. Holmes County was an isolated localistic area dominated by Pennsylvania Dutch and some recent German immigrants. It was a Democratic stronghold and few men dared speak out in favor of conscription. Local politicians denounced Lincoln and Congress as despotic, seeing the draft law as a violation of their local autonomy. In June 1863, small scale disturbances broke out; they ended when the Army sent in armed units.
