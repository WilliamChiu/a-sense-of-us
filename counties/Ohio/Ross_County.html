Household Populations

Ross County, Ohio

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 7.0075154%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 9514 people in Ross County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 58402 people in Ross County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2000, 5429 people in Ross County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 73345 people in Ross County, Ohio lived in the county.

In 2010, 5375 people in Preble County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 36509 people in Preble County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2010, 386 people in Preble County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 42270 people in Preble County, Ohio lived in the county.

As of 1848, Ross County was described as having almost "one hundred enclosures of various sizes, and five hundred mounds," as well as numerous tumuli created by Indigenous peoples of the Americas by Ephraim George Squier and Edwin Hamilton Davis in their book, ''Ancient Monuments of the Mississippi Valley''. They describe the earthworks as ranging from five to 30 feet in size, and enclosures of one to 50 acres large.
