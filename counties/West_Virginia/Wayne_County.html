Wayne County, West Virginia

In 2000, 5128 people in Wayne County, West Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 37580 people in Wayne County, West Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1423 people in Tyler County, West Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7718 people in Tyler County, West Virginia lived in family households.

Wayne County, West Virginia was originally Wayne County, Virginia, which was created from part of Cabell County in 1842. The county was named for General "Mad" Anthony Wayne.
