Kanawha County, West Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.9781599%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 34741 people in Kanawha County, West Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 162173 people in Kanawha County, West Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 3159 people in Kanawha County, West Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 200073 people in Kanawha County, West Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 3963 people in Jackson County, West Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 25068 people in Jackson County, West Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 180 people in Jackson County, West Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 29211 people in Jackson County, West Virginia lived in the county.

The county began taking formation on November 14, 1788 under authorization of the Virginia General Assembly, and was founded on October 5, 1789. The county was named for the Kanawha River, which in turn was named after the Indian tribe that lived in the area. The county was the site of a major Textbook controversy in 1974 that included bombings and received national attention.
