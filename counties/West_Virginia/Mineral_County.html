Mineral County, West Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.42273998%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 3549 people in Mineral County, West Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 23012 people in Mineral County, West Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 517 people in Mineral County, West Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 27078 people in Mineral County, West Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 635 people in Mason County, West Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 27324 people in Mason County, West Virginia lived in the county.

Indigenous peoples lived throughout the highlands along rivers in this area for thousands of years. Archeologists have identified artifacts of the Adena culture, dating from 1000 BC to 200 BC. They were among the several early Native American cultures who built major earthwork mounds for ceremonial and burial use. Remnants of their culture have been found throughout West Virginia. They were followed by other indigenous peoples.
