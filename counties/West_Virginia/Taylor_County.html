Taylor County, West Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.2869825%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2099 people in Taylor County, West Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 13527 people in Taylor County, West Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 463 people in Taylor County, West Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 16089 people in Taylor County, West Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 2359 people in Roane County, West Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 12469 people in Roane County, West Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 98 people in Roane County, West Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 14926 people in Roane County, West Virginia lived in the county.

Some of the first Europeans to visit the area are thought to have been British Army deserters from Fort Pitt, who reportedly fled their post in 1761 during the French and Indian War and roamed northwestern Virginia for several years thereafter. A European trader with the Hudson's Bay Company reportedly set foot in these lands as early as 1764.
