Jefferson County, West Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.4731169%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 6208 people in Jefferson County, West Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 34837 people in Jefferson County, West Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 1145 people in Jefferson County, West Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 42190 people in Jefferson County, West Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 11239 people in Harrison County, West Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 56975 people in Harrison County, West Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 885 people in Harrison County, West Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 69099 people in Harrison County, West Virginia lived in the county.

Jefferson County was formed from Berkeley County in 1801 because the citizens of southeastern Berkeley county felt they had to travel too far to the county seat of Martinsburg. Charles Washington, the founder of Charles Town and brother to George Washington petitioned for a new county to be formed. It was named for Thomas Jefferson, author of the Declaration of Independence and third President of the United States. Virginia previously had a Jefferson County, which was lost to form the new state of Kentucky. Accordingly, in the State records of Virginia, there will be listings for Jefferson County from 1780-1792 and Jefferson County from 1801-1863. Neither is still located in Virginia and despite naming a county after him twice, Virginia no longer has a county named for its hero Thomas Jefferson.
