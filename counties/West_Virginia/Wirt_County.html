From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.22587776%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2010, 9154 people in Webster County, West Virginia lived in the county.

Wirt County was created from parts of Jackson and Wood counties on January 19, 1848. The county was named for William Wirt (1772-1834).
