Raleigh County, West Virginia

In 2000, 10975 people in Raleigh County, West Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 64584 people in Raleigh County, West Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 4691 people in Preston County, West Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 26451 people in Preston County, West Virginia lived in family households.

Alfred Beckley (1802–1888) said he gave Raleigh County its name in honor of Sir Walter Raleigh (1552–1618), the “enterprising and far-seeing patron of the earliest attempts to colonize our old Mother State of Virginia,” according to ''Raleigh County: West Virginia'' by Jim Wood.
