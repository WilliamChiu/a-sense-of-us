Randolph County, West Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 5.326283%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4019 people in Randolph County, West Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 22705 people in Randolph County, West Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 1538 people in Randolph County, West Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 28262 people in Randolph County, West Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 6892 people in Putnam County, West Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 48369 people in Putnam County, West Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 225 people in Putnam County, West Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 55486 people in Putnam County, West Virginia lived in the county.

Randolph County is a county located in the state of West Virginia. As of the 2010 census, the population was 29,405. Its county seat is Elkins. The county was founded in 1787 and is named for Edmund Jennings Randolph.
