Lincoln County, West Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.3258934%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2391 people in Lincoln County, West Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 19647 people in Lincoln County, West Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 70 people in Lincoln County, West Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 22108 people in Lincoln County, West Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 152329 people in Kanawha County, West Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 3163 people in Kanawha County, West Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 193063 people in Kanawha County, West Virginia lived in the county.

Lincoln County was created by an act of the West Virginia Legislature on February 23, 1867, from parts of Boone, Cabell, Kanawha and Putnam counties. By 1869, the county had returned much of its Putnam County territory and absorbed the northern portion of Logan County and a portion of Wayne County. In 1869, Harts Creek Township (later district) was created from this latter region. Lincoln County is one of five counties created by West Virginia since the Civil War. Hamlin, seat of government for the county, was established in 1853.
