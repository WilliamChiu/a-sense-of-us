Roane County, West Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -7.0560336%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1964 people in Roane County, West Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 13370 people in Roane County, West Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 112 people in Roane County, West Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 15446 people in Roane County, West Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 4712 people in Randolph County, West Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 22420 people in Randolph County, West Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 2273 people in Randolph County, West Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 29405 people in Randolph County, West Virginia lived in the county.

Roane County was created by an act of the Virginia General Assembly on March 11, 1856. It was named for the jurist Spencer Roane of Virginia, born in Essex County April 4, 1762. The county's seat Spencer was also named for judge Roane.
