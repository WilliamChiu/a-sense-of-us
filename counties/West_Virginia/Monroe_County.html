Monroe County, West Virginia

In 2000, 1744 people in Monroe County, West Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 11372 people in Monroe County, West Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 3815 people in Mingo County, West Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 22939 people in Mingo County, West Virginia lived in family households.

Monroe County was formed on January 14, 1799 from portions of Greenbrier County. It was named after James Monroe, a Virginia statesman and senator, and the future fifth President of the United States.
