Tyler County, West Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.9305885%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1153 people in Tyler County, West Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 8332 people in Tyler County, West Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 107 people in Tyler County, West Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 9592 people in Tyler County, West Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 2435 people in Taylor County, West Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 13949 people in Taylor County, West Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 511 people in Taylor County, West Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 16895 people in Taylor County, West Virginia lived in the county.

According to the U.S. Census Bureau, the county has a total area of 261 sqmi, of which 256 sqmi is land and 4.4 sqmi (1.7%) is water.
