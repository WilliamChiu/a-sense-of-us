Middlesex County, Connecticut

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.005422%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 25849 people in Middlesex County, Connecticut lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 122995 people in Middlesex County, Connecticut lived in family households.

In 2000, 6227 people in Middlesex County, Connecticut lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 155071 people in Middlesex County, Connecticut lived in the county.

In 2010, 128952 people in Fairfield County, Connecticut lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 768709 people in Fairfield County, Connecticut lived in family households.

In 2010, 19168 people in Fairfield County, Connecticut lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 916829 people in Fairfield County, Connecticut lived in the county.

Middlesex County is a county located in the south central part of the U.S. state of Connecticut. As of the 2010 census, the population was 165,676. The county was created in May 1785 from portions of Hartford County and New London County.
