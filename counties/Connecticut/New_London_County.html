Household Populations

New London County, Connecticut

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 3.2583594%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 40481 people in New London County, Connecticut lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 206727 people in New London County, Connecticut lived in family households.

In 2000, 11880 people in New London County, Connecticut lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 259088 people in New London County, Connecticut lived in the county.

In 2010, 30633 people in Litchfield County, Connecticut lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 156490 people in Litchfield County, Connecticut lived in family households.

In 2010, 2804 people in Litchfield County, Connecticut lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 189927 people in Litchfield County, Connecticut lived in the county.

Southeastern New England was dominated by the powerful Pequot people at the time of English encounter; they spoke the Mohegan-Pequot language and were one of the Algonquian-speaking tribes in the coastal areas. After years of conflict, the English and their Indian allies killed many and disrupted the Pequot in the Pequot War of 1637, ending their dominance. Two descendant Pequot tribes are recognized by the state today, as are three other tribes, all descended from Algonquian peoples.
