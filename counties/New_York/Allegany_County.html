Allegany County, New York

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -6.491685%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 7376 people in Allegany County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 38247 people in Allegany County, New York lived in family households.

In 2000, 4304 people in Allegany County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 49927 people in Allegany County, New York lived in the county.

In 2010, 694 people in Union County, New Mexico lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 3193 people in Union County, New Mexico lived in family households.

In 2010, 662 people in Union County, New Mexico lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 4549 people in Union County, New Mexico lived in the county.

* Allegany County was created on April 7, 1806 when Genesee County, New York was partitioned so that 1570 sqmi was given over to the new county. The first County Seat was established at Angelica, New York where it remained for half a century. It was later moved to Belmont, New York on the Genesee River.
