Cortland County, New York

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 5.1751733%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 9391 people in Cortland County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 36088 people in Cortland County, New York lived in family households.

In 2000, 3120 people in Cortland County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 48599 people in Cortland County, New York lived in the county.

In 2010, 8678 people in Chenango County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 41003 people in Chenango County, New York lived in family households.

In 2010, 796 people in Chenango County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 50477 people in Chenango County, New York lived in the county.

Located in the glaciated Appalachian Plateau area of Central New York, midway between Syracuse and Binghamton, this predominantly rural county is the southeastern gateway to the Finger Lakes Region. Scattered archaeological evidence indicates the Iroquois also known as the Haudenosaunee controlled the area beginning about AD 1500.
