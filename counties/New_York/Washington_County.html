Washington County, New York

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -7.33881%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 8227 people in Washington County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 49067 people in Washington County, New York lived in family households.

In 2000, 3748 people in Washington County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 61042 people in Washington County, New York lived in the county.

In 2010, 29327 people in Tompkins County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 59005 people in Tompkins County, New York lived in family households.

In 2010, 13232 people in Tompkins County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 101564 people in Tompkins County, New York lived in the county.

When counties were established in New York State in 1683, the present Washington County was part of Albany County. This was an enormous county, including the northern part of New York State as well as all of the present State of Vermont and, in theory, extending westward to the Pacific Ocean. This county was reduced in size on July 3, 1766 by the creation of Cumberland County, and further on March 16, 1770 by the creation of Gloucester County, both containing territory now in Vermont.
