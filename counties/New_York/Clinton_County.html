Clinton County, New York

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 4.4497013%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 13495 people in Clinton County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 59302 people in Clinton County, New York lived in family households.

In 2000, 7097 people in Clinton County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 79894 people in Clinton County, New York lived in the county.

In 2010, 25221 people in Chautauqua County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 103170 people in Chautauqua County, New York lived in family households.

In 2010, 6514 people in Chautauqua County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 134905 people in Chautauqua County, New York lived in the county.

When counties were established in New York State in 1683, the present Clinton County was part of Albany County. This was an enormous county, including the northern part of New York State as well as all of the present state of Vermont and, in theory, extending westward to the Pacific Ocean. This county was reduced in size on July 3, 1766, by the creation of Cumberland County, and further on March 16, 1770, by the creation of Gloucester County, both containing territory now in Vermont. On March 12, 1772, what was left of Albany County was split into three parts, one remaining under the name Albany County. One of the other pieces, Charlotte County, contained the eastern portion.
