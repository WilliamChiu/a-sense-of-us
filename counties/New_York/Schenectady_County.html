Schenectady County, New York

In 2000, 25889 people in Schenectady County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 116004 people in Schenectady County, New York lived in family households.

In 2010, 31052 people in Rockland County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 273452 people in Rockland County, New York lived in family households.

This area of the river valley was historically occupied by the Mohawk people, the easternmost of the Five Nations comprising the Iroquois Confederacy or ''Haudenosaunee''. They cultivated maize fields in the flats along the Mohawk River and had villages in the hills.
