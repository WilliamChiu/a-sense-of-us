Otsego County, New York

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 5.2744985%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 10878 people in Otsego County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 45786 people in Otsego County, New York lived in family households.

In 2000, 5012 people in Otsego County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 61676 people in Otsego County, New York lived in the county.

In 2010, 43141 people in Orange County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 317442 people in Orange County, New York lived in family households.

In 2010, 12230 people in Orange County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 372813 people in Orange County, New York lived in the county.

In 1789, Ontario County was split off from Montgomery. The area split off from Montgomery County was much larger than the present county, as it included the present Allegany, Cattaraugus, Chautauqua, Erie, Genesee, Livingston, Monroe, Niagara, Orleans, Steuben, Wyoming, Yates, and part of Schuyler and Wayne counties.
