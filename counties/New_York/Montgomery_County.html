Montgomery County, New York

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -6.529957%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 8220 people in Montgomery County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 40278 people in Montgomery County, New York lived in family households.

In 2000, 1210 people in Montgomery County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 49708 people in Montgomery County, New York lived in the county.

In 2010, 11627 people in Livingston County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 48008 people in Livingston County, New York lived in family households.

In 2010, 5758 people in Livingston County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 65393 people in Livingston County, New York lived in the county.

In 1784, following end of the American Revolutionary War, the name of Tryon County was changed to Montgomery County. This change was to honor the general, Richard Montgomery, who had captured several places in Canada and died in 1775 attempting to capture the city of Quebec during the Revolutionary War. It replaced the name formerly honoring the last provincial governor of New York.
