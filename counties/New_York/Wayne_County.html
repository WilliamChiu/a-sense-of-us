Wayne County, New York

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -4.6937885%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 12431 people in Wayne County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 79605 people in Wayne County, New York lived in family households.

In 2000, 1729 people in Wayne County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 93765 people in Wayne County, New York lived in the county.

In 2010, 34471 people in Ulster County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 136249 people in Ulster County, New York lived in family households.

In 2010, 11773 people in Ulster County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 182493 people in Ulster County, New York lived in the county.

Prior to the arrival of Europeans, the land Wayne County encompasses was originally part of the Iroquois Confederacy, which had existed from around August 31, 1142. When counties were established in New York State on November 1, 1683, it became part of Albany County. This was an enormous county, including the northern part of New York State and all of the present State of Vermont, extending (in theory) westward to the Pacific Ocean. In 1804, after numerous acts of subdividing, Seneca County was formed by the splitting of Cayuga County, which, in turn, was reduced in size by combining portions of Seneca and the remainder of Cayuga County to form Tompkins County in 1817.
