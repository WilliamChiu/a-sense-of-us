Onondaga County, New York

In 2000, 82392 people in Onondaga County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 362438 people in Onondaga County, New York lived in family households.

In 2010, 580982 people in New York County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 937518 people in New York County, New York lived in family households.

The name ''Onondaga'' derives from the name of the Native American tribe who historically lived in this area at the time of European contact, one of the original Five Nations of the ''Haudenosaunee''. They called themselves (autonym) ''Onoda'gega'', sometimes spelled ''Onontakeka.'' The word means "People of the Hills." Sometimes the term was ''Onondagaono'' ("The People of the Hills"). The federally recognized Onondaga Nation has a reservation of about9.3 sqmi within the county, on which they have self-government.
