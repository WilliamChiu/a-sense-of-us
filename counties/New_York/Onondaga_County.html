Household Populations

Onondaga County, New York

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.3410926%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 82392 people in Onondaga County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 362438 people in Onondaga County, New York lived in family households.

In 2000, 13506 people in Onondaga County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 458336 people in Onondaga County, New York lived in the county.

In 2010, 580982 people in New York County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 937518 people in New York County, New York lived in family households.

In 2010, 67373 people in New York County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 1585873 people in New York County, New York lived in the county.

The name ''Onondaga'' derives from the name of the Native American tribe who historically lived in this area at the time of European contact, one of the original Five Nations of the ''Haudenosaunee''. They called themselves (autonym) ''Onoda'gega'', sometimes spelled ''Onontakeka.'' The word means "People of the Hills." Sometimes the term was ''Onondagaono'' ("The People of the Hills"). The federally recognized Onondaga Nation has a 9.3 sqmi reservation within the county, on which they have self-government.
