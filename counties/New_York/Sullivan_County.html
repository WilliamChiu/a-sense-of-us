Sullivan County, New York

In 2000, 11435 people in Sullivan County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 57706 people in Sullivan County, New York lived in family households.

In 2010, 5791 people in Seneca County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 26651 people in Seneca County, New York lived in family households.

When the colony that is now New York State established its first twelve counties in 1683, the present Sullivan County was part of Ulster County. In 1809, Sullivan County was split from Ulster County.
