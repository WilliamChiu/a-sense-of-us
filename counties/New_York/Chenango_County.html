Chenango County, New York

In 2000, 7925 people in Chenango County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 42218 people in Chenango County, New York lived in family households.

In 2010, 13961 people in Cayuga County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 61820 people in Cayuga County, New York lived in family households.

This was long the territory of the Oneida people, one of the first Five Nations of the Iroquois Confederacy. They occupied the area until after the Revolutionary War, when they were forced off the land although they had been allies of the patriot colonists.
