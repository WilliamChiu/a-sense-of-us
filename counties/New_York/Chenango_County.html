Chenango County, New York

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.9287875%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 7925 people in Chenango County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 42218 people in Chenango County, New York lived in family households.

In 2000, 1258 people in Chenango County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 51401 people in Chenango County, New York lived in the county.

In 2010, 13961 people in Cayuga County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 61820 people in Cayuga County, New York lived in family households.

In 2010, 4245 people in Cayuga County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 80026 people in Cayuga County, New York lived in the county.

This was long the territory of the Oneida people, one of the first Five Nations of the Iroquois Confederacy or ''Haudenosaunee''. They occupied the area until after the Revolutionary War, when they were forced off the land, although they had been allies of the patriot colonists. They were granted a small reservation, which settlers continued to encroach on.
