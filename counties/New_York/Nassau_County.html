Household Populations

Nassau County, New York

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -5.4548383%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 122699 people in Nassau County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 1190187 people in Nassau County, New York lived in family households.

In 2000, 21658 people in Nassau County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 1334544 people in Nassau County, New York lived in the county.

In 2010, 12157 people in Madison County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 56152 people in Madison County, New York lived in family households.

In 2010, 5133 people in Madison County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 73442 people in Madison County, New York lived in the county.

The area now designated Nassau County was originally the eastern 70% of Queens County, one of the original 12 counties formed in 1683, and was then contained within two towns: Hempstead and Oyster Bay. In 1784, the Town of North Hempstead, was formed through secession by the northern portions of the Town of Hempstead. Nassau County was formed in 1899 by the division of Queens County, after the western portion of Queens had become a borough of New York City in 1898, as the 3 Easternmost towns seceded from the county.
