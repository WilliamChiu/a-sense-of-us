Oneida County, New York

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 5.2738905%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 36950 people in Oneida County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 183105 people in Oneida County, New York lived in family households.

In 2000, 15414 people in Oneida County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 235469 people in Oneida County, New York lived in the county.

In 2010, 133013 people in Nassau County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1184853 people in Nassau County, New York lived in family households.

In 2010, 21666 people in Nassau County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 1339532 people in Nassau County, New York lived in the county.

When colonial counties were established by England in New York State in 1683, the territory of present Oneida County was included in a very large, mostly undeveloped Albany County. This was an enormous county, including the northern part of New York State as well as all of the present state of Vermont and, in theory, extending westward to the Pacific Ocean. This county was reduced in size on July 3, 1766 by the creation of Cumberland County, and further on March 16, 1770 by the creation of Gloucester County, both containing territory now in Vermont.
