Westchester County, New York

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.4759898%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 122685 people in Westchester County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 777121 people in Westchester County, New York lived in family households.

In 2000, 23653 people in Westchester County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 923459 people in Westchester County, New York lived in the county.

In 2010, 12601 people in Warren County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 52368 people in Warren County, New York lived in family households.

In 2010, 738 people in Warren County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 65707 people in Warren County, New York lived in the county.

At the time of European contact in the 16th and 17th centuries, the Native American inhabitants of present Westchester County were part of the Algonquian peoples, whose name for themselves was Lenape, meaning ''the people''. They called the region Lenapehoking, which consisted of the area around and between the Delaware and Hudson Rivers. Several different tribes occupied the area, including The Manhattans, and the Weckquaesgeek and Siwanoy bands of the Wappinger in the south, and Tankiteke, Sintsink and Kitchawank Wappinger in the north.
