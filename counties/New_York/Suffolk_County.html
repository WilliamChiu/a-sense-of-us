Suffolk County, New York

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.08524656%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 141331 people in Suffolk County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 1249460 people in Suffolk County, New York lived in family households.

In 2000, 28578 people in Suffolk County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 1419369 people in Suffolk County, New York lived in the county.

In 2010, 3278 people in Schuyler County, New York lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 14711 people in Schuyler County, New York lived in family households.

In 2010, 354 people in Schuyler County, New York lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 18343 people in Schuyler County, New York lived in the county.

Suffolk County was an original county of the Province of New York, one of twelve created in 1683. From 1664 until 1683 it had been the East Riding of Yorkshire. Its boundaries were essentially the same as at present, with only minor changes in the boundary with its western neighbor, which was originally Queens County but has been Nassau County since the separation of Nassau from Queens in 1899.
