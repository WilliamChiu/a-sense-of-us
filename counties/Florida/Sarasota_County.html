Sarasota County, Florida

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.5239129%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 67136 people in Sarasota County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 252348 people in Sarasota County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2000, 6473 people in Sarasota County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 325957 people in Sarasota County, Florida lived in the county.

In 2010, 29714 people in St. Johns County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 157527 people in St. Johns County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2010, 2798 people in St. Johns County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 190039 people in St. Johns County, Florida lived in the county.

Sarasota County was created in 1921. After acquisition by the United States as a territory and once Florida obtained state status, the area now included in the county had been governed by Hillsborough County, then Manatee County as the state was divided into smaller and smaller governmental organizations. Manatee County was carved up to allow the creation of three more counties as well.
