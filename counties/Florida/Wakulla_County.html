Wakulla County, Florida

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -9.9094925%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 19077 people in Wakulla County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2000, 1144 people in Wakulla County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 22863 people in Wakulla County, Florida lived in the county.

In 2010, 3254 people in Taylor County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 22570 people in Taylor County, Florida lived in the county.

In 1528, Panfilo de Narvaez found his way to what would be Wakulla County from the future Pinellas County, Florida, camping at the confluence of the Wakulla and St. Marks rivers. Narvaez determined this was a very suitable spot for a fort. In 1539, Hernando de Soto's expedition passed through ''La Florida'' with a similar route.
