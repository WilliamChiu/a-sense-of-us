Baker County, Florida

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 8.799208%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1759 people in Baker County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 18395 people in Baker County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2000, 2105 people in Baker County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 22259 people in Baker County, Florida lived in the county.

In 2010, 32539 people in Sussex County, Delaware lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 161669 people in Sussex County, Delaware lived in family households.

In 2010, 2937 people in Sussex County, Delaware lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 197145 people in Sussex County, Delaware lived in the county.

Baker County was founded in 1861. It was named for James McNair Baker, a judge and Confederate senator. In 1864 the Battle of Olustee, which was the only major American Civil War battle in Florida, was fought near Lake City, Florida in Baker County.
