Manatee County, Florida

In 2000, 47161 people in Manatee County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 210836 people in Manatee County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2010, 6714 people in Levy County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 33452 people in Levy County, Florida lived in family households.

The area now known as Manatee County was inhabited by Native Americans for thousands of years. The southern mouth of the Manatee River was likely the landing site of the De Soto Expedition and is the location of the U.S. National Park Service's De Soto National Memorial.
