Holmes County, Florida

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 9.03883%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2292 people in Holmes County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14553 people in Holmes County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2000, 1719 people in Holmes County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 18564 people in Holmes County, Florida lived in the county.

In 2010, 27544 people in Hernando County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 143406 people in Hernando County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2010, 1828 people in Hernando County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 172778 people in Hernando County, Florida lived in the county.

The county's namesake is a point of debate. Holmes Creek - the county's eastern boundary - bore that name before the county was created, but it was originally named Weekaywehatchee (a Creek Indian name meaning "spring creek"). One claim is that the county was named for Thomas J. Holmes, who came from North Carolina to settle in the area about 1830. Another is that it is named after Holmes, a half-breed American Indian chief who settled in the area with his band of Red Stick Creek Indians after 1814. He was subsequently killed in 1818 by a raiding party sent by Andrew Jackson during the First Seminole War.
