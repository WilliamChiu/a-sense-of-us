Gadsden County, Florida

In 2000, 5265 people in Gadsden County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 37400 people in Gadsden County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2010, 53085 people in Escambia County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 226575 people in Escambia County, Florida lived in family households.

Gadsden County was created in 1823. It was named for James Gadsden of South Carolina, who served as Andrew Jackson's aide-de-camp in Florida in 1818. Gadsden County is historically known for its tobacco crop which is obsolete today.
