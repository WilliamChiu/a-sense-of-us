Hamilton County, Florida

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 14.904213%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1392 people in Hamilton County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 9434 people in Hamilton County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2000, 2501 people in Hamilton County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 13327 people in Hamilton County, Florida lived in the county.

In 2010, 2204 people in Gilchrist County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 13607 people in Gilchrist County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2010, 1128 people in Gilchrist County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 16939 people in Gilchrist County, Florida lived in the county.

Hamilton County was created in 1827 from portions of Jefferson County. It was named for Alexander Hamilton, first United States Secretary of the Treasury.
