Okaloosa County, Florida

In 2000, 25134 people in Okaloosa County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 139857 people in Okaloosa County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2010, 346058 people in Miami-Dade County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 2110320 people in Miami-Dade County, Florida lived in family households.

Okaloosa County was created by an act passed June 3, 1915. Okaloosa is a Choctaw word meaning "black water". "Oka" means water, and "lusa" is black in the Choctaw language.
