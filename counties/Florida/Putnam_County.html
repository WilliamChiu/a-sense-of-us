Putnam County, Florida

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.7796049%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 10055 people in Putnam County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 58970 people in Putnam County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2000, 1398 people in Putnam County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 70423 people in Putnam County, Florida lived in the county.

In 2010, 77973 people in Pasco County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 381050 people in Pasco County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2010, 5674 people in Pasco County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 464697 people in Pasco County, Florida lived in the county.

Putnam County was created in 1849. It was Florida's 28th county created from parts of St. Johns, Alachua, Orange, Duval, and Marion counties. The county was named for Benjamin A. Putnam, who was a soldier in the First Seminole War, a lawyer, Florida legislator, and the first president of the Florida Historic Society. The Putnam County (Fla.) Historical Society has determined that Benjamin A. Putnam is the grandson of Israel Putnam, for whom other counties and places in the United States are named. Benjamin A. Putnam died in the county seat of Palatka in 1869.
