Monroe County, Florida

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.78909993%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 20601 people in Monroe County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 57626 people in Monroe County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2000, 1362 people in Monroe County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 79589 people in Monroe County, Florida lived in the county.

In 2010, 55408 people in Marion County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 267651 people in Marion County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2010, 8239 people in Marion County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 331298 people in Marion County, Florida lived in the county.

Monroe County was created in 1823. It was named for James Monroe, the fifth President of the United States, who served from 1817 to 1825.
