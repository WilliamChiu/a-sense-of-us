Hernando County, Florida

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -19.404613%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 18378 people in Hernando County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 110315 people in Hernando County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2000, 2109 people in Hernando County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 130802 people in Hernando County, Florida lived in the county.

In 2010, 1702 people in Hamilton County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 10033 people in Hamilton County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2010, 3064 people in Hamilton County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 14799 people in Hamilton County, Florida lived in the county.

Around 1840, Fort DeSoto was established in present-day Hernando County in the northeast edge of present-day Brooksville to protect settlers in the area from Native Americans. Fort DeSoto became a small community center, trading post, and way station on the route to Tampa. When settlement by the fort began around 1845, it was alternatively known as Pierceville.
