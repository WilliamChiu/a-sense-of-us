Washington County, Florida

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 4.6396255%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2639 people in Washington County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 16885 people in Washington County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2000, 1449 people in Washington County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 20973 people in Washington County, Florida lived in the county.

In 2010, 98112 people in Volusia County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 383672 people in Volusia County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2010, 12809 people in Volusia County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 494593 people in Volusia County, Florida lived in the county.

Washington County, Florida was created in 1825, and was nearly twice the size of the State of Delaware, stretching all the way to the Gulf of Mexico. After a century of boundary shifts, the county, with over 382000 acre of rolling hills covered in thick, stately pines and mixed hardwood forests, now covers a large portion of the central Florida Panhandle.
