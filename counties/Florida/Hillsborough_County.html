Hillsborough County, Florida

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -3.274262%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 174650 people in Hillsborough County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 806871 people in Hillsborough County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2000, 17427 people in Hillsborough County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 998948 people in Hillsborough County, Florida lived in the county.

In 2010, 4703 people in Hendry County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 32495 people in Hendry County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2010, 1942 people in Hendry County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 39140 people in Hendry County, Florida lived in the county.

Hillsborough County was created on January 25, 1834, from Alachua and Monroe counties, during the U.S. territorial period (1822-1845). It was named for Wills Hill, the Earl of Hillsborough, who served as British Secretary of State for the Colonies from 1768 to 1772.
