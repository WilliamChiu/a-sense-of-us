Columbia County, Florida

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 3.781402%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 7294 people in Columbia County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 46292 people in Columbia County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2000, 2927 people in Columbia County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 56513 people in Columbia County, Florida lived in the county.

In 2010, 26764 people in Citrus County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 112221 people in Citrus County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2010, 2251 people in Citrus County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 141236 people in Citrus County, Florida lived in the county.

Columbia County was created in 1832. It took the name of Columbia, the poetic form for the United States.
