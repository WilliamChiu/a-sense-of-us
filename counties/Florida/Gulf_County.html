Gulf County, Florida

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 6.5013647%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1550 people in Gulf County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10385 people in Gulf County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2000, 1397 people in Gulf County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 13332 people in Gulf County, Florida lived in the county.

In 2010, 5963 people in Gadsden County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 38265 people in Gadsden County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2010, 2161 people in Gadsden County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 46389 people in Gadsden County, Florida lived in the county.

Gulf County, created in 1925, was named for the Gulf of Mexico. Wewahitchka was its first county seat and the 1927 Gulf County Courthouse is still in existence. In 1965 the county seat was moved to Port Saint Joe, which under its original name Saint Joseph, had been the site of Florida's first Constitutional Convention in 1838.
