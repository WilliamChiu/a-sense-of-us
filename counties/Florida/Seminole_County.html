Seminole County, Florida

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.112736225%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2010, 3053 people in St. Lucie County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 277789 people in St. Lucie County, Florida lived in the county.

On July 21, 1821, there were two counties that formed Florida: Escambia to the west and St. Johns to the east. In 1824, the area to the south of St. Johns County was designated Mosquito County, with its seat at Enterprise. The county's name was changed to Orange County in 1845 when Florida became a state, and over the next 70 years several other counties were created. Seminole County was one of the last to split.
