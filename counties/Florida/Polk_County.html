Polk County, Florida

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.1084199%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 67812 people in Polk County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 403566 people in Polk County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2000, 12546 people in Polk County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 483924 people in Polk County, Florida lived in the county.

In 2010, 253937 people in Palm Beach County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1046225 people in Palm Beach County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2010, 19972 people in Palm Beach County, Florida lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 1320134 people in Palm Beach County, Florida lived in the county.

The first people to call Polk County home arrived close to 12,000 years ago during the last ice age as the first paleo-indians following big game southward arrived on the peninsula of Florida.
