Highlands County, Florida

In 2000, 14563 people in Highlands County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 71484 people in Highlands County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2010, 3004 people in Hardee County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 22743 people in Hardee County, Florida lived in family households.

Highlands County was created in 1921 along with Charlotte, Glades, and Hardee, when they were separated from DeSoto County. Highlands was named for the terrain of the county. Highlands County boasts the fifth-oldest population in America in 2012.
