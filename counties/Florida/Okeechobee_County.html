Okeechobee County, Florida

In 2000, 4969 people in Okeechobee County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 28846 people in Okeechobee County, Florida lived in family households.

In 2010, 19878 people in Monroe County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 51192 people in Monroe County, Florida lived in family households.

Okeechobee County was incorporated in 1917. It was named for the Lake Okeechobee, which was itself named for Hitchiti words ''oka'' (water) and ''chobi'' (big).
