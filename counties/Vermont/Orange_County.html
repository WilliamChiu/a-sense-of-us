Orange County, Vermont

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.2083769%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4233 people in Orange County, Vermont lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 23347 people in Orange County, Vermont lived in family households.

In 2000, 646 people in Orange County, Vermont lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 28226 people in Orange County, Vermont lived in the county.

In 2010, 7258 people in Franklin County, Vermont lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 39959 people in Franklin County, Vermont lived in family households.

In 2010, 529 people in Franklin County, Vermont lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 47746 people in Franklin County, Vermont lived in the county.

Orange County is a county located in the U.S. state of Vermont. As of the 2010 census, the population was 28,936. Its shire town is Chelsea. Orange County was organized on 2 February 1781 as an original county within the state.
