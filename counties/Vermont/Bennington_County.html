Bennington County, Vermont

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 3.2282114%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 6070 people in Bennington County, Vermont lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 29651 people in Bennington County, Vermont lived in family households.

In 2000, 1273 people in Bennington County, Vermont lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 36994 people in Bennington County, Vermont lived in the county.

In 2010, 374 people in Wayne County, Utah lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 2395 people in Wayne County, Utah lived in family households.

In 2010, 9 people in Wayne County, Utah lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 2778 people in Wayne County, Utah lived in the county.

Bennington is the oldest county in Vermont still in existence, created by the first general assembly on 17 March 1778. Vermont was organized into two original counties, with Bennington in the west and Unity (a few days later renamed Cumberland) in the east. On 16 February 1781 Rutland County was created from Bennington County. On 13 April 1781 Bennington gained the gore east of the town of Bromley (now Peru) from Windham and Windsor Counties.
