Lamoille County, Vermont

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.3769736%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4432 people in Lamoille County, Vermont lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 18204 people in Lamoille County, Vermont lived in family households.

In 2000, 597 people in Lamoille County, Vermont lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 23233 people in Lamoille County, Vermont lived in the county.

In 2010, 1203 people in Essex County, Vermont lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5087 people in Essex County, Vermont lived in family households.

In 2010, 16 people in Essex County, Vermont lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 6306 people in Essex County, Vermont lived in the county.

The area was buried in a mile of ice during the ice age. When it melted partially, it created Lake Stowe. When it melted completely, the water from the lake ran out through the Lamoille River valley.
