Monroe County, Alabama

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.0063300133%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2840 people in Monroe County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 21243 people in Monroe County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2000, 241 people in Monroe County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 24324 people in Monroe County, Alabama lived in the county.

In 2010, 3134 people in Monroe County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 19704 people in Monroe County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2010, 230 people in Monroe County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 23068 people in Monroe County, Alabama lived in the county.

For thousands of years the area was inhabited by indigenous peoples. In historic times, it was primarily the territory of the Creek peoples, who became known to European-American settlers as one of the Five Civilized Tribes of the Southeast.
