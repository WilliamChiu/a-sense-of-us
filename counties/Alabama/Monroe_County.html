Monroe County, Alabama

In 2000, 2840 people in Monroe County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 21243 people in Monroe County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2010, 3134 people in Monroe County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 19704 people in Monroe County, Alabama lived in family households.

For thousands of years the area was inhabited by indigenous peoples. In historic times, it was primarily the territory of the Creek peoples, who became known to European-American settlers as one of the Five Civilized Tribes of the Southeast.
