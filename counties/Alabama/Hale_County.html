Hale County, Alabama

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.43917894%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1959 people in Hale County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14936 people in Hale County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2000, 290 people in Hale County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 17185 people in Hale County, Alabama lived in the county.

In 2010, 2245 people in Hale County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 13181 people in Hale County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2010, 334 people in Hale County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 15760 people in Hale County, Alabama lived in the county.

Hale County was established following the end of the American Civil War, on January 30, 1867. Located in the west-central section of the state, it was created from portions of Greene, Marengo, Perry, and Tuscaloosa counties. The vast majority came from Greene County. The first American settlers hailed from Georgia, Tennessee, Kentucky, and the Carolinas.
