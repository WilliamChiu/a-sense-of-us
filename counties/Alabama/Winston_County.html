Winston County, Alabama

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.0023841858%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 3136 people in Winston County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 21401 people in Winston County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2000, 306 people in Winston County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 24843 people in Winston County, Alabama lived in the county.

In 2010, 3493 people in Winston County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 20690 people in Winston County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2010, 301 people in Winston County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 24484 people in Winston County, Alabama lived in the county.

Winston County was established under the name Hancock County on February 12, 1850, from territory formerly in Walker County. It was originally named for John Hancock, Governor of Massachusetts and famous signer of the American Declaration of Independence, with its county seat at Houston. On January 22, 1858, the county was renamed Winston County to honor Alabama Gov. John A. Winston.
