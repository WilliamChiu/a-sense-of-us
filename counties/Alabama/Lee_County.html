Lee County, Alabama

In 2000, 26442 people in Lee County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 84183 people in Lee County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2010, 31281 people in Lee County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 104556 people in Lee County, Alabama lived in family households.

Lee County was established by act of the Alabama state legislature on December 5, 1866, out of parts of Macon, Tallapoosa, Chambers, and Russell Counties. In an election to determine the county seat, Opelika was chosen over Auburn and Salem.
