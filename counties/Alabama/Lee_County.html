Household Populations

Lee County, Alabama

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.7665396%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 26442 people in Lee County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 84183 people in Lee County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2000, 4467 people in Lee County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 115092 people in Lee County, Alabama lived in the county.

In 2010, 31281 people in Lee County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 104556 people in Lee County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2010, 4410 people in Lee County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 140247 people in Lee County, Alabama lived in the county.

Lee County was established by act of the Alabama state legislature on December 5, 1866, out of parts of Macon, Tallapoosa, Chambers, and Russell Counties. In an election to determine the county seat, Opelika was chosen over Auburn and Salem.
