Lowndes County, Alabama

In 2000, 1452 people in Lowndes County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 11974 people in Lowndes County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2010, 1402 people in Lowndes County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 9801 people in Lowndes County, Alabama lived in family households.

Lowndes County was formed from Montgomery, Dallas and Butler counties, by an act of the Alabama General Assembly on January 20, 1830. The county is named for South Carolina statesman William Lowndes.
