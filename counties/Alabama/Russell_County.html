Russell County, Alabama

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.30078888%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 7265 people in Russell County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 41804 people in Russell County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2000, 687 people in Russell County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 49756 people in Russell County, Alabama lived in the county.

In 2010, 8358 people in Russell County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 44015 people in Russell County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2010, 574 people in Russell County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 52947 people in Russell County, Alabama lived in the county.

Russell County was established by an act of the state general assembly on December 18, 1832, from lands ceded to the state by the Creek Indians. The county seat has changed several times: Girard (1833–1839), Crawford originally Crockettsville (1839–1868), Seale (1868–1935) and Phenix City (1935–present). It was named for War of 1812, Col. Gilbert Christian Russell, Sr., 1782–1861, 3rd U.S. Infantry.
