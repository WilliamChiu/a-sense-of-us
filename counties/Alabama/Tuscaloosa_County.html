Tuscaloosa County, Alabama

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.08794069%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 28867 people in Tuscaloosa County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 127317 people in Tuscaloosa County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2000, 8691 people in Tuscaloosa County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 164875 people in Tuscaloosa County, Alabama lived in the county.

In 2010, 41023 people in Tuscaloosa County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 143210 people in Tuscaloosa County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2010, 10423 people in Tuscaloosa County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 194656 people in Tuscaloosa County, Alabama lived in the county.

Tuscaloosa County was established on February 6, 1818. During the antebellum years, the principal crop was cotton, cultivated and processed by African-American slaves. By 1860, shortly before the state seceded from the Union, the county had a total of 12,971 whites, 84 "free colored" and 10,145 slaves; the latter comprised 43.7 percent of the total population. The war brought significant changes, including migration out of the county by blacks. "By the 1870 census, the white population of Tuscaloosa County had decreased about 9% to 11,787, while the "colored" population decreased about 19% to 8,294." Some freedmen moved to nearby counties and larger cities for more opportunities and to join with other freedmen in communities less subject to white supervision.
