Dallas County, Alabama

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.6386101%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 5842 people in Dallas County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 39984 people in Dallas County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2000, 539 people in Dallas County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 46365 people in Dallas County, Alabama lived in the county.

In 2010, 6222 people in Dallas County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 36812 people in Dallas County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2010, 786 people in Dallas County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 43820 people in Dallas County, Alabama lived in the county.

Dallas County was created by the Alabama territorial legislature on February 9, 1818, from Montgomery County. This was a portion of the Creek cession of lands to the US government of August 9, 1814. The Creek were known as one of the Five Civilized Tribes of the Southeast. The county was named for U.S. Treasury Secretary Alexander J. Dallas of Pennsylvania.
