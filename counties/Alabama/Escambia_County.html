Escambia County, Alabama

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.5759895%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 4776 people in Escambia County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 30659 people in Escambia County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2000, 3005 people in Escambia County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 38440 people in Escambia County, Alabama lived in the county.

In 2010, 5111 people in Escambia County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 30009 people in Escambia County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2010, 3199 people in Escambia County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 38319 people in Escambia County, Alabama lived in the county.

Historic American Indian tribes in the area included the Muskogean-speaking Creek, Choctaw, and Alabama, who had inhabited the lands for centuries and had many settlements. The former two tribes were among those in the Southeast whom the European-American settlers called the Five Civilized Tribes, as they adopted some European-American cultural ways in an attempt to survive alongside the encroachment of settlers moving into the area in the early nineteenth century. Most of these peoples were removed by United States forces in the 1830s to Indian Territory west of the Mississippi River.
