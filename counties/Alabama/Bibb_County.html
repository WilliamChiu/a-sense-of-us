Bibb County, Alabama

In 2000, 2086 people in Bibb County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 17501 people in Bibb County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2010, 2523 people in Bibb County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 18168 people in Bibb County, Alabama lived in family households.

'''Cahawba County''' was established ("erected") on February 7, 1818, named for the Cahawba River, (now more commonly spelled as Cahaba River). This name came from the Choctaw language word meaning "water above." On December 4, 1820, it was renamed as Bibb County.
