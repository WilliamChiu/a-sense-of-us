Household Populations

Montgomery County, Alabama

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.5098214%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 34040 people in Montgomery County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 177427 people in Montgomery County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2000, 12043 people in Montgomery County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 223510 people in Montgomery County, Alabama lived in the county.

In 2010, 38114 people in Montgomery County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 182167 people in Montgomery County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2010, 9082 people in Montgomery County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 229363 people in Montgomery County, Alabama lived in the county.

Montgomery County was established by dividing Monroe County on December 6, 1816, by the Mississippi Territorial Legislature. It is named for Lemuel P. Montgomery, a young U.S. Army officer killed at the Battle of Horseshoe Bend, the final battle of the Creek Indian war, which was waged concurrently with the War of 1812.
