Wilcox County, Alabama

In 2000, 1502 people in Wilcox County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 11380 people in Wilcox County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2010, 1481 people in Wilcox County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 10081 people in Wilcox County, Alabama lived in family households.

Wilcox County was established on December 13, 1819. The county was named after Joseph M. Wilcox, a US Army lieutenant who was killed in Alabama during the Creek War.
