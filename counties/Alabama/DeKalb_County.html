DeKalb County, Alabama

In 2000, 7622 people in DeKalb County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 55966 people in DeKalb County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2010, 8584 people in DeKalb County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 61743 people in DeKalb County, Alabama lived in family households.

DeKalb County was created by the Alabama legislature on January 9, 1836, from land ceded to the Federal government by the Cherokee Nation. It was named for Major General Baron Johann de Kalb, a hero of the American Revolution.
