Household Populations

Franklin County, Alabama

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.43518543%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 3777 people in Franklin County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 27044 people in Franklin County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2000, 402 people in Franklin County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 31223 people in Franklin County, Alabama lived in the county.

In 2010, 4027 people in Franklin County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 27405 people in Franklin County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2010, 272 people in Franklin County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 31704 people in Franklin County, Alabama lived in the county.

Colbert County was originally established on February 6, 1867 after it split from Franklin County over political issues after the American Civil War. It was abolished eight months later by an Alabama constitutional convention and then reestablished on February 24, 1870.
