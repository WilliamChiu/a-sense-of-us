Mobile County, Alabama

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.37693977%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 51453 people in Mobile County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 340322 people in Mobile County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2000, 8068 people in Mobile County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 399843 people in Mobile County, Alabama lived in the county.

In 2010, 60376 people in Mobile County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 345808 people in Mobile County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2010, 6808 people in Mobile County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 412992 people in Mobile County, Alabama lived in the county.

This area was occupied for thousands of years by varying cultures of indigenous peoples. The historic Choctaw had occupied this area along what became called the Mobile River when encountered by early French traders and colonists, who founded Mobile in the early eighteenth century. The British took over the territory in 1763 (along with other French territories east of the Mississippi River) after defeating the French in the Seven Years' War. During the American Revolutionary War, it came under Spanish rule as part of Spanish Florida. Spain ceded the territory to the United States after the War of 1812.
