Household Populations

Etowah County, Alabama

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.022298098%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 13672 people in Etowah County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 87744 people in Etowah County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2000, 2043 people in Etowah County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 103459 people in Etowah County, Alabama lived in the county.

In 2010, 15232 people in Etowah County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 87113 people in Etowah County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2010, 2085 people in Etowah County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 104430 people in Etowah County, Alabama lived in the county.

Etowah was originally the southern part of DeKalb County, Alabama. However Baine County was established on December 7, 1866, named for General David W. Baine of the Confederate Army, with its county seat at Gadsden. On December 1, 1868 the county was abolished and Etowah County was created from the same territory.
