Cleburne County, Alabama

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.3919065%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1678 people in Cleburne County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 12332 people in Cleburne County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2000, 113 people in Cleburne County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 14123 people in Cleburne County, Alabama lived in the county.

In 2010, 1975 people in Cleburne County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 12819 people in Cleburne County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2010, 178 people in Cleburne County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 14972 people in Cleburne County, Alabama lived in the county.

Cleburne County was established on December 6, 1866, by an act of the state legislature. The county was made from territory in Benton (now Calhoun), Randolph, and Talladega counties. In 1867, Edwardsville was made the county seat. An election was held in 1905 to move the county seat to Heflin. The result of that election, which agreed to move the seat, was appealed to the Supreme Court, who decided on July 1, 1906, to uphold the election results. Heflin is still the county seat. Heflin was at one point thought of as a hub for nearby farmers to send their cotton. Shortly after the Civil War, a group of northern investors created the town of Fruithurst in Cleburne County as part of a wine-growing project. Fruithurst became a boomtown shortly thereafter.
