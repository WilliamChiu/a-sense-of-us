From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -3.5682023%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2354 people in Sumter County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 12191 people in Sumter County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2000, 253 people in Sumter County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 14798 people in Sumter County, Alabama lived in the county.

In 2010, 2599 people in Sumter County, Alabama lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 10446 people in Sumter County, Alabama lived in family households.

In 2010, 718 people in Sumter County, Alabama lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 13763 people in Sumter County, Alabama lived in the county.

Sumter County was established on December 18, 1832. From 1797 to 1832, Sumter County was part of the Choctaw Nation, which was made up of four main villages. The first settlers in Sumter County were French explorers who had come north from Mobile. They built and settled at Fort Tombecbee, near the modern-day town of Epes. In 1830, with the Treaty of Dancing Rabbit Creek, the Choctaw Indians ceded the land that is now Sumter County to the government.
