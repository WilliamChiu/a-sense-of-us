Putnam County, Illinois

In 2000, 761 people in Putnam County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 5314 people in Putnam County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 2453 people in Pike County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 13350 people in Pike County, Illinois lived in family households.

Putnam County is the least extensive county in the U.S. state of Illinois. According to the 2010 census, it had a population of 6,006. The county seat is Hennepin. The county was formed in 1825 out of Fulton County and named after Israel Putnam, who was a general in the American Revolution.
