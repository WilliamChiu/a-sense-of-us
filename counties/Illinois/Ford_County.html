Ford County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.1160483%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1935 people in Ford County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 11903 people in Ford County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 403 people in Ford County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 14241 people in Ford County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 1044 people in Edwards County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5625 people in Edwards County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 52 people in Edwards County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 6721 people in Edwards County, Illinois lived in the county.

Ford County was formed February 17, 1859. It was the last of Illinois' 102 counties to be formed, and was created at the behest of some residents of Vermilion County, who complained to the General Assembly that they were located too far from the Iroquois County county seat. Ford County was named in honor of Thomas Ford, who served as Governor of Illinois from 1842 to 1846.
