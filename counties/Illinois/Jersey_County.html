Jersey County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.7615845%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2601 people in Jersey County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 18170 people in Jersey County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 897 people in Jersey County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 21668 people in Jersey County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 18702 people in Jackson County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 37429 people in Jackson County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 4087 people in Jackson County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 60218 people in Jackson County, Illinois lived in the county.

Jersey County lies just northeast of where the great Mississippi and Illinois rivers meet. It is the former home of the Kickapoo, Menomini, Potawatomi, and Illiniwek Confederation American Indians. The first European explorers to visit the area, Father Marquette and Louis Jolliet, arrived in 1673, where they encountered the fearsome painting of the Piasa bird. The present day Pere Marquette State Park, located near Grafton, is named in Father Marquette's honor, and a monument to him is located at the park.
