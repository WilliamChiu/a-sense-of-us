Washington County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.8590629%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1858 people in Washington County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 13028 people in Washington County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 262 people in Washington County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 15148 people in Washington County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 13115 people in Vermilion County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 65607 people in Vermilion County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 2903 people in Vermilion County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 81625 people in Vermilion County, Illinois lived in the county.

Washington County was formed on January 2, 1818 out of St. Clair County. It was named for George Washington. Despite its relative proximity to the Illinois suburbs of St. Louis, Missouri, the county is not considered part of the St. Louis metropolitan area, also known as the Metro-East.
