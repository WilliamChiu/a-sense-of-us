Marshall County, Illinois

In 2000, 1755 people in Marshall County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 11148 people in Marshall County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 7303 people in Macoupin County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 39586 people in Macoupin County, Illinois lived in family households.

Marshall County was formed in 1839 out of Putnam County. It was named in honor of John Marshall, Chief Justice of the United States Supreme Court, who died in 1835.
