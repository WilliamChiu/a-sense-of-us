Douglas County, Illinois

In 2000, 2369 people in Douglas County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 17253 people in Douglas County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 1482 people in Cumberland County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 9449 people in Cumberland County, Illinois lived in family households.

Douglas County was formed in 1859 out of Coles County. It was named in honor of Stephen A. Douglas, who was elected to the United States Senate in 1858, following the Lincoln-Douglas Debates.
