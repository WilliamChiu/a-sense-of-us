Mercer County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.15037656%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1963 people in Mercer County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14781 people in Mercer County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 213 people in Mercer County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 16957 people in Mercer County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 2409 people in Mason County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 12051 people in Mason County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 206 people in Mason County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 14666 people in Mason County, Illinois lived in the county.

In May 1812, Congress passed an act which set aside lands in Arkansas, Michigan, and Illinois as payment to volunteer soldiers in the War of 1812. Mercer County was part of this "Military Tract."
