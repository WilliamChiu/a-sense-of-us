Coles County, Illinois

In 2000, 12836 people in Coles County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 35806 people in Coles County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 2586 people in Clark County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 13528 people in Clark County, Illinois lived in family households.

Coles County was organized on December 25, 1830 out of Clark and Edgar counties. It was named after Edward Coles, the second governor of Illinois from 1822 to 1826.
