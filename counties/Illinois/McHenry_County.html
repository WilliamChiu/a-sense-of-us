McHenry County, Illinois

In 2000, 25280 people in McHenry County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 233278 people in McHenry County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 5772 people in Livingston County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 29788 people in Livingston County, Illinois lived in family households.

McHenry County was formed in 1836 out of Cook and LaSalle counties. The county was named for Major William McHenry, an Indian fighter who died in Vandalia in 1835.
