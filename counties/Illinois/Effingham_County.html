Effingham County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.039494038%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 4338 people in Effingham County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 29459 people in Effingham County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 467 people in Effingham County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 34264 people in Effingham County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 120467 people in DuPage County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 784317 people in DuPage County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 12140 people in DuPage County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 916924 people in DuPage County, Illinois lived in the county.

Effingham County was formed in 1855 out of Fayette and Crawford counties. It may have been named after Thomas Howard, 3rd Earl of Effingham, who resigned his commission as general in the British army in 1775, refusing to serve in the war against the Colonies. The name is Anglo-Saxon for "Effa's house". New information suggests that the county was named after a surveyor who surveyed the area whose last name was Effingham. There is no written proof that the county was named after Lord Effingham.
