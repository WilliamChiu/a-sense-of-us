Macoupin County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.9413352%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 6518 people in Macoupin County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 41311 people in Macoupin County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 1190 people in Macoupin County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 49019 people in Macoupin County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 33457 people in McHenry County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 273656 people in McHenry County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 1647 people in McHenry County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 308760 people in McHenry County, Illinois lived in the county.

The region was previously inhabited by Illinoisan Indians when the first white settlers arrived. Indeed, ''macoupin'' is the Miami-Illinois term for the American lotus, ''Nelumbo lutea''. None of the native Indians remain, although some descendants of the earliest European settlers claim partial ancestry. The first European contact was by French explorers in the seventeenth century, travelling southward down the major rivers. The main European settlement was from the southwest, as people moved inland from the established transportation route of the Mississippi River.
