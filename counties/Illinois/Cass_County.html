Cass County, Illinois

In 2000, 2025 people in Cass County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 11458 people in Cass County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 5510 people in Bureau County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 29012 people in Bureau County, Illinois lived in family households.

Cass County was formed in 1837 out of Morgan County. It was named in honor of Lewis Cass, a general in the War of 1812, Governor of the Michigan Territory, and United States Secretary of State in 1860. Cass was serving as Andrew Jackson's Secretary of War just before the County was named.
