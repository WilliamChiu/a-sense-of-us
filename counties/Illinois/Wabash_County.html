Wabash County, Illinois

In 2000, 1856 people in Wabash County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10904 people in Wabash County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 20359 people in Tazewell County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 112192 people in Tazewell County, Illinois lived in family households.

Wabash County was formed in 1824 out of Edwards County, to avert an armed confrontation between the militias of Albion and Mt. Carmel after the county seat was moved from a town near the current city of Mount Carmel to Albion.
