Adams County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.7504325%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 10426 people in Adams County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 55101 people in Adams County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 2750 people in Adams County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 68277 people in Adams County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 11371 people in Twin Falls County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 64787 people in Twin Falls County, Idaho lived in family households.

In 2010, 1072 people in Twin Falls County, Idaho lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 77230 people in Twin Falls County, Idaho lived in the county.

Adams County was formed in 1825 out of Pike County. Its name is in honor of the sixth President of the United States, John Quincy Adams.
