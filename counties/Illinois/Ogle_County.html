Ogle County, Illinois

In 2000, 6077 people in Ogle County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 44341 people in Ogle County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 4533 people in Montgomery County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 23152 people in Montgomery County, Illinois lived in family households.

Ogle County was formed in 1836 out of Jo Daviess and LaSalle counties, and named in honor of Captain Joseph Ogle, a veteran of the Revolutionary War who came to Illinois in 1785. Ogle did not have an organized government until 1837, so for a year it remained attached to Jo Daviess County. The county was divided three years later in 1839, when Lee County was formed.
