Moultrie County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.3180485%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1617 people in Moultrie County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 12201 people in Moultrie County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 469 people in Moultrie County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 14287 people in Moultrie County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 3841 people in Monroe County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 28773 people in Monroe County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 343 people in Monroe County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 32957 people in Monroe County, Illinois lived in the county.

Moultrie County was formed in 1843 out of Shelby and Coles counties. It is named for South Carolina General, and later Governor, William Moultrie. General Moultrie defended Sullivan's Island, South Carolina from British attack in 1776. The site was later renamed Fort Moultrie. Nearby Jasper County was named for Sgt. William Jasper, another hero of the defense of Sullivan's Island.
