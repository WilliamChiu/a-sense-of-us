Jefferson County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 5.1751018%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 5551 people in Jefferson County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 31924 people in Jefferson County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 2570 people in Jefferson County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 40045 people in Jefferson County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 4450 people in Iroquois County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 24800 people in Iroquois County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 468 people in Iroquois County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 29718 people in Iroquois County, Illinois lived in the county.

The first settler in Jefferson County is believed to have been Andrew Moore. In 1810, he settled near the southeast corner of the county, near where the Goshen Road emerges from the forest of Hamilton County into what is now known as Moore's Prairie. It is interesting that Moore arrived from the Goshen Settlement, near Edwardsville. His migration was therefore retrograde, from the west toward the interior of the State.
