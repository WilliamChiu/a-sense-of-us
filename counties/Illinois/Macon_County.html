Macon County, Illinois

In 2000, 18505 people in Macon County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 92628 people in Macon County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 9197 people in McDonough County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 19379 people in McDonough County, Illinois lived in family households.

Macon County was formed on January 19, 1829 out of Shelby County. It was named for Nathaniel Macon, who served as a Colonel in the Revolutionary War. Macon later served as senator from North Carolina until his resignation in 1828.
