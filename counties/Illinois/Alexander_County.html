Alexander County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 5.901277%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1452 people in Alexander County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 7543 people in Alexander County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 595 people in Alexander County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 9590 people in Alexander County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 1858 people in Valley County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7938 people in Valley County, Idaho lived in family households.

In 2010, 66 people in Valley County, Idaho lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 9862 people in Valley County, Idaho lived in the county.

Alexander County was organized out of Union County in 1819. It was named for William M. Alexander, a physician who practiced in the town of America (the first county seat). Alexander became Speaker of the Illinois House of Representatives in 1822. The county seat was moved to Unity in 1833, then to Thebes in 1843, and finally to Cairo in 1860. America, the first county seat, is now within Pulaski County, which was formed from Alexander and Johnson Counties in 1843.
