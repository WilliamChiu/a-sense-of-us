Lake County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.2878537%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 64822 people in Lake County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 558556 people in Lake County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 20978 people in Lake County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 644356 people in Lake County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 15635 people in Kankakee County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 92707 people in Kankakee County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 5107 people in Kankakee County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 113449 people in Kankakee County, Illinois lived in the county.

The county, which was primarily unsettled prairie and was still home to its native Potawatomi Indians, was created by the Illinois State Legislature in 1839. At that time, Libertyville, then known as Independence Grove, was the first county seat. In 1841, however, the county's residents voted to move the county government to Little Fort, now Waukegan, where the commissioners had purchased a section of land from the state. Lake County's first courthouse was built on part of that land in 1844 and the remainder was sold to pay for the $4,000 construction cost.
