Menard County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.56805015%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1516 people in Menard County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10788 people in Menard County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 182 people in Menard County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 12486 people in Menard County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 1896 people in Marshall County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 10489 people in Marshall County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 255 people in Marshall County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 12640 people in Marshall County, Illinois lived in the county.

Menard County was formed in 1839 out of Sangamon County. The County was named for Pierre Menard, the first lieutenant governor of Illinois.
