Menard County, Illinois

In 2000, 1516 people in Menard County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10788 people in Menard County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 1896 people in Marshall County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 10489 people in Marshall County, Illinois lived in family households.

Menard County was formed in 1839 out of Sangamon County. The County was named for Pierre Menard, the first lieutenant governor of Illinois.
