Kankakee County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 3.3401608%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 13924 people in Kankakee County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 85813 people in Kankakee County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 4096 people in Kankakee County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 103833 people in Kankakee County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 3818 people in Jo Daviess County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 18693 people in Jo Daviess County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 167 people in Jo Daviess County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 22678 people in Jo Daviess County, Illinois lived in the county.

French Canadian Settlers came to Kankakee County in 1834, after the federal government signed the Treaty of Camp Tippecanoe in 1832. They were soon joined by migrants from New York and Vermont who had moved their way west, mostly locating in Momence, Illinois. In the 1840s most of the migrants were French Canadians or Metis and they settled in such places as Bourbonnais.
