Perry County, Illinois

In 2000, 3046 people in Perry County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 17605 people in Perry County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 1991 people in Moultrie County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 12460 people in Moultrie County, Illinois lived in family households.

Perry County was formed in 1827 out of Jackson and Randolph counties. It was named in honor of Oliver Hazard Perry who defeated the British fleet at the decisive Battle of Lake Erie in the War of 1812.
