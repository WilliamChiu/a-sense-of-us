Jasper County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.6682277%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1212 people in Jasper County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 8827 people in Jasper County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 78 people in Jasper County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 10117 people in Jasper County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 7352 people in Henry County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 42410 people in Henry County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 724 people in Henry County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 50486 people in Henry County, Illinois lived in the county.

Jasper County was formed in 1831 out of Clay and Crawford Counties. It was named for Sgt. William Jasper, Revolutionary War hero from South Carolina. In the defense of Fort Moultrie in 1776, when the staff of the American flag was shot away, Sgt. Jasper erected a temporary staff, and waved the flag at the British until a new staff was erected.
