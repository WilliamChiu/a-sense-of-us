Madison County, Illinois

In 2000, 38255 people in Madison County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 214907 people in Madison County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 34975 people in McLean County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 123921 people in McLean County, Illinois lived in family households.

Madison County was established on September 14, 1812. It was formed from parts of Randolph and St. Clair counties and named for President James Madison.
