Pope County, Illinois

In 2000, 622 people in Pope County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 3504 people in Pope County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 3131 people in Perry County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 16719 people in Perry County, Illinois lived in family households.

First permanent settlement in 1798 at the modern-day site of Golconda, operating as a ferry point across the Ohio River. Pope County was formed in 1816 from portions of Gallatin and Johnson counties.
