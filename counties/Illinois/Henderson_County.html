Henderson County, Illinois

In 2000, 1146 people in Henderson County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6990 people in Henderson County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 1274 people in Hamilton County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7066 people in Hamilton County, Illinois lived in family households.

Henderson County was formed in 1841 out of Warren County. It was named for Henderson County, Kentucky, which was named for Richard Henderson, founder of the Transylvania Company, an early attempt to organize what later became Kentucky around 1775.
