Vermilion County, Illinois

In 2000, 12922 people in Vermilion County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 67888 people in Vermilion County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 8020 people in Stephenson County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 38856 people in Stephenson County, Illinois lived in family households.

Vermilion County is named after the Vermilion River, which passes through the county and empties into the Wabash River in Indiana near Cayuga; the river was so named because of the color of the earth along its route.
