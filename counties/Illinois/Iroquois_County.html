Iroquois County, Illinois

In 2000, 4059 people in Iroquois County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 26589 people in Iroquois County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 776 people in Hardin County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 3527 people in Hardin County, Illinois lived in family households.

Iroquois County was created on February 26, 1833 out of a portion of Vermilion County. It was named for the Iroquois River, which was itself named for the Iroquois people.
