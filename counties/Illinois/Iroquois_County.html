Iroquois County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.83599%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4059 people in Iroquois County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 26589 people in Iroquois County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 686 people in Iroquois County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 31334 people in Iroquois County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 776 people in Hardin County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 3527 people in Hardin County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 17 people in Hardin County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 4320 people in Hardin County, Illinois lived in the county.

Iroquois County was created on February 26, 1833 out of a portion of Vermilion County. It was named for the Iroquois River, which was itself named for the Iroquois people.
