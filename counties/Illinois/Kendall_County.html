Kendall County, Illinois

In 2000, 4754 people in Kendall County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 49598 people in Kendall County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 1501 people in Johnson County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 9528 people in Johnson County, Illinois lived in family households.

The county is named after Amos Kendall. Kendall was the editor of the Frankfort, Kentucky newspaper, and went on to be an important advisor to President Andrew Jackson. Kendall became the U.S. Postmaster General in 1835.
