Pulaski County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 3.675425%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1068 people in Pulaski County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 5989 people in Pulaski County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 291 people in Pulaski County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 7348 people in Pulaski County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 2316 people in Piatt County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 14341 people in Piatt County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 72 people in Piatt County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 16729 people in Piatt County, Illinois lived in the county.

Pulaski County was formed on March 3, 1843, out of parts of Alexander and Johnson counties. It was named in honor of Kazimierz Pułaski who was killed at the Siege of Savannah in the Revolutionary War.
