Schuyler County, Illinois

In 2000, 1024 people in Schuyler County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6060 people in Schuyler County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 41146 people in St. Clair County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 224459 people in St. Clair County, Illinois lived in family households.

Schuyler County was formed in 1825 out of Pike and Fulton counties. It is named in honor of Philip Schuyler, member of the Continental Congress and Senator from New York.
