Pike County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.54773%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2341 people in Pike County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14321 people in Pike County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 722 people in Pike County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 17384 people in Pike County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 35303 people in Peoria County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 146212 people in Peoria County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 4979 people in Peoria County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 186494 people in Peoria County, Illinois lived in the county.

Pike County was formed on January 31, 1821 out of Madison County. It was named in honor of Zebulon Pike, leader of the Pike expedition in 1806 to map out the south and west portions of the Louisiana Purchase. Pike served at the Battle of Tippecanoe, and was killed in 1813 in the War of 1812.
