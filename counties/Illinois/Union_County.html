Union County, Illinois

In 2000, 2608 people in Union County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14770 people in Union County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 875 people in Stark County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5027 people in Stark County, Illinois lived in family households.

Union County was formed out of Johnson County on January 2, 1818, the same year Illinois joined the Union. It was named for a joint revival meeting of the Baptists and Dunkards, called a "union meeting". The county seal depicts the leaders of these two groups shaking hands.
