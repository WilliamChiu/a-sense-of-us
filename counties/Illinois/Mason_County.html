Mason County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.028961897%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2122 people in Mason County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 13697 people in Mason County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 219 people in Mason County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 16038 people in Mason County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 45778 people in Madison County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 219750 people in Madison County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 3754 people in Madison County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 269282 people in Madison County, Illinois lived in the county.

Mason County was created in 1841 out of portions of Tazewell and Menard counties.
