Woodford County, Illinois

In 2000, 3446 people in Woodford County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 30925 people in Woodford County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 63248 people in Will County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 605765 people in Will County, Illinois lived in family households.

Woodford County is part of what was formerly the homelands of several Native American peoples, including the Potawatomi, the Meskwaki, and the Sauk peoples. It was located just south of the land of the Illiniwek. The western portion of the county in particular shows extensive archeological evidence of supporting extensive First Nations populations.
