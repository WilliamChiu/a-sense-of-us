Fulton County, Illinois

In 2000, 5312 people in Fulton County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 30376 people in Fulton County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 3193 people in Fayette County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 17163 people in Fayette County, Illinois lived in family households.

Fulton County was organized in 1823 from Pike County. It is named for Robert Fulton, inventor of the steamboat. It was the home of famous poet/writer Edgar Lee Masters in his early days; he wrote the famous Spoon River Anthology (1915). Fulton County was home to Camp Ellis during World War II.
