Livingston County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 7.198882%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 5163 people in Livingston County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 30892 people in Livingston County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 3623 people in Livingston County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 39678 people in Livingston County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 18044 people in LaSalle County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 92930 people in LaSalle County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 2950 people in LaSalle County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 113924 people in LaSalle County, Illinois lived in the county.

Livingston was established on February 27, 1837. It was formed from parts of McLean, LaSalle, and Iroquois counties, and named after Edward Livingston, a prominent politician who was mayor of New York City and represented New York in the United States House of Representatives and Louisiana in both houses of Congress. He later served as Andrew Jackson's Secretary of State and as Minister to France. Although he had no connections to Illinois, the General Assembly found him accomplished enough to name a county after him.
