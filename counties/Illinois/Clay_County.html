Clay County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -4.9057665%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2112 people in Clay County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 11971 people in Clay County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 477 people in Clay County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 14560 people in Clay County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 55445 people in Champaign County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 129507 people in Champaign County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 16129 people in Champaign County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 201081 people in Champaign County, Illinois lived in the county.

Clay County was formed in 1824 out of portions of Wayne, Crawford, and Fayette counties. Its name is in honor of Henry Clay, famous American statesman, member of the United States Senate from Kentucky and United States Secretary of State in the 19th century. Clay was an unsuccessful candidate for President in the year the County was named.
