White County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -3.6253452%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2417 people in White County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 12527 people in White County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 427 people in White County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 15371 people in White County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 2730 people in Warren County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 13861 people in Warren County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 1116 people in Warren County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 17707 people in Warren County, Illinois lived in the county.

White County was organized from Gallatin County in 1815, and was named after Captain Leonard White (some accounts say Captain Isaac White), a Gallatin County legislator who is credited with the idea of extending the Illinois-Wisconsin border a few miles north of the southern tip of Lake Michigan and was also in charge of the salt works at Equality. He was killed in 1811 at the Battle of Tippecanoe. The county seat, Carmi, was founded in 1814, and incorporated in 1816. The first courthouse was in the log cabin of John Craw.
