Clinton County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.1767259%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4066 people in Clinton County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 29103 people in Clinton County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 2366 people in Clinton County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 35535 people in Clinton County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 5639 people in Christian County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 27551 people in Christian County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 1610 people in Christian County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 34800 people in Christian County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 1805, prior to the establishment of the county, the territorial government established a post road from its capital (Vincennes, Indiana) to St. Louis, Missouri, passing through the county.
