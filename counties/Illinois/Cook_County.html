Cook County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.47486424%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 865791 people in Cook County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 4417333 people in Cook County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 93617 people in Cook County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 5376741 people in Cook County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 2247 people in Clay County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 11263 people in Clay County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 305 people in Clay County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 13815 people in Clay County, Illinois lived in the county.

Cook County was created on January 15, 1831, out of Putnam County by an act of the Illinois General Assembly. It was the 54th county established in Illinois and was named after Daniel Cook, one of the earliest and youngest statesmen in Illinois history, who served as the second U.S. Representative from Illinois and the state's first Attorney General. In 1839, DuPage County was carved out of Cook County.
