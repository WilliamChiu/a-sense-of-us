Lee County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 5.8820844%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4880 people in Lee County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 28176 people in Lee County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 3006 people in Lee County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 36062 people in Lee County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 75260 people in Lake County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 607493 people in Lake County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 20709 people in Lake County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 703462 people in Lake County, Illinois lived in the county.

Lee County was formed in 1839 out of Ogle County. The County is named in honor of Lighthorse Harry Lee, officer in the Revolutionary War, or Richard Henry Lee, member of the Continental Congress. The Declaration of Independence was adopted pursuant to Lee's Resolution. President Ronald Reagan lived in Dixon as a boy and attended Dixon High School.
