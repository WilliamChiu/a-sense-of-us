Lee County, Illinois

In 2000, 4880 people in Lee County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 28176 people in Lee County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 607493 people in Lake County, Illinois lived in family households.

Lee County was formed in 1839 out of Ogle County. The County is named in honor of Lighthorse Harry Lee, officer in the Revolutionary War, or Richard Henry Lee, member of the Continental Congress. The Declaration of Independence was adopted pursuant to Lee's Resolution. President Ronald Reagan lived in Dixon as a boy and attended Dixon High School.
