Hancock County, Illinois

In 2000, 2855 people in Hancock County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 16906 people in Hancock County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 2082 people in Greene County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 11520 people in Greene County, Illinois lived in family households.

Hancock County was part of the "Military Tract" set aside by Congress to reward veterans of the War of 1812. Actual settlement of the interior of the County was delayed by concerns about hostile American Indians. After their defeat in the Blackhawk War in 1832, settlement proceeded quickly.
