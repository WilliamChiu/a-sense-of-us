Bond County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 14.509762%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2099 people in Bond County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 13120 people in Bond County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 2414 people in Bond County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 17633 people in Bond County, Illinois lived in the county.

Bond County was formed in 1817 out of Madison County. It was named for Shadrach Bond, who was then the delegate from the Illinois Territory to the United States Congress, and who thereupon became the first governor of Illinois, serving from 1818 to 1822.
