Carroll County, Illinois

In 2000, 2442 people in Carroll County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 13984 people in Carroll County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 853 people in Brown County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 3974 people in Brown County, Illinois lived in family households.

Carroll County was formed in 1839 out of Jo Daviess County. The county is named for Charles Carroll who signed the Declaration of Independence. Carroll, who died in 1832, was the last signer to die.
