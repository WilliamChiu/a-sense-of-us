Marion County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.3720632%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 5893 people in Marion County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 34829 people in Marion County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 969 people in Marion County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 41691 people in Marion County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 19560 people in Macon County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 87149 people in Macon County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 4059 people in Macon County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 110768 people in Macon County, Illinois lived in the county.

Marion County was organized on 24 January 1823 from portions of Jefferson and Fayette counties. It was named in honor of Revolutionary War Gen. Francis Marion, the "Swamp Fox".
