Marion County, Illinois

In 2000, 5893 people in Marion County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 34829 people in Marion County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 19560 people in Macon County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 87149 people in Macon County, Illinois lived in family households.

Marion County was organized on 24 January 1823 from portions of Jefferson and Fayette counties. It was named in honor of Revolutionary War Gen. Francis Marion, the "Swamp Fox".
