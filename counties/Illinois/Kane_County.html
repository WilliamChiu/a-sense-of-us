Kane County, Illinois

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.1142006%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 40709 people in Kane County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 356907 people in Kane County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2000, 6503 people in Kane County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 404119 people in Kane County, Illinois lived in the county.

In 2010, 3080 people in Jersey County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 19057 people in Jersey County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 848 people in Jersey County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 22985 people in Jersey County, Illinois lived in the county.

Kane County was formed out of LaSalle County in 1836. The County was named in honor of Elias Kane, United States Senator from Illinois, and the first Secretary of State of Illinois.
