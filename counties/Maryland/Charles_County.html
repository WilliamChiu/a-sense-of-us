Charles County, Maryland

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.20335913%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 12387 people in Charles County, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 106790 people in Charles County, Maryland lived in family households.

In 2000, 1369 people in Charles County, Maryland lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 120546 people in Charles County, Maryland lived in the county.

In 2010, 4392 people in Caroline County, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 28232 people in Caroline County, Maryland lived in family households.

In 2010, 442 people in Caroline County, Maryland lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 33066 people in Caroline County, Maryland lived in the county.

Charles County was created in 1658 by an Order in Council. There was an also an earlier Charles County from 1650 to 1653, sometimes referred to in historic documents as Old Charles County.
