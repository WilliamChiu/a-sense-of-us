Kent County, Maryland

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 5.7118773%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 3014 people in Kent County, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14835 people in Kent County, Maryland lived in family households.

In 2000, 1348 people in Kent County, Maryland lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 19197 people in Kent County, Maryland lived in the county.

In 2010, 4314 people in Garrett County, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 25268 people in Garrett County, Maryland lived in family households.

In 2010, 515 people in Garrett County, Maryland lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 30097 people in Garrett County, Maryland lived in the county.

In 1642, the governor and council appointed commissioners for the '''Isle and County of Kent.''' This act appears to have led to the establishment of Kent County. In 1675, the first county seat was New Yarmouth. The seat was briefly moved upriver to Quaker Neck, and then to the site of modern Chestertown. Before the American Revolution New Town on Chester, now Chestertown, was a port entry for the counties of Cecil, Kent, and Queen Anne's.
