Worcester County, Maryland

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.4561305%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 7983 people in Worcester County, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 37879 people in Worcester County, Maryland lived in family households.

In 2000, 681 people in Worcester County, Maryland lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 46543 people in Worcester County, Maryland lived in the county.

In 2010, 6568 people in Talbot County, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 30831 people in Talbot County, Maryland lived in family households.

In 2010, 383 people in Talbot County, Maryland lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 37782 people in Talbot County, Maryland lived in the county.

Worcester County was created by the division of the formerly larger Eastern Shore's Somerset County in 1742. The county seat, which was previously located near the confluence of Dividing Creek with the Pocomoke River, was later transferred to the river port of Snow Hill, at the head of navigation of the Pocomoke, now near the center of the new county.
