Talbot County, Maryland

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.93569756%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 5507 people in Talbot County, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 27693 people in Talbot County, Maryland lived in family households.

In 2000, 612 people in Talbot County, Maryland lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 33812 people in Talbot County, Maryland lived in the county.

In 2010, 5968 people in Queen Anne's County, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 41404 people in Queen Anne's County, Maryland lived in family households.

In 2010, 426 people in Queen Anne's County, Maryland lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 47798 people in Queen Anne's County, Maryland lived in the county.

The founding date of Talbot County is not known. It existed by February 12, 1661, when a writ was issued to its sheriff. It was initially divided into nine Hundreds and three parishes: St. Paul's, St. Peter's and St. Michael's.
