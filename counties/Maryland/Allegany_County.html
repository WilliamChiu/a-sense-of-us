Allegany County, Maryland

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 7.546854%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 12756 people in Allegany County, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 56016 people in Allegany County, Maryland lived in family households.

In 2000, 6158 people in Allegany County, Maryland lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 74930 people in Allegany County, Maryland lived in the county.

In 2010, 7313 people in Waldo County, Maine lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 30972 people in Waldo County, Maine lived in family households.

In 2010, 501 people in Waldo County, Maine lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 38786 people in Waldo County, Maine lived in the county.

The western part of Maryland (including the present Allegany County) was originally part of Prince George's County when Maryland was formed in 1696. This county included six current counties, and by repeated splitting, new ones were generated: Frederick from Prince George's in 1748; and Montgomery and Washington from Frederick in 1776.
