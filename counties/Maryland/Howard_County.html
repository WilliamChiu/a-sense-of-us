Howard County, Maryland

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.33700466%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 31156 people in Howard County, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 213068 people in Howard County, Maryland lived in family households.

In 2000, 3618 people in Howard County, Maryland lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 247842 people in Howard County, Maryland lived in the county.

In 2010, 30092 people in Frederick County, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 199111 people in Frederick County, Maryland lived in family households.

In 2010, 4182 people in Frederick County, Maryland lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 233385 people in Frederick County, Maryland lived in the county.

The lands of prehistoric Howard County were populated by Native Americans. The Maryland Historical Trust has documented sites along the Patapsco, Patuxent, Middle and Little Patuxent River valleys. In 1652, the Susquehannock tribes signed a peace treaty with Maryland, giving up their provenance over the territory that is now Howard County. In 1800, the mean center of U.S. population as calculated by the US Census Bureau was found in what is now Howard County.
