Harford County, Maryland

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.84272623%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 23650 people in Harford County, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 193378 people in Harford County, Maryland lived in family households.

In 2000, 1562 people in Harford County, Maryland lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 218590 people in Harford County, Maryland lived in the county.

In 2010, 5599 people in Dorchester County, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 26513 people in Dorchester County, Maryland lived in family households.

In 2010, 506 people in Dorchester County, Maryland lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 32618 people in Dorchester County, Maryland lived in the county.

Harford County was formed in 1773 from the eastern part of Baltimore County. It contains Tudor Hall, birthplace of Abraham Lincoln's assassin, John Wilkes Booth. Harford County also hosted the signers of the Bush Declaration, a precursor document to the American Revolution.
