Caroline County, Maryland

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.1241734%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 3720 people in Caroline County, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 25599 people in Caroline County, Maryland lived in family households.

In 2000, 453 people in Caroline County, Maryland lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 29772 people in Caroline County, Maryland lived in the county.

In 2010, 77091 people in Anne Arundel County, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 446432 people in Anne Arundel County, Maryland lived in family households.

In 2010, 14133 people in Anne Arundel County, Maryland lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 537656 people in Anne Arundel County, Maryland lived in the county.

Caroline County was created in 1774 from parts of Dorchester and Queen Anne's counties. The county derives its name from Lady Caroline Eden, wife of Maryland's last colonial governor, Robert Eden. At the time of its creation, seven commissioners were appointed: Charles Dickinson, Benson Stainton, Thomas White, William Haskins, Richard Mason, Joshua Clark, and Nathaniel Potter. These men bought 4 acre of land at Pig Point (now Denton) on which to build a courthouse and jail.
