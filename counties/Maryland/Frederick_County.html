Frederick County, Maryland

In 2000, 23154 people in Frederick County, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 167468 people in Frederick County, Maryland lived in family households.

In 2010, 12986 people in Cecil County, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 86571 people in Cecil County, Maryland lived in family households.

In 1776, Frederick County was divided into three parts. The westernmost portion became Washington County, named after George Washington, the southernmost portion became Montgomery County, named after another Revolutionary War general, Richard Montgomery. The northern portion remained Frederick County.
