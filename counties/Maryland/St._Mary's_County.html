Household Populations

St. Mary's County, Maryland

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.3881793%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 10719 people in St. Mary's County, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 72710 people in St. Mary's County, Maryland lived in family households.

In 2000, 2782 people in St. Mary's County, Maryland lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 86211 people in St. Mary's County, Maryland lived in the county.

In 2010, 144268 people in Montgomery County, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 818609 people in Montgomery County, Maryland lived in family households.

In 2010, 8900 people in Montgomery County, Maryland lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 971777 people in Montgomery County, Maryland lived in the county.

The settlement of Lord Baltimore's Maryland began with the arrival of passengers from England at St. Clement's Island in the Potomac River in what is now southwestern St. Mary's County on March 25, 1634 (the anniversary of this landing being annually celebrated as Maryland Day). The passengers arrived in two vessels, the ''Ark'' and the ''Dove,'' which had set sail from the Isle of Wight on November 22, 1633. The county is also home to the first Catholic Mass celebrated in one of the original thirteen colonies -after they'd become English colonies (masses were said in 1526-7 at San Miguel de Gualdape).
