Household Populations

Baltimore city, Maryland

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.832056%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 138641 people in Baltimore city, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 486760 people in Baltimore city, Maryland lived in family households.

In 2000, 25753 people in Baltimore city, Maryland lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 651154 people in Baltimore city, Maryland lived in the county.

In 2010, 22347 people in Washington County, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 116658 people in Washington County, Maryland lived in family households.

In 2010, 8425 people in Washington County, Maryland lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 147430 people in Washington County, Maryland lived in the county.

The city is named after Cecil Calvert, second Lord Baltimore, (1605–1675), a member of the Irish House of Lords and the founding proprietor of the Colony and Province of Maryland. Baltimore is an anglicization of the Irish name ''Baile an Tí Mhóir'', meaning "town of the big house", which was the name of the estate in County Longford on which the Calvert family lived, in Ireland.
