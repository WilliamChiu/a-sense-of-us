Garrett County, Maryland

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.1319757%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 3663 people in Garrett County, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 25566 people in Garrett County, Maryland lived in family households.

In 2000, 617 people in Garrett County, Maryland lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 29846 people in Garrett County, Maryland lived in the county.

In 2010, 15928 people in Charles County, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 129218 people in Charles County, Maryland lived in family households.

In 2010, 1405 people in Charles County, Maryland lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 146551 people in Charles County, Maryland lived in the county.

In the early 20th century, the railroad and tourism started to decline. Coal mining and timber production continued at a much slower pace. Today, tourism has made a dramatic rebound in the county with logging and farming making up the greatest part of the economic base. Due to a cold climate and lack of any large city, Garrett County has remained a sparsely populated rural area.
