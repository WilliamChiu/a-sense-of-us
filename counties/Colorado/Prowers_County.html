Prowers County, Colorado

In 2000, 1904 people in Prowers County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 12279 people in Prowers County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2010, 2939 people in Park County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 13175 people in Park County, Colorado lived in family households.

Prowers County is one of the 64 counties in the U.S. state of Colorado. As of the 2010 census, the population was 12,551. The county seat is Lamar. The county is named in honor of John W. Prowers, a leading pioneer in the lower Arkansas valley region.
