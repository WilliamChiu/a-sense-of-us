Washington County, Colorado

In 2000, 649 people in Washington County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 4238 people in Washington County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2010, 468 people in Sedgwick County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1876 people in Sedgwick County, Colorado lived in family households.

Washington County is one of the 64 counties of the U.S. state of Colorado. As of the 2010 census, the population was 4,814. The county seat is Akron. The county was named in honor of the United States President George Washington.
