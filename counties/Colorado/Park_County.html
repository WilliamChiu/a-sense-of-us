Park County, Colorado

In 2000, 2157 people in Park County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 12296 people in Park County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2010, 3732 people in Morgan County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 23863 people in Morgan County, Colorado lived in family households.

Park County is one of the 64 counties in the U.S. state of Colorado. As of the 2010 census, the population was 16,206. The county seat is Fairplay. The county was named after the large geographic region known as South Park, which was named by early fur traders and trappers in the area.
