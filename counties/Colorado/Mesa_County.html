Mesa County, Colorado

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.7868867%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 17845 people in Mesa County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 95126 people in Mesa County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2000, 3284 people in Mesa County, Colorado lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 116255 people in Mesa County, Colorado lived in the county.

In 2010, 2731 people in Las Animas County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 11918 people in Las Animas County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2010, 858 people in Las Animas County, Colorado lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 15507 people in Las Animas County, Colorado lived in the county.

Mesa County is one of the 64 counties of the U.S. state of Colorado. As of the 2010 census, the population was 146,723. The county seat is Grand Junction. The county was named for the many large mesas in the area, including Grand Mesa.
