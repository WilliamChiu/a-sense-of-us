Kit Carson County, Colorado

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 6.9452763%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1028 people in Kit Carson County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6452 people in Kit Carson County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2000, 531 people in Kit Carson County, Colorado lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 8011 people in Kit Carson County, Colorado lived in the county.

In 2010, 295 people in Jackson County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1097 people in Jackson County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2010, 2 people in Jackson County, Colorado lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 1394 people in Jackson County, Colorado lived in the county.

Kit Carson County is one of the 64 counties in the U.S. state of Colorado. As of the 2010 census, the population was 8,270. The county seat is Burlington. The county was established in 1889 and named for American frontiersman and Indian fighter Kit Carson.
