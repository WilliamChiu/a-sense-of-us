Phillips County, Colorado

In 2000, 608 people in Phillips County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 3783 people in Phillips County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2010, 3085 people in Otero County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 15313 people in Otero County, Colorado lived in family households.

Phillips County is one of the 64 counties in the U.S. state of Colorado. As of the 2010 census, the population was 4,442. The county seat is Holyoke. The county was named in honor of R.O. Phillips, a secretary of the Lincoln Land Company, who organized several towns in Colorado.
