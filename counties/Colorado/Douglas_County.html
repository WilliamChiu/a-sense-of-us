Douglas County, Colorado

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.0438552%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 14609 people in Douglas County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 160691 people in Douglas County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2000, 466 people in Douglas County, Colorado lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 175766 people in Douglas County, Colorado lived in the county.

In 2010, 4723 people in Delta County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 25516 people in Delta County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2010, 713 people in Delta County, Colorado lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 30952 people in Delta County, Colorado lived in the county.

Douglas County was one of the original 17 counties created in the Colorado Territory by the Colorado Territorial Legislature on November 1, 1861. The county was named in honor of U.S. Senator Stephen A. Douglas of Illinois, who died five months before the county was created. The county seat was originally Franktown, but was moved to California Ranch in 1863, and then to Castle Rock in 1874. Although the county's boundaries originally extended eastward to the Kansas state border, in 1874 most of the eastern portion of the county became part of Elbert County.
