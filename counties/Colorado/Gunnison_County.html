Household Populations

Gunnison County, Colorado

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 5.8255553%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4359 people in Gunnison County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 8622 people in Gunnison County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2000, 975 people in Gunnison County, Colorado lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 13956 people in Gunnison County, Colorado lived in the county.

In 2010, 8365 people in Garfield County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 47140 people in Garfield County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2010, 884 people in Garfield County, Colorado lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 56389 people in Garfield County, Colorado lived in the county.

Archeological studies have dated the Ute people's appearance in the Uncompahgre region of Colorado as early as 1150 A.D. Possibilities exist that they are descendents of an earlier people living in the area as far back as 1500 B.C. They were a nomadic race of dark skin moving about the Western Slope of Colorado in the various parts of the year. In the early to mid-1600s the Spaniards of New Mexico introduced the horse which changed their patterns of hunting taking them across the divide to the eastern slopes and into conflict with the Plains Indians which soon became their bitter enemies.
