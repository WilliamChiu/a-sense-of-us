San Miguel County, Colorado

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.1701941%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2462 people in San Miguel County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 4115 people in San Miguel County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2000, 17 people in San Miguel County, Colorado lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 6594 people in San Miguel County, Colorado lived in the county.

In 2010, 5605 people in Routt County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 17569 people in Routt County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2010, 335 people in Routt County, Colorado lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 23509 people in Routt County, Colorado lived in the county.

San Miguel County was given the Spanish language name for "Saint Michael" due to the nearby San Miguel River. On 27 February 1883, Ouray County was split to form San Miguel County. Originally the San Miguel County portion was to retain the name Ouray County with the new portion called Uncompahgre County.
