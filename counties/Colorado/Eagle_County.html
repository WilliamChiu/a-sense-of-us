Eagle County, Colorado

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.8309593%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 10923 people in Eagle County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 30383 people in Eagle County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2000, 353 people in Eagle County, Colorado lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 41659 people in Eagle County, Colorado lived in the county.

In 2010, 176323 people in Denver County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 407854 people in Denver County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2010, 15981 people in Denver County, Colorado lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 600158 people in Denver County, Colorado lived in the county.

Eagle County was created by the Colorado legislature on February 11, 1883, from portions of Summit County. It was named after the Eagle River, which runs through the county. The county seat was originally set in Red Cliff, Colorado, but was moved to the town of Eagle in 1921.
