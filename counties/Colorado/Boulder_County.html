Boulder County, Colorado

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.7806182%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 68816 people in Boulder County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 213959 people in Boulder County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2000, 8513 people in Boulder County, Colorado lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 291288 people in Boulder County, Colorado lived in the county.

In 2010, 705 people in Baca County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 3001 people in Baca County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2010, 82 people in Baca County, Colorado lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 3788 people in Baca County, Colorado lived in the county.

Boulder County was one of the original 17 counties created by the Territory of Colorado on November 1, 1861. The county was named for Boulder City and Boulder Creek, so named because of the abundance of boulders in the area. Boulder County retains essentially the same borders as in 1861, although a 27.5 sqmi of its southeastern corner and its approximate population of 40,000 became part of the City and County of Broomfield in 2001.
