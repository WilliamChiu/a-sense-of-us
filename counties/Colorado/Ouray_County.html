Ouray County, Colorado

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.8361101%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 554 people in Ouray County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 3170 people in Ouray County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2000, 18 people in Ouray County, Colorado lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 3742 people in Ouray County, Colorado lived in the county.

In 2010, 5942 people in Montrose County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 34792 people in Montrose County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2010, 542 people in Montrose County, Colorado lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 41276 people in Montrose County, Colorado lived in the county.

Ouray County was formed out of San Juan County on 18 January 1877, the first county designated by the newly formed Colorado State Legislature. It was named for Chief Ouray, a distinguished Ute Indian chief. Ouray was designanted county seat on 8 March 1877. On 19 February 1881, Dolores County was formed out of Ouray County.
