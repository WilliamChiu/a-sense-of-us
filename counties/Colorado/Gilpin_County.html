Gilpin County, Colorado

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.5845528%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1077 people in Gilpin County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 3656 people in Gilpin County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2000, 24 people in Gilpin County, Colorado lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 4757 people in Gilpin County, Colorado lived in the county.

In 2010, 93403 people in El Paso County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 509719 people in El Paso County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2010, 19141 people in El Paso County, Colorado lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 622263 people in El Paso County, Colorado lived in the county.

Gilpin County is the second-least extensive of the 64 counties of the U.S. state of Colorado, just behind Denver itself. As of the 2010 census, the population was 5,441. The county seat is Central City. The county was formed in 1861, while Colorado was still a Territory, and was named after Colonel William Gilpin, the first Governor of the Territory of Colorado.
