Cheyenne County, Colorado

In 2000, 303 people in Cheyenne County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 1898 people in Cheyenne County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2010, 73565 people in Boulder County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 212053 people in Boulder County, Colorado lived in family households.

Cheyenne County was created with its present borders by the Colorado State Legislature on March 25, 1889, out of portions of northeastern Bent County and southeastern Elbert County. It was named after the Cheyenne Indians who occupied eastern Colorado.
