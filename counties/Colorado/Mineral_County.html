Mineral County, Colorado

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -18.74253%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 147 people in Mineral County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 683 people in Mineral County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2000, 1 people in Mineral County, Colorado lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 831 people in Mineral County, Colorado lived in the county.

In 2010, 840 people in Lincoln County, Colorado lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 3597 people in Lincoln County, Colorado lived in family households.

In 2010, 1030 people in Lincoln County, Colorado lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 5467 people in Lincoln County, Colorado lived in the county.

Mineral County is one of the 64 counties in the U.S. state of Colorado. As of the 2010 census, the population was 712, making it the second-least populous county in Colorado. The county seat and only incorporated municipality in the county is Creede. The county was named for the many valuable minerals found in the mountains and streams of the area.
