Aleutians East Borough, Alaska

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -14.0749035%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 226 people in Aleutians East Borough, Alaska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 1188 people in Aleutians East Borough, Alaska lived in family households.

In 2000, 1283 people in Aleutians East Borough, Alaska lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 2697 people in Aleutians East Borough, Alaska lived in the county.

In 2010, 243 people in Aleutians East Borough, Alaska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1172 people in Aleutians East Borough, Alaska lived in family households.

In 2010, 1726 people in Aleutians East Borough, Alaska lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 3141 people in Aleutians East Borough, Alaska lived in the county.

According to archaeological evidence, the area has been inhabited by the Unanga since the last ice age. Early contact was with Russian fur traders who sought sea otters in these islands. Whaling, fishing and cannery operations brought an influx of Scandinavian and European fishermen in the early 1900s. During World War II, the area was a strategic military site for the Aleutian Campaign, and many locals were evacuated to Ketchikan.
