Sitka City and Borough, Alaska

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.0016074%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1372 people in Sitka City and Borough, Alaska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 7192 people in Sitka City and Borough, Alaska lived in family households.

In 2000, 271 people in Sitka City and Borough, Alaska lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 8835 people in Sitka City and Borough, Alaska lived in the county.

In 2010, 694 people in Petersburg Census Area, Alaska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 3078 people in Petersburg Census Area, Alaska lived in family households.

In 2010, 43 people in Petersburg Census Area, Alaska lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 3815 people in Petersburg Census Area, Alaska lived in the county.

Sitka's location was originally settled by the Tlingit people over 10,000 years ago. The Russians settled Old Sitka in 1799, calling it Redoubt Saint Michael (, t ''''). The governor of Russian America, Alexander Baranov, arrived under the auspices of the Russian-American Company, a colonial trading company chartered by tsar Paul I. In 1802, Tlingit warriors "clad in animal-headed helmets and armour" destroyed the original establishment, killing 24 Russians and 200 Aleuts, enslaving the rest, with only a few managing to escape. Baranov was forced to levy 10,000 rubles in ransom for the safe return of the surviving settlers.
