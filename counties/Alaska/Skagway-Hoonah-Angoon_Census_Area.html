Skagway-Hoonah-Angoon Census Area, Alaska

In 2000, 616 people in Skagway-Hoonah-Angoon Census Area, Alaska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 2800 people in Skagway-Hoonah-Angoon Census Area, Alaska lived in family households.

In 2010, 956 people in Prince of Wales-Hyder Census Area, Alaska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 4553 people in Prince of Wales-Hyder Census Area, Alaska lived in family households.

The census area was significantly larger in the 1990 census, at which time it was the Skagway-Yakutat-Angoon Census Area. After Yakutat was incorporated as a unified city-borough on September 22, 1992, it was renamed Skagway-Hoonah-Angoon Census Area; When Skagway followed suit on June 20, 2007, the census area assumed its current name.
