Yakutat City and Borough, Alaska

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 17.64214%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 134 people in Yakutat City and Borough, Alaska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 552 people in Yakutat City and Borough, Alaska lived in family households.

In 2000, 122 people in Yakutat City and Borough, Alaska lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 808 people in Yakutat City and Borough, Alaska lived in the county.

In 2010, 400 people in Wade Hampton Census Area, Alaska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7050 people in Wade Hampton Census Area, Alaska lived in family households.

In 2010, 9 people in Wade Hampton Census Area, Alaska lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 7459 people in Wade Hampton Census Area, Alaska lived in the county.

The original settlers in the Yakutat area are believed to have been Eyak-speaking people from the Copper River area. Tlingits migrated into the area and assimilated the Eyaks before the arrival of Europeans in Alaska. Yakutat was only one of a number of Tlingit and mixed Tlingit-Eyak settlements in the region, although all the others have been depopulated or abandoned.
