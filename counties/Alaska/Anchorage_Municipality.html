Anchorage Municipality, Alaska

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.2063632%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 41681 people in Anchorage Municipality, Alaska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 211588 people in Anchorage Municipality, Alaska lived in family households.

In 2000, 7014 people in Anchorage Municipality, Alaska lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 260283 people in Anchorage Municipality, Alaska lived in the county.

In 2010, 49834 people in Anchorage Municipality, Alaska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 233542 people in Anchorage Municipality, Alaska lived in family households.

In 2010, 8450 people in Anchorage Municipality, Alaska lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 291826 people in Anchorage Municipality, Alaska lived in the county.

Russian presence in south central Alaska was well established in the 19th century. In 1867, U. S. Secretary of State William H. Seward brokered a deal to purchase Alaska from Imperial Russia for $7.2 million (about two cents an acre). His political rivals lampooned the deal as "Seward's folly", "Seward's icebox" and "Walrussia". By 1888, gold was discovered along Turnagain Arm.
