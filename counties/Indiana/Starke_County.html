Starke County, Indiana

In 2000, 2740 people in Starke County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 20506 people in Starke County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 3366 people in Scott County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 20503 people in Scott County, Indiana lived in family households.

Starke County was created in 1835 and organized in 1850. It was named for Gen. John Stark, who commanded New Hampshire troops at the Battle of Bunker Hill in 1775 in the Revolutionary War and defeated the British at the Battle of Bennington in 1777.
