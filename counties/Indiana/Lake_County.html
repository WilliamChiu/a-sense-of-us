Lake County, Indiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -5.78267%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 64396 people in Lake County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 414520 people in Lake County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 5648 people in Lake County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 484564 people in Lake County, Indiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 6778 people in Knox County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 29017 people in Knox County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 2645 people in Knox County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 38440 people in Knox County, Indiana lived in the county.

Lake County was formed in 1837. From 1832 to 1836 the area that was to become Lake County was part of La Porte County. From 1836 to 1837 it was part of Porter County. It was named for its location on Lake Michigan. The original county seat was Liverpool until Lake Court House, which later became Crown Point was chosen in 1840.
