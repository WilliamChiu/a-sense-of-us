Cass County, Indiana

In 2000, 5938 people in Cass County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 33830 people in Cass County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 6725 people in Boone County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 49341 people in Boone County, Indiana lived in family households.

Cass County was formed in 1828. It is named for Gen. Lewis Cass, Governor of Michigan Territory through 1831. Cass was U.S. Secretary of State just prior to the outbreak of the Civil War.
