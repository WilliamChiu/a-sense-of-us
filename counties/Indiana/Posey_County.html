Posey County, Indiana

In 2000, 3033 people in Posey County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 23756 people in Posey County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 2846 people in Perry County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 14960 people in Perry County, Indiana lived in family households.

Posey County was formed on November 11, 1814 from Gibson and Warrick counties. It was named for Revolutionary War Gen. Thomas Posey, who was then serving as Governor of the Indiana Territory. Mount Vernon became the county seat in 1825.
