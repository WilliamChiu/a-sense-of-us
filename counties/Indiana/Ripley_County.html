Ripley County, Indiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.11063814%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2997 people in Ripley County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 23173 people in Ripley County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 353 people in Ripley County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 26523 people in Ripley County, Indiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 1864 people in Pulaski County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 11345 people in Pulaski County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 193 people in Pulaski County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 13402 people in Pulaski County, Indiana lived in the county.

Ripley County was formed in 1816. It was named for Gen. Eleazer Wheelock Ripley, an officer in the War of 1812, who was mainly remembered for the Battle of Lundy's Lane and the Siege of Fort Erie, in 1814.
