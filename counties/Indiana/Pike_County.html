Pike County, Indiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.43257475%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1658 people in Pike County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 11007 people in Pike County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 172 people in Pike County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 12837 people in Pike County, Indiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 3045 people in Owen County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 18333 people in Owen County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 197 people in Owen County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 21575 people in Owen County, Indiana lived in the county.

Formed on December 21, 1816, Pike County was the first county formed after Indiana became a state. It was formed from Gibson and Perry counties, and was named for Zebulon Pike, famous for his Pike Expedition of 1806 - 1807, exploring the southwest portion of the Louisiana Purchase. Pike commanded the 4th Infantry Regiment at the Battle of Tippecanoe in 1811. He was killed as a brigadier general during the War of 1812 after his men had captured York (now Toronto), Canada.
