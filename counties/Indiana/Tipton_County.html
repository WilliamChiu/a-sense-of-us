Tipton County, Indiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -9.604752%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2030 people in Tipton County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14346 people in Tipton County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 201 people in Tipton County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 16577 people in Tipton County, Indiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 2779 people in Sullivan County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 16398 people in Sullivan County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 2298 people in Sullivan County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 21475 people in Sullivan County, Indiana lived in the county.

Prior to the arrival of non-indigenous settlers in the 1830s, the area now known as Tipton County was inhabited by the Miami and Delaware tribes. Tipton County was formed in 1844. It was named for John Tipton, a soldier of the Battle of Tippecanoe in 1811. Tipton served as United States Senator for Indiana from 1831 until shortly before his death in 1839.
