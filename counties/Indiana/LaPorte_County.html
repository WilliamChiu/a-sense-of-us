LaPorte County, Indiana

In 2000, 15085 people in LaPorte County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 88531 people in LaPorte County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 10327 people in Kosciusko County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 65463 people in Kosciusko County, Indiana lived in family households.

LaPorte County was formed in 1832. ''La porte'' means "the door" or "the port" in French. French travelers or explorers so named the area after discovering a natural opening in the dense forests that used to exist in this region, providing a gateway to lands further west.
