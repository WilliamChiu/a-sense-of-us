Johnson County, Indiana

In 2000, 13121 people in Johnson County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 98485 people in Johnson County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 3009 people in Jay County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 18001 people in Jay County, Indiana lived in family households.

Johnson County was formed in 1823. It was named for John Johnson, a judge of the Indiana Supreme Court. This is probably John Johnson of Knox County, delegate to the State constitutional convention, appointed to the Supreme Court in 1816.
