Household Populations

Johnson County, Indiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.0480394%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 13121 people in Johnson County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 98485 people in Johnson County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 3603 people in Johnson County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 115209 people in Johnson County, Indiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 3009 people in Jay County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 18001 people in Jay County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 243 people in Jay County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 21253 people in Jay County, Indiana lived in the county.

Johnson County was formed in 1823. It was named for John Johnson, a judge of the Indiana Supreme Court. This is probably John Johnson of Knox County, delegate to the State constitutional convention, appointed to the Supreme Court in 1816.
