DeKalb County, Indiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.7169306%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 5128 people in DeKalb County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 34703 people in DeKalb County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 454 people in DeKalb County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 40285 people in DeKalb County, Indiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 3780 people in Daviess County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 27287 people in Daviess County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 581 people in Daviess County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 31648 people in Daviess County, Indiana lived in the county.

DeKalb County was formed in 1837. It was named for the heroic General Johann de Kalb, a Continental Army officer from Bavaria, who was fatally wounded at the Battle of Camden, South Carolina. The original settlers to arrive in DeKalb County were migrants from New England who were settling the wilderness of what was then known as the Northwest Territory. These people were "Yankee" migrants, that is to say they were descended from the English Puritans who settled New England in the colonial era. In the 1870s immigrants from Ireland and Germany began arriving in DeKalb County, in large numbers.
