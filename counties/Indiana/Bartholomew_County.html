Bartholomew County, Indiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.3984323%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 9426 people in Bartholomew County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 61109 people in Bartholomew County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 900 people in Bartholomew County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 71435 people in Bartholomew County, Indiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 4220 people in Woodford County, Illinois lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 33423 people in Woodford County, Illinois lived in family households.

In 2010, 1021 people in Woodford County, Illinois lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 38664 people in Woodford County, Illinois lived in the county.

Bartholomew County was formed February 12, 1821 and was named for Lt. Col. Joseph Bartholomew, wounded at the Battle of Tippecanoe.
