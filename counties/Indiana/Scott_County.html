Scott County, Indiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.6580889%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2778 people in Scott County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 19974 people in Scott County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 208 people in Scott County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 22960 people in Scott County, Indiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 3424 people in Ripley County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 24945 people in Ripley County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 449 people in Ripley County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 28818 people in Ripley County, Indiana lived in the county.

Scott County was formed in 1820 from portions of Clark, Jackson, Jefferson, Jennings, and Washington counties. It was named for Gen. Charles Scott, who was Governor of Kentucky from 1808 to 1812. Much of its water supply comes from Hardy Lake.
