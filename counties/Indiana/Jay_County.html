Jay County, Indiana

In 2000, 2773 people in Jay County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 18806 people in Jay County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 5054 people in Huntington County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 30740 people in Huntington County, Indiana lived in family households.

Jay County was formed in 1836. It is the only county in the United States named for John Jay, co-author of ''The Federalist'' Papers, Secretary of Foreign Affairs under the Articles of Confederation, and first Chief Justice of the United States. John Jay died in 1829.
