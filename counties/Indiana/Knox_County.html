Knox County, Indiana

In 2000, 6475 people in Knox County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 30285 people in Knox County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 5129 people in Jefferson County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 25394 people in Jefferson County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 1790, Winthrop Sargent, Secretary of Northwest Territory, organized the first Indiana county, Knox County. It was named for Major General Henry Knox, U.S. Secretary of War.
