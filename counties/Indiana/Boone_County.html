Boone County, Indiana

In 2000, 5095 people in Boone County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 40183 people in Boone County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 11012 people in Bartholomew County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 64635 people in Bartholomew County, Indiana lived in family households.

Boone County was formed April 1, 1830, and named for frontiersman Daniel Boone. The county commissioners met near the center of the county on May 1, 1831 to identify a county seat, which by law had to be within 2 mi of the county's center; the city of Lebanon was selected to serve this purpose.
