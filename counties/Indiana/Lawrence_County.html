Lawrence County, Indiana

In 2000, 6245 people in Lawrence County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 38960 people in Lawrence County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 2962 people in LaGrange County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 33842 people in LaGrange County, Indiana lived in family households.

Lawrence County was formed in 1818 from land given by Orange County. It was named for Capt. James Lawrence, who uttered the famous words "Don't give up the ship" after being mortally wounded during the War of 1812.
