Decatur County, Indiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.63022375%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2996 people in Decatur County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 21264 people in Decatur County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 295 people in Decatur County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 24555 people in Decatur County, Indiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 1565 people in Crawford County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 9086 people in Crawford County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 62 people in Crawford County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 10713 people in Crawford County, Indiana lived in the county.

Decatur County was formed in 1822. It was named for Commodore Stephen Decatur, Jr., naval officer in the First and Second Barbary Wars, and in the War of 1812. Decatur was mortally wounded in a duel in 1820.
