Randolph County, Indiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.20329952%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 23442 people in Randolph County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 311 people in Randolph County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 27401 people in Randolph County, Indiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 3154 people in Posey County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 22514 people in Posey County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 242 people in Posey County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 25910 people in Posey County, Indiana lived in the county.

The Indiana General Assembly authorized the formation of Randolph County from Wayne County in January 1818 to take effect in August 1818. The county was almost certainly named for Randolph County, North Carolina, where the area's first settlers came from. That county was named for Peyton Randolph, the first President of the Continental Congress under the Articles of Confederation.
