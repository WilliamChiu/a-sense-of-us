Newton County, Indiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -9.676027%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1642 people in Newton County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 12734 people in Newton County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 190 people in Newton County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 14566 people in Newton County, Indiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 42894 people in Monroe County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 80104 people in Monroe County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 14976 people in Monroe County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 137974 people in Monroe County, Indiana lived in the county.

The original Newton County was formed by statute on February 7, 1835, and was a roughly square area some 30 miles on a side, encompassing what is now the northern half of the county, the northern half of Jasper County, and a large section to the north. The northern border was cut back to the Kankakee River on February 1, 1836, with all land north of the Kankakee River going to Lake and Porter counties. The county was abolished and combined with Jasper County in 1839. On December 8, 1859, the county was re-created and the borders were redrawn to essentially their current state.
