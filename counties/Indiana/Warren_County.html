Warren County, Indiana

In 2000, 943 people in Warren County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 7363 people in Warren County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 2447 people in Vermillion County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 13556 people in Vermillion County, Indiana lived in family households.

In the centuries before the arrival of European settlers, the area that became Warren County was on the boundary between the Miami and Kickapoo tribes. By the late 18th century, many Miami had moved further south; most of Indiana north of the Wabash was then occupied by the Potawatomi people. The first non-indigenous settler in the area was probably Zachariah Cicott, a French-Canadian who first traded with the Kickapoo and Potawatomi people around 1802. When General William Henry Harrison took an army from Vincennes to the Battle of Tippecanoe in late 1811, Cicott served as a scout; the trail taken by Harrison's army passed through the area that later became Warren County on its way to and from the battle site in Tippecanoe County.
