Dearborn County, Indiana

In 2000, 4947 people in Dearborn County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 40649 people in Dearborn County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 4026 people in Clinton County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 28368 people in Clinton County, Indiana lived in family households.

Dearborn County was formed in 1803. It was named for Dr. Henry Dearborn (DR). Dearborn was U.S. Secretary of War at the time the County was named. Early growth was centered around Lawrenceburg which was an important railroad junction connecting two of the regions major rail lines.
