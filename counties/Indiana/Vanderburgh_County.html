Vanderburgh County, Indiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -4.4242563%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 31738 people in Vanderburgh County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 133085 people in Vanderburgh County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 7099 people in Vanderburgh County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 171922 people in Vanderburgh County, Indiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 42959 people in Tippecanoe County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 115358 people in Tippecanoe County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 14463 people in Tippecanoe County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 172780 people in Tippecanoe County, Indiana lived in the county.

Vanderburgh County was formed on January 7, 1818, from Gibson, Posey, and Warrick counties. It was named for Captain Henry Vanderburgh, Revolutionary War veteran and judge for the Indiana Territory.
