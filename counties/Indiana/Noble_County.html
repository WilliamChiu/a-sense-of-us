Noble County, Indiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.6647995%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 5488 people in Noble County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 40125 people in Noble County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 662 people in Noble County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 46275 people in Noble County, Indiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 5609 people in Montgomery County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 31344 people in Montgomery County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 1171 people in Montgomery County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 38124 people in Montgomery County, Indiana lived in the county.

Noble County was formed in 1836. It was named for Governor Noah Noble. The original settlers in Noble County were settlers from New England known as "Yankee's", these were people descended from the English Puritans who settled New England in the 1600s. They were part of a wave of New Englanders who migrated west to what was then the Northwest Territory during the early 1800s. This migration was sparked as a result of the completion of the Erie Canal and conclusion of the Black Hawk War. They founded the towns of Kendallville and Albion.
