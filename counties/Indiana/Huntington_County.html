Huntington County, Indiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.515913%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4770 people in Huntington County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 31870 people in Huntington County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 1435 people in Huntington County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 38075 people in Huntington County, Indiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 15516 people in Hendricks County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 126572 people in Hendricks County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 3360 people in Hendricks County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 145448 people in Hendricks County, Indiana lived in the county.

Huntington County was formed in 1832. It was named for Samuel Huntington, who signed the Declaration of Independence and the Articles of Confederation. He was also President of the Continental Congress under the Articles of Confederation.
