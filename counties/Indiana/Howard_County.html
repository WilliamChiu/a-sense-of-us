Howard County, Indiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.1932621%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 12991 people in Howard County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 70816 people in Howard County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 1157 people in Howard County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 84964 people in Howard County, Indiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 5043 people in Harrison County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 33860 people in Harrison County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 461 people in Harrison County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 39364 people in Harrison County, Indiana lived in the county.

This county was organized in 1844. It was first known as Richardville County. Its first name honored Jean Baptiste Richardville, a chief of the Miami. Richardville's name was ''Pe-che-wa'', which translates to “Wildcat”, hence Wildcat Creek.
