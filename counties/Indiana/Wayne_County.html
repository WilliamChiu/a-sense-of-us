Wayne County, Indiana

In 2000, 10960 people in Wayne County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 57892 people in Wayne County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 1119 people in Warren County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7305 people in Warren County, Indiana lived in family households.

Wayne County was formed in 1811. It was named for Gen. "Mad" Anthony Wayne, who was an officer during the Revolutionary War. Wayne is mainly remembered for his service in the 1790s in the Northwest Indian War, which included many actions in Indiana and Ohio.
