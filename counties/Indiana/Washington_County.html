Washington County, Indiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -4.419017%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 3220 people in Washington County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 23669 people in Washington County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 334 people in Washington County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 27223 people in Washington County, Indiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 4797 people in Wabash County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 26252 people in Wabash County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 1839 people in Wabash County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 32888 people in Wabash County, Indiana lived in the county.

As early as 1802, a man named Frederick Royce lived among the Ox Indians at a place known as the Lick, two miles east of Salem and is probably the first white man to inhabit this county. He was a hunter-trader and salt manufacturer. In 1803, Thomas Hopper was the first to settle in this county near Hardinsburg.
