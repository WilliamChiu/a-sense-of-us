Household Populations

Floyd County, Indiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.6746888%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 9477 people in Floyd County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 60307 people in Floyd County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 1039 people in Floyd County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 70823 people in Floyd County, Indiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 5518 people in Dubois County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 35478 people in Dubois County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 893 people in Dubois County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 41889 people in Dubois County, Indiana lived in the county.

Floyd County, originally the Shawnee Indians hunting ground, was conquered for the United States by George Rogers Clark during the American Revolutionary War from the British. He was awarded large tracts of land in Indiana, including almost all of present day Floyd County. Clark sold land to the settlers who began arriving as soon as peace returned.
