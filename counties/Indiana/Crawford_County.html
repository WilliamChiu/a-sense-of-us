Crawford County, Indiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.43834448%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1356 people in Crawford County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 9286 people in Crawford County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 101 people in Crawford County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 10743 people in Crawford County, Indiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 18056 people in Clark County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 90661 people in Clark County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 1515 people in Clark County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 110232 people in Clark County, Indiana lived in the county.

Crawford County was formed in 1818. Some say it was named for William H. Crawford, who was U.S. Treasury Secretary in 1818. Others say it was named for Col. William Crawford, who fought in the French and Indian War and Revolutionary War, and who was burned and scalped by Indians in 1782 in what is now Wyandot County, Ohio. The county seat was in Leavenworth for several decades but eventually moved to English.
