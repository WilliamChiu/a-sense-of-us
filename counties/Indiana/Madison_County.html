Madison County, Indiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.8266191%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 20009 people in Madison County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 108023 people in Madison County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 5326 people in Madison County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 133358 people in Madison County, Indiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 71215 people in Lake County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 418441 people in Lake County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 6349 people in Lake County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 496005 people in Lake County, Indiana lived in the county.

Madison County was formed in 1823. It was named for James Madison, co-author of ''The Federalist'' Papers and the fourth President of the United States (1809 to 1817).
