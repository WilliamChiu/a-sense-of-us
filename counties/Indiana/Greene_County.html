Greene County, Indiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.5812168%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4653 people in Greene County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 27958 people in Greene County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 546 people in Greene County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 33157 people in Greene County, Indiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 2956 people in Fulton County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 17656 people in Fulton County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 224 people in Fulton County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 20836 people in Fulton County, Indiana lived in the county.

Greene County was formed in 1821. It was named for Gen. Nathanael Greene, who commanded the southern theater in the Revolutionary War, eventually forcing the British army under Charles Cornwallis to retreat to Yorktown. There it was forced to surrender, ensuring American independence.
