Monroe County, Indiana

In 2000, 33419 people in Monroe County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 72813 people in Monroe County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 5824 people in Marshall County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 40557 people in Marshall County, Indiana lived in family households.

Monroe County was formed in 1818 from portions of Orange County. It was named for James Monroe, fifth President of the United States, who was serving at the time the county was organized.
