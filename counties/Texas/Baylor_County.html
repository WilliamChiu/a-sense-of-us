Baylor County, Texas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.28566718%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

Baylor County is a county located in the U.S. state of Texas. As of the 2010 census, its population was 3,726. Its county seat is Seymour. The county is named for Henry Weidner Baylor, a surgeon in the Texas Rangers during the Mexican-American War.
