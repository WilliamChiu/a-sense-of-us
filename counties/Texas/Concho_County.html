Concho County, Texas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 50.37292%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 320 people in Concho County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 2276 people in Concho County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2000, 1370 people in Concho County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 3966 people in Concho County, Texas lived in the county.

In 2010, 2804 people in Colorado County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 17742 people in Colorado County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 328 people in Colorado County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 20874 people in Colorado County, Texas lived in the county.

*8000 BC – 1800’s Paleo-Indians in the county leave behind archaeological remains of a burned-rock midden. Athabascan-speaking Indians associated with the prehorse Plains culture live in this part of Texas. Later native inhabitants include Jumano, Tonkawa, Comanche and Lipan Apache.
