Denton County, Texas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -13.68965%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 63281 people in Denton County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 360545 people in Denton County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2000, 9150 people in Denton County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 432976 people in Denton County, Texas lived in the county.

In 2010, 1435 people in Dawson County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 10252 people in Dawson County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 2146 people in Dawson County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 13833 people in Dawson County, Texas lived in the county.

Before the arrival of white settlers, various Native American peoples, including the Kichai and the Lenape, infrequently populated the area. The area was settled by Peters Colony landowners in the early 1840s. Until the annexation of Texas, the area was considered part of Fannin County. On April 11, 1846, the First Texas Legislature established Denton County. The county was named for John B. Denton, who was killed while raiding a Native American village in Tarrant County in 1841. Originally, the county seat was set at Pickneyville. This was later changed to Alton, where the Old Alton Bridge currently stands, and then moved finally to Denton.
