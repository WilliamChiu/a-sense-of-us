Duval County, Texas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -8.830553%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1205 people in Duval County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 11331 people in Duval County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2000, 584 people in Duval County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 13120 people in Duval County, Texas lived in the county.

In 2010, 361 people in Dickens County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1768 people in Dickens County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 315 people in Dickens County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 2444 people in Dickens County, Texas lived in the county.

Even though Duval County lies in the United States, it has long been Mexican in character. A Mexican first surveyed it in 1804, Jose Contrerras, surveyor general of San Luis Potosi. Luis Muniz was born there in 1828, the county's first recorded birth. The important colonists came from Mier, Tamaulipas - and Anglos later respected their descendants as the old Mexican families.
