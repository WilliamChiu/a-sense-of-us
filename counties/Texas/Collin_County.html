Collin County, Texas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.8291175%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 61604 people in Collin County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 426739 people in Collin County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2000, 3332 people in Collin County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 491675 people in Collin County, Texas lived in the county.

In 2010, 342 people in Cochran County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 2707 people in Cochran County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 78 people in Cochran County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 3127 people in Cochran County, Texas lived in the county.

Both the county and the county seat were named after Collin McKinney (1766-1861), one of the five men who drafted the Texas Declaration of Independence and the oldest of the 59 men who signed it.
