Knox County, Texas

In 2000, 557 people in Knox County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 3564 people in Knox County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 27 people in King County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

Knox County is a county located in the U.S. state of Texas. As of the 2010 census, its population was 3,719. Its county seat is Benjamin. The county is named for Henry Knox, an American Revolutionary War general.
