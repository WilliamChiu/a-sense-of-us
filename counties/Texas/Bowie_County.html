Bowie County, Texas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 7.1215153%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 10982 people in Bowie County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 71545 people in Bowie County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2000, 6779 people in Bowie County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 89306 people in Bowie County, Texas lived in the county.

In 2010, 1506 people in Blanco County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 8885 people in Blanco County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 106 people in Blanco County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 10497 people in Blanco County, Texas lived in the county.

The farming Caddoan Mississippian culture dates as early as the Late Archaic Period 1500 BCE in Bowie County. The Hernando de Soto expedition of 1541 resulted in violent encounters. Spanish and French missionaries brought a smallpox, measles malaria and influenza epidemics against which the Caddo had no immunity. Eventually, these issues and problems with the Osage, forced the Caddo to abandon their reservations. Settlers had peaceful relations with the 19th Century Shawnee, Delaware, and Kickapoo in the area.
