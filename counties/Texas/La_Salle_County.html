La Salle County, Texas

In 2000, 518 people in La Salle County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 4745 people in La Salle County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 7206 people in Lamar County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 41874 people in Lamar County, Texas lived in family households.

The area of present-day La Salle County was occupied by the Coahuiltecan Indians until the 18th century, when they were squeezed out by the Spanish from the south and the Apache from the north. After the Mexican War of Independence, the Mexican government used land grants to encouraged settlement, but very few settled in the area. By 1836, the area was entirely populated by Indians.
