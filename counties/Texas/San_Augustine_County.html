San Augustine County, Texas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.1721134%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1174 people in San Augustine County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 7505 people in San Augustine County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2000, 267 people in San Augustine County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 8946 people in San Augustine County, Texas lived in the county.

In 2010, 1391 people in Runnels County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 8916 people in Runnels County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 194 people in Runnels County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 10501 people in Runnels County, Texas lived in the county.

San Augustine County was formed in 1837. It was supposedly named after the Saint, Augustine of Hippo. However, it seems plausible that the county was named for the town of San Augustine, which had been established five years earlier and whose name was based upon an 18th-century Spanish presidio (fortress).
