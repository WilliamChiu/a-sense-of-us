Wise County, Texas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.1851668%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4406 people in Wise County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 43234 people in Wise County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2000, 1153 people in Wise County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 48793 people in Wise County, Texas lived in the county.

In 2010, 51941 people in Williamson County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 365641 people in Williamson County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 5097 people in Williamson County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 422679 people in Williamson County, Texas lived in the county.

On November 10, 1837 the Battle of the Knobs was fought in what is now Wise County between about 150 Indian warriors and just 18 Republic of Texas soldiers under Lieutenant A. B. Benthuysen. Despite being heavily outnumbered, the Texas soldiers held their ground, with an estimated 50 Indians killed or wounded and 10 of the Texans dead. Settlers began coming into the area not long afterwards. Wise County itself was founded in 1856. It was named after Virginia Congressman Henry A. Wise, who had supported Texas annexation by the United States.
