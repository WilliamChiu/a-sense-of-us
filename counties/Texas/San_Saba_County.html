San Saba County, Texas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 7.359624%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 722 people in San Saba County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 4895 people in San Saba County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2000, 569 people in San Saba County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 6186 people in San Saba County, Texas lived in the county.

In 2010, 1287 people in San Augustine County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7355 people in San Augustine County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 223 people in San Augustine County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 8865 people in San Augustine County, Texas lived in the county.

United Confederate Veterans organized a chapter known as the "William P. Rogers Camp" in San Saba County after the death in 1889 of Confederate President Jefferson Davis. Rogers, a hero of the Battle of Corinth in Mississippi, was a native of Georgia. He did not live in San Saba, but his daughter, Fannie, married one of Rogers' officers, George Harris, who moved there in 1880. A former county judge, Harris served as a commander of Rogers Camp, named for his father-in-law. The veterans' organization lasted until the early 1930s.
