Hudspeth County, Texas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.2714455%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 276 people in Hudspeth County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 3036 people in Hudspeth County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2000, 32 people in Hudspeth County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 3344 people in Hudspeth County, Texas lived in the county.

In 2010, 4388 people in Hopkins County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 30342 people in Hopkins County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 431 people in Hopkins County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 35161 people in Hopkins County, Texas lived in the county.

Prehistoric Jornada Mogollón peoples were practicing agriculture in the Rio Grande floodplain A.D. 900–1350. These people left behind artifacts and pictographs as evidence of their presence.
