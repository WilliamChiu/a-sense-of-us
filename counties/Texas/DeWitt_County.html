DeWitt County, Texas

In 2000, 2300 people in DeWitt County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 15929 people in DeWitt County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 1862 people in Deaf Smith County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 17139 people in Deaf Smith County, Texas lived in family households.

Archeological digs indicate early habitation from the Paleo-Indians Hunter-gatherers period. Later, Tonkawa, Aranamas, Tamiques, Karankawa. Tawakoni, Lipan Apache and Comanche lived and hunted in the county.
