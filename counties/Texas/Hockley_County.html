Hockley County, Texas

In 2000, 2173 people in Hockley County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 19933 people in Hockley County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 11194 people in Henderson County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 66096 people in Henderson County, Texas lived in family households.

Hockley County was formed in 1876 from portions of Bexar and Young counties. It was named for George Washington Hockley, the commander of artillery in the Battle of San Jacinto and later Secretary of War of the Republic of Texas.
