Freestone County, Texas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 6.994581%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2156 people in Freestone County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14168 people in Freestone County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2000, 1543 people in Freestone County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 17867 people in Freestone County, Texas lived in the county.

In 2010, 218 people in Foard County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1088 people in Foard County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 30 people in Foard County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 1336 people in Foard County, Texas lived in the county.

The farming Kichai band of the Caddoan Mississippian culture dates as far back as 200 BCE in the area. The Hernando de Soto expedition of 1541 resulted in violent encounters. Spanish and French missionaries brought smallpox, measles, malaria, and influenza epidemics against which the Caddo had no immunity. Eventually, the Caddo were forced to reservations.
