Cass County, Texas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.6659508%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 3952 people in Cass County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 26007 people in Cass County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2000, 479 people in Cass County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 30438 people in Cass County, Texas lived in the county.

In 2010, 27518 people in Cameron County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 374972 people in Cameron County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 3730 people in Cameron County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 406220 people in Cameron County, Texas lived in the county.

Cass County was formed in 1846 from sections of Bowie County. It was named for Lewis Cass, a U.S. Senator from Michigan who had favored the annexation of Texas to the United States. From 1861 to 1871, this county was known as '''Davis County''', after Jefferson Davis, the president of the Confederate States of America. (It should not be confused with the still extant Jeff Davis County in west Texas.)
