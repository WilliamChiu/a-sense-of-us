Household Populations

Gregg County, Texas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.2422562%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 15454 people in Gregg County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 92831 people in Gregg County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2000, 3094 people in Gregg County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 111379 people in Gregg County, Texas lived in the county.

In 2010, 2387 people in Gonzales County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 17109 people in Gonzales County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 311 people in Gonzales County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 19807 people in Gonzales County, Texas lived in the county.

This area was among early sections settled by United States immigrants before Texas became an independent republic and, after 1848, a state of the United States. It was an area developed as cotton plantations dependent on slave labor of African Americans. Lumbering of the pine forests was also pursued, especially in the early years of clearing the land for cultivation.
