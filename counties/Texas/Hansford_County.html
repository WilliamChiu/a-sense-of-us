Hansford County, Texas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -6.2226415%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 558 people in Hansford County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 4725 people in Hansford County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2000, 86 people in Hansford County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 5369 people in Hansford County, Texas lived in the county.

In 2010, 2802 people in Hale County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 36273 people in Hale County, Texas lived in the county.

Artifacts of the Antelope Creek Indian culture abound along the Canadian valley in Hutchinson County. Archaeologists have found 1300 acre of Alibates flint in the area that was used as a quarry for shaping flint tools. Nomadic Plains Apache also camped in this area as did Comanche, Arapaho, Kiowa, and Cheyenne.
