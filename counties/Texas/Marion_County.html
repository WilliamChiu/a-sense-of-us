Marion County, Texas

In 2000, 1695 people in Marion County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 9157 people in Marion County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 38744 people in McLennan County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 187077 people in McLennan County, Texas lived in family households.

The Hernando de Soto expedition of 1541 resulted in violent encounters. Spanish and French missionaries brought a smallpox, measles malaria and influenza epidemics against which the Caddo had no immunity. Eventually, the Caddo were forced to reservations. Shashidahnee (Timber Hill) is the last known permanent Marion County settlement of the Caddo people. The 19th Century saw Shawnee, Delaware, and Kickapoo in the area.
