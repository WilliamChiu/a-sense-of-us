Williamson County, Texas

In 2000, 25564 people in Williamson County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 219477 people in Williamson County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 21190 people in Wichita County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 98293 people in Wichita County, Texas lived in family households.

Much of Williamson County has been the site of human habitation for at least 11,200 years. The earliest known inhabitants of the area lived during the late Pleistocene (Ice Age), and are linked to the Clovis culture around 9,200 BC (11,200 years old) based on evidence found at Bell County's much-studied Gault Site.
