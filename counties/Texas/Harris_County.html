Harris County, Texas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.17220974%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 461016 people in Harris County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 2897428 people in Harris County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2000, 42134 people in Harris County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 3400578 people in Harris County, Texas lived in the county.

In 2010, 554 people in Hansford County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 4999 people in Hansford County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 60 people in Hansford County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 5613 people in Hansford County, Texas lived in the county.

According to the U.S. Census Bureau, the county has a total area of 1777 sqmi, of which 1703 sqmi is land and 74 sqmi (4.2%) is water. Both its total area and land area are larger than the state of Rhode Island.
