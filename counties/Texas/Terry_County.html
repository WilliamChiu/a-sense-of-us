Terry County, Texas

In 2000, 1143 people in Terry County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10646 people in Terry County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 251592 people in Tarrant County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1536808 people in Tarrant County, Texas lived in family households.

In 1877, the ill-fated Nolan Expedition crossed the county in search of livestock stolen by Comanche renegades. The various Indian tribes had moved on by the time of white settlement, due to the depletion of the buffalo herds by hunters.
