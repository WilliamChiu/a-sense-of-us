Throckmorton County, Texas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.865237%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 249 people in Throckmorton County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 1579 people in Throckmorton County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2000, 22 people in Throckmorton County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 1850 people in Throckmorton County, Texas lived in the county.

In 2010, 21992 people in Taylor County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 104227 people in Taylor County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 5287 people in Taylor County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 131506 people in Taylor County, Texas lived in the county.

The Spanish explorer Pedro Vial is considered to be the earliest European to travel through what is now known as Throckmorton County. Vial passed between the Clear Fork and Main Fork of the Brazos River in 1786 while searching for a direct route between San Antonio and Santa Fe. No other major activity is recorded in the county until 1849, when Captain Randolph B. Marcy, commander of a U.S. military escort expedition led by Lieutenant J. E. Johnson, passed through the county.
