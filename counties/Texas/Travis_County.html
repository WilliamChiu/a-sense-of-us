Travis County, Texas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.8028855%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 191797 people in Travis County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 599777 people in Travis County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2000, 20706 people in Travis County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 812280 people in Travis County, Texas lived in the county.

In 2010, 264 people in Throckmorton County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1364 people in Throckmorton County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 13 people in Throckmorton County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 1641 people in Throckmorton County, Texas lived in the county.

Evidence of habitation of the Balcones Escarpment region of Texas can be traced to at least 11,000 years ago. Two of the oldest Paleolithic archeological sites in Texas, the Levi Rock Shelter and Smith Rock Shelter, are located in southwest and southeast Travis County, respectively. Several hundred years before the arrival of European settlers, the area was inhabited by a variety of nomadic Native American tribes. These indigenous peoples fished and hunted along the creeks, including present-day Barton Springs, which proved to be a reliable campsite. At the time of the first permanent settlement of the area, the Tonkawa tribe was the most common, with the Comanches and Lipan Apaches also frequenting the area.
