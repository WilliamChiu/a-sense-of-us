Jones County, Texas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 30.987692%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1779 people in Jones County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14041 people in Jones County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2000, 4965 people in Jones County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 20785 people in Jones County, Texas lived in the county.

In 2010, 569 people in Jim Hogg County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 4715 people in Jim Hogg County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 16 people in Jim Hogg County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 5300 people in Jim Hogg County, Texas lived in the county.

Jones County is a county located in the U.S. state of Texas. As of the 2010 census, its population was 20,202. Its county seat is Anson. Both the county and its county seat are named for Anson Jones, the fifth president of the Republic of Texas. Jones County is one of 46 prohibition, or entirely dry, counties in the state of Texas.
