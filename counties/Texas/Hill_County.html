Hill County, Texas

In 2000, 4070 people in Hill County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 27431 people in Hill County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 392 people in Hemphill County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 3375 people in Hemphill County, Texas lived in family households.

Hill County is a county located in Northern Central Texas. As of the 2010 census, the population was 35,089. Its county seat is Hillsboro. The county is named for George Washington Hill, secretary of war and secretary of the navy under the Republic of Texas. Hill County is not a part of Texas Hill Country.
