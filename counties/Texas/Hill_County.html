From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.5250444%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. In 2010, 392 people in Hemphill County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 3375 people in Hemphill County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 40 people in Hemphill County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 3807 people in Hemphill County, Texas lived in the county.

Hill County is a county located in the U.S. state of Texas. As of the 2010 census, the population was 35,089. Its county seat is Hillsboro. The county is named for George Washington Hill, secretary of war and secretary of the navy under the Republic of Texas. Hill County is part of Central Texas, though not included in Texas Hill Country.
