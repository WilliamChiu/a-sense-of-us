Midland County, Texas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.41761994%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 13562 people in Midland County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 100853 people in Midland County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2000, 1594 people in Midland County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 116009 people in Midland County, Texas lived in the county.

In 2010, 2950 people in Maverick County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 50339 people in Maverick County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 969 people in Maverick County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 54258 people in Maverick County, Texas lived in the county.

In 1968, the county lost before the Supreme Court in ''Avery v. Midland County'' which required local districts to be nearly equal. The city of Midland had most of the county's population but only elected one of the five county commissioners which was found to violate the Fourteenth Amendment.
