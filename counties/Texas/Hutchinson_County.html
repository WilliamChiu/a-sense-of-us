Hutchinson County, Texas

In 2000, 2648 people in Hutchinson County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 20912 people in Hutchinson County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 4222 people in Howard County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 24710 people in Howard County, Texas lived in family households.

Artifacts of the Antelope Creek Indian culture abound along the Canadian valley in Hutchinson County. Archaeologists have found 1300 acre of Alibates flint in the area that was used as a quarry for shaping flint tools. Nomadic Plains Apache also camped in this area as did Comanche, Arapaho, Kiowa, and Cheyenne.
