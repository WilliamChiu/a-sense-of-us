McMullen County is a county located in the U.S. state of Texas. As of the 2010 census, its population was 707, making it the fifth-least populous county in Texas. Its county seat is Tilden. The county was officially established from parts of Bexar, Atascosa, and Live Oak counties in 1858. It is named for John McMullen, founder of a colony in Texas.
