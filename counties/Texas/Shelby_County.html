Shelby County, Texas

In 2000, 3052 people in Shelby County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 21792 people in Shelby County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 351 people in Schleicher County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 3110 people in Schleicher County, Texas lived in family households.

Shelby County was formed in 1836. It was named for Isaac Shelby, a soldier from Tennessee during the American Revolution, and first Governor of Kentucky.
