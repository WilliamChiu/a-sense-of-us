Falls County, Texas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 11.437834%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2310 people in Falls County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14180 people in Falls County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2000, 2086 people in Falls County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 18576 people in Falls County, Texas lived in the county.

In 2010, 13360 people in Ellis County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 134640 people in Ellis County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 1610 people in Ellis County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 149610 people in Ellis County, Texas lived in the county.

The Brazos River valley served as hunting grounds for several tribes, including Wacos, Tawakonis, and Anadarkos. The Comanches were often a more aggressive band who forced other tribes off the land. The Tawakoni branch of Wichita Indians originated north of Texas, but migrated south into east Texas. From 1843 onward, the Tawakoni were part of treaties made by both the Republic of Texas and the United States.
