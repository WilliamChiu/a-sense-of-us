Jeff Davis County, Texas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -9.826708%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 68 people in Jeff Davis County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 2207 people in Jeff Davis County, Texas lived in the county.

Prehistoric peoples camped at Phantom Lake Spring, in northeastern Jeff Davis County, and may have used the springs for irrigation. Indian pictographs in The Painted Comanche Camp of Limpia Canyon were discovered by the Whiting and Smith Expedition of 1849.
