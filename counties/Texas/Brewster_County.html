Brewster County, Texas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.2964716%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1796 people in Brewster County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6670 people in Brewster County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2000, 400 people in Brewster County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 8866 people in Brewster County, Texas lived in the county.

In 2010, 12839 people in Bowie County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 73520 people in Bowie County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 6206 people in Bowie County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 92565 people in Bowie County, Texas lived in the county.

Paleo-Indians Hunter-gatherers existed at least 9000 years ago. Mescalaro Apaches emerged later and conducted raids that discouraged settlers. Between 1779 and 1787 Col. Juan de Ugalde drove the Mescalaros back across the Rio Grande and into the Chisos Mountains. The three leading Mescalero chiefs, Patula Grande, Quemado, and Zapato Tuerto, agreed in March 1789 to submit to Spanish rule. Comanche raiding parties continued through much of the 19th century.
