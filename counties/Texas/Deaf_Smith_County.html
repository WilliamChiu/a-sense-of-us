Deaf Smith County, Texas

In 2000, 1520 people in Deaf Smith County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 16787 people in Deaf Smith County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 837 people in Dallam County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5827 people in Dallam County, Texas lived in family households.

In 1876 the state legislature identified and named the county, but it was not organized until 1890, with the town of LaPlata as the original county seat. The county was named for Erastus "Deaf" Smith (1787â€“1837), a partially deaf scout and soldier who served in the Texas Revolution. He was the first to reach the Alamo after its fall. The pronunciation of "Deaf", like that of Smith himself, is "Deef". However, most people in Deaf Smith County pronounce it "Deaf".
