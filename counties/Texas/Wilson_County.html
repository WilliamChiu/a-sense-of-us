Wilson County, Texas

In 2000, 2622 people in Wilson County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 29268 people in Wilson County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 2029 people in Wilbarger County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 10891 people in Wilbarger County, Texas lived in family households.

Archeological evidence in the Wilson County area reveals early habitation from the Paleo-Indians Hunter-gatherers period. Later, the area was a hunting range for Tonkawa, Aranamas, Tamiques, Karankawa. Tawakoni, Lipan Apache and Comanche lived and hunted in the county.
