King County, Texas

In 2000, 20 people in King County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 279 people in King County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 157 people in Kent County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 599 people in Kent County, Texas lived in family households.

Apache and Comanche were early tribes in the area. The Red River War of 1874-1875 was a United States Army campaign to force the removal of Natives in Texas and their relocation to reservations, to open the region to white settlers.
