Cochran County, Texas

In 2000, 315 people in Cochran County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 3340 people in Cochran County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 5872 people in Cherokee County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 42122 people in Cherokee County, Texas lived in family households.

Cochran County is a county located in the U.S. state of Texas. As of the 2010 census, its population was 3,127. The county seat is Morton. The county is named for Robert E. Cochran, a defender of the Alamo.
