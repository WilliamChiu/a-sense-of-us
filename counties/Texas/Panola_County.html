Panola County, Texas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -11.994648%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2689 people in Panola County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 19615 people in Panola County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2000, 452 people in Panola County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 22756 people in Panola County, Texas lived in the county.

In 2010, 221 people in Oldham County, Texas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1549 people in Oldham County, Texas lived in family households.

In 2010, 282 people in Oldham County, Texas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 2052 people in Oldham County, Texas lived in the county.

Panola County was formed in 1846 from sections of Harrison County and Shelby County. It was named after a Choctaw/ Chickasaw word for cotton.
