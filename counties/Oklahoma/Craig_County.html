Craig County, Oklahoma

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 7.1170926%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1864 people in Craig County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 11949 people in Craig County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2000, 1137 people in Craig County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 14950 people in Craig County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In 2010, 844 people in Coal County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5020 people in Coal County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2010, 61 people in Coal County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 5925 people in Coal County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In the early 1800s, this area was part of the hunting grounds of the Osage nations and other Plains tribes. The Cherokee began moving into the area during the 1830s, particularly after the "Trail of Tears." It was sparsely populated until after the Civil War. The Texas Road and the East Shawnee Cattle Trail ran through the eastern part of the present-day Craig County.
