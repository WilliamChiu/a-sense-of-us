Haskell County, Oklahoma

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -17.394602%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1359 people in Haskell County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10294 people in Haskell County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2000, 139 people in Haskell County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 11792 people in Haskell County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In 2010, 854 people in Greer County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 4239 people in Greer County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2010, 1146 people in Greer County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 6239 people in Greer County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

The area now comprising Haskell County was created at statehood from the former San Boise County of the Choctaw Nation in Indian Territory. An election in 1908 picked Stigler over Keota and Whitefield as the county seat.
