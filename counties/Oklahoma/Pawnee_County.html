Pawnee County, Oklahoma

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.4661958%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1872 people in Pawnee County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14576 people in Pawnee County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2000, 164 people in Pawnee County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 16612 people in Pawnee County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In 2010, 5611 people in Okmulgee County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 33084 people in Okmulgee County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2010, 1374 people in Okmulgee County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 40069 people in Okmulgee County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

The Osage Tribe used the area that contains present day Pawnee County as buffalo hunting grounds. In 1825, The Osage ceded parts of present-day Missouri, Arkansas and most of the future state of Oklahoma to the federal government.
