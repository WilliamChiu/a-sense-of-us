Major County, Oklahoma

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.0448575%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 920 people in Major County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6505 people in Major County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2000, 120 people in Major County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 7545 people in Major County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In 2010, 3712 people in McClain County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 30600 people in McClain County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2010, 194 people in McClain County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 34506 people in McClain County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

Upon statehood in 1907, Major County was created from southern part of a territorial county. Fairview, which had been settled following the Land Run of 1893, was designated the county seat and voters reaffirmed the choice on December 22, 1908. The county's commissioners rented office space until a brick courthouse was constructed. A second courthouse, made of stone, was erected in 1928.
