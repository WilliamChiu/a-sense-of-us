Atoka County, Oklahoma

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 11.376774%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1608 people in Atoka County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10715 people in Atoka County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2000, 1556 people in Atoka County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 13879 people in Atoka County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In 2010, 3437 people in Wyandot County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 18927 people in Wyandot County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2010, 251 people in Wyandot County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 22615 people in Wyandot County, Ohio lived in the county.

The area encompassed by the present Atoka County was originally part of Shappaway County in the Pushmataha District of the Choctaw Nation. About 1854, the area was formally designated Atoka County. The name, which honored Choctaw Chief Atoka, a leader of a party which migrated from Georgia to Indian Territory, was retained when Oklahoma became a state.
