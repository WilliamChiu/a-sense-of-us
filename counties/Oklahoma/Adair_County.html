Adair County, Oklahoma

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.8197367%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2158 people in Adair County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 18496 people in Adair County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2000, 384 people in Adair County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 21038 people in Adair County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In 2010, 5750 people in Williams County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 30902 people in Williams County, Ohio lived in family households.

In 2010, 990 people in Williams County, Ohio lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 37642 people in Williams County, Ohio lived in the county.

The county was created in 1906 from the Goingsnake and Flint districts of the Cherokee Nation. There was a decade-long struggle over what town would become the county seat between Stilwell and Westville.
