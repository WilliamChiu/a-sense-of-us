Jackson County, Oklahoma

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.6393185%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 3393 people in Jackson County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 24276 people in Jackson County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2000, 770 people in Jackson County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 28439 people in Jackson County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In 2010, 553 people in Harper County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 3091 people in Harper County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2010, 41 people in Harper County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 3685 people in Harper County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

After a dispute over the Adams-Onís Treaty of 1819, both the governments of the United States and the state of Texas claimed ownership of some 1500000 acre in what was then operated as Greer County, Texas. Litigation followed, and in the case of ''United States v. State of Texas'' , issued on March 16, the Supreme Court, having original jurisdiction over the case, decided in favor of the United States. Greer County was then assigned to the Oklahoma Territory on May 4, 1896, and when Oklahoma became a state, in addition to becoming Jackson County, the region was also further split into Greer, Harmon, and part of Beckham counties.
