Alfalfa County, Oklahoma

In 2000, 755 people in Alfalfa County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 4287 people in Alfalfa County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2010, 24878 people in Wood County, Ohio lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 94380 people in Wood County, Ohio lived in family households.

Indigenous peoples inhabited and hunted in this area for thousands of years. By 1750, the Osage had become a dominant tribe in the area. About one third belonged to the band led by Chief Black Dog (''Manka - Chonka''). Before 1800 they made the Black Dog Trail starting east of Baxter Springs, Kansas and going northwest to their summer hunting grounds at the Great Salt Plains in present-day Alfalfa County.
