Payne County, Oklahoma

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 7.8968167%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 15827 people in Payne County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 45370 people in Payne County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2000, 6993 people in Payne County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 68190 people in Payne County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In 2010, 6103 people in Osage County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 39865 people in Osage County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2010, 1504 people in Osage County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 47472 people in Osage County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

This county was established and named as the Sixth County by the Oklahoma Organic Act of 1890. It included land settled during the Land Run of 1889. The Organic Act settled a dispute between the towns of Stillwater and Perkins over which should be the county seat.
