Carter County, Oklahoma

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.27555227%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 6048 people in Carter County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 38476 people in Carter County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2000, 1097 people in Carter County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 45621 people in Carter County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In 2010, 7066 people in Bryan County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 34216 people in Bryan County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2010, 1134 people in Bryan County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 42416 people in Bryan County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

Prior to statehood, the present Carter County, Oklahoma, was part of Pickens County in the Chickasaw Nation of Indian Territory. After the Civil War, the government of the United States forced the Chickasaw government to allow railroads built across its territory. The Gulf, Colorado and Santa Fe Railway (controlled by the Atchison, Topeka and Santa Fe Railway, AT&SF) built a line north from Texas to Purcell.
