Cotton County, Oklahoma

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.523757%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 847 people in Cotton County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 5592 people in Cotton County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2000, 175 people in Cotton County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 6614 people in Cotton County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In 2010, 46579 people in Cleveland County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 198615 people in Cleveland County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2010, 10561 people in Cleveland County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 255755 people in Cleveland County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

The eastern part of what is now Cotton County was opened to settlement by non-native Americans by the 1901 Kiowa-Comanche-Apache Opening, which distributed land by a lottery system. In 1906, the remainder of the present county, then known as the Big Pasture was opened through a sealed bid process. Most of this territory became part of Comanche County at statehood in 1907.
