Harmon County, Oklahoma

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.7564764%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 453 people in Harmon County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 2676 people in Harmon County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2000, 154 people in Harmon County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 3283 people in Harmon County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In 2010, 6470 people in Grady County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 44879 people in Grady County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2010, 1082 people in Grady County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 52431 people in Grady County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

Following an election on May 22, 1909, Harmon County was created by proclamation of Governor Lee Cruce on June 2. Carved from adjacent Greer County, the new county was named in honor of Judson Harmon, who was Governor of Ohio at the time. The area now covered by Harmon County had been a part of Texas until the U. S. Supreme Court awarded it to Oklahoma Territory in 1896.
