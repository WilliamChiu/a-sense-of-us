Noble County, Oklahoma

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.0522127%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1465 people in Noble County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 9672 people in Noble County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2000, 274 people in Noble County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 11411 people in Noble County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In 2010, 5520 people in Mayes County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 35172 people in Mayes County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2010, 567 people in Mayes County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 41259 people in Mayes County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

During the 18th and 19th centuries, the area now occupied by Noble County was used as a hunting ground by the Osage Indians. In 1835, a treaty with the Cherokees made it part of the so-called Cherokee Outlet. During the time of Cherokee ownership, white cattlemen who belonged to the Cherokee Strip Live Stock Association leased grazing land from the tribe. Later, reservations were created in the northeastern part of the county for the Otoe and Ponca tribes. These reservations existed until 1904, when the lands were allotted and added to Noble County.
