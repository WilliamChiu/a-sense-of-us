Roger Mills County, Oklahoma

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -3.455913%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 478 people in Roger Mills County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 2920 people in Roger Mills County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2000, 38 people in Roger Mills County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 3436 people in Roger Mills County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In 2010, 5959 people in Pontotoc County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 29837 people in Pontotoc County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2010, 1696 people in Pontotoc County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 37492 people in Pontotoc County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

Roger Mills County takes its name from Roger Q. Mills, a senator from Texas. The town of Cheyenne in Roger Mills County is the location of the Battle of Washita River (also called Battle of the Washita; Washita Battlefield and the Washita Massacre), where George Armstrong Custer’s 7th U.S. Cavalry attacked Chief Black Kettle’s Cheyenne village on the Washita River on November 26, 1868.
