Johnston County, Oklahoma

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -8.977008%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1323 people in Johnston County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 8936 people in Johnston County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2000, 254 people in Johnston County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 10513 people in Johnston County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In 1820, the U.S. government granted the land now known as Johnston County to the Choctaw tribe. Many of the Choctaws began moving to the new land in Indian Territory in 1830. The rest followed Chickasaw tribe, who were closely related to the Choctaw, formally separated from the Choctaw Nation in the late 1830s, relocating to the western part of the Choctaw Nation. The Chickasaw Nation named the town of Tishomingo as its capital and built a brick capitol building there in 1856.
