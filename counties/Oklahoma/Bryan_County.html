Bryan County, Oklahoma

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.9036055%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 5295 people in Bryan County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 30283 people in Bryan County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2000, 956 people in Bryan County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 36534 people in Bryan County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In 2010, 673 people in Beaver County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 4920 people in Beaver County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2010, 43 people in Beaver County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 5636 people in Beaver County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

The area now known as Bryan County was occupied by the Choctaw Tribe in 1831-2, and became part of Choctaw Nation's Blue County. In 1845, the tribe opened Armstrong Academy for boys near the community of Bokchito. The academy served as Chahta Tamaha, the Choctaw capital, during the Civil War. Bloomfield Academy, a school for Chickasaw girls opened in 1852, just south of the present town of Achille.
