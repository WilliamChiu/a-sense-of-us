Custer County, Oklahoma

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 4.2663455%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4473 people in Custer County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 20405 people in Custer County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2000, 1264 people in Custer County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 26142 people in Custer County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In 2010, 877 people in Cotton County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5268 people in Cotton County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2010, 48 people in Cotton County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 6193 people in Cotton County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

Custer County was formed on 1891 as an original county from Cheyenne land, and called G County. On November 6, 1896 it was renamed Custer County after General George Armstrong Custer, who had massacred the Southern Cheyenne Indians at the Battle of the Washita 20 miles west in Roger Mills County, and was killed at the Battle of Little Bighorn. The county was settled by white settlers during the third official land run of April 19, 1892. On this day the first newspaper of the county appeared, the Arapaho Arrow.
