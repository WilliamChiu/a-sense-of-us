Coal County, Oklahoma

In 2000, 810 people in Coal County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 5145 people in Coal County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2010, 2418 people in Choctaw County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 12615 people in Choctaw County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

Coal County was formed at statehood from the former Shappaway County (later renamed Atoka County) of the Pushmataha District of the Choctaw Nation in Indian Territory. A 3.5 mi strip of Coal County was taken from the Pontotoc District of the Chickasaw Nation. Initially, the Oklahoma legislature named Lehigh as the county seat, but a special election held in 1908 resulted in the citizens choosing Colgate as the county seat. Lehigh tried to sue, because more people voted than were registered, but no court would hear the case.
