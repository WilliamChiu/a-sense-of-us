Grant County, Oklahoma

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.1177361%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 686 people in Grant County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 99 people in Grant County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 5144 people in Grant County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In 2010, 9830 people in Garfield County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 48920 people in Garfield County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2010, 1830 people in Garfield County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 60580 people in Garfield County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

Grant County was part of the Cherokee Nation's Cherokee Outlet until it was opened to non-Indian settlers in response to public demand on September 16, 1893. Settlers named the county after President Ulysses S. Grant in a general election held November 6, 1894. Congress originally designated this area as County L in Oklahoma Territory, with the county seat at Pond Creek. Medford became the county seat through an election held on May 27, 1908.
