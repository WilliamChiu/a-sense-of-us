Household Populations

Marshall County, Oklahoma

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.7316351%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1769 people in Marshall County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 11140 people in Marshall County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2000, 275 people in Marshall County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 13184 people in Marshall County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In 2010, 4523 people in McCurtain County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 28174 people in McCurtain County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2010, 454 people in McCurtain County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 33151 people in McCurtain County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

The area covered by Marshall County was part of the territory set aside by the U.S. government for resettlement of the Choctaw tribe and the closely related Chickasaw tribe from their lands in the southeastern United States. The Chickasaws began relocating to this area in 1837. The U.S. Army built Fort Washita in 1842 to protect the new arrivals from raids by other tribes. In 1857, the Chickasaw Nation formally separated from the Choctaw Nation. This area became part of Pickens County in the Chickasaw Nation.
