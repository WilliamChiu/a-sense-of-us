Muskogee County, Oklahoma

In 2000, 9177 people in Muskogee County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 57102 people in Muskogee County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2010, 2296 people in Marshall County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 13215 people in Marshall County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

According to archaeological studies, prehistoric people lived in this area as long ago as the Paleo-Indian period (before 6,000 B. C.). However, archaeologists have made more extensive studies of those people known as the Mound Builders who lived here during the Caddoan Stage (A.D. 300 – 1200).
