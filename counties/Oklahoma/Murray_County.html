Murray County, Oklahoma

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.8494248%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1591 people in Murray County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10676 people in Murray County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2000, 356 people in Murray County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 12623 people in Murray County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In 2010, 1045 people in Major County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 6405 people in Major County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2010, 77 people in Major County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 7527 people in Major County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

The area now occupied by Murray County was part of the land granted to the Choctaw Nation by the Treaty of Dancing Rabbit Creek in 1820. The Chickasaw received part of the land under the Treaty of Doaksville in 1838. The area became part of the Chickasaw Nation in 1855. There was an extended conflict before the U. S. Civil War between the Plains Indians and the newly arrived Choctaws and Chickasaws. The U. S. Army built Fort Towson (1824); Fort Washita (1842) and Fort Arbuckle (1851) to protect the Chickasaws. After the conflict between the tribes was settled, various bands of outlaws moved in and continued to create law enforcement problems.
