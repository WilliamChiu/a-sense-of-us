Household Populations

Jefferson County, Oklahoma

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 4.637933%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 947 people in Jefferson County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 5529 people in Jefferson County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2000, 342 people in Jefferson County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 6818 people in Jefferson County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In 2010, 1670 people in Haskell County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 11021 people in Haskell County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2010, 78 people in Haskell County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 12769 people in Haskell County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In the 1750s, the Taovaya Indians, a Wichita tribe, established twin villages along the Red River, in Jefferson County and across the river near present-day Spanish Fort, Texas. The village became “a lively emporium where Comanches brought Apache slaves, horses and mules to trade for French packs of powder, balls, knives, and textiles and for Taovaya-grown maize, melons, pumpkins, squash, and tobacco.”
