Household Populations

Logan County, Oklahoma

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.0400534%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 3997 people in Logan County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 27850 people in Logan County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2000, 2077 people in Logan County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 33924 people in Logan County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In 2010, 1489 people in Latimer County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 9091 people in Latimer County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2010, 574 people in Latimer County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 11154 people in Latimer County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

Logan County was created as County One by the Organic Act of 1890. The town of Guthrie was designated as the county seat and the capital of Oklahoma Territory. The county was named on August 5, 1890 for U. S. Senator, John A. Logan, of Illinois.
