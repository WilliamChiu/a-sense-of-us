Caddo County, Oklahoma

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -4.1017714%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 30150 people in Caddo County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In 2010, 3196 people in Beckham County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 17016 people in Beckham County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2010, 1907 people in Beckham County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 22119 people in Beckham County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

Caddo County was organized on August 6, 1901, when the Federal Government allotted the Kiowa, Comanche and Arapaho reservations and sold the surplus land to white settlers. The reservation land was part of Oklahoma Territory until Oklahoma became a state on November 16, 1907. Part of its land was taken at statehood to form neighboring Grady County. Some additional land was taken in 1911 and also awarded to Grady County.
