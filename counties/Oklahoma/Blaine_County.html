Blaine County, Oklahoma

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 9.232116%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1404 people in Blaine County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 8980 people in Blaine County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2000, 1592 people in Blaine County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 11976 people in Blaine County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

In 2010, 1865 people in Atoka County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 11567 people in Atoka County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2010, 750 people in Atoka County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 14182 people in Atoka County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

Blaine County was one of several counties created by the Land Run of 1892. It was designated as county "C" beginning in 1890 before the land run. The area was settled by Creek and Seminole, who the federal government relocated to the area in the 1820s and 1830s. The Cheyenne and Arapaho tribes were relocated to the area from Colorado in 1869, after the Reconstruction Treaties of 1866 had taken the land back from the Creek and Seminole.
