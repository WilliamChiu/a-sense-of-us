Allegheny County, Pennsylvania

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.4811039%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 241308 people in Allegheny County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 999741 people in Allegheny County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2000, 40617 people in Allegheny County, Pennsylvania lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 1281666 people in Allegheny County, Pennsylvania lived in the county.

In 2010, 1441 people in Wheeler County, Oregon lived in the county.

Allegheny County is a county in the southwestern part of the U.S. state of Pennsylvania. As of 2014 the population was 1,231,225, making it the second-most populous county in Pennsylvania, following Philadelphia County. The county seat is Pittsburgh.
