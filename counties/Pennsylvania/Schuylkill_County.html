Schuylkill County, Pennsylvania

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.09905%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 23212 people in Schuylkill County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 119898 people in Schuylkill County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2000, 7226 people in Schuylkill County, Pennsylvania lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 150336 people in Schuylkill County, Pennsylvania lived in the county.

In 2010, 57383 people in Philadelphia County, Pennsylvania lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 1526006 people in Philadelphia County, Pennsylvania lived in the county.

In the year 1754, the area that would become Schuylkill County was settled by Germans, as were areas that are now part of Berks, Dauphin, Lebanon, and Lehigh counties. The earliest settlers in southeastern Schuylkill County, which was then part of Northampton County, were primarily Moravian missionaries from Saxony. Other early settlers in southern Schuylkill County were from the Palatines. An early mill in the county was built in 1744 by John Finscher, but it later burned down. The first log church in the county was built in 1755. Native American massacres were commonplace in Schuylkill County between 1755 and 1765. Warrant for tracts of land in the vicinity of McKeansburg were in existence as early as 1750.
