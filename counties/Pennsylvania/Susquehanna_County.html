Susquehanna County, Pennsylvania

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -4.8583865%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 5668 people in Susquehanna County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 36136 people in Susquehanna County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2000, 434 people in Susquehanna County, Pennsylvania lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 42238 people in Susquehanna County, Pennsylvania lived in the county.

In 2010, 5457 people in Snyder County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 31928 people in Snyder County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2010, 2317 people in Snyder County, Pennsylvania lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 39702 people in Snyder County, Pennsylvania lived in the county.

The first settlers began to move into the area from Philadelphia and Connecticut in the mid 1700s. At the time, the area was part of Luzerne County. As more and more people from Connecticut moved in, there began to be some conflict. Under Connecticut's land grant, they owned everything from present day Connecticut to the Pacific Ocean. This meant their land grant overlapped with Pennsylvania's land grant. Soon fighting began. In the end, the Connecticut government was asked to surrender their claim on the area, which they did.
