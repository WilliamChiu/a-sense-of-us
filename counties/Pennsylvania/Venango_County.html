Venango County, Pennsylvania

In 2000, 7881 people in Venango County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 47854 people in Venango County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2010, 6824 people in Susquehanna County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 36250 people in Susquehanna County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

Venango County was created on March 12, 1800 from parts of Allegheny and Lycoming Counties. The name "Venango" comes from the Native American name of the region, ''Onenge'', meaning ''Otter.'' This was corrupted in English as the ''Venango River''.
