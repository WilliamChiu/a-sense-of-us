Venango County, Pennsylvania

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.6116133%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 7881 people in Venango County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 47854 people in Venango County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2000, 1830 people in Venango County, Pennsylvania lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 57565 people in Venango County, Pennsylvania lived in the county.

In 2010, 6824 people in Susquehanna County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 36250 people in Susquehanna County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2010, 282 people in Susquehanna County, Pennsylvania lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 43356 people in Susquehanna County, Pennsylvania lived in the county.

Venango County was created on March 12, 1800 from parts of Allegheny and Lycoming Counties. The name "Venango" comes from the Native American name of the region, ''Onenge'', meaning ''Otter.'' This was corrupted in English as the ''Venango River''.
