Lycoming County, Pennsylvania

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.9438148%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 18895 people in Lycoming County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 95636 people in Lycoming County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2000, 5513 people in Lycoming County, Pennsylvania lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 120044 people in Lycoming County, Pennsylvania lived in the county.

In 2010, 19394 people in Lebanon County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 110517 people in Lebanon County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2010, 3657 people in Lebanon County, Pennsylvania lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 133568 people in Lebanon County, Pennsylvania lived in the county.

Lycoming County was formed from Northumberland County on April 13, 1795. At the time it was formed, the county was much larger than it is today. It took up most of the land that is now north central Pennsylvania. The following counties have been formed from land that was once part of Lycoming County: Armstrong, Bradford, Centre, Clearfield, Clinton, Indiana, Jefferson, McKean, Potter, Sullivan, Tioga, Venango, Warren, Forest, Elk and Cameron. Lycoming County was originally named Jefferson County in honor of Thomas Jefferson. This name proved to be unsatisfactory. The name change went through several steps. First a change to Lycoming County was rejected, next the name Susquehanna County was struck down as was Muncy County, before the legislature revisited and settled on Lycoming County for Lycoming Creek, the stream that was the center of the pre-Revolutionary border dispute.
