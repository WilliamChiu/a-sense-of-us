Forest County, Pennsylvania

In 2000, 773 people in Forest County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 3797 people in Forest County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2010, 5335 people in Elk County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 26256 people in Elk County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

Forest County was created on April 11, 1848, from part of Jefferson County. The county was enlarged on October 31, 1866, when part of Venango County was incorporated into the county. Forest County was named for the forests contained within its limits.
