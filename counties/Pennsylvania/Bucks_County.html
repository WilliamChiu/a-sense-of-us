Bucks County, Pennsylvania

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.4338553%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 70989 people in Bucks County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 517622 people in Bucks County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2000, 9024 people in Bucks County, Pennsylvania lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 597635 people in Bucks County, Pennsylvania lived in the county.

In 2010, 60751 people in Berks County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 338668 people in Berks County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2010, 12023 people in Berks County, Pennsylvania lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 411442 people in Berks County, Pennsylvania lived in the county.

Bucks County is one of the three original counties created by colonial proprietor William Penn in 1682. Penn named the county after Buckinghamshire, the county where he lived in England. Penn built a country estate called Pennsbury Manor in Falls Township, Bucks County.
