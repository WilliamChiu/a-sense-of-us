Wayne County, Pennsylvania

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.6920209%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 6356 people in Wayne County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 39465 people in Wayne County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2000, 1901 people in Wayne County, Pennsylvania lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 47722 people in Wayne County, Pennsylvania lived in the county.

In 2010, 8617 people in Venango County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 45070 people in Venango County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2010, 1297 people in Venango County, Pennsylvania lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 54984 people in Venango County, Pennsylvania lived in the county.

Wayne is a sixth-class county in Pennsylvania. The county's population was 52,822 at the time of the 2010 United States Census. The County seat is Honesdale. The county was formed from part of Northampton County on March 21, 1798, and was named for the Revolutionary War General Anthony Wayne.
