Cumberland County, Pennsylvania

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.16611814%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 33222 people in Cumberland County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 166500 people in Cumberland County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2000, 13952 people in Cumberland County, Pennsylvania lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 213674 people in Cumberland County, Pennsylvania lived in the county.

In 2010, 7122 people in Clinton County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 29493 people in Clinton County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2010, 2623 people in Clinton County, Pennsylvania lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 39238 people in Clinton County, Pennsylvania lived in the county.

Cumberland County was first settled by a majority of Scots-Irish immigrants who arrived in this area about 1730. English and German settlers constituted about ten percent of the early population. The settlers originally mostly devoted the area to farming and later developed other trades. These settlers built the Middle Spring Presbyterian Church, among the oldest houses of worship in central Pennsylvania, in 1738 near present day Shippensburg, Pennsylvania.
