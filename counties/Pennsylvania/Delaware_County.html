Delaware County, Pennsylvania

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.4359603%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 79157 people in Delaware County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 449966 people in Delaware County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2000, 21741 people in Delaware County, Pennsylvania lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 550864 people in Delaware County, Pennsylvania lived in the county.

In 2010, 14399 people in Crawford County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 70491 people in Crawford County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2010, 3875 people in Crawford County, Pennsylvania lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 88765 people in Crawford County, Pennsylvania lived in the county.

Delaware County lies in the river and bay drainage area named "Delaware" in honor of Thomas West, 3rd Baron De La Warr, Governor of the nearby English colony of Virginia. The land was "discovered" and explored by Henry Hudson in 1609, and over the next several decades it was variously claimed and settled by the Swedes, the Dutch, and the English. Its original human inhabitants were the Lenni-Lenape tribe of American Indians.
