Franklin County, Pennsylvania

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.3004413%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 16908 people in Franklin County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 109376 people in Franklin County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2000, 3029 people in Franklin County, Pennsylvania lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 129313 people in Franklin County, Pennsylvania lived in the county.

In 2010, 50591 people in Erie County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 217100 people in Erie County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2010, 12875 people in Erie County, Pennsylvania lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 280566 people in Erie County, Pennsylvania lived in the county.

Originally part of Lancaster County (1729), then Cumberland County (1750), Franklin County became an independent jurisdiction on September 9, 1784. It is named in honor of Founding Father Benjamin Franklin.
