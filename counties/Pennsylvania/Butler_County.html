Butler County, Pennsylvania

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.82615614%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 23132 people in Butler County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 144536 people in Butler County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2000, 6415 people in Butler County, Pennsylvania lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 174083 people in Butler County, Pennsylvania lived in the county.

In 2010, 22973 people in Blair County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 100444 people in Blair County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2010, 3672 people in Blair County, Pennsylvania lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 127089 people in Blair County, Pennsylvania lived in the county.

Some famous inventions and discoveries were made in Butler County. It was in Saxonburg, that the designer of the Brooklyn Bridge, John Roebling, invented his revolutionary "wire rope." At what is now known as Oil Creek, Butler County resident William Smith and Edwin Drake first proved that oil could be tapped from underground for consistent supply. The Jeep was developed in Butler County by American Bantam in 1941.
