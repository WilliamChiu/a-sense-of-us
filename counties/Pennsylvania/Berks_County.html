Berks County, Pennsylvania

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.4628282%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 53961 people in Berks County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 307258 people in Berks County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2000, 12419 people in Berks County, Pennsylvania lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 373638 people in Berks County, Pennsylvania lived in the county.

In 2010, 11202 people in Armstrong County, Pennsylvania lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 57089 people in Armstrong County, Pennsylvania lived in family households.

In 2010, 650 people in Armstrong County, Pennsylvania lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 68941 people in Armstrong County, Pennsylvania lived in the county.

Reading developed during the 1740s when the inhabitants of northern Lancaster County sent several petitions requesting that a separate county be established.
