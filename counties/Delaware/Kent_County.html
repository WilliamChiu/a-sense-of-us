Kent County, Delaware

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.8519819%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 17090 people in Kent County, Delaware lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 105977 people in Kent County, Delaware lived in family households.

In 2000, 3630 people in Kent County, Delaware lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 126697 people in Kent County, Delaware lived in the county.

In 2010, 12782 people in New London County, Connecticut lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 274055 people in New London County, Connecticut lived in the county.

In about 1670 the English began to settle in the valley of the St. Jones River earlier known as Wolf Creek. On June 21, 1680, The Duke of York chartered St. Jones County, which was carved out of New Amstel/New Castle County and Hoarkill/Sussex County. St. Jones County was transferred to William Penn on August 24, 1682, and became part of Penn's newly chartered Delaware Colony.
