Jackson County, Oregon

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.630939%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 29831 people in Jackson County, Oregon lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 147761 people in Jackson County, Oregon lived in family households.

In 2000, 3677 people in Jackson County, Oregon lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 181269 people in Jackson County, Oregon lived in the county.

In 2010, 1380 people in Grant County, Oregon lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5960 people in Grant County, Oregon lived in family households.

In 2010, 105 people in Grant County, Oregon lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 7445 people in Grant County, Oregon lived in the county.

Modoc, Shasta, Takelma, Latgawa, and Umpqua Indian tribes are all native to the region of present Jackson County. In the early 1850s, both the Klickitats from the north and the Deschutes from the south raided and settled the area.
