Columbia County, Oregon

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -5.3539515%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 5499 people in Columbia County, Oregon lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 37813 people in Columbia County, Oregon lived in family households.

In 2000, 248 people in Columbia County, Oregon lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 43560 people in Columbia County, Oregon lived in the county.

In 2010, 23645 people in Benton County, Oregon lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 56891 people in Benton County, Oregon lived in family households.

In 2010, 5043 people in Benton County, Oregon lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 85579 people in Benton County, Oregon lived in the county.

The Chinook and Clatskanie Native Americans inhabited this region for centuries prior to the arrival of Robert Gray, captain of the ship ''Columbia Rediviva'', in 1792. The Lewis and Clark Expedition traveled and camped along the Columbia River shore in the area later known as Columbia County in late 1805 and on their return journey in early 1806.
