Marion County, Oregon

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.1415591%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 40772 people in Marion County, Oregon lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 233474 people in Marion County, Oregon lived in family households.

In 2000, 10588 people in Marion County, Oregon lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 284834 people in Marion County, Oregon lived in the county.

In 2010, 10317 people in Lincoln County, Oregon lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 34955 people in Lincoln County, Oregon lived in family households.

In 2010, 762 people in Lincoln County, Oregon lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 46034 people in Lincoln County, Oregon lived in the county.

Marion County was created by the Provisional Legislature of Oregon on July 5, 1843 as the Champooick District, one of the original four districts of the Oregon Country along with Twality (later Washington), Clackamas, and Yamhill counties. The four districts were redesignated as counties in 1845.
