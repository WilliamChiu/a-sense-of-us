Clackamas County, Oregon

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -5.370176%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 47036 people in Clackamas County, Oregon lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 288477 people in Clackamas County, Oregon lived in family households.

In 2000, 2878 people in Clackamas County, Oregon lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 338391 people in Clackamas County, Oregon lived in the county.

In 2010, 2914 people in Woodward County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 15927 people in Woodward County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

In 2010, 1240 people in Woodward County, Oklahoma lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 20081 people in Woodward County, Oklahoma lived in the county.

Originally named '''Clackamas District''', it was one of the four original Oregon districts created by Oregon's Provisional Legislature on July 5, 1843 along with Twality (later Washington), Champooick (later Marion), and Yamhill. The four districts were redesignated as counties in 1845. At the time of its creation, Clackamas County covered portions of four present-day states and a Canadian province. The Columbia River became the northern boundary of the county in 1844. Soon after John McLoughlin staked a land claim in Oregon City and built a house that in 2003 became a unit of the Fort Vancouver National Historic Site.
