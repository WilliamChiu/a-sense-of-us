Linn County, Oregon

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -4.832679%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 103069 people in Linn County, Oregon lived in the county.

In 2010, 6009 people in Lake County, Oregon lived in family households.

In 2010, 454 people in Lake County, Oregon lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 7895 people in Lake County, Oregon lived in the county.

On December 28, 1847 the Provisional Legislature created Linn County from the southern portion of Champoeg (later Marion) County. The boundaries were altered in 1851 and 1854 with the creation of Lane and Wasco Counties. The county seat was originally located in Calapooia (later known as Brownsville), but in 1851 the Territorial Legislature passed an act establishing Albany as the county seat. A special election in 1856 reaffirmed Albany as the county seat.
