Sherman County, Oregon

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.2058506%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 282 people in Sherman County, Oregon lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 1652 people in Sherman County, Oregon lived in family households.

In 2000, 0 people in Sherman County, Oregon lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 1934 people in Sherman County, Oregon lived in the county.

In 2010, 1235 people in Morrow County, Oregon lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 9915 people in Morrow County, Oregon lived in family households.

In 2010, 23 people in Morrow County, Oregon lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 11173 people in Morrow County, Oregon lived in the county.

Sherman County was created on February 25, 1889 out of the northeast corner of Wasco County. The county's borders have been changed only once, in 1891, when the Legislative Assembly moved the county line 18 mi farther south into Wasco County.
