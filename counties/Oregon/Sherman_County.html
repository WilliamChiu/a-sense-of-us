Sherman County, Oregon

In 2000, 282 people in Sherman County, Oregon lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 1652 people in Sherman County, Oregon lived in family households.

In 2010, 1235 people in Morrow County, Oregon lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 9915 people in Morrow County, Oregon lived in family households.

Sherman County was created on February 25, 1889 out of the northeast corner of Wasco County. The county's borders have been changed only once, in 1891, when the Legislative Assembly moved the county line 18 mi farther south into Wasco County.
