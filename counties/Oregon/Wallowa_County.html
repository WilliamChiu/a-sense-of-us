Wallowa County, Oregon

In 2000, 1087 people in Wallowa County, Oregon lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6030 people in Wallowa County, Oregon lived in family households.

In 2010, 4813 people in Tillamook County, Oregon lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 19961 people in Tillamook County, Oregon lived in family households.

In 1871, the first white settlers came to the area, crossing the mountains in search of livestock feed in the Wallowa Valley. The county was established on February 11, 1887, from the eastern portion of Union County. Boundary changes occurred with Union County in 1890, 1900, and 1915.
