Morrow County, Oregon

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.6903708%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1072 people in Morrow County, Oregon lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 9883 people in Morrow County, Oregon lived in family households.

In 2000, 40 people in Morrow County, Oregon lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 10995 people in Morrow County, Oregon lived in the county.

In 2010, 18492 people in Linn County, Oregon lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 96953 people in Linn County, Oregon lived in family households.

In 2010, 1227 people in Linn County, Oregon lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 116672 people in Linn County, Oregon lived in the county.

Morrow County was created in 1885 from the western portion of Umatilla County and a small portion of eastern Wasco County. Heppner was designated the temporary county seat at the time the county was created and narrowly defeated Lexington in the election held in 1887 to determine the permanent county seat.
