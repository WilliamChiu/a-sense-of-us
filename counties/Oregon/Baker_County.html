Baker County, Oregon

In 2000, 2560 people in Baker County, Oregon lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 13759 people in Baker County, Oregon lived in family households.

In 2010, 1627 people in Washita County, Oklahoma lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 9796 people in Washita County, Oklahoma lived in family households.

The first groups from the eastern U.S. following the Oregon Trail passed through the area on their way to the Willamette Valley, unaware of the potential wealth they passed over. At Flagstaff Hill, near Baker City, 15 mi of wagon ruts left by immigrants can still be seen.
