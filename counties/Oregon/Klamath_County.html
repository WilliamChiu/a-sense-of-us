Klamath County, Oregon

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.13215542%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 9875 people in Klamath County, Oregon lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 52887 people in Klamath County, Oregon lived in family households.

In 2000, 1013 people in Klamath County, Oregon lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 63775 people in Klamath County, Oregon lived in the county.

In 2010, 38144 people in Jackson County, Oregon lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 161570 people in Jackson County, Oregon lived in family households.

In 2010, 3492 people in Jackson County, Oregon lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 203206 people in Jackson County, Oregon lived in the county.

The Klamath or ''Clamitte'' tribe of Indians, for which Klamath County was named, are the descendants of varying cultures of indigenous peoples, who have lived in the area for more than 10,000 years.
