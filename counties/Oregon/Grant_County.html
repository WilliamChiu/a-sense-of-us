Grant County, Oregon

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.409483%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1194 people in Grant County, Oregon lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6569 people in Grant County, Oregon lived in family households.

In 2000, 172 people in Grant County, Oregon lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 7935 people in Grant County, Oregon lived in the county.

In 2010, 28196 people in Deschutes County, Oregon lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 128293 people in Deschutes County, Oregon lived in family households.

In 2010, 1244 people in Deschutes County, Oregon lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 157733 people in Deschutes County, Oregon lived in the county.

Grant County was established on October 14, 1864, from parts of old Wasco and old Umatilla counties. Prior to its creation, cases brought to court were tried in The Dalles, county seat of the vast Wasco County. The great distance to The Dalles made law enforcement a difficult problem, and imposed a heavy burden on citizens who had a need to transact business at the courthouse. In 1889, more than half of the southern part of the original Grant County was taken to form Harney County. Also in 1899, a small part of northwestern Grant County was taken (along with parts of Crook and Gilliam counties) to form Wheeler County.
