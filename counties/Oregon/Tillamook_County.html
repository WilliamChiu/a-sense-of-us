Tillamook County, Oregon

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.756979%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 4071 people in Tillamook County, Oregon lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 19725 people in Tillamook County, Oregon lived in family households.

In 2000, 466 people in Tillamook County, Oregon lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 24262 people in Tillamook County, Oregon lived in the county.

In 2010, 197859 people in Multnomah County, Oregon lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 517892 people in Multnomah County, Oregon lived in family households.

In 2010, 19583 people in Multnomah County, Oregon lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 735334 people in Multnomah County, Oregon lived in the county.

Tillamook County, the 12th county in Oregon to be organized, was established on December 15, 1853, when the Territorial Legislature approved an act to create the new county out of an area previously included in Clatsop, Yamhill and Polk counties. Boundary changes were enacted with Clatsop County (1855, 1870, and 1893), Lincoln County in 1893, Washington County (1893, 1898), and Yamhill County (1887).
