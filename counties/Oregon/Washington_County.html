Washington County, Oregon

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.9516945%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 72544 people in Washington County, Oregon lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 368697 people in Washington County, Oregon lived in family households.

In 2000, 4101 people in Washington County, Oregon lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 445342 people in Washington County, Oregon lived in the county.

In 2010, 4754 people in Union County, Oregon lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 20259 people in Union County, Oregon lived in family households.

In 2010, 735 people in Union County, Oregon lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 25748 people in Union County, Oregon lived in the county.

The Provisional Legislature of Oregon created the county as Twality District on July 5, 1843. Twality was one of the original four districts of the Provisional Government of Oregon in Oregon Country along with Clackamas, Champooick (later Marion), and Yamhill counties. Columbia, later known as Hillsboro, was selected as the county seat in 1850. Washington County obtained its present boundaries in 1854 with the creation of Columbia County to the north and Multnomah County to the east.
