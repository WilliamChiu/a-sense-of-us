Eaton County, Michigan

In 2000, 14414 people in Eaton County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 87507 people in Eaton County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2010, 2369 people in Crawford County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 11508 people in Crawford County, Michigan lived in family households.

Eaton County was formed in 1837 from open territory. It was named after John H. Eaton, an American Secretary of War. The county is one of the so-called Cabinet counties because it was named after a member of the cabinet of US President Andrew Jackson.
