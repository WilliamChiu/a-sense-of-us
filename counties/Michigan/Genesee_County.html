Genesee County, Michigan

In 2000, 64989 people in Genesee County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 365805 people in Genesee County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2010, 4717 people in Dickinson County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 20998 people in Dickinson County, Michigan lived in family households.

Flint was created on March 28, 1835 and fully organized on April 4, 1836, and was named after Genesee County, New York. Many local place names in the county are also from New York and Pennsylvania, reflecting the pattern of settlement. A major attraction for visitors is Crossroads Village, a living history village north of Flint.
