Gratiot County, Michigan

In 2000, 5004 people in Gratiot County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 32224 people in Gratiot County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2010, 4058 people in Gladwin County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 21345 people in Gladwin County, Michigan lived in family households.

The county is named for Captain Charles Gratiot, who supervised the building of Port Huron's Fort Gratiot. It was set off in 1831; organized in 1855.
