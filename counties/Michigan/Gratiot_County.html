Gratiot County, Michigan

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 12.306202%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 5004 people in Gratiot County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 32224 people in Gratiot County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2000, 5057 people in Gratiot County, Michigan lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 42285 people in Gratiot County, Michigan lived in the county.

In 2010, 4058 people in Gladwin County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 21345 people in Gladwin County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2010, 289 people in Gladwin County, Michigan lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 25692 people in Gladwin County, Michigan lived in the county.

The county is named for Captain Charles Gratiot, who supervised the building of Port Huron's Fort Gratiot. It was set off in 1831; organized in 1855.
