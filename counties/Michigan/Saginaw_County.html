Saginaw County, Michigan

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.47810674%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 29487 people in Saginaw County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 174952 people in Saginaw County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2000, 5600 people in Saginaw County, Michigan lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 210039 people in Saginaw County, Michigan lived in the county.

In 2010, 35053 people in Ottawa County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 220487 people in Ottawa County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2010, 8261 people in Ottawa County, Michigan lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 263801 people in Ottawa County, Michigan lived in the county.

What is today Saginaw County was inhabited by the Ojibwe before they were driven out by Euro-Americans. The Ojibwe were still the dominant force in the area in the 1820s and in 1827 they were attacked by a two groups of Winnebago people coming from Wisconsin. The Ojibwe prevailed in this fight with the aid of local Euro-American settlers.
