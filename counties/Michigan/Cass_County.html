Cass County, Michigan

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.8050382%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 6573 people in Cass County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 43787 people in Cass County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2000, 744 people in Cass County, Michigan lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 51104 people in Cass County, Michigan lived in the county.

In 2010, 25727 people in Berrien County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 127559 people in Berrien County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2010, 3527 people in Berrien County, Michigan lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 156813 people in Berrien County, Michigan lived in the county.

The county is named for Lewis Cass, the Michigan Territorial Governor at the time the county was created in 1829. Cass later served as the United States Secretary of War under President Andrew Jackson, thus making a case for including Cass County as one of Michigan's "cabinet counties".
