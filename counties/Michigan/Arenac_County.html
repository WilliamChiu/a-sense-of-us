Arenac County, Michigan

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 3.984964%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2340 people in Arenac County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14125 people in Arenac County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2000, 804 people in Arenac County, Michigan lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 17269 people in Arenac County, Michigan lived in the county.

In 2010, 14164 people in Allegan County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 96290 people in Allegan County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2010, 954 people in Allegan County, Michigan lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 111408 people in Allegan County, Michigan lived in the county.

Arenac County was organized in 1883 and the name ''Arenac'', coined by Henry Schoolcraft, is a derivation of the Latin "''arena''" and the Native American "''ac''". The combined words mean “A sandy place for a good footing.”
