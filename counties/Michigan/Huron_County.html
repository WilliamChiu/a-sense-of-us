Huron County, Michigan

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -11.415232%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 5060 people in Huron County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 30337 people in Huron County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2000, 682 people in Huron County, Michigan lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 36079 people in Huron County, Michigan lived in the county.

In 2010, 5659 people in Gratiot County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 31257 people in Gratiot County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2010, 5560 people in Gratiot County, Michigan lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 42476 people in Gratiot County, Michigan lived in the county.

Huron County was attached originally to Sanilac and Tuscola counties. It was created by Michigan Law on April 1, 1840 and was fully organized by an Act of Legislature on January 25, 1859. Sand Beach (now Harbor Beach) was then county seat, until 1865, when the court house burned with nearly all the records. It was moved to Port Austin and remained there until 1873, when the Board of Supervisors permanently made Bad Axe the County Seat.
