Mason County, Michigan

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.0279179%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4186 people in Mason County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 23553 people in Mason County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2000, 535 people in Mason County, Michigan lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 28274 people in Mason County, Michigan lived in the county.

In 2010, 128952 people in Macomb County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 704594 people in Macomb County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2010, 7432 people in Macomb County, Michigan lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 840978 people in Macomb County, Michigan lived in the county.

The county is named for Stevens T. Mason, Governor of Michigan. It was set off in 1840 as Notipekago, but its name was changed in 1843. The county was organized in 1855.
