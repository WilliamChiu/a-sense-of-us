Washtenaw County, Michigan

In 2000, 74259 people in Washtenaw County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 227334 people in Washtenaw County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2010, 9985 people in Shiawassee County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 59842 people in Shiawassee County, Michigan lived in family households.

The earliest histories mention trade conducted in the area at the Potawatomi Trail and Pontiac Trail crossings of the Huron River by French traders, and later English then American settlers. The first successful settlement was established at the present site of Ypsilanti about 1809 by French traders.
