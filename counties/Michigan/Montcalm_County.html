Montcalm County is a county located in the U.S. state of Michigan. As of the 2010 census, the population was 63,342. The county seat is Stanton. The county is named for General Marquis Louis-Joseph de Montcalm, French military commander of troops in Canada during the French and Indian War. The county was set off in 1831 and organized in 1850.
