Presque Isle County, Michigan

In 2000, 2214 people in Presque Isle County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 11976 people in Presque Isle County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2010, 1535 people in Oscoda County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7040 people in Oscoda County, Michigan lived in family households.

Early Native Americans living in the area were nomadic, using the land as hunting grounds. To them the land between the Ocqueoc and Swan Rivers was sacred ground. The name "Presque Isle" was given to the area by fur traders who portaged over the strip of land that attaches Presque Isle to the main land
