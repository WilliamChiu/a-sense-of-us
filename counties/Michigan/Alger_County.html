Alger County, Michigan

In 2000, 1426 people in Alger County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 7450 people in Alger County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2010, 219259 people in Suffolk County, Massachusetts lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 455536 people in Suffolk County, Massachusetts lived in family households.

The county was named for lumber baron Russell Alexander Alger, who was elected as a Michigan Governor, and U.S. Senator, and appointed as U.S. Secretary of War during the William McKinley Presidential administration.
