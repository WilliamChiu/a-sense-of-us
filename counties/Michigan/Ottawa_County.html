Ottawa County, Michigan

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.8164158%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. In 2000, 202469 people in Ottawa County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2000, 238314 people in Ottawa County, Michigan lived in the county.

In 2010, 3374 people in Osceola County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 455 people in Osceola County, Michigan lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 23528 people in Osceola County, Michigan lived in the county.

Ottawa County is a county located in the U.S. state of Michigan. As of the 2010 census, the population was 263,801. The county seat is Grand Haven. The county is named for the Ottawa Nation. It was set off in 1831 and organized in 1837.
