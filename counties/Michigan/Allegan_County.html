Allegan County, Michigan

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.6015887%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 12084 people in Allegan County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 91664 people in Allegan County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2000, 1917 people in Allegan County, Michigan lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 105665 people in Allegan County, Michigan lived in the county.

In 2010, 127370 people in Worcester County, Massachusetts lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 644137 people in Worcester County, Massachusetts lived in family households.

In 2010, 27045 people in Worcester County, Massachusetts lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 798552 people in Worcester County, Massachusetts lived in the county.

The boundaries of Allegan County were laid out by the legislature in 1831. The county was later organized in 1835. At that time there were only about four families permanently settled in the area. Among the first settlers of Allegan County were Giles Scott and Turner Aldrich.
