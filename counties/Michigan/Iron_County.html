Iron County, Michigan

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.3024082%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2374 people in Iron County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10189 people in Iron County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2000, 575 people in Iron County, Michigan lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 13138 people in Iron County, Michigan lived in the county.

In 2010, 68485 people in Ingham County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 193932 people in Ingham County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2010, 18478 people in Ingham County, Michigan lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 280895 people in Ingham County, Michigan lived in the county.

Iron County was organized in 1885 from territory taken from Marquette and Menominee counties. In 1890 the population of the county was 4,432. It was named for the valuable iron ore found within its borders.
