Kent County, Michigan

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -3.8956404%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 88284 people in Kent County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 473262 people in Kent County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2000, 12789 people in Kent County, Michigan lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 574335 people in Kent County, Michigan lived in the county.

In 2010, 24171 people in Jackson County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 126405 people in Jackson County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2010, 9672 people in Jackson County, Michigan lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 160248 people in Jackson County, Michigan lived in the county.

The Grand River runs through the county. On its west bank are burial mounds, remnants of the Hopewell Indians who once lived there. The valley of the river served as an important center for the fur trade in the early 19th century. After the War of 1812, Rix Robinson and Louis Campau were the earliest traders in the area.
