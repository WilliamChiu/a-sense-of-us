Clare County, Michigan

In 2000, 4737 people in Clare County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 26014 people in Clare County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2010, 4184 people in Charlevoix County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 21486 people in Charlevoix County, Michigan lived in family households.

The county was set off in 1840 as Kaykekee County from Mackinac County. It was renamed Clare in 1843 after County Clare in Ireland. It was officially organized in 1871. Farwell was the first county seat; in 1877 the county seat was moved to Harrison.
