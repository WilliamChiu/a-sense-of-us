Ingham County, Michigan

In 2000, 63073 people in Ingham County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 199420 people in Ingham County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2010, 6498 people in Hillsdale County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 38549 people in Hillsdale County, Michigan lived in family households.

Ingham County was established by an act of the Michigan Territorial Legislature on October 29, 1829, from portions of Shiawassee County, Washtenaw County and unorganized territory. It was attached for administrative purposes to Washtenaw County until 1838 when county government was established for Ingham.
