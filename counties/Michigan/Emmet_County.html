Emmet County, Michigan

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.5977392%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4878 people in Emmet County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 25847 people in Emmet County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2000, 712 people in Emmet County, Michigan lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 31437 people in Emmet County, Michigan lived in the county.

In 2010, 6607 people in Delta County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 29839 people in Delta County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2010, 623 people in Delta County, Michigan lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 37069 people in Delta County, Michigan lived in the county.

Ottawa history records that Emmet County was thickly populated by a race of Indians that they called the Mush-co-desh, which means "the prairie tribe". The Mush-co-desh had an agrarian society and were said to have "shaped the land by making the woodland into prairie as they abandoned their old worn out gardens which formed grassy plains". Ottawa tradition claims that they slaughtered from forty to fifty thousand Mush-co-desh and drove the rest from the land after the Mush-co-desh insulted an Ottawa war party.
