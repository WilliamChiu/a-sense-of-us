Oscoda County is a county located in the U.S. state of Michigan. As of the 2010 census, the population was 8,640, making it the fifth-least populous county in Michigan. The county seat is Mio. The county was founded in 1840 and organized in 1881. The name is a Henry Schoolcraft neologism thought to be a combination of two Ojibwa words, "ossin" (stone) and "muskoda" (prairie) -- hence 'pebbly prairie.'
