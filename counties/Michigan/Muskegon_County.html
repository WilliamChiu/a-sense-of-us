Muskegon County, Michigan

In 2000, 22973 people in Muskegon County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 141168 people in Muskegon County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2010, 19698 people in Monroe County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 130861 people in Monroe County, Michigan lived in family households.

Around 1812, Jean Baptiste Recollect and Pierre Constant set up trading posts in the area. By the Treaty of Washington (1836), Native Americans ceded parts of Michigan, including future Muskegon County, opening the area to greater settlement.
