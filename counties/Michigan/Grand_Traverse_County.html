Grand Traverse County, Michigan

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.9675026%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 12340 people in Grand Traverse County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 63491 people in Grand Traverse County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2000, 1823 people in Grand Traverse County, Michigan lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 77654 people in Grand Traverse County, Michigan lived in the county.

In 2010, 69545 people in Genesee County, Michigan lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 350272 people in Genesee County, Michigan lived in family households.

In 2010, 5973 people in Genesee County, Michigan lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 425790 people in Genesee County, Michigan lived in the county.

In 1840 it was separated and originally named Omeena County. Grand Traverse County was organized by an act of the state legislature on April 7, 1851. Grand Traverse is derived from a French phrase meaning "long crossing" and the county is so named because it is situated at the Grand Traverse Bay. The first permanent settlement in the county was the mission now known as Old Mission.
