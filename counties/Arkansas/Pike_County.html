Pike County, Arkansas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.15748739%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1369 people in Pike County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 9752 people in Pike County, Arkansas lived in family households.

In 2000, 182 people in Pike County, Arkansas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 11303 people in Pike County, Arkansas lived in the county.

In 2010, 1415 people in Perry County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 8878 people in Perry County, Arkansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 152 people in Perry County, Arkansas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 10445 people in Perry County, Arkansas lived in the county.

The first known residents of the area now considered Pike County were Native Americans. The Quapaw tribe was prominent in the area, as well as the Kadohadacho, and Cahinnio tribes. Expeditions led by Hernando de Soto and Sieur de La Salle passed through the area. Around 1800, the Kadohadocho tribe migrated to Texas to avoid further repeated attacks by the Osage, who would venture in from the Oklahoma area.
