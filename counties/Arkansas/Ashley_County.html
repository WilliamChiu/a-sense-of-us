Ashley County, Arkansas

In 2000, 2793 people in Ashley County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 21176 people in Ashley County, Arkansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 19620 people in Yuma County, Arizona lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 170210 people in Yuma County, Arizona lived in family households.

Ashley County, the fifth-largest county in Arkansas in terms of land area, was formed on November 30, 1848 from portions of Drew, Chicot and Union Counties. It was named after Chester Ashley, a US Senator and land speculator. The final borders were laid in 1861. The courtroom in the courthouse has a one-of-a-kind architecture: it is round, and the seats are arranged so that members of the audience can see always each other.
