Conway County, Arkansas

In 2000, 2512 people in Conway County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 17475 people in Conway County, Arkansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 992 people in Cleveland County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7645 people in Cleveland County, Arkansas lived in family households.

Conway County was formed on October 20, 1825 from a portion of Pulaski County and named for Henry Wharton Conway who was the territorial delegate to the U.S. Congress. From 1831 until 1883, Lewisburg was the county seat.
