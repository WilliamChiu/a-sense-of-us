Household Populations

Montgomery County, Arkansas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.47677755%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1168 people in Montgomery County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 7968 people in Montgomery County, Arkansas lived in family households.

In 2000, 109 people in Montgomery County, Arkansas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 9245 people in Montgomery County, Arkansas lived in the county.

In 2010, 6580 people in Mississippi County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 39133 people in Mississippi County, Arkansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 767 people in Mississippi County, Arkansas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 46480 people in Mississippi County, Arkansas lived in the county.

Stone spear and dart points found in the area verify that people from the Dalton Culture were present in Mongomery County around 8500 BC. Early signs of houses and American Indian cemeteries are present in and around Caddo Gap, Arkansas, indicating the definite presence of the Caddo Indians having settled in the area in the 13th century and 14th century. In 1541, the explorer Hernando de Soto fought the Tula Indians at Caddo Gap, and that he was injured during that battle.
