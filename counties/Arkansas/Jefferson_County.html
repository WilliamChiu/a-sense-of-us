Jefferson County, Arkansas

In 2000, 10361 people in Jefferson County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 68628 people in Jefferson County, Arkansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 2145 people in Izard County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 10782 people in Izard County, Arkansas lived in family households.

The area that would later become Jefferson County was occupied by the Quapaw when French explorers established the Arkansas Post in the 17th century, in what was claimed as Louisiana, part of New France.
