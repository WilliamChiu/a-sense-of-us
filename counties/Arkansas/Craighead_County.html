Craighead County, Arkansas

In 2000, 12883 people in Craighead County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 66482 people in Craighead County, Arkansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 4152 people in Columbia County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 19085 people in Columbia County, Arkansas lived in family households.

Craighead County was part of the territory claimed by France on April 9, 1682 by René Robert Cavelier, Sieur de La Salle who laid claim to all of the land drained by the Mississippi River and its assorted tributaries. LaSalle's claim was named Louisiana in honor of Louis XIV, King of France.
