Boone County, Arkansas

In 2000, 4563 people in Boone County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 28785 people in Boone County, Arkansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 7406 people in Baxter County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 33512 people in Baxter County, Arkansas lived in family households.

Boone County was formed from the eastern portion of Carroll County. Contrary to popular belief, it was not named for frontiersman Daniel Boone. It was originally called ''Boon'', since the residents believed it would be a "boon" to all who settled there. The ''e'' was added to the name later.
