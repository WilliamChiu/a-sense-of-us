Greene County, Arkansas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.6914735%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 4662 people in Greene County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 32081 people in Greene County, Arkansas lived in family households.

In 2000, 588 people in Greene County, Arkansas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 37331 people in Greene County, Arkansas lived in the county.

In 2010, 17395 people in Garland County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 76463 people in Garland County, Arkansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 2166 people in Garland County, Arkansas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 96024 people in Garland County, Arkansas lived in the county.

Greene County was formed on 5 November 1833 out of portions of Lawrence County and contained portions of present Clay and Craighead counties. The county was named after Revolutionary War hero Nathanael Greene. The original county seat was in Benjamin Crowley's home. By 1836, when Arkansas became a state, the county seat was located at a settlement called "Paris" (not to be confused with present-day Paris, Arkansas).
