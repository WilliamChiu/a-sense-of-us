Garland County, Arkansas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.36069155%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 14601 people in Garland County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 71606 people in Garland County, Arkansas lived in family households.

In 2000, 1861 people in Garland County, Arkansas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 88068 people in Garland County, Arkansas lived in the county.

In 2010, 2364 people in Franklin County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 15314 people in Franklin County, Arkansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 447 people in Franklin County, Arkansas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 18125 people in Franklin County, Arkansas lived in the county.

Garland County is Arkansas's 68th county, was formed on April 5, 1873, from portions of Saline County. It was named for Augustus H. Garland, eleventh governor of Arkansas.
