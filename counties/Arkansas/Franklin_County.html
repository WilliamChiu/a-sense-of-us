Franklin County, Arkansas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.5658259%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2193 people in Franklin County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 15095 people in Franklin County, Arkansas lived in family households.

In 2000, 483 people in Franklin County, Arkansas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 17771 people in Franklin County, Arkansas lived in the county.

In 2010, 2860 people in Drew County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 14864 people in Drew County, Arkansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 785 people in Drew County, Arkansas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 18509 people in Drew County, Arkansas lived in the county.

Franklin County was carved out of Crawford County in December 1837. At that time, Franklin was significantly larger than it is at present, encompassing part of present-day Logan County which was formed in 1871.
