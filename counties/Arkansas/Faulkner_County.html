Faulkner County, Arkansas

In 2000, 12360 people in Faulkner County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 69582 people in Faulkner County, Arkansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 1932 people in Desha County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 11020 people in Desha County, Arkansas lived in family households.

Faulkner County was formed from parts of Conway and Pulaski counties on April 12, 1873, and is named after Colonel Sanford C. 'Sandy' Faulkner who was the composer of the popular fiddle tune "The Arkansas Traveler".
