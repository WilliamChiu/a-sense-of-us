Clark County, Arkansas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 10.041857%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 3975 people in Clark County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 17243 people in Clark County, Arkansas lived in family households.

In 2000, 2328 people in Clark County, Arkansas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 23546 people in Clark County, Arkansas lived in the county.

In 2010, 4691 people in Carroll County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 22525 people in Carroll County, Arkansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 230 people in Carroll County, Arkansas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 27446 people in Carroll County, Arkansas lived in the county.

Clark County was Arkansas' third county, formed on December 15, 1818, alongside Hempstead and Pulaski counties. The county is named after William Clark who at the time was Governor of the Missouri Territory, which included present-day Arkansas. Arkadelphia was named as the county seat in 1842.
