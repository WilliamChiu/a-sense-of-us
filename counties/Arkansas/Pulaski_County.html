Pulaski County, Arkansas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -3.1818569%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 61829 people in Pulaski County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 291392 people in Pulaski County, Arkansas lived in family households.

In 2000, 8253 people in Pulaski County, Arkansas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 361474 people in Pulaski County, Arkansas lived in the county.

In 2010, 8991 people in Pope County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 49433 people in Pope County, Arkansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 3330 people in Pope County, Arkansas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 61754 people in Pope County, Arkansas lived in the county.

An 1863 American Civil War battle, the Battle of Bayou Fourche, occurred in Pulaski County. Pulaski County is also home to Willow Springs Water Park, which is one of the oldest waterparks in the nation, opening in 1928.
