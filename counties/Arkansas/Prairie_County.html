Prairie County, Arkansas

In 2000, 1229 people in Prairie County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 8170 people in Prairie County, Arkansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 2966 people in Polk County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 17541 people in Polk County, Arkansas lived in family households.

The county was at first land given to Cherokee Indian settlers from Tennessee and was formerly the '''Western band of Cherokee''' Reservation from 1812 to 1836. Today, an estimated 2,000 residents have some degree of American Indian ancestry.
