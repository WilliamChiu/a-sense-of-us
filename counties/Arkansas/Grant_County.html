Grant County, Arkansas

In 2000, 1707 people in Grant County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14576 people in Grant County, Arkansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 1912 people in Fulton County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 10168 people in Fulton County, Arkansas lived in family households.

Formed on February 4, 1869, Grant County is named for former U.S. President Ulysses S. Grant. It is an alcohol prohibition or dry county.
