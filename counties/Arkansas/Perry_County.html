Perry County, Arkansas

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.94007254%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. In 2000, 1201 people in Perry County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 8851 people in Perry County, Arkansas lived in family households.

In 2000, 157 people in Perry County, Arkansas lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 10209 people in Perry County, Arkansas lived in the county.

In 2010, 1370 people in Newton County, Arkansas lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 6909 people in Newton County, Arkansas lived in family households.

In 2010, 51 people in Newton County, Arkansas lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 8330 people in Newton County, Arkansas lived in the county.

Perry County is a county located in the U.S. state of Arkansas. Its population was 10,445 at the 2010 United States Census. The county seat is Perryville. The county was formed on December 18, 1840, and named for Commodore Oliver Hazard Perry, naval hero in the War of 1812. It is an alcohol prohibition or dry county.
