Poweshiek County, Iowa

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 6.3091755%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 3015 people in Poweshiek County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14342 people in Poweshiek County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2000, 1458 people in Poweshiek County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 18815 people in Poweshiek County, Iowa lived in the county.

In 2010, 1339 people in Pocahontas County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5830 people in Pocahontas County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 141 people in Pocahontas County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 7310 people in Pocahontas County, Iowa lived in the county.

Poweshiek County was formed in 1843. It was named for the chief of the Fox Indians. The Poweshiek County Courthouse, completed in 1859, is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.
