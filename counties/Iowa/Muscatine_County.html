Muscatine County, Iowa

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.43607354%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 5473 people in Muscatine County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 35525 people in Muscatine County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2000, 724 people in Muscatine County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 41722 people in Muscatine County, Iowa lived in the county.

In 2010, 1766 people in Monona County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7277 people in Monona County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 200 people in Monona County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 9243 people in Monona County, Iowa lived in the county.

Muscatine County was formed on December 7, 1836. One history of the county claims it was named for Muscatine Island in the Mississippi River.
