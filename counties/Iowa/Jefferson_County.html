Jefferson County, Iowa

In 2000, 2806 people in Jefferson County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 12747 people in Jefferson County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 2472 people in Iowa County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 13593 people in Iowa County, Iowa lived in family households.

Jefferson County is a county located in the U.S. state of Iowa. As of the 2010 census, the population was 16,843. The county seat is Fairfield. The county was formed on January 21, 1839 and named after President Thomas Jefferson.
