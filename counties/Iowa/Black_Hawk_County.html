Black Hawk County, Iowa

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 4.2994976%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 24469 people in Black Hawk County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 97066 people in Black Hawk County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2000, 6477 people in Black Hawk County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 128012 people in Black Hawk County, Iowa lived in the county.

In 2010, 2428 people in Appanoose County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 10333 people in Appanoose County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 126 people in Appanoose County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 12887 people in Appanoose County, Iowa lived in the county.

Black Hawk County was formed on February 17, 1843 from sections of Buchanan County. It was named after Black Hawk, a Sauk leader during the 1832 Black Hawk War.
