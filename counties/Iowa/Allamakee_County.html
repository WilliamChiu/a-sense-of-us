Allamakee County, Iowa

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.5411496%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2124 people in Allamakee County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 12139 people in Allamakee County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2000, 412 people in Allamakee County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 14675 people in Allamakee County, Iowa lived in the county.

In 2010, 4577 people in Whitley County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 28279 people in Whitley County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 436 people in Whitley County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 33292 people in Whitley County, Indiana lived in the county.

Allamakee County was formed on February 20, 1847. The derivation of the name is debated, some believing it was the name of an Indian chief, others think it was named for Allen Magee, an early historic trader. The first Allamakee County Courthouse, built in 1861, now serves as the Allamakee County Historical Museum. The present Allamakee County Court House was built in 1940. Both courthouse buildings are listed on the National Register of Historic Places.
