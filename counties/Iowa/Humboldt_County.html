Humboldt County, Iowa

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.36004186%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1580 people in Humboldt County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 8641 people in Humboldt County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2000, 160 people in Humboldt County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 10381 people in Humboldt County, Iowa lived in the county.

In 2010, 2168 people in Harrison County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 12477 people in Harrison County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 283 people in Harrison County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 14928 people in Harrison County, Iowa lived in the county.

Humboldt County was established in 1851 as the smallest county in Iowa. It was named after Alexander von Humboldt. On July 1, 1855, the county lines were newly established, adding some land from Kossuth County and Webster County. On February 26, 1857, the old borders were reestablished, and Webster County would not return the land, leaving Humboldt County with only twelve townships. In 1872, Humboldt College was opened and closed in 1916 because there was no agreement with the county about taxation.
