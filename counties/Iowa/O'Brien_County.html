O'Brien County, Iowa

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.4796844%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2122 people in O'Brien County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 12389 people in O'Brien County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2000, 591 people in O'Brien County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 15102 people in O'Brien County, Iowa lived in the county.

In 2010, 1223 people in Monroe County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 6625 people in Monroe County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 122 people in Monroe County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 7970 people in Monroe County, Iowa lived in the county.

O'Brien County was founded in 1851. The county was named for William Smith O'Brien, a leader for Irish independence in 1848. The present courthouse was completed in 1917, and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.
