Warren County, Iowa

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.4704108%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4196 people in Warren County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 34760 people in Warren County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2000, 1715 people in Warren County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 40671 people in Warren County, Iowa lived in the county.

In 2010, 2295 people in Union County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 9887 people in Union County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 352 people in Union County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 12534 people in Union County, Iowa lived in the county.

Warren County was formed in 1846. It was named for General Joseph Warren, a hero in the American Revolutionary War. The present Warren County Court House was opened in 1939.
