Household Populations

Davis County, Iowa

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.42889118%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1069 people in Davis County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 7288 people in Davis County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2000, 184 people in Davis County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 8541 people in Davis County, Iowa lived in the county.

In 2010, 8473 people in Clinton County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 39791 people in Clinton County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 852 people in Clinton County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 49116 people in Clinton County, Iowa lived in the county.

Davis County was named in honor of Garrett Davis, a Congressman from Kentucky from March 4, 1839, till March 3, 1847, and later a US Senator from Kentucky.
