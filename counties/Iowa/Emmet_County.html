Emmet County, Iowa

In 2000, 1790 people in Emmet County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 8700 people in Emmet County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 7244 people in Des Moines County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 32396 people in Des Moines County, Iowa lived in family households.

Emmet County was authorized by the Iowa Legislature in 1851,. There were finally enough settlers in the area to organize the county in 1859. The county was named for the Irish patriot Robert Emmet, who was hanged as a traitor to the British government at the age of 23.
