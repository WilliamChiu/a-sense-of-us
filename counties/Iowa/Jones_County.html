Jones County, Iowa

In 2000, 2697 people in Jones County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 15959 people in Jones County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 5485 people in Jasper County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 29709 people in Jasper County, Iowa lived in family households.

Jones County is a county located in the U.S. state of Iowa. As of the 2010 census, the population was 20,638. The county seat is Anamosa. The county was founded in 1837 and named after George Wallace Jones, a United States Senator and member of Congress.
