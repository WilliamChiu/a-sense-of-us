Taylor County, Iowa

In 2000, 1056 people in Taylor County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

Taylor County is a county located in the U.S. state of Iowa. As of the 2010 census, the population was 6,317, making it the fourth-least populous county in Iowa. The county seat is Bedford. The county was formed in 1847 and named after General and afterward President Zachary Taylor.
