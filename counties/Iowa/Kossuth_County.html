Kossuth County, Iowa

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -4.268426%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2402 people in Kossuth County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14450 people in Kossuth County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2000, 311 people in Kossuth County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 17163 people in Kossuth County, Iowa lived in the county.

In 2010, 38274 people in Johnson County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 84751 people in Johnson County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 7857 people in Johnson County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 130882 people in Johnson County, Iowa lived in the county.

Kossuth County was founded on January 15, 1851. It was named after Lajos Kossuth, Regent-President of Hungary who went to exile to America following the war of independence of Hungary from Austria. The county was enlarged northward in 1857 by the inclusion of the former Bancroft County.
