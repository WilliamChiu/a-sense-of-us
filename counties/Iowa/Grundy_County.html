Grundy County, Iowa

In 2000, 1561 people in Grundy County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10635 people in Grundy County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 1658 people in Franklin County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 8824 people in Franklin County, Iowa lived in family households.

Grundy County was formed on January 15, 1851, and became self-governing in 1856. It was named after Felix Grundy of Tennessee, a statesman, Senator, member of the House of Representatives and Attorney General under President James K. Polk.
