Louisa County, Iowa

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.28663874%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1449 people in Louisa County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10567 people in Louisa County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2000, 167 people in Louisa County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 12183 people in Louisa County, Iowa lived in the county.

In 2010, 2575 people in Kossuth County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 12711 people in Kossuth County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 257 people in Kossuth County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 15543 people in Kossuth County, Iowa lived in the county.

Louisa County was formed on December 7, 1836. It was named after Louisa Massey, who was very well known here at the time because she avenged the murder of her brother when she shot the party responsible.
