Floyd County, Iowa

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.677382%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2403 people in Floyd County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14002 people in Floyd County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2000, 495 people in Floyd County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 16900 people in Floyd County, Iowa lived in the county.

In 2010, 15643 people in Dubuque County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 73742 people in Dubuque County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 4268 people in Dubuque County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 93653 people in Dubuque County, Iowa lived in the county.

Floyd County was established in 1854 and was named for Sgt. Charles Floyd of the Lewis and Clark Expedition, who died in 1804 near what is now Sioux City, Iowa, and who was the only member thereof to die on the Expedition.
