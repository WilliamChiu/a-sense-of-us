Sioux County, Iowa

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 6.7979217%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2980 people in Sioux County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 25961 people in Sioux County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2000, 2648 people in Sioux County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 31589 people in Sioux County, Iowa lived in the county.

In 2010, 1786 people in Sac County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 8341 people in Sac County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 223 people in Sac County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 10350 people in Sac County, Iowa lived in the county.

Sioux County was formed on January 15, 1851. It has been self-governed since January 20, 1860. It was named after the Sioux tribe.
