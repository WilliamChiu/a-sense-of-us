Lyon County, Iowa

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.7411957%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1274 people in Lyon County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10287 people in Lyon County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2000, 202 people in Lyon County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 11763 people in Lyon County, Iowa lived in the county.

In 2010, 40438 people in Linn County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 165622 people in Linn County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 5166 people in Linn County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 211226 people in Linn County, Iowa lived in the county.

The land that makes up Lyon County was ceded to the federal government by the Sioux Indians through a treaty signed on July 23, 1851. The boundaries of the county were set on January 15, 1851 and attached to Woodbury County (then called Wahkaw County) for administration purposes. Lyon County officially split from Woodbury County on January 1, 1872.
