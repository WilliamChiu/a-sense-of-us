Johnson County, Iowa

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 6.6814065%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 31367 people in Johnson County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 71633 people in Johnson County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2000, 8006 people in Johnson County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 111006 people in Johnson County, Iowa lived in the county.

In 2010, 3180 people in Jackson County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 16467 people in Jackson County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 201 people in Jackson County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 19848 people in Jackson County, Iowa lived in the county.

Johnson County was created on December 21, 1837 by the legislature of the Wisconsin Territory. The county was carved out of territory formerly in Dubuque County, and was not initially provided with a civil government, instead being governed by Cedar County officials. It was named for the US Vice President Richard M. Johnson.
