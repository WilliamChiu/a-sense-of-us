Keokuk County, Iowa

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -8.837867%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1642 people in Keokuk County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 9578 people in Keokuk County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2000, 180 people in Keokuk County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 11400 people in Keokuk County, Iowa lived in the county.

In 2010, 3314 people in Jefferson County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 11798 people in Jefferson County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 1731 people in Jefferson County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 16843 people in Jefferson County, Iowa lived in the county.

Keokuk County was formed in 1837. It was named for the eponymous chief of the Sac Indians, who advocated peace with the white settlers. In May 1843, the county opened for public settlement and was self-governed from 1844. Previously it was governed by Washington County. After a debate lasting a few years for the location for the county seat, the right went to Sigourney in 1856.
