Household Populations

Mahaska County, Iowa

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.7567277%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 3196 people in Mahaska County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 18561 people in Mahaska County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2000, 578 people in Mahaska County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 22335 people in Mahaska County, Iowa lived in the county.

In 2010, 1431 people in Lucas County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7389 people in Lucas County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 78 people in Lucas County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 8898 people in Lucas County, Iowa lived in the county.

Mahaska County was formed on February 17, 1843. The county has been self-governing since February 5, 1844. It was named after Chief Mahaska of the Iowa tribe. The county was the first in Iowa to have a sheriff and a justice of peace.
