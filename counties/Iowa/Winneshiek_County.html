Winneshiek County, Iowa

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 10.465061%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 3119 people in Winneshiek County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 15898 people in Winneshiek County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2000, 2293 people in Winneshiek County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 21310 people in Winneshiek County, Iowa lived in the county.

In 2010, 1059 people in Wayne County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5253 people in Wayne County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 91 people in Wayne County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 6403 people in Wayne County, Iowa lived in the county.

The county was originally settled in 1848 in present day Bloomfield Township (near Castalia, Iowa) and in Washington Township (near Fort Atkinson, Iowa). It was organized in 1847, and named after a chief of the Winnebago tribe.
