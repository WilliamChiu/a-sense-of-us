Washington County, Iowa

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.1808395%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2816 people in Washington County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 17351 people in Washington County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2000, 503 people in Washington County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 20670 people in Washington County, Iowa lived in the county.

In 2010, 1219 people in Van Buren County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 6254 people in Van Buren County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 97 people in Van Buren County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 7570 people in Van Buren County, Iowa lived in the county.

Washington County was originally formed on January 18, 1838 as Slaughter County in honor of William B. Slaughter, the secretary of Wisconsin Territory. A short time later it was renamed Washington County, in honor of George Washington. The first settlers arrived in Washington County in 1835. The homesteads did not start until 1836. A town was built in the present day township of Oregon named Astoria. It became the first county seat and housed the first court house. In 1839, Washington became the final location of the seat of justice.
