Dallas County, Iowa

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.46192408%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 5312 people in Dallas County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 34979 people in Dallas County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2000, 459 people in Dallas County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 40750 people in Dallas County, Iowa lived in the county.

In 2010, 3068 people in Clayton County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 14774 people in Clayton County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 287 people in Clayton County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 18129 people in Clayton County, Iowa lived in the county.

The land that now forms Dallas County was ceded by the Sac and Fox nation to the United States in a treaty signed on October 11, 1842, and the county was formed in 1846. In 1847 the first pioneers selected a site for the county seat, which was first called ''Penoach'' and then later Adel. Once the prairie sod was "broken" by the agricultural plow, settlement was rapid in the 1850s and 1860s, with the population crossing the 12,000 mark by 1870.
