Plymouth County, Iowa

In 2000, 2964 people in Plymouth County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 21506 people in Plymouth County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 1043 people in Osceola County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5307 people in Osceola County, Iowa lived in family households.

Plymouth County was formed on January 15, 1851. Settlement began in the county in 1856. In October, 1859, the first courthouse was built and the first public school opened its doors with 32 pupils. In 1872, the county seat was moved to Le Mars and a new courthouse and a jail were built there in 1873. The courthouse in present use was built in 1900 of red sandstone.
