Wayne County, Iowa

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.4817605%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 978 people in Wayne County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 5620 people in Wayne County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2000, 132 people in Wayne County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 6730 people in Wayne County, Iowa lived in the county.

In 2010, 6314 people in Wapello County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 28444 people in Wapello County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 867 people in Wapello County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 35625 people in Wapello County, Iowa lived in the county.

Wayne County was formed in 1846 but was still attached to other counties for governmental purposes. It was named after General Anthony Wayne.
