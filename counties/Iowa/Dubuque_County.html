Dubuque County, Iowa

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 4.0805697%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 12650 people in Dubuque County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 71947 people in Dubuque County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2000, 4546 people in Dubuque County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 89143 people in Dubuque County, Iowa lived in the county.

In 2010, 2468 people in Delaware County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 15078 people in Delaware County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 218 people in Delaware County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 17764 people in Delaware County, Iowa lived in the county.

Dubuque County is named for French trader Julien Dubuque, the first European settler of Iowa, and an early lead mining pioneer in what is now Dubuque County. Dubuque was French Canadian, and had (by most accounts) a friendly relationship with the local Fox tribe of Native Americans. He and other early pioneers established a lucrative mining and trading industry in the area. When lead deposits began becoming exhausted, the pioneers developed boat building, lumber yards, milling, brewing, and machinery manufacturing to take its place.
