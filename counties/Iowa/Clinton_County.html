Clinton County, Iowa

In 2000, 7571 people in Clinton County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 41555 people in Clinton County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2010, 1494 people in Clarke County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7640 people in Clarke County, Iowa lived in family households.

Clinton County was formed on December 21, 1837. It was named for DeWitt Clinton, a Governor of New York and most ardent advocate for the construction of the Erie Canal. The cities of DeWitt and Clinton were also named after him.
