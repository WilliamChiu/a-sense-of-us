Linn County, Iowa

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.3392448%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 33060 people in Linn County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 153588 people in Linn County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2000, 5053 people in Linn County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 191701 people in Linn County, Iowa lived in the county.

In 2010, 1702 people in Keokuk County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 140 people in Keokuk County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 10511 people in Keokuk County, Iowa lived in the county.

Linn County was organized by the first legislative assembly of the Iowa Territory on January 15, 1839. A site was selected for its first county seat along Indian Creek, and was named Marion, after the Revolutionary War general Francis Marion. As early as 1855, there were debates over moving the county seat to the fast-growing Cedar Rapids, southwest of Marion, but it was not until November 6, 1919, that there were enough votes in favor of the move (9,960 to 4,823). The first rail line was built through Cedar Rapids in 1859, and made the town (and the county) a major commercial hub in eastern Iowa.
