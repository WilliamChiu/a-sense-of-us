Adams County, Iowa

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.4709711%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 718 people in Adams County, Iowa lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 3644 people in Adams County, Iowa lived in family households.

In 2000, 120 people in Adams County, Iowa lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 4482 people in Adams County, Iowa lived in the county.

In 2010, 3529 people in White County, Indiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 20807 people in White County, Indiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 307 people in White County, Indiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 24643 people in White County, Indiana lived in the county.

Adams County was established by the state legislature in 1851 and named in honor of the second President of the United States, John Adams, or his son, the sixth President, John Quincy Adams (sources differ). The county was finally organized and separated from Pottawattamie County on March 12, 1853. Its original size was later reduced by the creation of Montgomery and Union counties.
