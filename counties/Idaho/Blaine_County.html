Blaine County, Idaho

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.3662934%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 3968 people in Blaine County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14670 people in Blaine County, Idaho lived in family households.

In 2000, 353 people in Blaine County, Idaho lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 18991 people in Blaine County, Idaho lived in the county.

In 2010, 708 people in Bear Lake County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5247 people in Bear Lake County, Idaho lived in family households.

In 2010, 31 people in Bear Lake County, Idaho lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 5986 people in Bear Lake County, Idaho lived in the county.

The Wood River Valley in present-day Blaine County was organized as part of Alturas County by the Idaho Territorial Legislature in 1864. By the 1880s the area became noted for its mining economy. In 1882 the county seat of Alturas County was moved from Rocky Bar in present-day Elmore County to Hailey, in response to a population shift from Rocky Bar – which would eventually become a ghost town – to the Wood River Valley.
