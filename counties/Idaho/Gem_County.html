Gem County, Idaho

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.0402381%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1640 people in Gem County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 13342 people in Gem County, Idaho lived in family households.

In 2000, 199 people in Gem County, Idaho lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 15181 people in Gem County, Idaho lived in the county.

In 2010, 3754 people in Elmore County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 22652 people in Elmore County, Idaho lived in family households.

In 2010, 632 people in Elmore County, Idaho lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 27038 people in Elmore County, Idaho lived in the county.

Named for the state nickname, "Gem State," the county was established on March 15, 1915, partitioned from Canyon County and Boise County. Fur trappers were in the area as early as 1818, and Alexander Ross explored Squaw Creek in 1824. Prospectors and miners moved through the county in 1862 en route to the gold rush in the Boise Basin around Idaho City, and by the next year irrigation began along the Payette River. The Black Canyon diversion dam on the river was built in the early 1920s, east of Emmett.
