Bonner County, Idaho

In 2000, 5542 people in Bonner County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 30983 people in Bonner County, Idaho lived in family households.

In 2010, 3902 people in Bingham County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 41382 people in Bingham County, Idaho lived in family households.

Bonner County was formed on February 21, 1907; it was formerly the northern section of Kootenai County. It was named for travel entrepreneur Edwin L. Bonner, a ferry operator. Bonners Ferry is now in Boundary County, which was partitioned in 1915 from Bonner County.
