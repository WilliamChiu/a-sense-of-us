Boise County, Idaho

In 2000, 887 people in Boise County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 5707 people in Boise County, Idaho lived in family households.

In 2010, 1513 people in Benewah County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7702 people in Benewah County, Idaho lived in family households.

The county was established February 4, 1864, with its county seat at Idaho City. It was named for the Boise River, which was named by French-Canadian explorers and trappers for the great variety of trees growing along its banks in the lower desert valley.
