Boise County, Idaho

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.38996935%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 887 people in Boise County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 5707 people in Boise County, Idaho lived in family households.

In 2000, 76 people in Boise County, Idaho lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 6670 people in Boise County, Idaho lived in the county.

In 2010, 1513 people in Benewah County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7702 people in Benewah County, Idaho lived in family households.

In 2010, 70 people in Benewah County, Idaho lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 9285 people in Benewah County, Idaho lived in the county.

The county was established February 4, 1864, with its county seat at Idaho City. It was named for the Boise River, which was named by French-Canadian explorers and trappers for the great variety of trees growing along its banks in the lower desert valley.
