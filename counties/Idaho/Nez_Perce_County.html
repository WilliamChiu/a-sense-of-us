Household Populations

Nez Perce County, Idaho

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.0782123%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 6493 people in Nez Perce County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 30255 people in Nez Perce County, Idaho lived in family households.

In 2000, 662 people in Nez Perce County, Idaho lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 37410 people in Nez Perce County, Idaho lived in the county.

In 2010, 553 people in Lincoln County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 4618 people in Lincoln County, Idaho lived in family households.

In 2010, 37 people in Lincoln County, Idaho lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 5208 people in Lincoln County, Idaho lived in the county.

Nez Perce County was originally organized in 1861, when the area was part of Washington Territory. It was reorganized in 1864 by the Idaho Territorial Legislature and was later subdivided into new counties. Rapid migration to the Palouse in the 1880s led to the formation of Latah County in 1888. Isolated from its county seat of Wallace in the Silver Valley, the southern portion of Shoshone County was annexed by Nez Perce County in 1904, then became Clearwater County in 1911. Lewis County was also formed from Nez Perce County in 1911.
