Ada County, Idaho

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.9912431%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 47985 people in Ada County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 245801 people in Ada County, Idaho lived in family households.

In 2000, 7118 people in Ada County, Idaho lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 300904 people in Ada County, Idaho lived in the county.

In 2010, 61 people in Kalawao County, Hawaii lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 26 people in Kalawao County, Hawaii lived in family households.

In 2010, 3 people in Kalawao County, Hawaii lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 90 people in Kalawao County, Hawaii lived in the county.

Ada County was created by the Idaho Territorial Legislature on December 22, 1864, partitioned from Boise County. It is named for Ada Riggs, the first pioneer child born in the area and the daughter of H.C. Riggs, a co-founder of Boise. Canyon County, which originally included Payette County and most of Gem County, was partitioned from western Ada County in 1891.
