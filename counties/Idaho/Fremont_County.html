Fremont County, Idaho

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.253127%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 974 people in Fremont County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10529 people in Fremont County, Idaho lived in family households.

In 2000, 316 people in Fremont County, Idaho lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 11819 people in Fremont County, Idaho lived in the county.

In 2010, 862 people in Custer County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 3485 people in Custer County, Idaho lived in family households.

In 2010, 21 people in Custer County, Idaho lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 4368 people in Custer County, Idaho lived in the county.

Fremont County was established in 1893. It is named for John C. Frémont.
