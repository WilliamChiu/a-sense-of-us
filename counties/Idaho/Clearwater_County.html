Clearwater County, Idaho

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 5.9738755%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1143 people in Clearwater County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 7188 people in Clearwater County, Idaho lived in family households.

In 2000, 599 people in Clearwater County, Idaho lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 8930 people in Clearwater County, Idaho lived in the county.

In 2010, 746 people in Caribou County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 6138 people in Caribou County, Idaho lived in family households.

In 2010, 79 people in Caribou County, Idaho lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 6963 people in Caribou County, Idaho lived in the county.

The Clearwater River and Lolo Pass, in the southeast corner of the county, were made famous by the exploration of Lewis and Clark in the early 19th century. Following an arduous trek through the Bitterroot Mountains, suffering through a mid-September snowstorm and near starvation, the Corps of Discovery expedition camped with the Nez Perce tribe on the Weippe Prairie outside of present-day Weippe in 1805. With the assistance of the Nez Perce, the expedition recuperated and constructed burned-out canoes at Canoe Camp in October 1805 and then paddled down the Clearwater, Snake, and Columbia rivers and met the Pacific Ocean a month later at present-day Astoria, Oregon.
