Camas County, Idaho

In 2000, 133 people in Camas County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 855 people in Camas County, Idaho lived in family households.

In 2010, 11919 people in Bonneville County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 91143 people in Bonneville County, Idaho lived in family households.

When the cavalry was stationed at Fort Boise the southern portion of the Camas Prairie was an important feeding ground for their horses. In 1869, a treaty ratified by the US Senate provided a portion of the "Kansas Prairie" instead of the "Camas Prairie" to be retained by the Bannock Indians. The error may have made by the person who transcribed the treaty. Since there was no "Kansas Prairie" in Idaho, the treaty rights of the Bannocks were ignored. When they found a few settlers were allowing their hogs to feed on the Bannocks' traditional food source, the camas root, they objected (without results), which was a major cause of the Bannock War of 1878.
