Household Populations

Bingham County, Idaho

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.2092888%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 3066 people in Bingham County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 38266 people in Bingham County, Idaho lived in family households.

In 2000, 403 people in Bingham County, Idaho lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 41735 people in Bingham County, Idaho lived in the county.

In 2010, 13128 people in Bannock County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 67919 people in Bannock County, Idaho lived in family households.

In 2010, 1792 people in Bannock County, Idaho lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 82839 people in Bannock County, Idaho lived in the county.

Bingham County was created January 13, 1885, and named after Henry H. Bingham, a congressman from Pennsylvania and friend of William Bunn, Idaho's Territorial Governor. The county was formed from Oneida County and was later partitioned itself to form Bannock (1893), Fremont (1893), Bonneville (1911), Power (1913), and Butte (1917) counties.
