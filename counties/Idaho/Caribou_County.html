Caribou County, Idaho

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.24200678%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 669 people in Caribou County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6572 people in Caribou County, Idaho lived in family households.

In 2000, 63 people in Caribou County, Idaho lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 7304 people in Caribou County, Idaho lived in the county.

In 2010, 392 people in Butte County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 2481 people in Butte County, Idaho lived in family households.

In 2010, 18 people in Butte County, Idaho lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 2891 people in Butte County, Idaho lived in the county.

Caribou County was formed on February 11, 1919, by the Idaho Legislature by a partition of Bannock County. It was the last county formed in the state, three days after Jerome County, and was named for the Caribou Mountains.
