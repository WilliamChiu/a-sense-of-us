Payette County, Idaho

In 2000, 2139 people in Payette County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 18322 people in Payette County, Idaho lived in family households.

In 2010, 7531 people in Nez Perce County, Idaho lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 30768 people in Nez Perce County, Idaho lived in family households.

The county was established in 1917, partitioned from Canyon County. It was named after the Payette River, which was named after French-Canadian François Payette. Payette, originally a fur trapper with the North West Company, was the first white man in the area in 1818.
