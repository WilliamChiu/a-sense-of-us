Lumpkin County, Georgia

In 2000, 2902 people in Lumpkin County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 16762 people in Lumpkin County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1173 people in Lincoln County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 6756 people in Lincoln County, Georgia lived in family households.

Lumpkin County was created on December 3, 1832. The county was named for former governor of Georgia, Wilson Lumpkin. Lumpkin's daughter, Martha Wilson Lumpkin Compton, was the source for the town named Marthasville, the early-1840s predecessor of Atlanta.
