Stewart County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 4.190898%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 740 people in Stewart County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 4243 people in Stewart County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 269 people in Stewart County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 5252 people in Stewart County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 1166 people in Seminole County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7463 people in Seminole County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 100 people in Seminole County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 8729 people in Seminole County, Georgia lived in the county.

The area was inhabited by Native Americans for thousands of years in the Pre-Columbian period. Roods Landing Site on the Chattahoochee River is a significant archaeological site located south of Omaha. Listed on the National Register of Historic Places, it includes major earthwork mounds built about 1100-1350 CE by peoples of the sophisticated Mississippian culture.
