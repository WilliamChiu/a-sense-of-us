Walton County, Georgia

In 2000, 5308 people in Walton County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 54837 people in Walton County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 3142 people in Union County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 17835 people in Union County, Georgia lived in family households.

Walton County was created on December 15, 1818. It is named for George Walton, one of the three men from Georgia who signed the United States Declaration of Independence. The other two were Button Gwinnett and Lyman Hall.
