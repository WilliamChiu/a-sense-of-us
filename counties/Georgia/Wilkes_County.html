Wilkes County, named for British politician and supporter of American independence, John Wilkes, is considered Georgia's first county established by European-Americans; it was the first of eight original counties created in the first state constitution on February 5, 1777. The other seven counties were organized from existing colonial parishes, but Wilkes was unique as it was land ceded in 1773 by the Creek and Cherokee Nations in their respective Treaties of Augusta. It is located in the Piedmont, above the fall line on the Savannah River.
