Cherokee County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -4.1985393%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 13634 people in Cherokee County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 127286 people in Cherokee County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 983 people in Cherokee County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 141903 people in Cherokee County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 50363 people in Chatham County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 201874 people in Chatham County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 12891 people in Chatham County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 265128 people in Chatham County, Georgia lived in the county.

Originally, Cherokee County was more like a territory than a county, covering everything northwest of the Chattahoochee River and Chestatee River except for Carroll County. This county was created December 26, 1831 by the state legislature. It was named after the Cherokee Indians who lived in the area at that time. Several other counties were carved out of these Cherokee lands as part of the Cherokee Land Lottery of 1832.
