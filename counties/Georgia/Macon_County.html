Macon County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.1156917%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1503 people in Macon County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 11588 people in Macon County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 983 people in Macon County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 14074 people in Macon County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 4624 people in Lumpkin County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 23560 people in Lumpkin County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1782 people in Lumpkin County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 29966 people in Lumpkin County, Georgia lived in the county.

Macon County was created in 1837 from parts of Houston ("house-ton") and Marion counties, effective December 14 of that year. The 91st county, it was named for the recently deceased General Nathaniel Macon of North Carolina, who served in the U.S. Congress for 37 years and ran for U.S. vice president. (The city of Macon, Georgia was also named for him, but is the seat of a different county.) The county was later reduced when parts were separated to organize Taylor and Peach counties, in 1852 and 1924, respectively.
