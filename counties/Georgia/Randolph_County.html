Randolph County, Georgia

In 2000, 1028 people in Randolph County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6460 people in Randolph County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 3040 people in Putnam County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 18006 people in Putnam County, Georgia lived in family households.

Jasper County was originally named for Randolph but, because of his opposition to U.S. entry into the War of 1812, the Georgia General Assembly changed its name in December 10, 1812. Eventually, John Randolph's reputation was restored, and in 1828, the General Assembly created the current Randolph County in the west of the state.
