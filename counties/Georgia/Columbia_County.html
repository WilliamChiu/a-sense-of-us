Columbia County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.5482197%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 6987 people in Columbia County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 81610 people in Columbia County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 691 people in Columbia County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 89288 people in Columbia County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 9068 people in Cobb County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 688078 people in Cobb County, Georgia lived in the county.

This area along the Savannah River had been inhabited for thousands of years by various cultures of indigenous peoples. The area had been home to the historic Muscogee-speaking Creek; Yuchi, people speaking a language isolate; and Iroquoian-speaking Cherokee for years prior to European colonization. The Yuchi had moved south from Tennessee because of pressure from the Cherokee, who continued to move into the Piedmont and soon dominated the Native American tribes. One of the oldest archaeological sites in the nation to contain pottery can be found on Stallings Island.
