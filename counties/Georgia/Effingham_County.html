Effingham County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.37497282%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 3200 people in Effingham County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 34087 people in Effingham County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 248 people in Effingham County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 37535 people in Effingham County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 15051 people in Douglas County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 115984 people in Douglas County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1368 people in Douglas County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 132403 people in Douglas County, Georgia lived in the county.

Effingham was among the original counties of the state of Georgia, created February 5, 1777 from the colonial parishes of St. Matthew and St. Phillip. Its name honors Lord Effingham, an English champion of colonial rights who resigned his commission rather than fight against Britain's American colonists during the American Revolution.
