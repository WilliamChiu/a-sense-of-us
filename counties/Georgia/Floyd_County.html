Floyd County, Georgia

In 2000, 11853 people in Floyd County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 74956 people in Floyd County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1472 people in Evans County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 9124 people in Evans County, Georgia lived in family households.

The county was established on December 3, 1832, by an act of the Georgia General Assembly and was created from land that was part of Cherokee County at the time. The county is named after United States Congressman John Floyd.
