Wheeler County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 10.336304%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 695 people in Wheeler County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 4404 people in Wheeler County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 1080 people in Wheeler County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 6179 people in Wheeler County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 2571 people in Washington County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 16720 people in Washington County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1896 people in Washington County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 21187 people in Washington County, Georgia lived in the county.

Wheeler County is named after Confederate General Joseph Wheeler. The constitutional amendment to create the county was proposed August 14, 1912, and ratified November 5, 1912.
