Lee County, Georgia

In 2000, 1776 people in Lee County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 22183 people in Lee County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 2406 people in Lamar County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 14456 people in Lamar County, Georgia lived in family households.

The land for Lee, Muscogee, Troup, Coweta, and Carroll counties was ceded by the Creek people in the 1825 Treaty of Indian Springs. The counties' boundaries were created by the Georgia General Assembly on June 9, but they were not named until December 14, 1826. The county was named in honor of Henry Lee III.
