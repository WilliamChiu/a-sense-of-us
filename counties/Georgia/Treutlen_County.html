Treutlen County, Georgia

In 2000, 789 people in Treutlen County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 5677 people in Treutlen County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 5460 people in Tift County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 33090 people in Tift County, Georgia lived in family households.

The state constitutional amendment to create the County was proposed by the Georgia General Assembly on August 21, 1917, and ratified November 5, 1918. The County is named for John A. Treutlen, Georgia's first state governor following adoption of the state Constitution of 1777.
