Cobb County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.62957406%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 94996 people in Cobb County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 505461 people in Cobb County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 7294 people in Cobb County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 607751 people in Cobb County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 506 people in Clay County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 2619 people in Clay County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 58 people in Clay County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 3183 people in Clay County, Georgia lived in the county.

Cobb County was one of nine Georgia counties carved out of the disputed territory of the Cherokee Nation in 1832. It was the 81st county in Georgia and named for Judge Thomas Willis Cobb, who served as a U.S. Senator, state congressman and Superior Court Judge. It is believed that the county seat of Marietta was named for Judge Cobb's wife, Mary.
