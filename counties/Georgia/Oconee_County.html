Oconee County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.27673244%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2158 people in Oconee County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 23827 people in Oconee County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 240 people in Oconee County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 26225 people in Oconee County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 4192 people in Murray County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 35182 people in Murray County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 254 people in Murray County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 39628 people in Murray County, Georgia lived in the county.

Oconee County was created from the southwestern part of Clarke County in 1875 by the Georgia General Assembly. The new county was created to satisfy southwestern Clarke County residents' demand for their own county after the county seat was moved from Watkinsville to Athens by the General Assembly in 1872. It is named for the river flowing along part of its eastern border.
