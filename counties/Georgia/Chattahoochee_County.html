Chattahoochee County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 47.658455%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 374 people in Chattahoochee County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 9632 people in Chattahoochee County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 4876 people in Chattahoochee County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 14882 people in Chattahoochee County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 7991 people in Catoosa County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 55490 people in Catoosa County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 461 people in Catoosa County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 63942 people in Catoosa County, Georgia lived in the county.

The Georgia General Assembly created Chattahoochee County on February 13, 1854, from portions of Muscogee and Marion counties. It is named for the Chattahoochee River that forms its western boundary. The county seat was named Cusseta to commemorate the Creek Indian town that once existed nearby. In 2004-2005, the US Census Bureau reported a 6.2% population decline, making top on the national list of counties with shrinking population.
