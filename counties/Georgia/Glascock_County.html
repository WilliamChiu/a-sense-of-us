Glascock County, Georgia

In 2000, 313 people in Glascock County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 2139 people in Glascock County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 2990 people in Franklin County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 18484 people in Franklin County, Georgia lived in family households.

The county is named after Thomas Glascock, a soldier in the War of 1812, general in the First Seminole War and U.S. representative.
