Grady County, Georgia

In 2000, 2771 people in Grady County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 20610 people in Grady County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 413 people in Glascock County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 2580 people in Glascock County, Georgia lived in family households.

The County is named for Henry W. Grady, editor of the ''Atlanta Constitution'' and noted orator.
