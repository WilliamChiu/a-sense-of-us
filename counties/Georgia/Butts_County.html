Butts County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 10.516953%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1883 people in Butts County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 15724 people in Butts County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 1915 people in Butts County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 19522 people in Butts County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 2735 people in Bryan County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 27400 people in Bryan County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 98 people in Bryan County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 30233 people in Bryan County, Georgia lived in the county.

Butts County was formed on December 24, 1825 as the sixty-fourth county in Georgia from portions of Henry County and Monroe County. It was named by the Georgia General Assembly in honor of Samuel Butts, an officer who was killed in the Creek War in 1814. A year later, Jackson was created as the first city in the new county and became the county seat. Other towns followed, including Indian Springs (1837); Flovilla (1883); Jenkinsburg (1889); and Pepperton (1897). Indian Springs later became unincorporated and Pepperton was merged with Jackson in 1966, leaving 3 incorporated cities in Butts County. In recent years, Indian Springs has again become a tourist destination including many historic sites, shops, eating establishment and the famous Indian Springs Hotel as its centerpiece.
