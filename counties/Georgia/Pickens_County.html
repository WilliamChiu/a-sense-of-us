Pickens County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.26804805%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2613 people in Pickens County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 212 people in Pickens County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 22983 people in Pickens County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 1894 people in Oglethorpe County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 177 people in Oglethorpe County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 14899 people in Oglethorpe County, Georgia lived in the county.

The Georgia General Assembly passed an act on December 5, 1853 to create Pickens County from portions of Cherokee and Gilmer counties. Pickens received several more land additions from Cherokee (1869) and Gilmer Counties (1858 and 1863); however several sections of Pickens County have also been transferred to other counties: Dawson County (1857), Gordon County (1860), and Cherokee County (1870).
