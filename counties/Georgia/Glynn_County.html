Glynn County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.5716255%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 10620 people in Glynn County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 55686 people in Glynn County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 1262 people in Glynn County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 67568 people in Glynn County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 210450 people in Fulton County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 678739 people in Fulton County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 31392 people in Fulton County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 920581 people in Fulton County, Georgia lived in the county.

Glynn County, one of the original eight counties in the state created on February 5, 1777, was named after John Glynn, a member of the British House of Commons who defended the cause of the American Colonies before the American Revolution.The Battle of Bloody Marsh was fought in Glynn County. James Oglethorpe built Fort Frederica which was used a base in the American Revolutionary War. Glynn Academy is the second oldest school in Georgia.
