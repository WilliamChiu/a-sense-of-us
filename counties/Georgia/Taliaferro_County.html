Taliaferro County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -26.839233%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 336 people in Taliaferro County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 1718 people in Taliaferro County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 23 people in Taliaferro County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 2077 people in Taliaferro County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 757 people in Stewart County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 3626 people in Stewart County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1675 people in Stewart County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 6058 people in Stewart County, Georgia lived in the county.

Taliaferro County was formed by an act of the Georgia Legislature meeting in Milledgeville on December 24, 1825. It was formed by taking portions of five other counties: Wilkes, Greene, Hancock, Oglethorpe, and Warren Counties.
