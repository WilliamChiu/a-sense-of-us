Heard County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.2425909%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1162 people in Heard County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 9741 people in Heard County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 109 people in Heard County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 11012 people in Heard County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 3493 people in Haralson County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 24933 people in Haralson County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 354 people in Haralson County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 28780 people in Haralson County, Georgia lived in the county.

Heard County was created by Act of the Legislature on December 22, 1830. It was named for the Honorable Stephen Heard, elected President of the Council on February 18, 1781, thus, in the absence of Governor Howley, becoming Governor de facto. Heard moved to Wilkes County from Virginia and fought in the American Revolutionary War where he distinguished himself at Kettle Creek. The first Sheriff, Jonathan Mewsick, was commissioned in 1832.
