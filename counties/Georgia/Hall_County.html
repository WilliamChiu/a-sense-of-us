Hall County, Georgia

In 2000, 15074 people in Hall County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 121906 people in Hall County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 2146 people in Greene County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 13680 people in Greene County, Georgia lived in family households.

The county is named for Dr. Lyman Hall, a signer of the Declaration of Independence and governor of Georgia as both colony and state.
