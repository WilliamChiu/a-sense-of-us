Irwin County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.7005553%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1092 people in Irwin County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 8470 people in Irwin County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 369 people in Irwin County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 9931 people in Irwin County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 132 people in Heard County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 11834 people in Heard County, Georgia lived in the county.

The territories of Appling, Irwin, and Early counties were land newly ceded in 1814 and 1818. These counties were created by a legislative act on December 15, 1818. All or portions of Irwin's five adjacent counties were created from Irwin county along with all of Cook, Colquitt, Lanier, Lowdnes, counties and portions of Atkinson, Brooks, Echols, Wilcox, and Worth counties. Irwin was divided into 16 districts of 20 miles and 10 chains square with lots of 70 chains square containing 490 acres according to the Act of 1818. In 1820 each lot was priced at $18, but by 1831 the price was down to $5 per lot.
