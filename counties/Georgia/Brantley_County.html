Brantley County, Georgia

In 2000, 1508 people in Brantley County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 13040 people in Brantley County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 2590 people in Berrien County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 16520 people in Berrien County, Georgia lived in family households.

Georgia voters passed a state constitutional amendment on November 2, 1920, to form Brantley County from pieces of Charlton, Pierce, and Wayne counties. The younger Brantley served as a state representative, state senator, and eight-term United States representative.
