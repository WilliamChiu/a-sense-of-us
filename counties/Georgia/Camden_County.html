Camden County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 3.1569004%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4305 people in Camden County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 37509 people in Camden County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 1850 people in Camden County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 43664 people in Camden County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 2886 people in Burke County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 20147 people in Burke County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 283 people in Burke County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 23316 people in Burke County, Georgia lived in the county.

The first recorded European to visit what is today Camden County was Captain Jean Ribault of France in 1562. Ribault was sent out by French Huguenots to find a suitable place for a settlement. Ribault named the rivers he saw the Seine and the Some, known today as the St. Marys and Satilla Rivers. Ribault described the area as, "Fairest, fruitfulest and pleasantest of all the world."
