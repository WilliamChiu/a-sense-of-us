Bleckley County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 3.9281726%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1409 people in Bleckley County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 9620 people in Bleckley County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 637 people in Bleckley County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 11666 people in Bleckley County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 2361 people in Ben Hill County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 14965 people in Ben Hill County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 308 people in Ben Hill County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 17634 people in Ben Hill County, Georgia lived in the county.

The county was named for Logan Edwin Bleckley, a soldier and Justice of the Supreme Court of Georgia. The state constitutional amendment to create the county was proposed by the Georgia General Assembly on July 30, 1912, and ratified November 5, 1912. Bleckley County was formerly home to Middle Georgia College, the oldest two-year public college in the nation. In 2013 it merged with Macon State College to become Middle Georgia State College.
