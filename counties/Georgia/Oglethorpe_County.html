Oglethorpe County, Georgia

In 2000, 1538 people in Oglethorpe County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10981 people in Oglethorpe County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 31649 people in Muscogee County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 151219 people in Muscogee County, Georgia lived in family households.

Oglethorpe County was originally part of a large tract of land surrendered by Creek and Cherokee Native Americans to the Colony of Georgia in the treaty of 1773. The county itself was founded on December 19, 1793, and is named for Georgia's founder, General James Oglethorpe.
