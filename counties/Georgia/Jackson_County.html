Jackson County, Georgia

In 2000, 4330 people in Jackson County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 36450 people in Jackson County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 19354 people in Henry County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 183635 people in Henry County, Georgia lived in family households.

On February 11, 1796, Jackson County was split off from part of Franklin County, Georgia. The new county was named in honor of Revolutionary War Lieutenant Colonel, Congressman, Senator and Governor James Jackson. The county originally covered an area of approximately 1800 sqmi, with Clarksboro as its first county seat.
