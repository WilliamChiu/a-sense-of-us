Sumter County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.766788%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4255 people in Sumter County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 27433 people in Sumter County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 1512 people in Sumter County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 33200 people in Sumter County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 8212 people in Spalding County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 54635 people in Spalding County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1226 people in Spalding County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 64073 people in Spalding County, Georgia lived in the county.

Sumter County was established by an act of the state legislature on December 26, 1831, four years after the Creek Indians vacated the region when the state acquired the territory from them in the 1825 Treaty of Indian Springs. Sumter, the state's eightieth county, was created after population increases by a division of Lee County, now situated to its south. The county was named for former General and United States senator Thomas Sumter (1734–1832) of South Carolina. when the county was organized, Sumter was ninety-seven years old and the last surviving general of the American Revolution (1775–83).
