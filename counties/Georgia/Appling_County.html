Appling County, Georgia

In 2000, 2094 people in Appling County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 15083 people in Appling County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 3592 people in Wakulla County, Florida lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 23756 people in Wakulla County, Florida lived in family households.

Appling County, the 42nd county created in Georgia, was established by an act of the Georgia General Assembly on December 15, 1818. The original county consisted of Creek lands ceded in the 1814 Treaty of Fort Jackson and the 1818 Treaty of the Creek Agency.
