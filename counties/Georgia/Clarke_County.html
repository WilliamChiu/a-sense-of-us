Clarke County, Georgia

In 2000, 32962 people in Clarke County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 60347 people in Clarke County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 767 people in Chattahoochee County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7233 people in Chattahoochee County, Georgia lived in family households.

Clarke County was created in 1801 by an act of the Georgia General Assembly on December 5. It was named for Revolutionary War hero Elijah Clarke and included 250 sqmi that was formerly part of Jackson County. Colonel Clarke played a leading role the 1779 victory at the Battle of Kettle Creek in Wilkes County. The Elijah Clarke Chapter of the Daughters of the American Revolution erected a monument to him in Broad Street in Athens.
