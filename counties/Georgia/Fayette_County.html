Fayette County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -3.441918%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 6603 people in Fayette County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 84082 people in Fayette County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 578 people in Fayette County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 91263 people in Fayette County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 3080 people in Emanuel County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 18602 people in Emanuel County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 916 people in Emanuel County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 22598 people in Emanuel County, Georgia lived in the county.

Fayette County was created on May 15, 1821, from territory ceded by the Creek people, who had historically inhabited the area. It was named for the Marquis de Lafayette, French hero of the American Revolutionary War. Located in the Piedmont, the county was originally developed for agriculture, primarily cotton cultivation.
