Worth County, Georgia

In 2000, 2270 people in Worth County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 19489 people in Worth County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1030 people in Wilcox County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 6206 people in Wilcox County, Georgia lived in family households.

Worth County was created from Dooly and Irwin counties on December 20, 1853, by an act of the Georgia General Assembly, becoming Georgia's 106th county. It was named for Major General William J. Worth of New York.
