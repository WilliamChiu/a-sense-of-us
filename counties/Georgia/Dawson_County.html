Dawson County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.4494846%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1714 people in Dawson County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14188 people in Dawson County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 97 people in Dawson County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 15999 people in Dawson County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 1549 people in Crawford County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 10948 people in Crawford County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 133 people in Crawford County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 12630 people in Crawford County, Georgia lived in the county.

Dawson County was created on December 3, 1857 from Gilmer and Lumpkin counties. It is named for William Crosby Dawson, a U.S. Senator from Georgia.
