Clay County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -5.8626833%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 471 people in Clay County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 2829 people in Clay County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 57 people in Clay County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 3357 people in Clay County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 3325 people in Chattooga County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 20749 people in Chattooga County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1941 people in Chattooga County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 26015 people in Chattooga County, Georgia lived in the county.
