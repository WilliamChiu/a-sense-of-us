Montgomery County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 7.013917%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1010 people in Montgomery County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6506 people in Montgomery County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 754 people in Montgomery County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 8270 people in Montgomery County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 843 people in Miller County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5114 people in Miller County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 168 people in Miller County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 6125 people in Miller County, Georgia lived in the county.

Montgomery County is named in honor of Richard Montgomery, an American Revolutionary War general killed in 1775 while attempting to capture Quebec City, Canada. It was created on December 19, 1793.
