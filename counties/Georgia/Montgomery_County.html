Montgomery County, Georgia

In 2000, 1010 people in Montgomery County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6506 people in Montgomery County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 843 people in Miller County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5114 people in Miller County, Georgia lived in family households.

Montgomery County is named in honor of Richard Montgomery, an American Revolutionary War general killed in 1775 while attempting to capture Quebec City, Canada. It was created on December 19, 1793.
