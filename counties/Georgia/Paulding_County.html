Paulding County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.1732578%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 6576 people in Paulding County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 74590 people in Paulding County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 512 people in Paulding County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 81678 people in Paulding County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 10083 people in Newton County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 88083 people in Newton County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1792 people in Newton County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 99958 people in Newton County, Georgia lived in the county.

Paulding County was created from Cherokee County by an act of the Georgia General Assembly on December 3, 1832. In 1851, a portion of Paulding County was used to help create Polk County. Other portions of Paulding County were annexed to neighboring counties (Campbell, Carroll, Cobb, Douglas, Haralson, and Polk) between 1832 and 1874. Between 1850 and 1874, Paulding County was expanded through annexation of parts of Carroll, Cobb, Douglas, and Polk counties.
