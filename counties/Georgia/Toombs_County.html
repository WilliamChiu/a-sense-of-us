Toombs County, Georgia

In 2000, 3661 people in Toombs County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 21932 people in Toombs County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1218 people in Terrell County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7827 people in Terrell County, Georgia lived in family households.

The County is named for Robert Toombs, United States representative and senator. During the Civil War, Toombs also served in the Confederate Provisional Congress, as Secretary of State of the Confederate States, and a brigadier general in the Confederate Army. Toombs County was founded in 1905.
