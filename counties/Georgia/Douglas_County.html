Douglas County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -8.104748%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 10369 people in Douglas County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 80956 people in Douglas County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 849 people in Douglas County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 92174 people in Douglas County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 21796 people in Dodge County, Georgia lived in the county.

Formed in 1870 during Reconstruction after the US Civil War, Douglas County was originally named by the reconstruction legislature after Frederick Douglass, the Civil War-era abolitionist. However, at the end of Reconstruction, the second "s" was quietly dropped and the designated honoree was changed to Stephen A.
