Jasper County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.41959286%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1264 people in Jasper County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10079 people in Jasper County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 83 people in Jasper County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 11426 people in Jasper County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 18327 people in Houston County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 119974 people in Houston County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1599 people in Houston County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 139900 people in Houston County, Georgia lived in the county.

This area was inhabited by indigenous peoples for thousands of years before the European encounter. At the time of European-Americans settlement, it was inhabited by the Cherokee and Muscogee Creek peoples, who became known as among the Five Civilized Tribes of the Southeast.
