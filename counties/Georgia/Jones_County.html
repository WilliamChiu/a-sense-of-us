Jones County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.6482949%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2280 people in Jones County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 21007 people in Jones County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 352 people in Jones County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 23639 people in Jones County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 2131 people in Jefferson County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 14272 people in Jefferson County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 527 people in Jefferson County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 16930 people in Jefferson County, Georgia lived in the county.

Jones County along with Morgan County, Putnam County, and Old Randolph were established by an act of the Georgia General Assembly passed on December 10, 1807 from land that had originally been part Baldwin County in 1803 and before that part of the Creek Nation. Jones County was originally bounded by a line running North 56° East to Commissioners Creek, then North 15° West to Cedar Creek, then up the creek to corner Randolph County and Putnam County, then along a line to Ocmulgee River, and then down the river to where the old county line between Wilkinson County and Baldwin County was. This excluded parts of what is now Bibb County east of the Ocmulgee River, including the location of Fort Benjamin Hawkins as they were part of a reserve guaranteed to the Creek Nation. Those areas were later added to Jones County after the Treaty of Indian Springs.
