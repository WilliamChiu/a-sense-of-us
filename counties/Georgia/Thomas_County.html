Thomas County, Georgia

In 2000, 5724 people in Thomas County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 35864 people in Thomas County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1311 people in Taylor County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7273 people in Taylor County, Georgia lived in family households.

Thomas County was created by an act of the Georgia General Assembly on December 24, 1825, from portions of Decatur and Irwin counties. Colquitt (1856), Brooks (1858), and Grady (1905) counties all were formed partially from lands within Thomas County's original borders.
