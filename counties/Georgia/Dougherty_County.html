Dougherty County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.9145122%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 13473 people in Dougherty County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 78111 people in Dougherty County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 4481 people in Dougherty County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 96065 people in Dougherty County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 143713 people in DeKalb County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 535131 people in DeKalb County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 13049 people in DeKalb County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 691893 people in DeKalb County, Georgia lived in the county.

The county was created by the Georgia General Assembly on December 15, 1853, from a part of Baker County and was named after Charles Dougherty, a respected judge and lawyer from Athens, Georgia. In 1854 and 1856 small areas were added from Worth County.
