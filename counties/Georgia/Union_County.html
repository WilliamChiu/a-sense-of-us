Union County, Georgia

In 2000, 2233 people in Union County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14613 people in Union County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 8809 people in Troup County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 56162 people in Troup County, Georgia lived in family households.

Union County was carved from Cherokee County territory during the Georgia Land Lottery of 1832. Originally inhabited by Native Americans, the area became more desirable to white settlers with the discovery of gold in the 1820s.
