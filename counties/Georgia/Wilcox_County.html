Wilcox County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -11.11182%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 888 people in Wilcox County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6214 people in Wilcox County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 1475 people in Wilcox County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 8577 people in Wilcox County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 713 people in Wheeler County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 4749 people in Wheeler County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1959 people in Wheeler County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 7421 people in Wheeler County, Georgia lived in the county.

It is unclear whether Wilcox County is named for General Mark Wilcox, a Georgia state legislator and one of the founders of the Georgia Supreme Court, or if it is named for his son John Wilcox. The formation of the county was led by Norman McDuffie of Pulaski County, Georgia. It was formed on December 22, 1857 from parts of Irwin, Pulaski, and Dooly counties. Wilcox County, was given land from Dooly County, Irwin County, and Pulaski County in 1857. When the county seat was named Abbeville the early settlers paid respect to their earlier home in Abbeville, South Carolina.
