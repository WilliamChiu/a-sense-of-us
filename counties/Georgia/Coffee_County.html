Coffee County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.4386764%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4367 people in Coffee County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 31587 people in Coffee County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 1459 people in Coffee County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 37413 people in Coffee County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 35334 people in Clayton County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 220053 people in Clayton County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 4037 people in Clayton County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 259424 people in Clayton County, Georgia lived in the county.

Coffee County was created by an act of the Georgia General Assembly on February 9, 1854, from portions of Clinch, Irwin, Telfair, and Ware counties. These lands were originally ceded by the Creek in the Treaty of Fort Jackson in (1814) and the Treaty of the Creek Agency (1818) and apportioned to the above Counties before becoming Coffee County.
