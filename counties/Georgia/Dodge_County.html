Dodge County, Georgia

In 2000, 2454 people in Dodge County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 15028 people in Dodge County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 2557 people in Dawson County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 19474 people in Dawson County, Georgia lived in family households.

Dodge County was created on October 26, 1870. The county was named for William E. Dodge, a New York U. S. Representative and businessman, abolitionist, and "Carpetbagger" who purchased large tracts of timberland in the area after the Civil War.
