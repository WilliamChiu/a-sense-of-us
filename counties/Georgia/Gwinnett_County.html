Gwinnett County, Georgia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.13043284%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 67889 people in Gwinnett County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 514174 people in Gwinnett County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2000, 6385 people in Gwinnett County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 588448 people in Gwinnett County, Georgia lived in the county.

In 2010, 6151 people in Gordon County, Georgia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 48365 people in Gordon County, Georgia lived in family households.

In 2010, 670 people in Gordon County, Georgia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 55186 people in Gordon County, Georgia lived in the county.

Created in 1818 by an act of the Georgia General Assembly, Gwinnett County was formed from parts of Jackson County (formerly part of Franklin County) and from lands gained through the session of Indian lands. Named for Button Gwinnett, one of the signatories of the Declaration of Independence, the first county court was held at the home of Elisha Winn, and the county seat was placed at Lawrenceville.
