Tishomingo County, Mississippi

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -14.512009%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2558 people in Tishomingo County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 16329 people in Tishomingo County, Mississippi lived in family households.

In 2000, 276 people in Tishomingo County, Mississippi lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 19163 people in Tishomingo County, Mississippi lived in the county.

In 2010, 1676 people in Tallahatchie County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 11281 people in Tallahatchie County, Mississippi lived in family households.

In 2010, 2421 people in Tallahatchie County, Mississippi lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 15378 people in Tallahatchie County, Mississippi lived in the county.

Tishomingo County was organized February 9, 1836, from Chickasaw lands that were ceded to the United States. In 1870 this land was divided into Alcorn, Prentiss and Tishomingo counties.
