Pike County, Mississippi

In 2000, 4743 people in Pike County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 33338 people in Pike County, Mississippi lived in family households.

In 2010, 4303 people in Panola County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 30089 people in Panola County, Mississippi lived in family households.

Pike County was formed from Marion County by an act of the Territorial General Assembly on December 9, 1815. Holmesville was selected as the county seat December 11, 1816 in honor of Major Andrew Hunter Holmes an army officer killed in the War of 1812. In 1873 Magnolia was voted in as the new county seat.
