From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.5157034%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

Lauderdale County is a county located in the U.S. state of Mississippi. As of the 2010 census, the population was 80,261. The county seat is Meridian. The county is named for colonel James Lauderdale, who was killed at the Battle of New Orleans in the War of 1812.
