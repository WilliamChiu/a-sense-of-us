Monroe County, Mississippi

In 2000, 4339 people in Monroe County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 33260 people in Monroe County, Mississippi lived in family households.

In 2010, 12387 people in Madison County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 81051 people in Madison County, Mississippi lived in family households.

The county is named in honor of U.S. President James Monroe. Part of the county east of the Tombigbee River originally made-up part of the Alabama Territory, belonging to Marion County, until new lines of demarcation put it in the State of Mississippi in 1821.
