Perry County, Mississippi

In 2000, 1260 people in Perry County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10760 people in Perry County, Mississippi lived in family households.

In 2010, 13513 people in Oktibbeha County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 29791 people in Oktibbeha County, Mississippi lived in family households.

Perry County is a county located in the U.S. state of Mississippi. As of the 2010 census, the population was 12,250. The county seat is New Augusta. The county is named after the War of 1812 naval hero, Oliver Hazard Perry.
