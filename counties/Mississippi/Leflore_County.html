Leflore County, Mississippi

In 2000, 4598 people in Leflore County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 30389 people in Leflore County, Mississippi lived in family households.

In 2010, 1622 people in Lawrence County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 11307 people in Lawrence County, Mississippi lived in family households.

Leflore County was formed in 1871 from portions of Carroll, Sunflower and Tallahatchie counties. It was named for Greenwood Leflore, a Choctaw chief. During the period of Indian Removal in the 1830s, he was one of the chiefs who signed the Treaty of 1830, by the terms of which the Choctaw sold to the US their remaining lands east of the Mississippi River. Most Choctaw migrated to the Indian Territory (now Oklahoma), but Leflore and some other remained in Mississippi. He became a state and US citizen, a planter with African-American slaves, and served at times as a politician.
