Hancock County, Mississippi

In 2000, 6168 people in Hancock County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 36347 people in Hancock County, Mississippi lived in family households.

In 2010, 2312 people in George County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 19702 people in George County, Mississippi lived in family households.

In 2005, the county was the scene of the final landfall of the eye of Hurricane Katrina, and its communities and infrastructure suffered some of the most intense damage inflicted by that storm. Over the entire 7 mi beach front, not one building or home was left intact. This is true for nearly the entire 1st block off of the beach for the entire 7 mi stretch.
