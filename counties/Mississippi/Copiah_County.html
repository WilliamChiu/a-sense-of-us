Copiah County, Mississippi

In 2000, 2967 people in Copiah County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 24544 people in Copiah County, Mississippi lived in family households.

In 2010, 2307 people in Clarke County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 14373 people in Clarke County, Mississippi lived in family households.

Copiah, from a Choctaw Indian word meaning ''calling panther'', was organized in 1823 as Mississippi's 18th county. In 2004 Calling Panther Lake, commemorating this name, was opened up just West and North of Crystal Springs near the Jack and New Zion community. In the year of organization, Walter Leake served as governor and James Monroe as President of the United States.
