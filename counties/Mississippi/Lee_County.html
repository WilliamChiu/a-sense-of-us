Household Populations

Lee County, Mississippi

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -3.0656874%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 9705 people in Lee County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 64700 people in Lee County, Mississippi lived in family households.

In 2000, 1350 people in Lee County, Mississippi lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 75755 people in Lee County, Mississippi lived in the county.

In 2010, 11929 people in Lauderdale County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 64485 people in Lauderdale County, Mississippi lived in family households.

In 2010, 3847 people in Lauderdale County, Mississippi lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 80261 people in Lauderdale County, Mississippi lived in the county.

Lee county was established on October 26, 1866, and named for the General-in-Chief of the Armies of the Confederate States, General Robert Edward Lee. It was carved from the counties of Itawamba and Pontotoc; therefore, the record and list of pioneers mentioned in those counties embrace a great number who were residents of what is now Lee county.
