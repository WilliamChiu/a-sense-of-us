Holmes County, Mississippi

In 2000, 2282 people in Holmes County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 18655 people in Holmes County, Mississippi lived in family households.

In 2010, 6905 people in Hancock County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 36477 people in Hancock County, Mississippi lived in family households.

Holmes County is a county in the U.S. state of Mississippi. As of the 2010 census, the population was 19,198. Its county seat is Lexington. It is named in honor of David Holmes, the first governor of Mississippi.
