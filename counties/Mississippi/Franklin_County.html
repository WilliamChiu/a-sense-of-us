Franklin County, Mississippi

In 2000, 940 people in Franklin County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

Franklin County is a county located in the U.S. state of Mississippi. As of the 2010 census, the population was 8,118, making it the fourth-least populous county in Mississippi. Its county seat is Meadville. The county was formed on December 21, 1809 from portions of Adams County and named for Founding Father Benjamin Franklin.
