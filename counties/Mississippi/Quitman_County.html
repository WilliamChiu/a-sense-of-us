Quitman County, Mississippi

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.876981%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1175 people in Quitman County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 8813 people in Quitman County, Mississippi lived in family households.

In 2000, 129 people in Quitman County, Mississippi lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 10117 people in Quitman County, Mississippi lived in the county.

In 2010, 5391 people in Pike County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 34148 people in Pike County, Mississippi lived in family households.

In 2010, 865 people in Pike County, Mississippi lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 40404 people in Pike County, Mississippi lived in the county.

The county was developed for cotton cultivation. Much of the bottomlands behind the riverfront were not developed until the late 19th century, and population continued to increase as the frontier was cleared and cultivated. The county reached its peak population in 1940. Agricultural mechanization reduced the need for farm labor, and workers were recruited to northern and midwestern industrial cities. Thousands of African Americans left in the Great Migration, many going upriver to St. Louis and Chicago.
