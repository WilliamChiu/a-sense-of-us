DeSoto County, Mississippi

In 2000, 10978 people in DeSoto County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 95643 people in DeSoto County, Mississippi lived in family households.

In 2010, 3548 people in Coahoma County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 21936 people in Coahoma County, Mississippi lived in family households.

The county is named for Spanish explorer Hernando de Soto, the discoverer of the Mississippi River. The county seat, Hernando, is also named in his honor. De Soto allegedly died in that area in May 1542, although some believe that he died near Lake Village, Arkansas. See here for a list of sites associated with the de Soto Expedition.
