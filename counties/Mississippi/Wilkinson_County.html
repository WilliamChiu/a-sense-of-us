Wilkinson County, Mississippi

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 10.012865%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1161 people in Wilkinson County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 8112 people in Wilkinson County, Mississippi lived in family households.

In 2000, 1039 people in Wilkinson County, Mississippi lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 10312 people in Wilkinson County, Mississippi lived in the county.

In 2010, 6755 people in Washington County, Mississippi lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 43834 people in Washington County, Mississippi lived in family households.

In 2010, 548 people in Washington County, Mississippi lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 51137 people in Washington County, Mississippi lived in the county.

In the nineteenth century, this county was developed by European-American settlers as cotton plantations along the Mississippi River, which forms the western border of the county. Much of the bottomlands and interior were undeveloped frontier until after the American Civil War. The intensive cultivation depended on the labor of enslaved African Americans; in the early nineteenth century, many were transported here from the Upper South in a major forced migration. The population quickly became majority black.
