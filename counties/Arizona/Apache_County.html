Apache County, Arizona

In 2000, 5257 people in Apache County, Arizona lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 62893 people in Apache County, Arizona lived in family households.

In 2010, 143 people in Yakutat City and Borough, Alaska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 501 people in Yakutat City and Borough, Alaska lived in family households.

Apache County was formed during the Tenth Territorial Legislation in 1879 out of the eastern section of Yavapai County; officially all land east of 119^o 45'. By 1895, Navajo County and parts of Graham, Greenlee and Gila Counties were formed from this land. The county seat was placed in the town of Snowflake, but was moved a year later to St. Johns. From 1880 to 1882, the county seat was temporarily in Springerville before being returned to St. Johns.
