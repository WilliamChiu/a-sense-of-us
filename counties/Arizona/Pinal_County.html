Pinal County, Arizona

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 6.994021%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 20258 people in Pinal County, Arizona lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 144239 people in Pinal County, Arizona lived in family households.

In 2000, 15230 people in Pinal County, Arizona lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 179727 people in Pinal County, Arizona lived in the county.

In 2010, 11565 people in Navajo County, Arizona lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 93657 people in Navajo County, Arizona lived in family households.

In 2010, 2227 people in Navajo County, Arizona lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 107449 people in Navajo County, Arizona lived in the county.

Pinal County was carved out of neighboring Maricopa County and Pima County on February 1, 1875 during the Eighth Legislature. Pinal County was the second fastest growing county in the U.S. between 2000 and 2010.
