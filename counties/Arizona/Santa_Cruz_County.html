Santa Cruz County, Arizona

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.9387364%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2751 people in Santa Cruz County, Arizona lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 35425 people in Santa Cruz County, Arizona lived in family households.

In 2000, 205 people in Santa Cruz County, Arizona lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 38381 people in Santa Cruz County, Arizona lived in the county.

In 2010, 187325 people in Pima County, Arizona lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 768799 people in Pima County, Arizona lived in family households.

In 2010, 24139 people in Pima County, Arizona lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 980263 people in Pima County, Arizona lived in the county.

Santa Cruz County, formed on March 15, 1899, out of what was then Pima County, is named after the Santa Cruz River, which was named in the late 17th century by Father Kino. Santa Cruz means "holy cross" in Spanish. After that Father Kino built the famous mission which still stands today at the Tumacácori National Historical Park.
