DeKalb County, Tennessee

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.6857157%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2302 people in DeKalb County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14806 people in DeKalb County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2000, 315 people in DeKalb County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 17423 people in DeKalb County, Tennessee lived in the county.

In 2010, 8057 people in Cumberland County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 47360 people in Cumberland County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 636 people in Cumberland County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 56053 people in Cumberland County, Tennessee lived in the county.

DeKalb County was formed in 1837 from land in Cannon, Warren and White counties. Historian Will T. Hale believes that the first settlers in the county were at Liberty and came from Maryland in 1797. If so, Adam Dale was the first settler. He may have come over the Cumberland Mountains, although some sources claim he came down the Ohio, up the Cumberland to Nashville, and then overland about 56 miles.
