White County, Tennessee

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.493%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2844 people in White County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 19953 people in White County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2000, 305 people in White County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 23102 people in White County, Tennessee lived in the county.

In 2010, 23670 people in Washington County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 94660 people in Washington County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 4649 people in Washington County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 122979 people in Washington County, Tennessee lived in the county.

On September 11, 1806, an act of the Tennessee General Assembly created White County out of Smith and Jackson counties, responding to a petition signed by 155 residents of the area. The county's original geographic area included all of what are now White and Warren counties, as well as parts of modern Cannon, Coffee, DeKalb, Franklin, Grundy, Putnam, and Van Buren counties.
