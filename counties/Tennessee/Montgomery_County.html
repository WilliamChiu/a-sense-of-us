Montgomery County, Tennessee

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.027166%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 15838 people in Montgomery County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 114606 people in Montgomery County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2000, 4324 people in Montgomery County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 134768 people in Montgomery County, Tennessee lived in the county.

In 2010, 11055 people in Maury County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 68892 people in Maury County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 1009 people in Maury County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 80956 people in Maury County, Tennessee lived in the county.

The county was named for John Montgomery, a soldier in the American Revolution and an early settler who founded the city of Clarksville. It was formed when Tennessee County, North Carolina, was split in 1796, when Tennessee became a state, to reduce confusion. The same year, much of the eastern portion of the county was combined with land taken from Sumner County to form Robertson County. Later acts of the Tennessee General Assembly further reduced the county to its current size and boundaries by 1871.
