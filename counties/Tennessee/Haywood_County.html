Haywood County, Tennessee

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -12.922329%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2435 people in Haywood County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 17109 people in Haywood County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2000, 253 people in Haywood County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 19797 people in Haywood County, Tennessee lived in the county.

In 2010, 3345 people in Hardeman County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 20083 people in Hardeman County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 3825 people in Hardeman County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 27253 people in Hardeman County, Tennessee lived in the county.

Haywood County was created from part of Madison County in 1823–24, and was named for Tennessee judge and historian John Haywood. Haywood County was part of Madison County when the Tennessee General Assembly created it in 1823–24. (Later, portions of Haywood would be carved out to create Lauderdale and Crockett Counties.) The legislature designated Brownsville as the county seat.
