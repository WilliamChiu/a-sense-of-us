Wilson County, Tennessee

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -5.181903%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 8819 people in Wilson County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 78842 people in Wilson County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2000, 88809 people in Wilson County, Tennessee lived in the county.

In 2010, 2244 people in Weakley County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 35021 people in Weakley County, Tennessee lived in the county.

Wilson County was created in 1799 from a portion of Sumner County, and named for Major David Wilson, a Revolutionary War veteran and statesman. The county remained predominantly agrarian throughout the 19th century. The arrival of the railroad after the Civil War boosted the county's timber sector, and several large factories were constructed in the county during the early 20th century.
