Bledsoe County, Tennessee

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 8.739197%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1286 people in Bledsoe County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 9908 people in Bledsoe County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2000, 1173 people in Bledsoe County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 12367 people in Bledsoe County, Tennessee lived in the county.

In 2010, 12079 people in Anderson County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 61867 people in Anderson County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 1183 people in Anderson County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 75129 people in Anderson County, Tennessee lived in the county.

Bledsoe County was formed in 1807 from land that was formerly Indian Land as well as land carved from Roane County. The county was named for Anthony Bledsoe (1739–1788), a soldier in the Revolutionary War and was an early settler of Sumner County. He was killed in an Indian attack at Bledsoe's Station.
