Lake County, Tennessee

In 2000, 871 people in Lake County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 4805 people in Lake County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 6814 people in Jefferson County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 42999 people in Jefferson County, Tennessee lived in family households.

The history of Lake County has been largely defined by Reelfoot Lake, a natural lake created by the New Madrid earthquakes of 1811 and 1812. The county was established by the state legislature in 1870. It was originally included in Obion County, but was created as a separate county due to difficulties its residents had to cross the swampy wetlands around Reelfoot Lake to reach Obion's county seat. Tiptonville was designated as Lake County's seat of government.
