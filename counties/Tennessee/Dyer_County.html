Dyer County, Tennessee

In 2000, 5004 people in Dyer County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 31666 people in Dyer County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 1900 people in Decatur County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 9644 people in Decatur County, Tennessee lived in family households.

Dyer County was founded by a Private Act of Tennessee, passed on October 16, 1823. The area was part of the territory in Tennessee that was previously legally occupied by Chickasaw Native American people ("Indian Lands").
