Jefferson County, Tennessee

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.7159462%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 5447 people in Jefferson County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 37185 people in Jefferson County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2000, 1662 people in Jefferson County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 44294 people in Jefferson County, Tennessee lived in the county.

In 2010, 1228 people in Houston County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7021 people in Houston County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 177 people in Houston County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 8426 people in Houston County, Tennessee lived in the county.

Jefferson County was established on June 11, 1792, by William Blount, Governor of the Southwest Territory. It had been a part of Caswell County during the State of Franklin period (1784–1789). Its county seat, Dandridge, was settled in 1783.
