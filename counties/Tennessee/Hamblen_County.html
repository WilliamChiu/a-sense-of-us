Hamblen County, Tennessee

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.7021427%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 7881 people in Hamblen County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 49457 people in Hamblen County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2000, 790 people in Hamblen County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 58128 people in Hamblen County, Tennessee lived in the county.

In 2010, 2882 people in Grainger County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 19624 people in Grainger County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 151 people in Grainger County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 22657 people in Grainger County, Tennessee lived in the county.

Hamblen County was created in 1870 from parts of Jefferson, Grainger, and Greene counties. The county is named in honor of Hezekiah Hamblen (1775–1854), an early settler, landowner, attorney, and member of the Hawkins County Court for many years. Governor Dewitt Clinton Senter, a resident of the county, used his influence to assist in its establishment.
