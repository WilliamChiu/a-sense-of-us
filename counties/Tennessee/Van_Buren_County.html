Van Buren County, Tennessee

In 2000, 667 people in Van Buren County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 4763 people in Van Buren County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 1018 people in Trousdale County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 6722 people in Trousdale County, Tennessee lived in family households.

Van Buren County was formed in 1840 from parts of White, Warren and Bledsoe counties. It was named for President Martin Van Buren. The county seat, Spencer, was home to Burritt College, one of the South's first coeducational colleges, during the 19th and early 20th centuries.
