Claiborne County, Tennessee

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.43935776%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 3560 people in Claiborne County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 25655 people in Claiborne County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2000, 647 people in Claiborne County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 29862 people in Claiborne County, Tennessee lived in the county.

In 2010, 9498 people in Carter County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 46435 people in Carter County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 1491 people in Carter County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 57424 people in Carter County, Tennessee lived in the county.

Claiborne County was established on October 29, 1801, created from Grainger and Hawkins counties and extended the southern boundary to Anderson County. It was named for Virginia tidewater aristocrat William C. C. Claiborne, one of the first judges of the Tennessee Superior Court and one of the first representatives in U.S. Congress from Tennessee.
