Decatur County, Tennessee

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.6431818%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1685 people in Decatur County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 9816 people in Decatur County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2000, 230 people in Decatur County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 11731 people in Decatur County, Tennessee lived in the county.

In 2010, 1871 people in Crockett County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 12521 people in Crockett County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 194 people in Crockett County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 14586 people in Crockett County, Tennessee lived in the county.

This county is named after naval hero Stephen Decatur, Jr., who gained national recognition in the First Barbary War and the Second Barbary War and the War of 1812 by his leadership and achievements at sea. The county was created in November 1845 from the part of Perry County west of the Tennessee River in response to a petition by citizens on the west side of the river who lacked easy access to the county seat, which was located on the east side of the river.
