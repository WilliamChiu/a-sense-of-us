Henderson County, Tennessee

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.04694462%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 3227 people in Henderson County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 21943 people in Henderson County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2000, 352 people in Henderson County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 25522 people in Henderson County, Tennessee lived in the county.

In 2010, 3824 people in Hardin County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 21831 people in Hardin County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 371 people in Hardin County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 26026 people in Hardin County, Tennessee lived in the county.

Henderson County was established in 1821; it was named for Colonel James Henderson, Jr. (1775-1814), of the Tennessee State Militia, who was killed in late December of 1814 below New Orleans during a clash with the British Army. Henderson is said to have served in earlier conflicts such as the Creek Indian war, which took place during the same overall time period as the War of 1812.
