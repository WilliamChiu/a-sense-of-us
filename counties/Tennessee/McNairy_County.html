McNairy County, Tennessee

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.3217449%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 3189 people in McNairy County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 20936 people in McNairy County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2000, 528 people in McNairy County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 24653 people in McNairy County, Tennessee lived in the county.

In 2010, 4553 people in Lincoln County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 28525 people in Lincoln County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 283 people in Lincoln County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 33361 people in Lincoln County, Tennessee lived in the county.

McNairy County was formed in 1823 from parts of Hardin County, and was named for Judge John McNairy.
