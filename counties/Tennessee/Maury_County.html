Maury County, Tennessee

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.7625442%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 8603 people in Maury County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 59657 people in Maury County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2000, 1238 people in Maury County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 69498 people in Maury County, Tennessee lived in the county.

In 2010, 14871 people in Madison County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 79005 people in Madison County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 4418 people in Madison County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 98294 people in Madison County, Tennessee lived in the county.

The county was formed in 1807 from Williamson County and Indian lands. Maury County was named in honor of Major Abram Poindexter Maury of Williamson County, who was a member of the Tennessee legislature, and an uncle of Commodore Matthew Fontaine Maury.
