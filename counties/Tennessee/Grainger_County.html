Grainger County, Tennessee

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -3.4947217%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2435 people in Grainger County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 18046 people in Grainger County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2000, 178 people in Grainger County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 20659 people in Grainger County, Tennessee lived in the county.

In 2010, 5421 people in Franklin County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 33855 people in Franklin County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 1776 people in Franklin County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 41052 people in Franklin County, Tennessee lived in the county.

Grainger County was formed from Knox and Hawkins Counties in 1796, the year Tennessee became a state. It is named for Mary Grainger Blount, wife of William Blount. Anderson, Claiborne, Campbell, Hamblen, Hancock, Scott and Union Counties were formed from parts of the original Grainger County.
