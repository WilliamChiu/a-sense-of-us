Cheatham County, Tennessee

In 2000, 3395 people in Cheatham County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 32116 people in Cheatham County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 1935 people in Cannon County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 11702 people in Cannon County, Tennessee lived in family households.

Cheatham County was created by an Act of the Tennessee General Assembly in 1856, from lands formerly of Davidson, Dickson, Montgomery, and Robertson counties. Cheatham County was named for Edward Saunders Cheatham, a state legislator.
