Coffee County, Tennessee

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.306962%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 6250 people in Coffee County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 40980 people in Coffee County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2000, 784 people in Coffee County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 48014 people in Coffee County, Tennessee lived in the county.

In 2010, 4488 people in Claiborne County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 26468 people in Claiborne County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 1257 people in Claiborne County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 32213 people in Claiborne County, Tennessee lived in the county.

Coffee County was formed in 1836 from parts of Bedford, Warren, and Franklin counties. It was named for John Coffee.
