Knox County, Tennessee

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.9107223%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 70545 people in Knox County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 298623 people in Knox County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2000, 12864 people in Knox County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 382032 people in Knox County, Tennessee lived in the county.

In 2010, 1900 people in Jackson County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 9561 people in Jackson County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 177 people in Jackson County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 11638 people in Jackson County, Tennessee lived in the county.

Knox County was created on June 11, 1792, by Governor William Blount from parts of Greene and Hawkins counties, one of the few counties created when the state was still known as the Southwest Territory. It is one of nine United States counties named for American Revolutionary War general and first United States Secretary of War Henry Knox. Parts of Knox County later became Blount (1795), Anderson (1801), Roane (1801), and Union (1850) counties.
