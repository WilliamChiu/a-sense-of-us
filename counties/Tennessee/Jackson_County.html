Jackson County, Tennessee

In 2000, 1554 people in Jackson County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 9294 people in Jackson County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 3114 people in Hickman County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 20063 people in Hickman County, Tennessee lived in family households.

Jackson County was created by an act of the Tennessee General Assembly on November 6, 1801. It was the eighteenth county established in the state. It was formed from part of Smith County plus Indian lands. The name honors Andrew Jackson, a U.S. congressman and senator, Tennessee Supreme Court judge, commander at the Battle of New Orleans, and the seventh President of the United States.
