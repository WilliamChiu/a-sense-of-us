Gibson County, Tennessee

In 2000, 6717 people in Gibson County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 40367 people in Gibson County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 4057 people in Fayette County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 33935 people in Fayette County, Tennessee lived in family households.

Gibson County is located in what was known as "Indian Land": territory that was legally occupied by Chickasaw Native American people. The Chickasaw Cession, proclaimed on January 7, 1819, eliminated those rights and opened the region for settlement and exploitation by white settlers and speculators.
