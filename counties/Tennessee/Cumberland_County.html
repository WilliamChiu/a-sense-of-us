Cumberland County, Tennessee

In 2000, 5760 people in Cumberland County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 40502 people in Cumberland County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 5542 people in Cocke County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 29826 people in Cocke County, Tennessee lived in family households.

Cumberland County was formed in 1856 from parts of Bledsoe, Roane, Morgan, Fentress, Rhea, Putnam, Overton, and White. During the Civil War, the county was nearly evenly-split between those supporting the Union and those supporting the Confederacy.
