Scott County, Tennessee

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.24799109%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2444 people in Scott County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 18483 people in Scott County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2000, 200 people in Scott County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 21127 people in Scott County, Tennessee lived in the county.

In 2010, 8014 people in Roane County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 45521 people in Roane County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 646 people in Roane County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 54181 people in Roane County, Tennessee lived in the county.

Scott County was formed in 1849 from portions of Anderson, Campbell, Fentress and Morgan counties. It is named for U.S. Army General Winfield Scott, a hero of the Mexican War.
