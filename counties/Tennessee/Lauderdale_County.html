Lauderdale County, Tennessee

In 2000, 3120 people in Lauderdale County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 21293 people in Lauderdale County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 2748 people in Johnson County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 13669 people in Johnson County, Tennessee lived in family households.

Lauderdale County was created in 1835 from parts of Tipton, Dyer and Haywood counties, and named for Lieutenant Colonel James Lauderdale, who was killed at the Battle of New Orleans in the War of 1812.
