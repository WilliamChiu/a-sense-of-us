Lauderdale County, Tennessee

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.10635257%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 3120 people in Lauderdale County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 21293 people in Lauderdale County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2000, 2688 people in Lauderdale County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 27101 people in Lauderdale County, Tennessee lived in the county.

In 2010, 2748 people in Johnson County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 13669 people in Johnson County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 1827 people in Johnson County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 18244 people in Johnson County, Tennessee lived in the county.

Lauderdale County was created in 1835 from parts of Tipton, Dyer and Haywood counties, and named for Lieutenant Colonel James Lauderdale, who was killed at the Battle of New Orleans in the War of 1812.
