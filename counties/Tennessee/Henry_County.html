Henry County, Tennessee

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.8968234%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4653 people in Henry County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 25938 people in Henry County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2000, 524 people in Henry County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 31115 people in Henry County, Tennessee lived in the county.

In 2010, 8073 people in Hawkins County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 48304 people in Hawkins County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 456 people in Hawkins County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 56833 people in Hawkins County, Tennessee lived in the county.

Henry County is a county located in the U.S. state of Tennessee. As of the 2010 census, the population was 32,330. Its county seat is Paris. The county is named for the Virginia orator Patrick Henry.
