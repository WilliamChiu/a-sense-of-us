Hamilton County, Tennessee

In 2000, 48309 people in Hamilton County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 251797 people in Hamilton County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 10404 people in Greene County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 56675 people in Greene County, Tennessee lived in family households.

Hamilton County was formed on October 25, 1819 from portions of Rhea County and Indian land. It was named after Alexander Hamilton, an officer in the American Revolutionary War, member of the Continental Congress, the first US Secretary of Treasury, and one of the founding fathers of the United States.
