Hardeman County, Tennessee

In 2000, 3036 people in Hardeman County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 21105 people in Hardeman County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 8872 people in Hamblen County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 52754 people in Hamblen County, Tennessee lived in family households.

Hardeman County was created by the Tennessee General Assembly in 1823 from parts of Hardin County and "Indian lands." It is named for Thomas Jones Hardeman (1788-1854), a Creek War and War of 1812 veteran and prominent figure in the fight for Texas independence, and a Republic of Texas congressman.
