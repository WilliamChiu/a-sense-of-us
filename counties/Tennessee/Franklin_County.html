Household Populations

Franklin County, Tennessee

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 3.0082822%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4430 people in Franklin County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 33180 people in Franklin County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2000, 1660 people in Franklin County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 39270 people in Franklin County, Tennessee lived in the county.

In 2010, 5519 people in Dyer County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 32300 people in Dyer County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 516 people in Dyer County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 38335 people in Dyer County, Tennessee lived in the county.

Euro-American settlement began around 1800, and the county was formally organized in 1807 and named for Benjamin Franklin. During the next several decades, the size of the county was reduced several times by reorganizations which created the neighboring counties of Coffee County, Moore County, and Grundy County. One of the most notable early settlers was frontiersman Davy Crockett, who came about 1812 but is not thought to have remained long.
