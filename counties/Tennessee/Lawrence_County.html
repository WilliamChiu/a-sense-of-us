Lawrence County, Tennessee

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.9556165%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 4675 people in Lawrence County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 34884 people in Lawrence County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2000, 367 people in Lawrence County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 39926 people in Lawrence County, Tennessee lived in the county.

In 2010, 85280 people in Knox County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 334598 people in Knox County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 12348 people in Knox County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 432226 people in Knox County, Tennessee lived in the county.

Created by an act of the Tennessee General Assembly on October 21, 1817, Lawrence County was formed from lands previously part of Hickman and Giles counties.
