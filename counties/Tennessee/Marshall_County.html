Marshall County, Tennessee

In 2000, 3330 people in Marshall County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 23089 people in Marshall County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 2895 people in Macon County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 19084 people in Macon County, Tennessee lived in family households.

Marshall County was created in 1836 from parts of Giles, Bedford, Lincoln and Maury counties, and was named after the American jurist, John Marshall, Chief Justice of the U.S. Supreme Court.
