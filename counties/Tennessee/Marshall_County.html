Marshall County, Tennessee

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.092208385%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 3330 people in Marshall County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 23089 people in Marshall County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2000, 348 people in Marshall County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 26767 people in Marshall County, Tennessee lived in the county.

In 2010, 2895 people in Macon County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 19084 people in Macon County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 269 people in Macon County, Tennessee lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 22248 people in Macon County, Tennessee lived in the county.

Marshall County was created in 1836 from parts of Giles, Bedford, Lincoln and Maury counties, and was named after the American jurist, John Marshall, Chief Justice of the U.S. Supreme Court.
