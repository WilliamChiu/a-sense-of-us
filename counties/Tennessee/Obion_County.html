Obion County, Tennessee

In 2000, 4285 people in Obion County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 27643 people in Obion County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 23045 people in Montgomery County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 145870 people in Montgomery County, Tennessee lived in family households.

Obion was established in 1823 and organized the following year. It was named for the Obion River, which flows through the county and is a tributary of the nearby Mississippi River. The word "Obion" is believed to be derived from a Native American word meaning "many forks."
