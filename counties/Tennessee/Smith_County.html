Smith County, Tennessee

In 2000, 2075 people in Smith County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 15489 people in Smith County, Tennessee lived in family households.

In 2010, 1854 people in Sequatchie County, Tennessee lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 12070 people in Sequatchie County, Tennessee lived in family households.

Smith County was established in 1799 from a portion of Sumner County, was named for Daniel Smith, a U.S. Senator and former Secretary of the Southwest Territory. The location of the county seat was hotly contested between Bledsoesborough (near modern Dixon Springs) and William Walton's ferry and tavern at the confluence of the Caney Fork and the Cumberland rivers. In 1804, voters chose Walton's site, and a town, named "Carthage," was platted the following year.
