Kenosha County, Wisconsin

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.7518759%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 21861 people in Kenosha County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 123692 people in Kenosha County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2000, 4024 people in Kenosha County, Wisconsin lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 149577 people in Kenosha County, Wisconsin lived in the county.

In 2010, 12936 people in Jefferson County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 67072 people in Jefferson County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2010, 3678 people in Jefferson County, Wisconsin lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 83686 people in Jefferson County, Wisconsin lived in the county.

Kenosha County is a county located in the southeastern corner of the U.S. state of Wisconsin. As of the 2010 census, the population was 166,426. Its county seat is Kenosha. The county is named after the county seat.
