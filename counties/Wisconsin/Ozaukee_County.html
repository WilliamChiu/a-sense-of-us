Ozaukee County, Wisconsin

In 2000, 9332 people in Ozaukee County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 71226 people in Ozaukee County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2010, 6775 people in Oneida County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 28613 people in Oneida County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

Ozaukee County was once part of neighboring Washington County to the west. Its name comes from the Ojibwe name for the Sacs. The word probably means "people living at the mouth of a river."
