Oneida County, Wisconsin

In 2000, 5798 people in Oneida County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 30070 people in Oneida County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2010, 6835 people in Monroe County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 36979 people in Monroe County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

Oneida County was formed in 1885 from sections of Lincoln County. It was named after the indigenous Oneida tribe, one of the five nations of the Iroquois.
