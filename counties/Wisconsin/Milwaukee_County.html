Milwaukee County, Wisconsin

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.612556%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 188586 people in Milwaukee County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 727468 people in Milwaukee County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2000, 24110 people in Milwaukee County, Wisconsin lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 940164 people in Milwaukee County, Wisconsin lived in the county.

In 2010, 2758 people in Marquette County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 12493 people in Marquette County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2010, 153 people in Marquette County, Wisconsin lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 15404 people in Marquette County, Wisconsin lived in the county.

Milwaukee County is a county in the State of Wisconsin. As of the 2010 census, the population was 947,735 and was estimated to be 956,406 in 2014. It is the most populous county in Wisconsin and the 45th most populous in the United States. Its county seat is Milwaukee, which is also the largest city in the state.
