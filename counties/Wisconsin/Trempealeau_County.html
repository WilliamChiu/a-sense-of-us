Trempealeau County, Wisconsin

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.26880503%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 4205 people in Trempealeau County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 22169 people in Trempealeau County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2000, 636 people in Trempealeau County, Wisconsin lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 27010 people in Trempealeau County, Wisconsin lived in the county.

In 2010, 18555 people in Sheboygan County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 93929 people in Sheboygan County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2010, 3023 people in Sheboygan County, Wisconsin lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 115507 people in Sheboygan County, Wisconsin lived in the county.

Patches of woodland are all that remain of the brush and light forest that once covered the county. In ancient times, the woodlands contained a great deal of timber, but Native Americans burned them periodically to encourage the growth of berries. They did little cultivation and had been almost completely removed from the area by 1837.
