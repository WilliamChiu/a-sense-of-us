Calumet County, Wisconsin

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.099384785%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 4653 people in Calumet County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 35618 people in Calumet County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2000, 360 people in Calumet County, Wisconsin lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 40631 people in Calumet County, Wisconsin lived in the county.

In 2010, 2328 people in Buffalo County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 11152 people in Buffalo County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2010, 107 people in Buffalo County, Wisconsin lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 13587 people in Buffalo County, Wisconsin lived in the county.

The county's name originated from the word ''calumet,'' which was a ceremonial pipe used by Native Americans in councils on the east shore of Lake Winnebago.
