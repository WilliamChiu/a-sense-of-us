Waushara County, Wisconsin

In 2000, 3287 people in Waushara County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 19397 people in Waushara County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2010, 52902 people in Waukesha County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 331339 people in Waukesha County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

Waushara County was established by an act of the Wisconsin Legislature on February 15, 1851. It originally consisted of a single organized Town of Waushara. The county seat was first located at Sacramento and was relocated to Wautoma in 1854 after a bitter fight between proponents of the two places. The name is of Native American origin and is believed to mean 'good land'.
