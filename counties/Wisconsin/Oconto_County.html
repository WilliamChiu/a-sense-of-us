Oconto County, Wisconsin

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.5954256%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 35634 people in Oconto County, Wisconsin lived in the county.

In 2010, 24490 people in Milwaukee County, Wisconsin lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 947735 people in Milwaukee County, Wisconsin lived in the county.

First visited by French explorers in the 17th century, Oconto County is among the oldest settlements in Wisconsin. Father Claude-Jean Allouez of the Roman Catholic Jesuit order said the first Mass in Oconto. Among the first settlers was Joseph Tourtilott, who explored much of the Oconto River watershed. Many of Tourtilott's descendants continue to reside in Oconto County, which has the highest percentage of residents with French ancestry in Wisconsin.
