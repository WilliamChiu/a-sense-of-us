Vernon County, Wisconsin

In 2000, 3824 people in Vernon County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 23759 people in Vernon County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2010, 3234 people in Taylor County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 17224 people in Taylor County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

Vernon County was renamed from '''Bad Ax County''' on 22 March 1862. Bad Ax County had been created on 1 March 1851 from territory that had been part of Richland and Crawford counties. The name ''Vernon'' was chosen to reflect the county's green fields of wheat and to evoke Mount Vernon.
