Douglas County, Wisconsin

In 2000, 8037 people in Douglas County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 33957 people in Douglas County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2010, 12974 people in Dodge County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 69593 people in Dodge County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

Douglas County, named after Illinois Senator Stephen A. Douglas, was established on February 8, 1854, from the larger La Pointe County, Wisconsin, and the City of Superior was immediately selected as the county seat.
