Wood County, Wisconsin

In 2000, 11409 people in Wood County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 62940 people in Wood County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2010, 19343 people in Waushara County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

Wood County is a county located in the U.S. state of Wisconsin. It is named after Joseph Wood, a member of the Wisconsin State Assembly. As of the 2010 census, the population was 74,749. Its county seat is Wisconsin Rapids.
