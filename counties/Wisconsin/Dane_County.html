Dane County, Wisconsin

In 2000, 104034 people in Dane County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 306685 people in Dane County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2010, 9012 people in Columbia County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 46295 people in Columbia County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

Dane County was formed in 1836 as a territorial county. It was named after Nathan Dane, a Massachusetts delegate to the Congress of the Confederation who helped carve Wisconsin out of the Northwest Territory. Dane County was first settled in the 1840s by settlers from New England.
