Pepin County, Wisconsin

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.071287155%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 941 people in Pepin County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6153 people in Pepin County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2000, 119 people in Pepin County, Wisconsin lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 7213 people in Pepin County, Wisconsin lived in the county.

In 2010, 28408 people in Outagamie County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 145248 people in Outagamie County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2010, 3039 people in Outagamie County, Wisconsin lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 176695 people in Outagamie County, Wisconsin lived in the county.

Pepin County was formed in 1858 from portions of Dunn County. It was named after the French conquistadors Pierre and Jean Pepin du Chardonnets.
