St. Croix County, Wisconsin

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.35312772%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 8376 people in St. Croix County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 936 people in St. Croix County, Wisconsin lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 63155 people in St. Croix County, Wisconsin lived in the county.

In 2010, 25355 people in Rock County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 132042 people in Rock County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2010, 2934 people in Rock County, Wisconsin lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 160331 people in Rock County, Wisconsin lived in the county.

St. Croix County was created on August 3, 1840 by the legislature of the Wisconsin Territory. It was named after the river on its western border. Sources vary on the origin of the name; the St. Croix River may have been named after Monsieur St. Croix, an explorer who drowned at the mouth of the river late in the seventeenth century. Another account credits Father Hennepin with giving this region the French name ''Ste Croix'' (Holy Cross) because of the burial markers located at the mouth of the river.
