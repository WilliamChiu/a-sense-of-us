Ashland County, Wisconsin

In 2000, 2903 people in Ashland County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 13168 people in Ashland County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2010, 3108 people in Wyoming County, West Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 20632 people in Wyoming County, West Virginia lived in family households.

Ashland County was named in honor of the Lexington estate of Kentucky statesman Henry Clay, as one of the founders of the city of Ashland was an admirer of Clay.
