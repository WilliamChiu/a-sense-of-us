Rusk County, Wisconsin

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.7954478%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2311 people in Rusk County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 12631 people in Rusk County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2000, 405 people in Rusk County, Wisconsin lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 15347 people in Rusk County, Wisconsin lived in the county.

In 2010, 3022 people in Richland County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 14663 people in Richland County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2010, 336 people in Richland County, Wisconsin lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 18021 people in Richland County, Wisconsin lived in the county.

Founded in 1901, Rusk County was originally named Gates County after Milwaukee land speculator James L. Gates. It was renamed Rusk County in 1905 after Jeremiah M. Rusk, governor of Wisconsin and the first U.S. Secretary of Agriculture. It was formed out of the northern portion of Chippewa County.
