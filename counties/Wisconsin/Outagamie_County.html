Outagamie County, Wisconsin

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.3010025%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 22894 people in Outagamie County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 134824 people in Outagamie County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2000, 3253 people in Outagamie County, Wisconsin lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 160971 people in Outagamie County, Wisconsin lived in the county.

In 2010, 5744 people in Oconto County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 31635 people in Oconto County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2010, 281 people in Oconto County, Wisconsin lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 37660 people in Oconto County, Wisconsin lived in the county.

"Outagamie," a French transliteration of the Anishinaabe term for the Meskwaki (Fox) Indians, meant "dwellers of other shore" or "dwellers on the other side of the stream," referring to their historic habitation along the St. Lawrence River and south of the Great Lakes. They had occupied considerable territory in Wisconsin prior to colonization.
