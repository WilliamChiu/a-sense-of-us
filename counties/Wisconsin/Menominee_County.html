Menominee County, Wisconsin

In 2000, 349 people in Menominee County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 4161 people in Menominee County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2010, 7513 people in Marinette County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 33163 people in Marinette County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

The county was created from the northeastern portion of Shawano County on July 3, 1959, in anticipation of the termination of the Menominee Indian Reservation in 1961. The reservation status was restored in 1973, and is now co-extensive with the county.
