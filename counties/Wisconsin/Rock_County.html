Rock County, Wisconsin

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.28437972%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 22761 people in Rock County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 126076 people in Rock County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2000, 3470 people in Rock County, Wisconsin lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 152307 people in Rock County, Wisconsin lived in the county.

In 2010, 29467 people in Racine County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 160946 people in Racine County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2010, 4995 people in Racine County, Wisconsin lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 195408 people in Racine County, Wisconsin lived in the county.

Rock County was created in 1836 as a territorial county on December 7, 1836 from Milwaukee County and fully organized Feb. 19, 1839. The county is named for the Rock River, which bisects the county from north to south.
