Winnebago County, Wisconsin

In 2000, 28046 people in Winnebago County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 120392 people in Winnebago County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2010, 8521 people in Waupaca County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 42254 people in Waupaca County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

The name ''Winnebago'' is of Algonquin origin and means 'people dwelling by the fetid or ill-smelling water', which may refer to a sulfur spring. The county seat, Oshkosh, was incorporated as a city in 1853, when it already had a population of nearly 2,800.
