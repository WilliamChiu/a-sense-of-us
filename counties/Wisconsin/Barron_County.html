Barron County, Wisconsin

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -5.4040074%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 6769 people in Barron County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 37502 people in Barron County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2000, 692 people in Barron County, Wisconsin lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 44963 people in Barron County, Wisconsin lived in the county.

In 2010, 3509 people in Adams County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 15934 people in Adams County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2010, 1432 people in Adams County, Wisconsin lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 20875 people in Adams County, Wisconsin lived in the county.

In 1865 Dallas County (named after Vice President George M. Dallas) was organized with the county seat located at Barron. On 4 March 1869 Barron County was created by renaming Dallas County. The county took the name ''Barron'' in the honor of Wisconsin lawyer and politician Henry D. Barron, who served as Circuit Judge of the Eleventh Judicial Circuit.
