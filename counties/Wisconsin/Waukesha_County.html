Waukesha County, Wisconsin

In 2000, 42566 people in Waukesha County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 312448 people in Waukesha County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

In 2010, 2872 people in Washburn County, Wisconsin lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 12834 people in Washburn County, Wisconsin lived in family households.

The part of Wisconsin that Waukesha County now occupies was a part of Michigan when Milwaukee County was organized in September 1834. On July 4, 1836, the Wisconsin Territory was formed, which included land currently in the state of Minnesota. In January 1846, part of Milwaukee County was split off into Waukesha County. Curtis Reed was the first county chairman. When voting took place to decide the county seat, Waukesha beat out Pewaukee by two votes. The name is derived from the Potawatomi word for 'fox' because the streams in the lower part of the county drain into the Fox River.
