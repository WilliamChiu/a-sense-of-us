Hillsborough County, New Hampshire

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -4.068196%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 58521 people in Hillsborough County, New Hampshire lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 314651 people in Hillsborough County, New Hampshire lived in family households.

In 2000, 7669 people in Hillsborough County, New Hampshire lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 380841 people in Hillsborough County, New Hampshire lived in the county.

In 2010, 14984 people in Cheshire County, New Hampshire lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 57506 people in Cheshire County, New Hampshire lived in family households.

In 2010, 4627 people in Cheshire County, New Hampshire lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 77117 people in Cheshire County, New Hampshire lived in the county.

Hillsborough was one of the five original counties identified for New Hampshire in 1769, and was named for Wills Hill, the Viscount Hillsborough who was British Secretary of State for the Colonies at the time. The county was organized at Amherst on March 19, 1771. In 1823 a number of towns were removed to become part of Merrimack County. Over several years ending in 1869, county administrative functions were moved from Amherst first to Milford in 1866 then to the current seats of Manchester and Nashua.
