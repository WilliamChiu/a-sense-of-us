Belknap County, New Hampshire

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.9223938%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 8722 people in Belknap County, New Hampshire lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 46390 people in Belknap County, New Hampshire lived in family households.

In 2000, 1213 people in Belknap County, New Hampshire lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 56325 people in Belknap County, New Hampshire lived in the county.

In 2010, 82855 people in Washoe County, Nevada lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 5272 people in Washoe County, Nevada lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 421407 people in Washoe County, Nevada lived in the county.

Belknap County was organized in 1840 by removing parts of northeastern Merrimack County and northwestern Strafford County. It is named for Dr. Jeremy Belknap, a renowned preacher, historian, and author of ''The History of New Hampshire''. The first County Court was held within the town of Meredith, at a village known as Meredith Bridge on the Winnipesaukee River. In 1855, the town of Laconia was separated from Meredith.
