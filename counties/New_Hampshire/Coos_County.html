Coos County, New Hampshire

In 2000, 5753 people in Coos County, New Hampshire lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 26712 people in Coos County, New Hampshire lived in family households.

In 2010, 10345 people in Belknap County, New Hampshire lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 48885 people in Belknap County, New Hampshire lived in family households.

Coös County was separated from the northern part of Grafton County, New Hampshire and organized at Berlin December 24, 1803, although the county seat was later moved to Lancaster, with an additional shire town at Colebrook. The name ''Coös'' derives from the Algonquian word meaning "small pines".
