Grafton County, New Hampshire

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 7.4858546%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 15017 people in Grafton County, New Hampshire lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 60338 people in Grafton County, New Hampshire lived in family households.

In 2000, 6388 people in Grafton County, New Hampshire lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 81743 people in Grafton County, New Hampshire lived in the county.

In 2010, 9269 people in Carroll County, New Hampshire lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 38112 people in Carroll County, New Hampshire lived in family households.

In 2010, 437 people in Carroll County, New Hampshire lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 47818 people in Carroll County, New Hampshire lived in the county.

Grafton was one of the five counties originally identified for New Hampshire in 1769. It was named for Augustus FitzRoy, 3rd Duke of Grafton, who had been a supporter of American causes in Parliament, and who was serving as British Prime Minister at the time. The county was organized at Woodsville in 1771, and originally included the entire northern frontier of New Hampshire, including a number of towns that are now in Vermont. In 1803, the northern area was removed for the formation of Coos County. The three counties to the south were Strafford, Hillsborough and Cheshire, and the eastern edge bordered the "District of Maine". In 1797, the county contained 50 townships and 17 locations, and had a population of 23,093.
