Fillmore County, Nebraska

In 2000, 979 people in Fillmore County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 5391 people in Fillmore County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2010, 6236 people in Dodge County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 29452 people in Dodge County, Nebraska lived in family households.

Fillmore County was established, and its boundaries defined, by the Nebraska Territorial Legislature in 1856. It was named for Millard Fillmore, the thirteenth president of the United States, who had left office in 1853.
