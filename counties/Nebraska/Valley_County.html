Valley County, Nebraska

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.1763155%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 732 people in Valley County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 3830 people in Valley County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2000, 85 people in Valley County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 4647 people in Valley County, Nebraska lived in the county.

In 2010, 914 people in Thayer County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 4158 people in Thayer County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2010, 156 people in Thayer County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 5228 people in Thayer County, Nebraska lived in the county.

Valley County was formed in 1871 and later organized in 1873. Its name derives from the local terrain: much of the county consists of valley land lying between the North and Middle Loup Rivers.
