Dawson County, Nebraska

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.78645945%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 3121 people in Dawson County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 20771 people in Dawson County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2000, 473 people in Dawson County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 24365 people in Dawson County, Nebraska lived in the county.

In 2010, 1860 people in Custer County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 8951 people in Custer County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2010, 128 people in Custer County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 10939 people in Custer County, Nebraska lived in the county.

Dawson County was formed in 1860. Sources differ on the origin of its name: some hold that it was named after a Democratic Pennsylvania Congressman, John Littleton Dawson, while others believe that it was named after Jacob Dawson, an early Nebraska settler.
