Stanton County is a county located in the U.S. state of Nebraska. As of the 2010 census, the population was 6,129. Its county seat is Stanton. The county was formed in 1856 and later organized in 1867. It was first called Izard County until 1862, when it was renamed for Edwin M. Stanton, Secretary of War during the administration of President Abraham Lincoln.
