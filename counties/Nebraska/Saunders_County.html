Saunders County, Nebraska

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.6888509%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2416 people in Saunders County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 17159 people in Saunders County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2000, 255 people in Saunders County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 19830 people in Saunders County, Nebraska lived in the county.

In 2010, 246 people in Rock County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1250 people in Rock County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2010, 30 people in Rock County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 1526 people in Rock County, Nebraska lived in the county.

Saunders County was established by an 1856 act of the Nebraska Territorial Legislature and later organized in 1866; its boundaries were redefined in 1858. The county was originally named after John C. Calhoun; in 1862, during the American Civil War, it was renamed after Nebraska territorial governor Alvin Saunders.
