Knox County, Nebraska

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.9976735%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1318 people in Knox County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 7812 people in Knox County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2000, 244 people in Knox County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 9374 people in Knox County, Nebraska lived in the county.

In 2010, 1558 people in Keith County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 6755 people in Keith County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2010, 55 people in Keith County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 8368 people in Keith County, Nebraska lived in the county.

Knox County was organized by the Territorial Legislature in 1857, and named L'Eau Qui Court, that being the French name for the river named by the Indians Niobrara--both names meaning, in English, Running Water. The name was changed to Knox by a statute passed February 21, 1873, which took effect April 1, 1873.
