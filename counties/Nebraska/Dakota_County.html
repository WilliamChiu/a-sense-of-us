Dakota County, Nebraska

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.4156947%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2540 people in Dakota County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 17422 people in Dakota County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2000, 291 people in Dakota County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 20253 people in Dakota County, Nebraska lived in the county.

In 2010, 1271 people in Colfax County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 9136 people in Colfax County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2010, 108 people in Colfax County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 10515 people in Colfax County, Nebraska lived in the county.

Succeeding cultures of indigenous peoples lived along the Missouri River for thousands of years. By 1775, the Omaha people had migrated west of the Missouri, where they established a major settlement, which they called ''Ton-wa-tonga,'' meaning the Big Village. It had about 1100 residents. From here, the Omaha controlled fur trading on the upper Missouri River with other tribes and with French-Canadian traders, often called ''voyageurs''. The Omaha were the first of the Northern Plains tribes to have adopted an equestrian culture.
