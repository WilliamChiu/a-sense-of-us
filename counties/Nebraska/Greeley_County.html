Greeley County, Nebraska

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.83949566%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 360 people in Greeley County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 2292 people in Greeley County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2000, 62 people in Greeley County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 2714 people in Greeley County, Nebraska lived in the county.

In 2010, 382 people in Garfield County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1637 people in Garfield County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2010, 30 people in Garfield County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 2049 people in Garfield County, Nebraska lived in the county.

Greeley County was created in 1871 and organized in 1872. It was named after Horace Greeley, a newspaper editor and politician of the mid-19th century. Greeley encourgaged western settlement with the motto "Go West, young man".
