Holt County, Nebraska

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.5862355%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1569 people in Holt County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 9757 people in Holt County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2000, 225 people in Holt County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 11551 people in Holt County, Nebraska lived in the county.

In 2010, 606 people in Harlan County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 2770 people in Harlan County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2010, 47 people in Harlan County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 3423 people in Harlan County, Nebraska lived in the county.

The county was created by an act of the Nebraska Legislature in 1862 and was later organized in 1876. It is named for Joseph Holt of Kentucky, who was Postmaster General and Secretary of War under President James Buchanan. It shares its name with Holt County, Missouri, though it is named for a different Holt.
