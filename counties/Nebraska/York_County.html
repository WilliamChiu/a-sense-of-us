York County, Nebraska

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -8.614063%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2078 people in York County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 11795 people in York County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2000, 725 people in York County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 14598 people in York County, Nebraska lived in the county.

In 2010, 1947 people in Wayne County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 6386 people in Wayne County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2010, 1262 people in Wayne County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 9595 people in Wayne County, Nebraska lived in the county.

York County was organized in 1870. Sources differ on the origin of the county's name: some state that it was named after York in England, while others maintain that it was named by early settlers from York County, Pennsylvania.
