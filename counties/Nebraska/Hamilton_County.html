Hamilton County, Nebraska

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.1027217%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 942 people in Hamilton County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 8313 people in Hamilton County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2000, 148 people in Hamilton County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 9403 people in Hamilton County, Nebraska lived in the county.

In 2010, 94 people in Grant County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 517 people in Grant County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2010, 3 people in Grant County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 614 people in Grant County, Nebraska lived in the county.

The first permanent settlers arrived in Hamilton County in 1866. Hamilton County was created in 1867 and later organized in 1870. It was named after Alexander Hamilton.
