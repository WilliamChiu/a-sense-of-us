Sarpy County, Nebraska

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.8006811%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 12922 people in Sarpy County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 108372 people in Sarpy County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2000, 1301 people in Sarpy County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 122595 people in Sarpy County, Nebraska lived in the county.

In 2010, 1627 people in Richardson County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 6581 people in Richardson County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2010, 155 people in Richardson County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 8363 people in Richardson County, Nebraska lived in the county.

Sarpy County is named after Colonel Peter Sarpy, an early fur trader at Fontenelle's Post located in the Bellevue area in the 1840s. Sarpy died in Plattsmouth, Nebraska Territory in 1865.
