Perkins County, Nebraska

In 2000, 421 people in Perkins County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 2725 people in Perkins County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2010, 880 people in Nuckolls County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 3561 people in Nuckolls County, Nebraska lived in family households.

Perkins County was organized in 1887. It is believed to have been named after Charles E. Perkins, the president of the Chicago, Burlington and Quincy Railroad.
