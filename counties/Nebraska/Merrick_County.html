Merrick County, Nebraska

In 2000, 1035 people in Merrick County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 7034 people in Merrick County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2010, 91 people in Loup County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 541 people in Loup County, Nebraska lived in family households.

Merrick County was formed in 1858 and later organized in 1864. It was named for Elvira Merrick, the maiden name of the wife of territorial legislator Henry W. DePuy, who introduced the bill creating the county.
