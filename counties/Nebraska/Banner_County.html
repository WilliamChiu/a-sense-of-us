Banner County, Nebraska

In 2000, 87 people in Banner County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 732 people in Banner County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2010, 5482 people in Adams County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 24328 people in Adams County, Nebraska lived in family households.

When Nebraska became a state in 1867, a single county encompassed the entire Panhandle. In 1870, the southern half of the Panhandle was organized as Cheyenne County. The arrival of increasing numbers of settlers led to the partitioning of the county in 1888: the eastern third became Deuel County; the middle third became present-day Cheyenne County; and the western third became Kimball, Scotts Bluff, and Banner Counties.
