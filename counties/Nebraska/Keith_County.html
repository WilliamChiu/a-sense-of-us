Keith County, Nebraska

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.26753545%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1331 people in Keith County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 7444 people in Keith County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2000, 100 people in Keith County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 8875 people in Keith County, Nebraska lived in the county.

In 2010, 1416 people in Jefferson County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 6026 people in Jefferson County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2010, 105 people in Jefferson County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 7547 people in Jefferson County, Nebraska lived in the county.

Keith County was formed in 1873. Sources differ on the Keith after whom it was named: either M. C. Keith of North Platte, whose grandson Keith Neville became Nebraska's 18th governor in 1917; or John Keith, also of North Platte.
