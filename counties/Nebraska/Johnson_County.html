Johnson County, Nebraska

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.1812797%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 714 people in Johnson County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 3712 people in Johnson County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2000, 62 people in Johnson County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 4488 people in Johnson County, Nebraska lived in the county.

In 2010, 124 people in Hooker County, Nebraska lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 586 people in Hooker County, Nebraska lived in family households.

In 2010, 26 people in Hooker County, Nebraska lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 736 people in Hooker County, Nebraska lived in the county.

Johnson County is a county located in the U.S. state of Nebraska. As of the 2010 census, the population was 5,217. Its county seat is Tecumseh. The county was formed in 1855 and later organized in 1857. It was named after Vice President Richard M. Johnson.
