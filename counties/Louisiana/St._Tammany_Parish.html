St. Tammany Parish, Louisiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.09987354%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 20134 people in St. Tammany Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 168788 people in St. Tammany Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 2346 people in St. Tammany Parish, Louisiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 191268 people in St. Tammany Parish, Louisiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 11396 people in St. Landry Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 70883 people in St. Landry Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 1105 people in St. Landry Parish, Louisiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 83384 people in St. Landry Parish, Louisiana lived in the county.

In 1699, Pierre Le Moyne d'Iberville, a French explorer, was the first European to visit the area of present-day St. Tammany Parish. While exploring lakes Pontchartrain and Maurepas, Iberville wrote in his journal, "The place where I am is one of the prettiest I have seen, fine level ground bare of canes. The land north of the lakes is a country of pine trees mixed with hard woods. The soil is sandy and many tracks of buffalo and deer can be seen."
