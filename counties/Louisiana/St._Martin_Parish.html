St. Martin Parish, Louisiana

In 2000, 4958 people in St. Martin Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 42833 people in St. Martin Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 1991 people in St. James Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 19907 people in St. James Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

The parish is split into two noncontiguous parts, because of a surveying error dating to 1868, when Iberia Parish was created by the Louisiana Legislature. St. Martin has the highest percentage of French-speaking residents of any county or parish in the United States.
