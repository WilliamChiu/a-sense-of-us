Jefferson Parish, Louisiana

In 2000, 66827 people in Jefferson Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 384282 people in Jefferson Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 9242 people in Iberia Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 63054 people in Iberia Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

Jefferson Parish was named in honor of U. S. President Thomas Jefferson of Virginia when the parish was established by the Louisiana Legislature on February 11, 1825, a year before Jefferson died. A bronze statue of Jefferson stands at the entrance of the General Government Complex on Derbigny Street at the parish seat in Gretna. The parish seat was in the City of Lafayette, until that city was annexed by New Orleans in 1854.
