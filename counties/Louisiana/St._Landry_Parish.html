St. Landry Parish, Louisiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.6739855%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 10240 people in St. Landry Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 75932 people in St. Landry Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 1528 people in St. Landry Parish, Louisiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 87700 people in St. Landry Parish, Louisiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 1564 people in St. Helena Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 9518 people in St. Helena Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 121 people in St. Helena Parish, Louisiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 11203 people in St. Helena Parish, Louisiana lived in the county.

The land which became St. Landry Parish was inhabited since at least 10,500 B.C., as deduced from excavations of three prehistoric dwelling sites. By the 15th century, the Appalousa Indians settled in the area situated between Atchafalaya River and Sabine River (at the border of Texas-Louisiana). The Appalousa were warlike and preyed on neighbors to defend their own territory.
