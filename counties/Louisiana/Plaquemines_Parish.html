Plaquemines Parish, Louisiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.892983%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2447 people in Plaquemines Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 23582 people in Plaquemines Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 728 people in Plaquemines Parish, Louisiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 26757 people in Plaquemines Parish, Louisiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 6998 people in Natchitoches Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 30378 people in Natchitoches Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 2190 people in Natchitoches Parish, Louisiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 39566 people in Natchitoches Parish, Louisiana lived in the county.

The name ''"Plaquemines,"'' in French Creole, was derived from the Atakapa word, ''piakimin'', meaning the local fruit persimmon. The French used it to name a military post they built on the banks of the Mississippi River, as the site was surrounded by numerous persimmon trees. Eventually the name was applied to the entire parish and to a nearby bayou.
