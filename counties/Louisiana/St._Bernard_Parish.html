St. Bernard Parish, Louisiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.63936114%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 8148 people in St. Bernard Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 58293 people in St. Bernard Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 788 people in St. Bernard Parish, Louisiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 67229 people in St. Bernard Parish, Louisiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 1198 people in Red River Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7729 people in Red River Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 164 people in Red River Parish, Louisiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 9091 people in Red River Parish, Louisiana lived in the county.

St. Bernard Parish contains a large community of Spanish descent. Sometimes referred to informally as "Spanish Cajuns", the ''Isleños'' are descended from Canary Islanders. This linguistically isolated group eventually developed its own dialect. This settlement was first called ''La Concepcion'' and ''Nueva Galvez'' by Spanish officials, but was later renamed ''Terre aux Boeufs'' ("boeuf" = "beef") (French) and ''Tierra de Bueyes'' (Spanish) for "land of cattle", because nearby areas were used for cattle grazing. '''Saint Bernard''', the patron saint of colonial governor Bernardo de Galvez, was used in documents to identify the area.
