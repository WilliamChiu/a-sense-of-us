Vermilion Parish, Louisiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.1370301%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 6365 people in Vermilion Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 46675 people in Vermilion Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 767 people in Vermilion Parish, Louisiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 53807 people in Vermilion Parish, Louisiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 864 people in Tensas Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 4372 people in Tensas Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 16 people in Tensas Parish, Louisiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 5252 people in Tensas Parish, Louisiana lived in the county.

Indigenous peoples lived in the area for thousands of years, in different cultures. By historic times, the Chitimacha and Atakapa inhabited the area and were the American Indians encountered by Spanish and French explorers and settlers. The tribes' numbers were drastically reduced as a result of exposure to European diseases to which they had no immunity.
