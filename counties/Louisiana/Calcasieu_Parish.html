Calcasieu Parish, Louisiana

In 2000, 23558 people in Calcasieu Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 155472 people in Calcasieu Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 2214 people in Bienville Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 11820 people in Bienville Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

The name ''Calcasieu'' comes from the Atakapan word, ''"quelqueshue"'', meaning ''"crying eagle"''. It was originally the name of an Atakapa chief, but became the name given to what was formerly the Rio Hondo River (''Rio Stondo'' or "Deep River"), now the Calcasieu River. The parish then inherited this name.
