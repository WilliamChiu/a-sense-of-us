Rapides Parish, Louisiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.7910013%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 16184 people in Rapides Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 104649 people in Rapides Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 5504 people in Rapides Parish, Louisiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 126337 people in Rapides Parish, Louisiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 22924 people in Ouachita Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 125262 people in Ouachita Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 5534 people in Ouachita Parish, Louisiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 153720 people in Ouachita Parish, Louisiana lived in the county.

In 1763, the land that is now Rapides Parish became the new home of the Apalachee tribe, who were settled there with the permission of Governor Kerlerec. The Native Americans had come after fleeing the British and their Creek Indian allies from what is now from Leon County, Florida. Many of their descendants remain in Natchitoches Parish.
