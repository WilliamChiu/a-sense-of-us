St. James Parish, Louisiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.43830872%. This change is fairly different compared to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1633 people in St. James Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 19318 people in St. James Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 265 people in St. James Parish, Louisiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 21216 people in St. James Parish, Louisiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 5310 people in St. Bernard Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 30294 people in St. Bernard Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 293 people in St. Bernard Parish, Louisiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 35897 people in St. Bernard Parish, Louisiana lived in the county.

St. James is one of the state's nineteen original parishes, created by act of the territorial legislature, March 31, 1807. The original seat of government was the community of St. James, on the west bank of the Mississippi, but this was moved in 1869 to what is now Convent, on the east bank.
