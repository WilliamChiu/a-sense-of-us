Orleans Parish, Louisiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -9.876072%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 91534 people in Orleans Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 375499 people in Orleans Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 17641 people in Orleans Parish, Louisiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 484674 people in Orleans Parish, Louisiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 1431 people in Madison Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 9071 people in Madison Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 1591 people in Madison Parish, Louisiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 12093 people in Madison Parish, Louisiana lived in the county.

La Nouvelle-Orléans (New Orleans) was founded May 7, 1718, by the French Mississippi Company, under the direction of Jean-Baptiste Le Moyne de Bienville, on land inhabited by the Chitimacha. It was named for Philippe d'Orléans, Duke of Orléans, who was Regent of the Kingdom of France at the time. His title came from the French city of Orléans.
