De Soto Parish, Louisiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -7.14857%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 3051 people in De Soto Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 22121 people in De Soto Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 322 people in De Soto Parish, Louisiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 25494 people in De Soto Parish, Louisiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 1254 people in Catahoula Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 8287 people in Catahoula Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 866 people in Catahoula Parish, Louisiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 10407 people in Catahoula Parish, Louisiana lived in the county.

It is a typical misconception that the parish was named after Hernando de Soto, the Spaniard who explored the future southeastern United States and discovered and named the Mississippi River. The parish was in fact named after the unrelated Marcel DeSoto, who led the first group of European settlers there, to a settlement historically known as Bayou Pierre. The parish's name is also commonly misspelled following the explorer's name as "De Soto Parish," but it is properly spelled following the settler's name as "DeSoto Parish."
