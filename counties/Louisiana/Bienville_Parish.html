Bienville Parish, Louisiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.555121%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2057 people in Bienville Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 13322 people in Bienville Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 373 people in Bienville Parish, Louisiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 15752 people in Bienville Parish, Louisiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 2724 people in Assumption Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 20498 people in Assumption Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 199 people in Assumption Parish, Louisiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 23421 people in Assumption Parish, Louisiana lived in the county.

In the 1830s, Ruben Drake moved his family from South Carolina to what he named Mount Lebanon, the first permanent settlement in the parish. As the Drakes were devout Baptists, they established a church and school, which evolved into Mount Lebanon University, the forerunner of Louisiana College in Pineville in Rapides Parish in Central Louisiana.
