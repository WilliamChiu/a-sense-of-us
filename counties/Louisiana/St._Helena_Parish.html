St. Helena Parish, Louisiana

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.1394143%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1210 people in St. Helena Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 9243 people in St. Helena Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2000, 72 people in St. Helena Parish, Louisiana lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 10525 people in St. Helena Parish, Louisiana lived in the county.

In 2010, 3256 people in Sabine Parish, Louisiana lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 20537 people in Sabine Parish, Louisiana lived in family households.

In 2010, 440 people in Sabine Parish, Louisiana lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 24233 people in Sabine Parish, Louisiana lived in the county.

The parish is one of the eight "Florida Parishes", areas that were once part of colonial West Florida. It was annexed to the Orleans Territory in 1810, after the short-lived Republic of West Florida capitulated to the United States. In 1832, the southern section of the parish was taken to form Livingston Parish; the courthouse was moved to Greensburg, where it remains today. St. Helena lost another portion of land in 1866, which was added to land from Washington and Livingston Parishes in 1868, to form Tangipahoa Parish.
