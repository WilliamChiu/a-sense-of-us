Chesapeake city, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.35858154%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2010, 431 people in Bristol city, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 17835 people in Bristol city, Virginia lived in the county.

In 1963, the new independent city of Chesapeake was created when the former independent city of South Norfolk consolidated with Norfolk County. The consolidation was approved and the new name selected by the voters of each community by referendum, and authorized by the Virginia General Assembly.
