Halifax County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.3612866%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 4967 people in Halifax County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 31464 people in Halifax County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 924 people in Halifax County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 37355 people in Halifax County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 2552 people in Grayson County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 12803 people in Grayson County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 178 people in Grayson County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 15533 people in Grayson County, Virginia lived in the county.

Occupied by varying cultures of indigenous peoples for thousands of years, in historic times English colonists encountered Siouan-speaking Native Americans. Halifax County was established in 1752 by English colonists from Lunenburg County. The county was named for George Montague-Dunk, 2nd Earl of Halifax.
