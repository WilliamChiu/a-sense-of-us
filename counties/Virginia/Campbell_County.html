Campbell County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -11.655527%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 6970 people in Campbell County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 43604 people in Campbell County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 504 people in Campbell County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 51078 people in Campbell County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 2466 people in Brunswick County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 12784 people in Brunswick County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 2184 people in Brunswick County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 17434 people in Brunswick County, Virginia lived in the county.

The area that would become Campbell County was first settled by Europeans, initially Scotch-Irish Presbyterians, in the late 1730s. After nearly fifty years of immigration and development, the newly formed county was established in 1782 from part of Bedford County. Campbell County was named for Revolutionary War hero, General William Campbell, who is known for the 1780 Battle of Kings Mountain. The independent city of Lynchburg, Virginia was subsequently formed from the county in 1786.
