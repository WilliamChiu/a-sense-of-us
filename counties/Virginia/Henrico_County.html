Henrico County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -27.758408%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 46867 people in Henrico County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 211621 people in Henrico County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 3812 people in Henrico County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 262300 people in Henrico County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 1264 people in Greensville County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7452 people in Greensville County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 3527 people in Greensville County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 12243 people in Greensville County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 1611, Thomas Dale founded the Citie of Henricus on an island in the James River that is now called Farrar's Island. Henricus was named for Henry Frederick, Prince of Wales and was destroyed during the Indian massacre of 1622.
