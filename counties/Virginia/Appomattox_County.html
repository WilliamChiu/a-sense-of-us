Appomattox County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.7515907%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 13705 people in Appomattox County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 281 people in Alleghany County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 16250 people in Alleghany County, Virginia lived in the county.

Appomattox County was formed in 1845 from Buckingham, Prince Edward, Campbell and Charlotte counties. In 1848, another part from Campbell County was added. It was named for the Appomattox River, which in turn was named for the Appamatuck, a historic Native American tribes in Virginia of the Algonquian-speaking Powhatan Confederacy.
