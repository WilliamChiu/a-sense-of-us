Pittsylvania County, Virginia

In 2000, 7252 people in Pittsylvania County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 54098 people in Pittsylvania County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 4374 people in Orange County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 28506 people in Orange County, Virginia lived in family households.

Originally "Pittsylvania" was a name suggested for an unrealized British colony located primarily in what is now West Virginia. Pittsylvania County would not have been within this proposed colony, subsequently known as Vandalia.
