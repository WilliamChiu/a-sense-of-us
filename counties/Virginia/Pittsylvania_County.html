Pittsylvania County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.1627555%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 7252 people in Pittsylvania County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 54098 people in Pittsylvania County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 395 people in Pittsylvania County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 61745 people in Pittsylvania County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 4374 people in Orange County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 28506 people in Orange County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 601 people in Orange County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 33481 people in Orange County, Virginia lived in the county.

Originally "Pittsylvania" was a name suggested for an unrealized British colony located primarily in what is now West Virginia. Pittsylvania County would not have been within this proposed colony, subsequently known as Vandalia.
