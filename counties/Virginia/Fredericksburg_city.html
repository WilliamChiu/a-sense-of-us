Fredericksburg city, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 13.286209%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 5510 people in Fredericksburg city, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 11450 people in Fredericksburg city, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 2319 people in Fredericksburg city, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 19279 people in Fredericksburg city, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 2293 people in Falls Church city, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 9997 people in Falls Church city, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 42 people in Falls Church city, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 12332 people in Falls Church city, Virginia lived in the county.

At the time of European encounter, the inhabitants of the area that became Fredericksburg were a Siouan-speaking tribe called the Manahoac. English colonists recorded the name of the Manahoac village there as ''Mahaskahod''.
