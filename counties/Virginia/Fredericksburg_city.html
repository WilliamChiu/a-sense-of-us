Fredericksburg city, Virginia

In 2000, 5510 people in Fredericksburg city, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 11450 people in Fredericksburg city, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 2293 people in Falls Church city, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 9997 people in Falls Church city, Virginia lived in family households.

At the time of European encounter, the inhabitants of the area that became Fredericksburg were a Siouan-speaking tribe called the Manahoac. English colonists recorded the name of the Manahoac village there as ''Mahaskahod''.
