Russell County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.5199413%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 3271 people in Russell County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 25547 people in Russell County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 1490 people in Russell County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 30308 people in Russell County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 13632 people in Roanoke County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 76416 people in Roanoke County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 2328 people in Roanoke County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 92376 people in Roanoke County, Virginia lived in the county.

On January 2, 1786, Russell County was established from a section of Washington County. L.P. Summers, a Washington County historian later wrote, "Washington County lost a great extent of country and many valuable citizens when Russell County was formed." The county was named for Culpeper County native Colonel William Russell. The first court met in May 1786 in the Castle's Woods settlement (present-day Castlewood) in the house of William Robinson. Later, a new place was built to house the County Seat. The structure used as a courthouse still stands, and is referred to as "The Old Courthouse." The present Courthouse, located in Lebanon, has been in use since 1874.
