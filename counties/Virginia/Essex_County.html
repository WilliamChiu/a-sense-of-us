Essex County, Virginia

In 2000, 1519 people in Essex County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 8310 people in Essex County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1488 people in Cumberland County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 8527 people in Cumberland County, Virginia lived in family households.

Essex County was established in 1692 from the old Rappahannock County, Virginia (not to be confused with the present day Rappahannock County, Virginia). The county is named for either the shire or county in England, or for the Earl of Essex.
