Smyth County, Virginia

In 2000, 4375 people in Smyth County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 27621 people in Smyth County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 4105 people in Russell County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 24358 people in Russell County, Virginia lived in family households.

Smyth County was formed on February 23, 1832, from Washington and Wythe counties. The county is named after Alexander Smyth, a general during the War of 1812 who was elected to the state Senate, House of Delegates, and as a Representative to the United States Congress.
