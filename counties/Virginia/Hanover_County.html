Hanover County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.7582536%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 8080 people in Hanover County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 76117 people in Hanover County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 2123 people in Hanover County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 86320 people in Hanover County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 2114 people in Greene County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 16152 people in Greene County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 137 people in Greene County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 18403 people in Greene County, Virginia lived in the county.

Located in the Piedmont of Virginia, Hanover County was created on November 26, 1719, from the area of New Kent County called St. Paul's Parish. It was named for the Electorate of Hanover in Germany, because King George I of Great Britain was Elector of Hanover at the time. The county was developed by planters moving west from the Virginia tidewater, where soils had been exhausted by tobacco monoculture.
