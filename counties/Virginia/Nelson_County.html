Nelson County, Virginia

In 2000, 2059 people in Nelson County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 12180 people in Nelson County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 5317 people in Mecklenburg County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 25670 people in Mecklenburg County, Virginia lived in family households.

At the time of English settlement in Virginia in the 17th century, the inhabitants of what became Nelson County were a Siouan-speaking tribe known as the Nahyssan. They were probably connected to the Manahoac.
