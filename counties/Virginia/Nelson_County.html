Nelson County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -3.9468944%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2059 people in Nelson County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 12180 people in Nelson County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 206 people in Nelson County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 14445 people in Nelson County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 5317 people in Mecklenburg County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 25670 people in Mecklenburg County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1740 people in Mecklenburg County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 32727 people in Mecklenburg County, Virginia lived in the county.

At the time of English settlement in Virginia in the 17th century, the inhabitants of what became Nelson County were a Siouan-speaking tribe known as the Nahyssan. They were probably connected to the Manahoac.
