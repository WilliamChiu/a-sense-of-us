Charlotte County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.056028366%. This change is reasionably similar to that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 1716 people in Charlotte County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10525 people in Charlotte County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 231 people in Charlotte County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 12472 people in Charlotte County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 3597 people in Caroline County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 24435 people in Caroline County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 513 people in Caroline County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 28545 people in Caroline County, Virginia lived in the county.

Charlotte County was established in 1764 from part of Lunenburg County. The new county was named in honor of Charlotte of Mecklenburg-Strelitz, the Queen and wife of King George III of England.
