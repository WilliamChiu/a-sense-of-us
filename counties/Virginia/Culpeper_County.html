Culpeper County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 3.7210703%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 3870 people in Culpeper County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 28677 people in Culpeper County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 1715 people in Culpeper County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 34262 people in Culpeper County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 36603 people in Chesterfield County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 274982 people in Chesterfield County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 4651 people in Chesterfield County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 316236 people in Chesterfield County, Virginia lived in the county.

At the time of European encounter, the inhabitants of the area that became Culpeper County were a Siouan-speaking sub-group of the Manahoac tribe called the Tegninateo. Culpeper County was established in 1749 from Orange County. The county is named for Thomas Culpeper. During the Civil War the Battle of Cedar Mountain took place on August 9, 1862 and the Battle of Brandy Station on June 9, 1863, in Culpeper County.
