Gloucester County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.6769171%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 3958 people in Gloucester County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 30488 people in Gloucester County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 334 people in Gloucester County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 34780 people in Gloucester County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 7994 people in Franklin County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1472 people in Franklin County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 56159 people in Franklin County, Virginia lived in the county.

The written history of Gloucester County began soon after the English settlement of Jamestown in 1607. Before then, the area was inhabited for thousands of years by hunter-gatherer indigenous peoples; artifacts have been dated to at least the late Woodland Period.
