Spotsylvania County, Virginia

In 2000, 8630 people in Spotsylvania County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 81211 people in Spotsylvania County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 6632 people in Shenandoah County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 34934 people in Shenandoah County, Virginia lived in family households.

At the time of European encounter, the inhabitants of the area that became Spotsylvania County were a Siouan-speaking tribe called the Manahoac.
