King and Queen County, Virginia

In 2000, 925 people in King and Queen County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 5705 people in King and Queen County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 416 people in Highland County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 1905 people in Highland County, Virginia lived in family households.

King and Queen County was established in 1691 from New Kent County. The county is named for King William III and Queen Mary II of England. King and Queen County is notable as one of the few counties in the United States of America to have recorded a larger population in the 1790 census than in the 2000 one.
