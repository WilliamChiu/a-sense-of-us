Dickenson County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.6677985%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2011 people in Dickenson County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14247 people in Dickenson County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 137 people in Dickenson County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 16395 people in Dickenson County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 787 people in Craig County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 4394 people in Craig County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 9 people in Craig County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 5190 people in Craig County, Virginia lived in the county.

Dickenson County, formed in 1880 from parts of Buchanan County, Russell County, and Wise County, is Virginia's youngest county. It was named for William J. Dickenson, delegate to the Virginia General Assembly from Russell County, 1859–1861, 1865–1867, and 1877-1882.
