Madison County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.3983965%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1433 people in Madison County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10868 people in Madison County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 219 people in Madison County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 12520 people in Madison County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 31333 people in Loudoun County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 279806 people in Loudoun County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1172 people in Loudoun County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 312311 people in Loudoun County, Virginia lived in the county.

Madison County was established in December 1792, created from Orange County. The county is named for the Madison family that owned land along the Rapidan River. President James Madison is a descendant of that family.
