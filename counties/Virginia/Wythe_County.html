Wythe County, Virginia

In 2000, 3867 people in Wythe County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 23322 people in Wythe County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 8626 people in Washington County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 44623 people in Washington County, Virginia lived in family households.

Wythe County was formed from Montgomery County in 1790. It was named after George Wythe, the first Virginian signer of the Declaration of Independence. During the Civil War the Battle of Cove Mountain was fought in the county.
