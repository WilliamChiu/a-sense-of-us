Wythe County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.5016139%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 3867 people in Wythe County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 23322 people in Wythe County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 410 people in Wythe County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 27599 people in Wythe County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 8626 people in Washington County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 44623 people in Washington County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1627 people in Washington County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 54876 people in Washington County, Virginia lived in the county.

Wythe County was formed from Montgomery County in 1790. It was named after George Wythe, the first Virginian signer of the Declaration of Independence. During the Civil War the Battle of Cove Mountain was fought in the county.
