Tazewell County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.9674728%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 5592 people in Tazewell County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 38187 people in Tazewell County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 819 people in Tazewell County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 44598 people in Tazewell County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 11086 people in Stafford County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 114282 people in Stafford County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 3593 people in Stafford County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 128961 people in Stafford County, Virginia lived in the county.

Before the arrival of pioneers, Tazewell County was a hunting ground for Native Americans. Although rare in the eastern United States, there are petroglyphs near the summit of Paintlick Mountain.
