Household Populations

Norfolk city, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 8.00283%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 45494 people in Norfolk city, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 165620 people in Norfolk city, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 23289 people in Norfolk city, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 234403 people in Norfolk city, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 2805 people in Martinsville city, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 10639 people in Martinsville city, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 377 people in Martinsville city, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 13821 people in Martinsville city, Virginia lived in the county.

In 1619, the Governor for the Virginia Colony, Sir George Yeardley incorporated four jurisdictions, termed ''citties,'' for the developed portion of the colony. These formed the basis for colonial representative government in the newly minted House of Burgesses. What would become Norfolk was put under the Elizabeth Cittie incorporation.
