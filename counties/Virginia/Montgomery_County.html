Montgomery County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 11.124635%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 24033 people in Montgomery County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 50436 people in Montgomery County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 9160 people in Montgomery County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 83629 people in Montgomery County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 1418 people in Mathews County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7466 people in Mathews County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 94 people in Mathews County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 8978 people in Mathews County, Virginia lived in the county.

Montgomery County was established in 1777 from Fincastle County. The county is named for Richard Montgomery, an American Revolutionary War general killed in 1775 while attempting to capture Quebec City, Canada.
