Montgomery County, Virginia

In 2000, 24033 people in Montgomery County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 50436 people in Montgomery County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1418 people in Mathews County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7466 people in Mathews County, Virginia lived in family households.

Montgomery County was established in 1777 from Fincastle County. The county is named for Richard Montgomery, an American Revolutionary War general killed in 1775 while attempting to capture Quebec City, Canada.
