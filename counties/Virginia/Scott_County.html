Scott County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 0.691247%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 3021 people in Scott County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 20039 people in Scott County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 343 people in Scott County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 23403 people in Scott County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 3965 people in Rockbridge County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 18167 people in Rockbridge County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 175 people in Rockbridge County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 22307 people in Rockbridge County, Virginia lived in the county.

The early settlers found evidence of a former native village at the mouth of Stony Creek on the Clinch river. Thomas McCulloch was the first white settler within the county, in 1769. Daniel Boone commanded several forts located here in 1774 during Dunmore's War, and several more were built in successive years. Even so, the Chickamauga Cherokee leader Bob Benge remained active in the area throughout this time, during the Chickamauga wars, up until he was killed in 1794. By the times houses were built in the 1790s, the largely Scots-Irish population had increased by reason of its situation along the Wilderness Road. After Scott County was formed in 1814, the first court took place in 1815, and the first public schools in 1870.
