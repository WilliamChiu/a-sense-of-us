Carroll County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -12.03484%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 3783 people in Carroll County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 24953 people in Carroll County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 509 people in Carroll County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 29245 people in Carroll County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 2224 people in Buckingham County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 12596 people in Buckingham County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 2326 people in Buckingham County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 17146 people in Buckingham County, Virginia lived in the county.

The first European settlers arrived in the region in the mid 18th century. These were primarily Scotch-Irish pioneers, who were used to high mountain altitudes. However, early settlement was slow, mostly due to the poor agricultural soil of the area. As a result, lead mining was one of the first economic activities in the region.
