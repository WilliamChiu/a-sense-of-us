Washington County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -19.33447%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 6919 people in Washington County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 42842 people in Washington County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 1342 people in Washington County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 51103 people in Washington County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 1579 people in Sussex County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 7915 people in Sussex County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 2593 people in Sussex County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 12087 people in Sussex County, Virginia lived in the county.

For thousands of years, indigenous peoples of varying cultures lived in the area. At the time of European encounter, the Chiska had a chief village near what is now Saltville, destroyed by the Spaniards in 1568. The Cherokee annexed the region from the Xualae around 1671, and ceded it to the Virginia Colony in 1770 at the Treaty of Lochaber.
