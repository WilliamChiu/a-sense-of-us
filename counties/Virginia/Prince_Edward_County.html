Prince Edward County, Virginia

In 2000, 2858 people in Prince Edward County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 13109 people in Prince Edward County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 3018 people in Patrick County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 15269 people in Patrick County, Virginia lived in family households.

Prince Edward County, Virginia was formed in the Virginia Colony in 1754 from Amelia County. It was named for Prince Edward, second son of Frederick, Prince of Wales and younger brother of George III of the United Kingdom.
