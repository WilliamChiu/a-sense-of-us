Prince Edward County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 22.148777%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2858 people in Prince Edward County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 13109 people in Prince Edward County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 19720 people in Prince Edward County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 203 people in Patrick County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 18490 people in Patrick County, Virginia lived in the county.

Prince Edward County, Virginia was formed in the Virginia Colony in 1754 from Amelia County. It was named for Prince Edward, second son of Frederick, Prince of Wales and younger brother of George III of the United Kingdom.
