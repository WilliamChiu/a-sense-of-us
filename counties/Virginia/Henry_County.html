Henry County, Virginia

In 2000, 7964 people in Henry County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 49529 people in Henry County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 5615 people in Halifax County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 29888 people in Halifax County, Virginia lived in family households.

The county was established in 1777 when it was carved from Pittsylvania County. The new county was initially named Patrick Henry County in honor of Patrick Henry, who was then serving as the first Governor of Virginia after independence. Living on his 10000 acre plantation called ''Leatherwood'' (for Leatherwood Creek), Gov. Henry was a sometime resident of the county, where he had relatives already settled.
