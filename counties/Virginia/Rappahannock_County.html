Rappahannock County, Virginia

In 2000, 949 people in Rappahannock County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6024 people in Rappahannock County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 3027 people in Prince George County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 27926 people in Prince George County, Virginia lived in family households.

Rappahannock County was founded by an act of the Virginia General Assembly in 1833, based on the growing population's need to have better access to a county seat. The county's land was carved from Culpeper County. Rappahannock county was named for the river that separates it from Fauquier County.
