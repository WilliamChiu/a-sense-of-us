Grayson County, Virginia

In 2000, 2465 people in Grayson County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14289 people in Grayson County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 2678 people in Giles County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 14472 people in Giles County, Virginia lived in family households.

Grayson County was founded in 1793 from part of Wythe County. It was named for William Grayson, delegate to the Continental Congress from 1784 to 1787 and one of the first two U.S. Senators from Virginia.
