Caroline County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.3152173%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2476 people in Caroline County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 19067 people in Caroline County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 578 people in Caroline County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 22121 people in Caroline County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 3483 people in Buchanan County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 19442 people in Buchanan County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1173 people in Buchanan County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 24098 people in Buchanan County, Virginia lived in the county.

Caroline County was established in the British Colony of Virginia in 1728 from Essex, King and Queen, and King William counties. It was named for Caroline of Ansbach, the wife of King George II of Great Britain, who had recently taken the British throne at the time.
