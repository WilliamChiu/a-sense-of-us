Shenandoah County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -0.471884%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 5055 people in Shenandoah County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 29507 people in Shenandoah County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 513 people in Shenandoah County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 35075 people in Shenandoah County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 9814 people in Rockingham County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 65029 people in Rockingham County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1471 people in Rockingham County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 76314 people in Rockingham County, Virginia lived in the county.

The Senedos, possibly an Iroquoian group, are thought to have occupied the area at one time, until they were said to have been slaughtered by the Catawba in the later 17th century. The name of both the Valley and of the County is most likely connected with this Native American group. It has also been attributed to General George Washington naming it in honor of John Skenando, an Oneida chief from New York who helped gain support of Oneida and Tuscarora warriors to aid the rebel colonists during the American Revolutionary War.
