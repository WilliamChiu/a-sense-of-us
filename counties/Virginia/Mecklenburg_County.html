Mecklenburg County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -4.6975136%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 4566 people in Mecklenburg County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 26245 people in Mecklenburg County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 1569 people in Mecklenburg County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 32380 people in Mecklenburg County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 1926 people in Lunenburg County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 9785 people in Lunenburg County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1203 people in Lunenburg County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 12914 people in Lunenburg County, Virginia lived in the county.

Mecklenburg County was organized on March 1, 1765, having split from Lunenburg County in 1764 as the result of the passage of an act by the Virginia General Assembly. Due to new settlement and population increases in the area, the legislature divided Lunenburg into three counties: Lunenburg, Charlotte, and Mecklenburg. It was named after Queen Charlotte of Mecklenburg-Strelitz.
