Augusta County, Virginia

In 2000, 7054 people in Augusta County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 56562 people in Augusta County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 4484 people in Amherst County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 26331 people in Amherst County, Virginia lived in family households.

Augusta County was formed in 1738 from Orange County, although, because few people lived there, the county government was not organized until 1745. It was named for Augusta of Saxe-Gotha, Princess of Wales and mother of the future King George III of the United Kingdom.
