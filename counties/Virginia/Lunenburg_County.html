Lunenburg County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 2.6306748%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1845 people in Lunenburg County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 10110 people in Lunenburg County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 1191 people in Lunenburg County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 13146 people in Lunenburg County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 3754 people in Lee County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 20127 people in Lee County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1706 people in Lee County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 25587 people in Lee County, Virginia lived in the county.

Lunenburg County was established on May 1, 1746, from Brunswick County. The county is named for the former Duchy of Brunswick-Lunenburg in Germany, because one of the titles also carried by Britain's Hanoverian kings was Duke of Brunswick-Lunenburg. It is nicknamed "The Old Free State" because during the buildup of the Civil War, it let Virginia know the county would break off if the state did not join The Confederacy.
