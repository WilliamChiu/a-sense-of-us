Greensville County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 27.426243%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1121 people in Greensville County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 7364 people in Greensville County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 3075 people in Greensville County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 11560 people in Greensville County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 2206 people in Goochland County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 18106 people in Goochland County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1405 people in Goochland County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 21717 people in Goochland County, Virginia lived in the county.

Greensville County was established in 1781 from Brunswick County. The county is probably named for Sir Richard Grenville, leader of the settlement on Roanoke Island, 1585. There is also belief that it may be named after Nathanael Greene, a Major General of the Continental Army and one of George Washington's brightest officers.
