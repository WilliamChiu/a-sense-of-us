Lexington city, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 26.194609%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 1570 people in Lexington city, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 3028 people in Lexington city, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 2269 people in Lexington city, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 6867 people in Lexington city, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 17201 people in Harrisonburg city, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 24130 people in Harrisonburg city, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 7583 people in Harrisonburg city, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 48914 people in Harrisonburg city, Virginia lived in the county.

Lexington was named in 1778. It was one of the first of what would be many American places named after Lexington, Massachusetts, known for being the place at which the first shot was fired in the American Revolution.
