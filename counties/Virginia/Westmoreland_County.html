Westmoreland County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -3.04026%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 2550 people in Westmoreland County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 14063 people in Westmoreland County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 105 people in Westmoreland County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 16718 people in Westmoreland County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 6494 people in Tazewell County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 36939 people in Tazewell County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 1645 people in Tazewell County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 45078 people in Tazewell County, Virginia lived in the county.

As originally established by the House of Burgesses from Northumberland County in 1653, the territory of Westmoreland County encompassed much of what later became the various counties and cities of Northern Virginia, including the city of Alexandria, Arlington County, Fairfax County, and Prince William County. These were part of Westmoreland until 1664, when Stafford County was formed.
