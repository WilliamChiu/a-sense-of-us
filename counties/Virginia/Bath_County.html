Bath County, Virginia

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 4.4686317%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 679 people in Bath County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 4135 people in Bath County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2000, 234 people in Bath County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 5048 people in Bath County, Virginia lived in the county.

In 2010, 1984 people in Appomattox County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 12933 people in Appomattox County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 56 people in Appomattox County, Virginia lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 14973 people in Appomattox County, Virginia lived in the county.

Bath County was established on December 14, 1790 from sections of Augusta, Botetourt, and Greenbrier counties. Due to the many mineral springs found in the area, the county was named for the English spa and resort city of Bath. The area's economy has focused on tourism and travel since the 1700s, particularly when The Homestead Resort was built in 1766.
