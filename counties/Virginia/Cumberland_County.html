Cumberland County, Virginia

In 2000, 1238 people in Cumberland County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 7743 people in Cumberland County, Virginia lived in family households.

In 2010, 2095 people in Clarke County, Virginia lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 11684 people in Clarke County, Virginia lived in family households.

Cumberland County was established in 1749 from Goochland County. The county is named for William Augustus, Duke of Cumberland, second son of King George II of Great Britain. Cumberland County was also home to the Fleming family, which included Judge John Fleming and his son Judge William Fleming.
