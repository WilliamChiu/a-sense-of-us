Norfolk County, Massachusetts

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -10.786713%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 104317 people in Norfolk County, Massachusetts lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 17432 people in Norfolk County, Massachusetts lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 650308 people in Norfolk County, Massachusetts lived in the county.

In 2010, 102805 people in Hampshire County, Massachusetts lived in family households.

In 2010, 20832 people in Hampshire County, Massachusetts lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 158080 people in Hampshire County, Massachusetts lived in the county.

Norfolk County, Massachusetts was created on March 26, 1793 by legislation signed by Gov. John Hancock. Most of the towns were originally part of Suffolk County, Massachusetts. The towns of Dorchester and Roxbury were part of Norfolk County when it was created but, as Boston annexed each town, they became part of Suffolk County again. Hingham and Hull were originally part of the Norfolk County legislation but petitioned to remain in Suffolk county and in June 1793 their removal to Norfolk county was repealed. Later, in 1803, they were moved into Plymouth County, Massachusetts.
