Household Populations

Berkshire County, Massachusetts

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 3.2636404%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 24858 people in Berkshire County, Massachusetts lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 103963 people in Berkshire County, Massachusetts lived in family households.

In 2000, 6132 people in Berkshire County, Massachusetts lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 134953 people in Berkshire County, Massachusetts lived in the county.

In 2010, 9394 people in Worcester County, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 41325 people in Worcester County, Maryland lived in family households.

In 2010, 735 people in Worcester County, Maryland lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 51454 people in Worcester County, Maryland lived in the county.

The Mahican (Muh-he-ka-neew) Native American tribe lived in the area that now makes up Berkshire County until the early 18th century, when the first English settlers and frontiersmen appeared and began setting up farms and homesteads. On April 25, 1724, “The English finally paid the Indians 460 pounds, 3 barrels of cider, and 30 quarts of rum for what is today Berkshire County.” This deal did not include modern Sheffield, Stockbridge, Richmond, and Lenox, which were added later. Berkshire County remained part of Hampshire County until 1760.
