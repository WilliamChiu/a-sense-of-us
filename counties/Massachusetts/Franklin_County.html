Franklin County, Massachusetts

In 2000, 14217 people in Franklin County, Massachusetts lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 55912 people in Franklin County, Massachusetts lived in family households.

In 2010, 87677 people in Bristol County, Massachusetts lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 444740 people in Bristol County, Massachusetts lived in family households.

Franklin County was created on 24 June 1811 from the northern third of Hampshire County. It was named for Benjamin Franklin. Franklin County was abolished by state government in 1997, at the county's request.
