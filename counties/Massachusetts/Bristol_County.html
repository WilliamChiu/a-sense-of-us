Bristol County, Massachusetts

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -1.5400231%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 77332 people in Bristol County, Massachusetts lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 443672 people in Bristol County, Massachusetts lived in family households.

In 2000, 13674 people in Bristol County, Massachusetts lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 534678 people in Bristol County, Massachusetts lived in the county.

In 2010, 152026 people in Baltimore city, Maryland lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 443736 people in Baltimore city, Maryland lived in family households.

In 2010, 25199 people in Baltimore city, Maryland lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 620961 people in Baltimore city, Maryland lived in the county.

Bristol County was created by the Plymouth Colony on June 2, 1685, and named after its "shire town" (county seat), Bristol. The Plymouth Colony merged into the Massachusetts Bay Colony in 1691.
