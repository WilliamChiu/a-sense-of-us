Nantucket County, Massachusetts

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 5.2077174%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 2374 people in Nantucket County, Massachusetts lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 6386 people in Nantucket County, Massachusetts lived in family households.

In 2000, 760 people in Nantucket County, Massachusetts lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 9520 people in Nantucket County, Massachusetts lived in the county.

In 2010, 78141 people in Hampden County, Massachusetts lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 370558 people in Hampden County, Massachusetts lived in family households.

In 2010, 14791 people in Hampden County, Massachusetts lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 463490 people in Hampden County, Massachusetts lived in the county.

Nantucket probably takes its name from a Wampanoag word, transliterated variously as ''natocke'', ''nantaticu'', ''nantican'', ''nautica'' or ''natockete'', which is part of Wampanoag lore about the creation of Martha's Vineyard and Nantucket.
