Essex County, Massachusetts

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by -2.4154663%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region suffered stagnated household-population growth perhaps as a result of regional economic issues.

In 2000, 109793 people in Essex County, Massachusetts lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 596737 people in Essex County, Massachusetts lived in family households.

In 2000, 16889 people in Essex County, Massachusetts lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 723419 people in Essex County, Massachusetts lived in the county.

In 2010, 27155 people in Berkshire County, Massachusetts lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 97905 people in Berkshire County, Massachusetts lived in family households.

In 2010, 6159 people in Berkshire County, Massachusetts lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 131219 people in Berkshire County, Massachusetts lived in the county.

The county was created by the General Court of the Massachusetts Bay Colony on May 10, 1643, when it was ordered "that the whole plantation within this jurisdiction be divided into four sheires". Named after the county in England, Essex then comprised the towns of Salem, Lynn, Wenham, Ipswich, Rowley, Newbury, Gloucester, and Andover, which were subdivided over the centuries to produce the modern composition of cities and towns.
