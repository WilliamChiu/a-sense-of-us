Middlesex County, Massachusetts

From 2000 to 2010, the number of people living in households has changed by 1.5916705%. This change is a drastic departure from that of the nation. Data from this state suggests that the region's economic circumstance as well as other variables helped to increase the number of household-population.

In 2000, 265702 people in Middlesex County, Massachusetts lived in nonfamily households.

In 2000, 1146804 people in Middlesex County, Massachusetts lived in family households.

In 2000, 52890 people in Middlesex County, Massachusetts lived in group quarters.

In 2000, 1465396 people in Middlesex County, Massachusetts lived in the county.

In 2010, 15655 people in Franklin County, Massachusetts lived in nonfamily households.

In 2010, 54236 people in Franklin County, Massachusetts lived in family households.

In 2010, 1481 people in Franklin County, Massachusetts lived in group quarters.

In 2010, 71372 people in Franklin County, Massachusetts lived in the county.

The county was created by the Massachusetts General Court on May 10, 1643, when it was ordered "that the whole plantation within this jurisdiction be divided into four sheires". Middlesex initially contained Charlestown, Cambridge, Watertown, Sudbury, Concord, Woburn, Medford, Wayland, and Reading. In the late 19th century and early 20th century, Boston annexed several adjacent cities and towns including Charlestown and Brighton from Middlesex County, resulting in an enlargement of Suffolk County.
